# Conservation and Restoration B.A.

## Program Overview

The Bachelor's program in Conservation and Restoration at FH Potsdam trains students to preserve and restore cultural heritage objects. The program offers three specialized fields of study: Wood, Stone, and Wall Painting.

## Fields of Study

- **Wood**: Conservation and restoration of wooden objects, furniture, and wooden artworks
- **Stone**: Conservation and restoration of stone monuments, sculptures, and architectural elements
- **Wall Painting**: Conservation and restoration of wall paintings and architectural surfaces

## Study Structure

| Semester | Focus |
|----------|-------|
| 1-2 | Foundations in conservation science, art history, and material analysis |
| 3-4 | Specialization in chosen field of study |
| 5-7 | Advanced practical work and projects |
| 8 | Bachelor thesis |

## Key Features

- **Three specializations**: Wood, Stone, Wall Painting
- **Scientific approach**: Material analysis and conservation science
- **Hands-on learning**: Workshop-based practical training
- **Heritage partnerships**: Collaboration with museums and heritage institutions
- **Long tradition**: One of the oldest conservation programs in Germany

## Focus Areas

- Conservation science and material analysis
- Traditional craft techniques
- Preventive conservation
- Art history and cultural heritage
- Documentation and research methods

## Admission

- General higher education entrance qualification (Abitur)
- Pre-study internship (Vorpraktikum) in a conservation-related field
- Aptitude assessment process

## Career Paths

Graduates work in:
- Museums and galleries
- Cultural heritage authorities (Denkmalschutz)
- Private conservation studios
- Research institutions
- Ecclesiastical institutions
- International heritage organizations
