# Conservation and Restoration M.A.

## Program Overview

The Master's program in Conservation and Restoration at FH Potsdam is a research-oriented consecutive program that deepens the knowledge and skills acquired in the Bachelor program. It focuses on advanced conservation methods, scientific analysis, and heritage management.

## Study Structure

| Semester | Focus |
|----------|-------|
| 1 | Advanced conservation methods and research approaches |
| 2 | Specialization and research project |
| 3 | Master project and research colloquium |
| 4 | Master thesis |

## Key Features

- **Research-oriented**: Strong emphasis on conservation research
- **Advanced methods**: Scientific analysis and documentation
- **Specialization**: Deepening expertise in chosen conservation field
- **Heritage management**: Understanding of institutional and policy contexts
- **Practical application**: Real-world conservation projects

## Focus Areas

- Advanced conservation techniques
- Conservation science and material research
- Preventive conservation strategies
- Cultural heritage management
- Documentation and digital methods
- Interdisciplinary collaboration

## Admission

- Bachelor's degree in Conservation and Restoration (210 ECTS, or 180 ECTS with professional experience)
- Proof of practical conservation experience
- Application through FH Potsdam portal

## Career Paths

Graduates work in:
- Senior conservation positions in museums
- Cultural heritage authorities
- Research institutions
- Private conservation studios (senior level)
- International heritage organizations (UNESCO, ICOMOS)
- Teaching and academia
