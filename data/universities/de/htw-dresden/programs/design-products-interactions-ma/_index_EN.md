# Design: Products and Interactions (M.A.)

## Program Overview

The **Master Design: Products and Interactions** at HTW Dresden is a consecutive master's program (konsekutiver Master) spanning 3 semesters (90 ECTS), building on the university's own bachelor program. The program has been accepting students since the 2022 summer semester, with **summer semester (April) intake only**.

The program's core focus is on **designing hybrid products and applications** (Hybride Produkte und Anwendungen), deeply integrating product design, interaction design, and user experience research to prepare students for design challenges at the intersection of digital and physical realms.

## Curriculum Structure

### Semester 1: Advanced Studies
- Product design advanced projects
- Interaction design advanced projects
- Design research methodology

### Semester 2: Specialization Projects
- Interdisciplinary design projects
- User experience research
- Design innovation and strategy

### Semester 3: Master Thesis
- Masterarbeit (master's thesis)
- Thesis defense

## Admission Requirements

### Prerequisites
- Bachelor's degree in a design-related field (210 ECTS)
- Previous studies in design (e.g., product design, communication design, interaction design)

### Application Materials
1. Portfolio (PDF format)
2. Letter of motivation (Motivationsschreiben)
3. Bachelor's degree certificate and transcripts
4. CV (Lebenslauf)
5. Language certificate (if applicable)

### Selection Process
1. Document review
2. Portfolio assessment
3. Interview

## Application Timeline
- **Application period**: Typically December to January 15 of the year before enrollment
- **Interviews**: January - February
- **Admission notification**: March
- **Start of studies**: April (summer semester)

## Faculty

The master's program involves largely the same faculty as the bachelor program, with particular emphasis on:
- **Marcus Keichel** — Design research and strategic design
- **Sebastian Schmieg** — Interface design
- **Dr. Florian A. Schmidt** — Design concepts and media theory
- **Karen Donndorf / Christian Scholz** — Product design
