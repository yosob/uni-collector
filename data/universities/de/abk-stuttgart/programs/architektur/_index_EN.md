## Program Overview

The Architecture program at ABK Stuttgart teaches architecture within an art and design context, emphasizing artistic and creative dimensions of architectural design. As an architecture course within an art academy, the program highlights the cultural dimension of architecture and design experimentation, preparing students to become architects with an artistic vision.

The curriculum covers architectural design, urban design, architectural history and theory, building construction and materials, and more. Students work in a studio-based learning environment, engaging in project-based learning to explore all aspects of architectural design in depth.

## Curriculum

- Foundations and advanced architectural design
- Urban design and planning
- Architectural history and theory
- Building construction and materials science
- Architectural representation (drawing, modeling, digital media)
- Architecture and society
- Energy and sustainability
- Design methods and processes

## Admission Requirements

### Academic Requirements
- General higher education entrance qualification (Allgemeine Hochschulreife)
- Or subject-specific higher education entrance qualification (Fachgebundene Hochschulreife)

### Internship Requirements
- Proof of relevant pre-study internship required

### Portfolio Requirements
- **Quantity**: Approximately 20 works
- **Type**: Architecture-related works, drawings, sketches, model photos, design projects, etc.
- **Focus**: Demonstrate design thinking and creative process

## Application Process

### Application Timeline
- **Application window**: Typically April–May
- **Entrance exam**: Typically June–July

### Application Steps
1. Apply online via [ABK-Campus-System](https://campus.abk-stuttgart.de/)
2. Submit portfolio (digital version)
3. If pre-selected, attend on-site entrance examination
4. Register online after admission
