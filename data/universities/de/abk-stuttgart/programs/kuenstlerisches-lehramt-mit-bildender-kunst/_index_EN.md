## Program Overview

The Art Education program trains future art teachers, equipping students with both artistic creation skills and art education competencies. The program provides in-depth artistic practice training within the artistic environment of ABK Stuttgart, combined with educational science courses.

During the B.F.A. phase, students receive comprehensive art training (painting, sculpture, printmaking, new media, etc.) and study foundations of pedagogy and psychology. The M.Ed. phase focuses on teaching methods and school practice. Graduates are qualified to teach art at Gymnasium-level secondary schools.

## Curriculum

### Art Practice
- Painting (Malerei)
- Sculpture (Bildhauerei)
- Printmaking (Graphik)
- New media art
- Intermedia creation

### Art Theory and Education
- Art history (Kunstgeschichte)
- Art education theory (Kunstpädagogik)
- Art science (Kunstwissenschaft)
- Pedagogy (Pädagogik)
- Psychology (Psychologie)

### Teaching Practice
- School internship (Schulpraktikum)
- Subject didactics seminar (Fachdidaktik)

## Admission Requirements

### Academic Requirements
- General higher education entrance qualification (Allgemeine Hochschulreife)
- Or subject-specific higher education entrance qualification (Fachgebundene Hochschulreife)

### Portfolio Requirements
- **Quantity**: Approximately 20 works
- **Type**: Free artistic works, painting, sculpture, printmaking, etc.
- **Focus**: Demonstrate artistic creation ability and artistic understanding

## Application Process

### Application Timeline
- **Application window**: April 13 – April 30
- **Entrance exam**: June 22 – June 26

### Application Steps
1. Apply online via [ABK-Campus-System](https://campus.abk-stuttgart.de/)

3. If pre-selected, attend on-site entrance examination
4. Register online after admission
