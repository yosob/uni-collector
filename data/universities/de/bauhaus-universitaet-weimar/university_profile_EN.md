# Bauhaus-Universität Weimar — University Profile

## University Overview

**Bauhaus-Universität Weimar** is a public university in Thuringia, Germany, with deep roots in the Bauhaus tradition. Its Faculty of Art and Design offers a distinctive project-based education model (*Weimarer Modell*) emphasizing interdisciplinary work, artistic freedom, and hands-on making. The university combines creative disciplines with architecture, civil engineering, and media studies.

- **Founded:** 1860 (as Großherzoglich-Sächsische Kunstschule); Bauhaus era 1919–1925
- **Location:** Weimar, Thuringia, Germany
- **Type:** Universität (Public University)
- **Website:** https://www.uni-weimar.de
- **Application Portal:** https://www.uni-weimar.de/de/studium/bewerbung/

## Program Details

### Product Design (BA)

Project-based program following the *Weimarer Modell*. Four professorships cover Design & Management, Transformative Design, Experimental Industrial Design, and Emerging Technologies. Focus areas include industrial design, transformation design, sustainable product cultures, digital fabrication, and design theory. Well-equipped workshops for wood, metal, plastic, ceramics, and digital fabrication.

### Product Design (MA)

Advanced 4-semester design research building on the Bachelor. Emphasis on design theory, research methods, and self-directed projects. Requires a first degree in design with above-average grades. Focus areas mirror the BA with added research depth.

### Visual Communication (BA)

Comprehensive program covering typography, photography, editorial design, digital media, film/animation, illustration, and visual systems. Strong emphasis on cross-media and practical project work.

### Visual Communication (MA)

Concentrated 2-semester advanced study covering graphic design, typography, information design, visual culture, and communication aesthetics. Requires a first degree in visual communication or related field.

### Media Art and Design (BFA)

Interdisciplinary media program spanning media environments, sound design, interface design, film/animation, experimental radio, and web/graphic design. 8-semester program with hands-on media production.

### Media Art (MFA)

Advanced 4-semester program in media art, media design, interface design, and acoustic ecologies. Builds on a first degree in media art/design or related field. Updated deadline: March 31 (previously May 31).

### Fine Art (Diplom)

Comprehensive 9-semester fine art program equivalent to a Master's degree. Focuses on experimental painting/drawing, sculpture/installation, video/new media, performance, photography, and curating.

### Public Art and New Artistic Strategies (MFA)

International English-language program focused on art in public spaces. Covers public art interventions, participatory art, urbanism, new media, and commemorative culture. Requires a first degree (minimum 8 semesters) from an art academy. Deadline: May 15 (March 31 for non-EU).

### Doctoral Studies

Research-based Ph.D. in art and design disciplines (artistic research, design research, practice-based research). Requires a qualified Master's degree, supervision agreement, and acceptance by the doctoral committee.

## Application Process Summary

1. **Online Application:** via Bauhaus.CampusPortal
2. **Registration:** Opens early February each year
3. **Deadlines:** Most programs — registration by March 31, document upload by April 30 (varies by program)
4. **Documents:** University entrance qualification, CV, portfolio/work samples, motivation letter
5. **Aptitude Test:** Most programs require homework assignment + practical exam + interview
6. **Language Requirement:** German DSH-2 / TestDaF TDN 4 (CEFR C1) for most programs; some Master's courses in English
