# Medienkunst M.F.A. (Media Art)

## Program Overview

The Master of Fine Arts in Medienkunst (Media Art) at Bauhaus-Universität Weimar is offered by the Faculty of Art and Design (Fakultät Kunst und Gestaltung).

The program duration is **2 semesters** for students entering with a 240 ECTS Bachelor degree, or **4 semesters** for students with a 180 ECTS Bachelor degree.

The program follows the **Weimarer Modell** (Weimar Model) of project-based, interdisciplinary study. Students deepen their individual artistic positions in media art and design through advanced project work across six professorships.

## Professorships

The program is taught across the same six professorships as the B.F.A.:

1. **Acoustic Ecologies and Sound Studies** (Jun.-Prof. Kerstin Ergenzinger)
2. **Electroacoustic Composition and Sound Art** (Prof. Maximilian Marcoll)
3. **Experimental Radio** (Prof. Nathalie Singer)
4. **Media Environments** (Prof. Ursula Damm)
5. **Interface Design** (Prof. Martin Hesselmeier)
6. **Media Events** (Prof. Wolfgang Kissel)

## Admission

Admission requires a first academic degree (Bachelor or Diplom) in media art, media design, or a related artistic/design field with above-average grades, along with successful completion of an artistic aptitude test (Eignungsprüfung). The aptitude test involves portfolio review and an interview.

German language proficiency at C1 level (DSH-2 or TestDaF TDN 4) is required.

## Application

- **Portal:** Bauhaus.CampusPortal (digital)
- **Deadline:** May 31 (for winter semester start)
- **Start:** Winter semester only
- **Note:** Deadline is later than other programs in the faculty
