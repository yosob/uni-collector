# Media Art and Design — MFA

## Program Overview

The **MFA program Medienkunst/Mediengestaltung** (Media Art and Design) at Bauhaus-Universität Weimar is offered in two language tracks:
- **German track:** Medienkunst/Mediengestaltung (M.F.A.)
- **English track:** Media Art and Design — MAD (M.F.A.)

The program follows the **Weimarer Modell** of project-based, interdisciplinary study. Students deepen their individual artistic position across six professorships.

### Study Variants (depending on prior degree)

| Prior Degree | Duration | ECTS | Note |
|-------------|----------|------|------|
| 180 ECTS Bachelor | 4 semesters | 120 | Standard track (2 bridge semesters) |
| 210 ECTS Bachelor | 3 semesters | 90 | 1 bridge semester |
| 240 ECTS Bachelor | 2 semesters | 60 | Fast-track |

| Prof. Nathalie Singer | Experimentelles Radio / Experimental Radio |

## Admission

- First professional university degree (Bachelor or Diplom) in Media Art, Media Design or a related field
- Above-average academic performance
- Aptitude test based on artistic portfolio (personal interview usually NOT required for MFA)

### Language Requirements

| Track | German | English |
|-------|--------|---------|
| German (Medienkunst/Mediengestaltung) | C1 (DSH-2 / TestDaF TDN 4) | — |
| English (Media Art and Design / MAD) | A2 | C1 (TOEFL ≥95 / IELTS ≥7.0) |

> **Note:** Full English curriculum is available only at the Interface Design professorship. Other professorships only partially offer English courses. Higher German proficiency than A2 is strongly recommended.

## Application

- **Portal:** [Bauhaus.CampusPortal](https://campus.uni-weimar.de/bewerbung)
- **⚠️ UPDATED deadline:** Registration from **February 2**; upload deadline **March 31** (previously May 31)
- **Start:** Winter semester only
- **Enrollment deadline:** September 30

### Required Documents

1. Artistic portfolio (PDF, max 20 MB; links to interactive/time-based works)
2. Motivation letter (~1 A4 page: study/career goals, desired focus area)
3. Tabular CV (with contact, name, email, address)
4. Copies of academic degrees (uncertified OK for application)
5. Certificates of internships, list of exhibitions/works/publications (if applicable)
6. School leaving certificate
7. Photo
8. ID / passport
9. Language proficiency proof

### Aptitude Test

- Assessment based on submitted portfolio
- Personal interview usually **NOT required** for MFA applicants
- Aptitude result valid for **2 academic years**
- Can be repeated
