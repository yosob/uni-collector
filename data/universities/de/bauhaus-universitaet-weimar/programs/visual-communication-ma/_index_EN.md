# Visual Communication — MA

## Program Overview

The **Master of Arts in Visual Communication** at Bauhaus-Universität Weimar is a **2-semester** consecutive Master's program offered by the Faculty of Art and Design.

The program follows the **Weimarer Modell** of project-based study. It requires a particularly high degree of **self-initiative (Eigeninitiative)**. Master projects are based on students' own content proposals, developed in dialogue with supervising professors. Each project is supervised by at least one professor from the practice area and one from the theory area.

The program opens up new fields within Visual Culture, including analytical visualization tools (mapping, image statistics, navigation). In a complex and dynamic world, knowledge of visual codes and their design conditions has become indispensable.

## Admission

- First academic degree in visual communication, communication design, or related field with above-average grades
- Artistic aptitude test: portfolio review and interview

## Application

- **Portal:** Bauhaus.CampusPortal
- **Deadline:** March 31
- **Required:** CV, motivation letter, portfolio, degree certificate, ID/passport

## Career Perspectives

Graduates pursue graphic design, corporate identity, typography, photography, film/video, information design, data visualization, visual culture research, doctoral studies, curating, media/publishing, and university teaching.

## Tuition

No tuition fees. Semester fee of approx. €200 includes semester ticket and student services.
