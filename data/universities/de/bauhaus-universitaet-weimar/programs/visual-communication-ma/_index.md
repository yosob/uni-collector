# Visual Communication M.A. (Visuelle Kommunikation)

## Program Overview

The Master of Arts in Visual Communication at Bauhaus-Universität Weimar is a 2-semester program offered by the Faculty of Art and Design (Fakultät Kunst und Gestaltung). The program focuses on Visual Culture, Visual Studies, and Visual Research.

In a complex and dynamic world, knowledge of visual codes and their design conditions and potentials has become indispensable. Visual rhetoric and different visualization strategies accompany knowledge processes and decision-making in science, research, business, and politics. The program opens up new teaching and research fields within Visual Culture, including analytical visualization tools (mapping, image statistics, navigation).

## Study Focus Areas

- Graphic Design (Grafikdesign)
- Typography (Typografie)
- Information Design (Informationsdesign)
- Staged and Documentary Photography (inszenierte und dokumentarische Fotografie)
- Image/Text Conception (Bild/Text-Konzeption)
- Advertising (Werbung)
- Film/Video
- Visual Culture (Visuelle Kultur)

## Study Structure

The program requires a high degree of self-initiative. Master projects are based on students' content proposals, developed in dialogue with supervising professors. Each project is supervised by at least one professor from the practice area and one from the theory area.

## Admission

- **Portal:** Bauhaus.CampusPortal (digital)
- **Registration opens:** February 2
- **Deadline:** March 31 (for winter semester start)
- **Start:** Winter semester only
- **Requirements:** Bachelor degree in visual communication/design related field, portfolio, aptitude test

## Career Paths

The interdisciplinary combination of scientific and design components prepares students for academic careers, including the Ph.D. program "Kunst und Design" at the Faculty of Design.
