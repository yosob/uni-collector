# Media Art and Design — BFA

## Program Overview

The **BFA in Medienkunst/Mediengestaltung (Media Art and Design)** at Bauhaus-Universität Weimar is an **8-semester** full-time program awarding **240 ECTS credits**, offered by the Faculty of Art and Design.

The program follows the **Weimarer Modell** (Weimar Model) of project-based, interdisciplinary study. Students explore artistic and design approaches to media across six professorships — from interface design and media environments through sound art and experimental radio to media events and acoustic ecologies. The program combines artistic practice with technical and theoretical knowledge from media studies and media informatics.

> *"The responsibility of the artist is not to give answers. It is to ask questions."*
> — Robert Wilson, Guest Professor at Bauhaus-Universität Weimar (2011–2012)

## Study Structure

**Foundation Phase (Semesters 1–2):** Technical-practical fundamentals — camera work, radio play, prototyping, interface concepts. Small groups, individual projects, hands-on exercises.

**Specialized Phase (Semesters 3–6):** Thematic deepening in an artistic focus area. Content intensification, experimental forms of expression, scientific reflection through art. Elective courses from media studies, media informatics, and other departments of the faculty.

**Bachelor Thesis (Semesters 7–8):** Preparation and defense of the bachelor thesis. Graduation with the academic degree *Bachelor of Fine Arts (B.F.A.)*.

## Infrastructure

- Media House with dedicated TV studio
- Audio Production Studio
- Digital Bauhaus Lab (research and laboratory building)
- Sound Lab, Radio Studio
- Electronics Lab, Prototype Lab, 3D Print Lab
- Studios and workshops

## Admission

- **General higher education entrance qualification** (Abitur) or equivalent
- **Artistic aptitude test**: homework assignment, portfolio (up to 20 work samples), aptitude interview
- In exceptional cases of outstanding artistic talent, the higher education qualification may be waived
- **German language proficiency** at C1 level (DSH-2 / TestDaF TDN 4)

## Application

- **Portal:** Bauhaus.CampusPortal
- **Three-phase process:**
  1. **Registration:** From February 2 until March 31
  2. **Homework:** Published at the beginning of April
  3. **Complete application:** Upload by April 30 (homework + CV + school certificate + portfolio)
- **Aptitude interview:** After pre-selection by commission, with presentation of original portfolio
- **Start:** Winter semester only
- **Enrollment deadline:** By September 30 of the current year

## Tuition

No tuition fees. Semester fee of approx. **€200** includes semester ticket and student services.
