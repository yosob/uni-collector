# Medienkunst/Mediengestaltung B.F.A. (Media Art and Design)

## Program Overview

The Bachelor of Fine Arts in Medienkunst/Mediengestaltung (Media Art and Design) at Bauhaus-Universität Weimar is an 8-semester program offered by the Faculty of Art and Design (Fakultät Kunst und Gestaltung).

The program follows the **Weimarer Modell** (Weimar Model) of project-based, interdisciplinary study. Students explore artistic and design approaches to media in all its forms — from interface design and media environments to sound art, experimental radio, and media events. The program combines artistic practice with technical and theoretical knowledge across six professorships.

## Professorships

The program is taught across six professorships:

1. **Acoustic Ecologies and Sound Studies** (Jun.-Prof. Kerstin Ergenzinger)
2. **Electroacoustic Composition and Sound Art** (Prof. Maximilian Marcoll)
3. **Experimental Radio** (Prof. Nathalie Singer)
4. **Media Environments** (Prof. Ursula Damm)
5. **Interface Design** (Prof. Martin Hesselmeier)
6. **Media Events** (Prof. Wolfgang Kissel)

## Admission

Admission requires a general higher education entrance qualification (Abitur) or equivalent, along with successful completion of an artistic aptitude test (Eignungsprüfung). The aptitude test involves a homework assignment and an interview with the selection commission.

German language proficiency at C1 level (DSH-2 or TestDaF TDN 4) is required.

## Application

- **Portal:** Bauhaus.CampusPortal (digital)
- **Phase 1 — Registration:** February 2 – March 31
- **Phase 2 — Homework:** Published beginning of April
- **Phase 3 — Complete application:** Due April 30 (including homework)
- **Start:** Winter semester only
