# Doctoral Studies (Ph.D. / Dr. phil.) — Bauhaus-Universität Weimar

## Overview

The Faculty of Art and Design (Fakultät Kunst und Gestaltung) at Bauhaus-Universität Weimar offers two doctoral pathways for researchers pursuing advanced degrees in art and design:

1. **Ph.D. Programme "Kunst und Design"** — A structured, practice-based doctoral programme combining artistic/design practice with academic research, culminating in a Ph.D. thesis that consists of both a scientific and an artistic/design component evaluated in equal measure. This dual qualification is unique in Germany.

2. **Promotion Dr. phil.** — The traditional German doctoral degree (Doctor philosophiae), completed through a written dissertation, oral defense (Disputation), and publication.

---

## Track 1: Ph.D. Programme "Kunst und Design"

### Programme Description

The Ph.D. programme provides an academic degree (Ph.D.) for artists and designers who have already completed a Master's or Diplom degree at an art or design university. Per exam regulations, the **four-year study programme** is completed with a Ph.D. thesis consisting of a scientific and an artistic or design component, evaluated in equal measure. This offers a dual qualification with a practical and academic degree that can be used in teaching and research, curatorial practice, cultural and scientific management, institutional education, and related fields.

The programme also offers the opportunity to pursue a doctorate in collaboration with the University of Music FRANZ LISZT Weimar.

### Mentoring System

Each student is personally supervised by **at least two university professors** (or senior lecturers) for the entire duration:
- **One mentor** represents academic methods
- **One mentor** represents artistic or design methods
- One mentor must be from Bauhaus-Universität Weimar; the second may come from another university

### Curriculum

The four-year study programme primarily serves to accompany and support research at the highest academic standards, developing foundational research competences and skills in artistic research or design research. The curriculum consists of:
- Graduate seminars
- Graduate colloquia
- Key qualifications
- Workshops
- Conferences
- Continuing education

### Application (Ph.D. Programme)

- **Portal:** Bauhaus.CampusPortal (digital only)
- **Application period:** February 2 – March 31
- **Start:** Winter semester
- **Places:** 12–15 applicants accepted per year

**Required documents:**
1. Convincing exposé describing an innovative doctoral project with specific research question and methodological positioning at the intersection of artistic/creative and academic discourse
2. Work plan and timetable (4-year project plan)
3. Portfolio of previous artistic/creative work (incl. exhibition catalogues, exhibition list, press/project reports)
4. Publication list of own publications
5. Proof of completed university degree (certificates + translations if not in German/English)
6. Complete CV in tabular form
7. Proof of language proficiency
8. Specialization choice: Art or Design
9. Nomination of at least two mentors with written confirmation (declaration of mentorship)
10. Optional: Short motivation letter

**Selection process:** Admission decided by Ph.D. graduate admissions committee based on quality criteria. After first round, selected applicants invited to a 15-minute interview. Criteria include excellent professional qualifications, high visibility, willingness and ability to work on long-term research project, innovative methods, clear depiction of theory-practice relationship, high degree of critical reflection.

### Language Requirements (Ph.D. Programme)

**Option A:** German C1 + English B2
- German: DSH-2 or TestDaF 4xTDN 4 (or equivalent)
- English: IELTS 6.5 / TOEFL iBT 85 / Cambridge B2 First (or equivalent)

**Option B:** English C1 + German B2
- English: IELTS 7.0 / TOEFL iBT 108 / Cambridge C1 Advanced (or equivalent)
- German: DSH-1 or TestDaF 4xTDN 3 (or equivalent)

## Track 2: Promotion Dr. phil.

### Programme Description

Bauhaus-Universität Weimar awards the academic degree "Doctor philosophiae" (Dr. phil.) through the Faculty of Art and Design. The doctorate demonstrates the ability for in-depth and independent academic work, verified through:

1. **Dissertation** — A scientifically significant written work
2. **Disputation** — A lecture followed by questioning of the doctoral candidate
3. **Veröffentlichung** — Publication of the dissertation

### Supervision and Admission (Dr. phil.)

- The dissertation is supervised by at least one professor of the Faculty of Art and Design
- Joint supervision with professors from Bauhaus-Universität Weimar and/or other universities is possible
- Supervision is formalized through a supervision agreement (Betreuungserklärung) and an additional mentor declaration (Mentorenerklärung)
- Admission is possible upon application and is **independent of general application or semester deadlines** (rolling admission)

### Application (Dr. phil.)

Written application to the Chair of the Dr. phil. Graduation Commission:
- Intended topic
- Current CV
- Exposé
- Supervision agreement (Betreuungserklärung)
- Mentor declaration (Mentorenerklärung)
