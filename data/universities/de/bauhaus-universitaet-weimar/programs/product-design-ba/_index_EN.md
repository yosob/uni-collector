# Product Design — BA

## Program Overview

The **Bachelor of Arts in Product Design** at Bauhaus-Universität Weimar is an **8-semester** program offered by the Faculty of Art and Design.

The program follows the **Weimarer Modell** (Weimar Model), a project-based study approach that emphasizes interdisciplinary collaboration, individual artistic development, and the integration of design theory with practical studio work. Students work across four professorships covering design & management, transformative design, experimental industrial design, and emerging technologies.

Design criteria encompass aesthetic, semantic, symbolic, fashion-related, technical, and material dimensions. Design work is developed through research, conceptual thinking, free sensuous exploration, sketching, experimentation, material studies, digital design tools, presentations, models and prototypes.

Designers are challenged to consider questions of careful production, energy consumption and resource conservation alongside aspects of aesthetics, comprehensibility, clarity and attractiveness.

### Workshops & Facilities

Excellent workshops for **wood, metal, plastic, and ceramics**, plus infrastructure for **digital fabrication**, support hands-on project work. The year-overlapping project study format creates a motivating community.

## Admission

Admission requires a general higher education entrance qualification (Abitur) or equivalent, along with successful completion of an artistic aptitude test (Eignungsprüfung) in three stages. Under Thuringian law, in exceptional cases admission may be granted solely through the aptitude test. German language proficiency at **C1 level** (DSH 2 or TestDaF TDN 4) is required. Passed aptitude tests remain valid for 2 years; may be retaken twice.

## Application

| Phase | Timeline | Action |
|-------|----------|--------|
| 1. Registration | Feb 2 – March 31 | Register via Bauhaus.CampusPortal for aptitude test |
| 2. Document Upload | Early April – April 30 | Upload homework, motivation letter (~1 A4 page), CV, school certificate, portfolio (up to 10 work samples) |
| 3. Aptitude Test | After pre-selection | Practical exam, personal interview, present up to 10 original portfolio works |
