# Visual Communication B.A. (Visuelle Kommunikation)

## Program Overview

The Bachelor of Arts in Visual Communication at Bauhaus-Universität Weimar is an 8-semester program offered by the Faculty of Art and Design (Fakultät Kunst und Gestaltung). The program follows the "Weimarer Modell" (Weimar Model) of interdisciplinary project-based study.

To develop innovative action and design models for a wide range of tasks, mastering conception-led artistic and design approaches is as important as applying theoretical, scientific, and technical knowledge. In a rapidly changing world, flexible and responsible action is of particular importance.

- **Grafikdesign** — Prof. Markus Weisbeck
- **Fotografie** — Prof. Dr. Birgit Wudtke
- Jun.-Prof. Katharina Köhler
- Prof. Burkhart von Scheven

## Key Skills Developed

- Methodical and independent thinking
- Critical ability (including self-criticism)
- Understanding of communicative and media processes
- Understanding of aesthetic, social, and ecological functions
- Conception-led artistic and design approaches
- Theoretical, scientific, and technical knowledge application

## Study Structure

The "interdisciplinary project study" method enables and promotes independent thinking. After 8 semesters of regular study, students can obtain the "Bachelor of Arts" degree as the first professional qualification.

The subsequent Master program develops transferable competencies for design professions in Visual Communication, focusing on problems and questions of Visual Cultures.

## Career Paths

Graduates work in:
- Magazine, catalog, and book design
- Wayfinding and signage systems
- Website design
- Photography
- Film and commercial production
- TV graphics
- Poster and postcard design
- Corporate design for cultural institutions, industry, retail, entertainment, and advertising
