# Fine Art — Diplom

## Program Overview

The **Diplom program in Freie Kunst (Fine Art)** at Bauhaus-Universität Weimar is a **9-semester** full-time program offered by the Faculty of Art and Design. The Diplom degree is equivalent to a Master's degree.

The program follows the **Weimarer Modell** (Weimar Model) of project-based, interdisciplinary study. Students develop their own artistic positions through intensive studio practice and close collaboration with professors. The program covers **all artistic media**: painting, drawing, printmaking, sculpture, installation, photography, video, performance, and digital art.

## Key Features

- Individual artistic development in studio-based settings
- Interdisciplinary project work across all professorships of the Faculty
- Close mentoring by professors in small groups
- The Diplom degree is recognized as equivalent to a Master's degree
- Access to fully equipped workshops (wood, metal, print, photo, plaster/mold, plastics, model-making, video)
- First-semester "Grundlehre" introductory project with rotating workshops

## First Semester (Grundlehre)

First-semester students take short projects in all three artistic professorships:
- **Objekt, Skulptur, Installation** with Prof. Björn Dahlem
- **Experimentelle Malerei und Zeichnung** with Prof. Jana Gunstheimer
- **Kunst und sozialer Raum** with Prof. Christine Hill

Students also receive introductions to the artistic workshops, enabling independent artistic work from the second semester onward. Visits to lectures, workshops, and artist talks complement the first-semester program.

## Admission

Admission requires:
- **General higher education entrance qualification** (Abitur) or equivalent
- **Exceptional cases**: aptitude test alone may qualify per § 61 Abs. 3 Thüringer Hochschulgesetz
- **Artistic aptitude** demonstrated through:
  1. Digital portfolio: ~20 work samples as single PDF (max 20 MB)
  2. Aptitude test with in-person interview with the commission

**German language proficiency** at C1 level is required: DSH-2, TestDaF TDN 4, or Telc C1 Hochschule.

## Application

- **Portal:** Bauhaus.CampusPortal → [campus.uni-weimar.de/bewerbung](https://campus.uni-weimar.de/bewerbung)
- **Application period:** **February 2 – March 31** (⚠️ UPDATED — previously March 1 – July 15)
- **Start:** Winter semester only
- **Required documents:** Higher education entrance certificate, CV, digital portfolio (~20 work samples as PDF, max 20 MB)
- **Selection process:** Pre-selection based on portfolio → in-person aptitude interview
- **Aptitude test validity:** 2 years; can be repeated twice
- **Regulation:** [Aptitude Test Regulations (PDF)](https://www.uni-weimar.de/fileadmin/user/uni/universitaetsleitung/kanzler/mdu_akad/22/24_2022.pdf)

## Portfolio Guidelines

- Format: Single PDF file
- Size: Maximum **20 MB**
- Content: About **20 work samples** showing individual interests and working methods
- Media: Paintings, drawings, objects, photographs, etc.
- Sketchbooks: Submit as short video (max 2 min) via link in PDF
- CV and certificates: Upload as separate files

## Tuition

No tuition fees. Standard semester fee applies, which includes the semester ticket and student services.

## Workshops

Students have access to fully equipped workshops:
- Holzwerkstatt (Wood Workshop)
- Metallwerkstatt (Metal Workshop)
- Druckwerkstatt (Print Workshop)
- Fotowerkstatt (Photo Workshop)
- Gips- und Formenbauwerkstatt (Plaster & Mold Workshop)
- Kunststoffwerkstatt (Plastics Workshop)
- Modellbauwerkstatt (Model-making Workshop)
- Videowerkstatt (Video Workshop)
