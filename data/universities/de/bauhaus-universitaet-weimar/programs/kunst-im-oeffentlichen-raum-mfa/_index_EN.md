# Public Art and New Artistic Strategies (M.F.A.)

The **Public Art and New Artistic Strategies / Kunst im öffentlichen Raum und neue künstlerische Strategien** programme at Bauhaus-Universität Weimar is a **4-semester** English-language Master of Fine Arts programme established in winter semester 2001/02. It was the **first and only** master's degree programme of its kind in Germany dedicated to preparing fine arts students for working in the public sphere.

> ⚠️ **Program Status: DISCONTINUED** — The programme is being phased out. Last enrollment was in WS 2025/26 for candidates who passed the aptitude test in 2024. The programme will end in WS 2029/30.

The programme is run by the **Fakultät Kunst und Gestaltung** (Faculty of Art and Design) and is taught entirely in English, requiring only basic German (A1) from non-native speakers.

## Programme Structure

The programme follows a modular structure across 4 semesters:

- **Semesters 1–2**: Core public art projects, each developed and installed in real public space in cooperation with various partners. Complemented by scientific/theoretical modules and specialist courses or workshops.
- **Semester 3**: Study abroad at one of **7 international partner schools** (for German students); international students remain in Weimar.
- **Semester 4**: Master's thesis — an artwork realised in public space, accompanied by a written thesis paper and an oral presentation.

### Prerequisites for Master's Examination (30 ECTS)
- 3 public art projects
- 3 scientific modules
- 3 specialist modules (or workshops, or 1 specialist course)

### Key Themes
- Temporary installations, sculptures, actions, performances in public space
- Digital media expanding public space into virtual environments
- Political, cultural and social issues in public art
- Integration of fine arts into architecture and landscapes
- Culture of commemoration, function of memorials and monuments
- Globalism, urbanism and migration

### Accompanying Programme
- **Monday Night Lectures** — public lecture series in collaboration with ACC Galerie Weimar
- International guest lectures and workshops

## Key Features

- **English-language** programme (basic German A1 required for non-native speakers)
- Project-based learning with real interventions in public space
- International orientation with 7 partner schools worldwide
- 3rd semester study abroad opportunity
- Small group sizes with close professor mentoring
- Access to fully equipped workshops (wood, metal, print, photo, plaster/mold, plastics, model-making, video)

## Admission

### Requirements
- **First art degree** from an art college/academy with **minimum 8 semesters** of study, or equivalent
- **Portfolio** of artistic work demonstrating individual position and suitability
- **English proficiency**: TOEFL iBT 79-80, IELTS 6.0, Cambridge CAE level C, or comparable
- **German proficiency**: Basic knowledge A1 (Goethe Certificate Start Deutsch 1) for non-native speakers

### Application Process
1. **Digital application** via Bauhaus.CampusPortal
2. Submit: proof of first art degree, portfolio, language certificates, CV
3. **Pre-selection** based on portfolio by the admissions commission
4. **Aptitude test** with personal interview

### Deadlines
- **Application period**: February – March
- **Winter semester deadline**: May 15
- **Non-EU applicants**: March 31
- **Aptitude test**: takes place in the year preceding enrollment

## Tuition

No tuition fees. Standard semester contribution applies (~**160 EUR/semester**), which includes:
- Student services (Studierendenwerk)
- Semester ticket for regional public transport
- Student discounts
