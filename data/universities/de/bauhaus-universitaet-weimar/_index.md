# Bauhaus-Universität Weimar

Bauhaus-Universität Weimar is a public university in Weimar, Thuringia, Germany, with approximately 4,000 students. Its roots trace back to the 1860 Great Ducal Art School, and it is deeply connected to the legacy of the Bauhaus movement (1919–1933). Today the university comprises four faculties:

- **Faculty of Architecture and Urbanism** (Fakultät Architektur und Urbanistik)
- **Faculty of Civil Engineering** (Fakultät Bauingenieurwesen)
- **Faculty of Art and Design** (Fakultät Kunst und Gestaltung) — *primary focus for design/art programs*
- **Faculty of Media** (Fakultät Medien)

The university is known for its interdisciplinary approach, combining artistic practice with academic research. Programs in art and design follow the "Weimarer Modell" — a project-based study system that encourages cross-disciplinary collaboration.

## Design/Art Programs (9 programs collected)

### Fine Art
- **Freie Kunst** — Diplom (10 semesters)
- **Kunst im öffentlichen Raum** — M.F.A. (4 semesters)
- **Lehramt an Gymnasien Kunst** — Staatsexamen (9 semesters)

### Media Art & Design
- **Medienkunst/Mediengestaltung** — B.F.A. (8 semesters)
- **Medienkunst** — M.F.A. (2–4 semesters)

### Product Design
- **Produktdesign** — B.A. (6 semesters)
- **Produktdesign** — M.A. (4 semesters)

### Visual Communication
- **Visuelle Kommunikation** — B.A. (8 semesters)
- **Visuelle Kommunikation** — M.A. (2 semesters)

## Application

Applications are submitted digitally via the **Bauhaus.CampusPortal**. Programs with an artistic aptitude test (Eignungsprüfung) typically have a March 31 deadline (with registration opening February 2). The Medienkunst B.F.A. has a special two-phase process with homework due April 30. The Medienkunst M.F.A. has a May 31 deadline.

## Fees

The university is tuition-free. Students pay a semester contribution (~200 EUR) that includes a semester ticket for public transport and student services.
