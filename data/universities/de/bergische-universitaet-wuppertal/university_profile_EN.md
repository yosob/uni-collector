# University of Wuppertal — University Profile

## University Overview

**Name**: Bergische Universität Wuppertal (University of Wuppertal)
**Location**: Wuppertal, North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany
**Founded**: 1972
**Students**: ~25,000
**Type**: Public University (Universität)
**Website**: https://www.uni-wuppertal.de

The University of Wuppertal is a modern university with approximately 25,000 students across 7 faculties. The Faculty for Design and Art (Fakultät für Design und Kunst, FK8) is one of the leading design faculties in Germany, offering programs across three departments: Industrial Design (ID), Media Design and Spatial Design (MR), and Art (Kunst). The faculty is located in Building I on the Grifflenberg campus.

**Tuition**: Tuition-free for EU students; semester fee of €300 (includes student services and public transport ticket). Non-EU students may be charged €1,500/semester starting from winter 2026/27.

## Program Summaries

### Bachelor Programs

**1. Industrial Design (B.A.)** — *8 semesters, Winter start*
A comprehensive program focused on shaping the future through smart technologies, sustainable materials, and intuitive user experiences. Unique among German design schools for its 8-semester structure, which includes a mandatory internship semester and industry cooperations with companies like Bosch, Kettler, and Sonos. No NC — admission based solely on aptitude test with portfolio. ~80% employment rate within 6 months. Portfolio deadline: May 19.

**2. Fine Art / Bildende Kunst (B.A./B.Ed.)** — *6 semesters, Winter start*
Oriented towards teacher education for all school types. Combines artistic practice (painting, sculpture, graphic arts, drawing, new media) with art pedagogy and art history. Available as combinational Bachelor (B.A.) or teaching degree (B.Ed.) for primary, secondary, grammar school, and vocational college levels. Aptitude test registration deadline: April 30.

**3. Media Design and Design Technology (B.A.)** — *6 semesters, Winter start*
Covers graphic design, typography, print and digital media production, and design technology. Combines artistic, technical, and theoretical approaches. Students develop skills in visual communication, editorial design, corporate identity, web design, and production workflows. Application deadline: July 15.

**4. Audiovisual Media Design (B.A.)** — *6 semesters, Winter start*
Focuses on film, video art, animation, sound design, visual effects, and media art. Combines artistic, technical, and theoretical approaches to audiovisual media production. Student works are showcased in the annual LOOP exhibition. Application deadline: July 15.

**5. Interactive Media Design (B.A.)** — *6 semesters, Winter start*
Covers UX/UI design, game design, interactive storytelling, web/app development, and VR/AR. Combines design thinking, programming, and artistic approaches. Students develop skills in concept development, prototyping, user research, and digital product design. Application deadline: July 15.

**6. Color Technology/Space Design/Surface Technology (B.A./B.Ed.)** — *6 semesters, Winter start*
A unique program combining artistic design with technical expertise in color, surfaces, and interior spaces. One of the few programs in Germany offering this specialized combination. Available as B.A. (combinatorial) and B.Ed. (teaching degree for vocational schools). Application deadline: July 15.

### Master Programs

**1. Strategic Product and Innovation Development (M.A.)** — *4 semesters, Winter + Summer start*
Interdisciplinary program combining design, engineering, and business perspectives for leadership roles in product innovation. Open admission (no NC) — requires Bachelor with 180 ECTS and grade 2.0+. 120 ECTS total, 30 per semester. Taught in German and English.

**2. Public Interest Design (M.A.)** — *4 semesters, Winter start*
Centered on participating in shaping society and public affairs through design. Core topics include design theory, social theory, urban research, participation, visual communication in public spaces, and performative design. For designers who want to contribute to society and public welfare through their creative work. Application deadline: July 15.

## Application Process

### General Application Information
- **Application Portal**: University application portal (https://www.uni-wuppertal.de)
- **Winter Semester**: Application by July 15 (varies by program)
- **Summer Semester**: Application by January 15 (Strategic Product and Innovation Development only)

### Design Program Application Steps
1. Apply via the university application portal
2. Register for aptitude test (Eignungsfeststellungsverfahren) — required for most design programs
3. Submit portfolio (format and requirements vary by program)
4. Attend practical/oral examination if shortlisted

### Language Requirements for International Students
- German: DSH-2 / TestDaF TDN 4 (CEFR C1)
- English: Recommended for Strategic Product and Innovation Development
