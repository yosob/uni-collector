# Fine Art B.A./B.Ed.

## Program Overview

The Fine Art program at the University of Wuppertal is oriented towards teacher education for all school types. The curriculum covers artistic practice in painting, sculpture, graphic arts, drawing, and new media, alongside art pedagogy and art history.

## Study Structure

The program is offered as a **combinational Bachelor** (two main subjects):
- **B.A.** (Bachelor of Arts) — combinational degree with a second subject
- **B.Ed.** (Bachelor of Education) — teaching degree for:
  - Grundschule (primary school)
  - Haupt-/Gesamtschule (secondary school)
  - Gymnasium/Gesamtschule (grammar school)
  - Berufskolleg (vocational college)

## Focus Areas

- **Painting**: Classical and contemporary painting techniques
- **Sculpture**: Three-dimensional design and sculptural work
- **Graphic Arts**: Printmaking and graphic techniques
- **Drawing**: Observational and expressive drawing
- **New Media**: Digital and time-based media

## Admission

1. **University application**: Via the standard university application portal
2. **Aptitude test (Eignungsfeststellungsverfahren)**: Separate registration required
   - Portfolio review of artistic works
   - Practical examination
   - Registration deadline: April 30 for winter semester

## Master Options

- **Master of Education (M.Ed.)**: Continuing to teaching certification for various school types
- **Master of Arts**: Further artistic specialization

## Department

Part of the Department Art (Abteilung Kunst) within the Faculty for Design and Art, located on the Grifflenberg campus.
