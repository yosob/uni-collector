# Industrial Design B.A.

## Program Overview

The Industrial Design Bachelor at Bergische Universität Wuppertal is an 8-semester program focused on shaping the future through smart technologies, sustainable materials, and intuitive user experiences.

The program positions itself away from the purely creative craft designer towards a design-competent strategy and process consultant. Industrial designers increasingly work at interfaces to other disciplines, taking on leading roles in development processes.

## Study Structure

| Semester | Focus |
|----------|-------|
| 1-2 | Artistic and theoretical foundations |
| 3-4 | Design development and design process |
| 5-6 | Application and deepening through industry cooperation (e.g., Kettler, Bosch, Sonos) |
| 7 | Internship and/or study abroad at partner universities in Europe, Asia, USA |
| 8 | Bachelor thesis |

## Key Features

- **No NC**: Admission based solely on aptitude test
- **Industry partnerships**: Cooperations with international companies
- **International network**: Partner universities worldwide
- **Strong career prospects**: ~80% of graduates have employment within 6 months

## Career Paths

Graduates work at companies such as:
- Airbus, Bosch, Braun, Ford
- GRO Design (Eindhoven)
- IKEA, John Deere, Microsoft
- Miele, VW, Ziba Design (Portland)
- And many more international agencies and corporations

## Admission Process

1. **Online registration**: April 1 - May 19, 9am
2. **Portfolio submission**: 10-15 free works + 3 works on annual assignment (2026: Heat Protection)
3. **Oral examination**: July 8-9, 2026 (if portfolio accepted)

### Portfolio Guidance

Monthly portfolio consultations from December to April. WhatsApp and email support available.
