# Public Interest Design M.A.

## Program Overview

Public Interest Design is a Master program centered on the fascinating idea of participating in shaping society and public affairs through design, taking responsibility towards the community, and shaping interest in the public through one's own public projects.

Participating in the development of society, asking relevant questions, and participating in social change through projects is a demanding and valuable challenge.

## Core Topics

- **Design Theory**: Theoretical foundations of design in public interest
- **Social Theory**: Understanding social structures and dynamics
- **Design Research Criticism**: Critical approaches to design research
- **Experimental Urban Research**: Investigating urban spaces and communities
- **Qualitative Social Research**: Methods for understanding social phenomena
- **Urban Society (Polis)**: Participatory shaping of urban communities
- **Built Environment (Baukultur)**: Effects of built environment on humans
- **Interactive**: Digitalization from perspectives of participation, aesthetics, and community
- **Performative Design**: Designing intended effects through performative approaches
- **Film**: Filmic storytelling about aspects of public welfare

## Philosophy

Cities are the cultural carriers of democratic society, free constitutional order, and open dynamic developments in education, knowledge, and technology. Urban culture, urban society, and cityscape are forms of the public — they are the image of public life and its designed expression.

Finding ways for citizen participation in establishing and shaping public space, and bringing them into effect as community designers of society, is one of the central tasks and challenges for the future.

## Who Should Apply?

Designers who want to:
- Contribute their creative work in responsibility to society and the public
- Participate in co-shaping society and the public through projects and themes
- Engage with questions of democracy, participation, and public welfare

## Department

Part of the Department Media Design and Spatial Design (MR) within the Faculty for Design and Art.
