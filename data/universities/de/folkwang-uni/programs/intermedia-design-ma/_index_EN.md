# Intermedia Design M.A. (formerly Communication Design)

The consecutive Master's program Intermedia Design (formerly Communication Design) deepens and sharpens the artistic, theoretical, and technological competencies established during the Bachelor's program. The goal is to enable students to develop their own positions, methods, and systems of design at the intersection of culture, technology, and theory.

Design is understood as experimental, authorial, and research-based practice. The disciplinary framework is defined by three fields: **Narration, Publication, and Interaction**, within which teaching areas such as Free Graphics, Moving Image, Illustration, Typography, Visual Identities, Interface Design, and Information Design are situated.

The program opens up a productive tension between specialization and generalization. In particular, the connections to the neighboring Master's program Design Futures expand the thematic and methodological possibilities. The program understands design as a practice of inquiry — of conventional design briefs as well as social, technological, and cultural narratives.
