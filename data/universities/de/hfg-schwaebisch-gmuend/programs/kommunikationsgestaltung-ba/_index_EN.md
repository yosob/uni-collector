# Communication Design (BA)

Communication designers make complex topics visible and understandable. The program evolved from classic graphic design and has expanded into many new professional fields. Typography plays a central role throughout. Students can start each year in the winter semester (October) or summer semester (March).

## Specializations (from semester 4)

1. **Connected Communication** — Blogs, news sites, online archives, platforms, responsive/dynamic layouts
2. **Didactic Communication** — Manuals, learning media, complex visualizations, diagrams, maps
3. **Spatial Communication** — Wayfinding systems, exhibition design, installations
4. **Transmedia Communication** — Cross-media concepts, corporate identity, brand identities

## Program Structure

- **Foundation Studies (semesters 1–3):** Typography, fundamentals of design, composition, theory (design history, design research). Project-based learning, few written exams.
- **Specialization (semesters 4–6):** Choose two focus areas, project-based work with presentations
- **Internship Semester:** 26 weeks before semester 6
- **Bachelor Thesis (semester 7):** Individual or team work possible

## Application

- **Online Application** via portal [me.hfg-gmuend.de](https://me.hfg-gmuend.de/)
  - WS: Early April to May 15 (exclusion deadline)
  - SS: Early October to November 15 (exclusion deadline)
- **Homework Assignment** mandatory (design task, program-specific, available for download ~6–8 weeks before)
- **Work Samples** optional for Communication Design (no traditional portfolio required)
- **Aptitude Test:** Online via Zoom, practical + oral (approx. 8:30–17:00)
  - Practical exam: Design and representational tasks on exam day
  - Oral exam (~15 min): Presentation with homework and optional work samples
- **Motivational Interview** with professors and students
- **Aptitude Test WS 2026/27:** June 3, 2026

## Application Documents

1. Certificate of university entrance qualification (certified copy)
2. CV
3. Proof of participation in study orientation procedure
4. Evidence of subject-related practical activities (if applicable)
