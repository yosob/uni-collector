# BA Design — Hochschule München

## Program Overview

The Design Bachelor's program (BA Design) at Munich University of Applied Sciences is one of Germany's most traditional applied design education programs, with over a century of design education history. The program offers three specializations: Photography Design (Fotodesign), Industrial Design (Industriedesign), and Communication Design (Kommunikationsdesign).

Students choose one specialization upon enrollment, but interdisciplinary exchange and collaboration opportunities exist across all three tracks. Teaching is project-based, emphasizing practical skills and creative thinking.

## Three Specializations

### 1. Fotodesign (Photography Design)
- Commercial and fine art photography
- Image processing and post-production
- Visual storytelling and documentary photography
- Contemporary photography theory and practice

### 2. Industriedesign (Industrial Design)
- Product design and development
- Interaction design
- Sustainable design and materials
- User experience and user research

### 3. Kommunikationsdesign (Communication Design)
- Graphic design and typography
- Type design and brand design
- Digital media and interactive design
- Illustration and visual communication

## Application Information

### Application Timeline
- **Application Period**: **May 2 — May 31** each year (winter semester)
- **Portfolio Submission Deadline**: May 31
- **Enrollment**: Winter semester only (October)
- ⚠️ First enrollment is only possible in the winter semester

### Application Process
1. **Online Application**: Submit via PRIMUSS portal (May 2 — May 31)
2. **Portfolio Upload**: Register and upload digital portfolio on HMster/NINE platform (nine.hm.edu)
3. **Aptitude Test**: After passing the portfolio screening, invited to an on-site aptitude test

### Admission Requirements
- General university entrance qualification (Abitur)
- Or University of Applied Sciences entrance qualification (Fachhochschulreife)
- Or subject-specific university entrance qualification
- Or vocational qualification admission (Meister, Techniker, etc.)
- Passing the aptitude test (Eignungsprüfung)

### Portfolio Requirements (Mappe)

#### General Materials
- Letter of motivation (Motivationsschreiben)
- CV and ID photo (optional)

#### Number of Works per Specialization
| Specialization | Number of Works | Format | Size Limit |
|----------------|----------------|--------|------------|
| Fotodesign | Max 25 works + 5 simulated portfolio photos + 5 sketchbook photos (optional) | .png, .jpg, .gif | Max 50 MB per file |
| Industriedesign | Max 35 works (e.g. 25 works + 10 sketchbook photos) | .png, .jpg, .gif | Max 50 MB per file |
| Kommunikationsdesign | Max 35 works (e.g. 25 works + 10 sketchbook photos) | .png, .jpg, .gif | Max 50 MB per file |

## Curriculum Structure

- Semesters 1–4: Foundation courses and specialization courses
- Semester 5: Practical semester (Praxissemester)
- Semesters 6–7: Advanced courses and Bachelor's thesis (Bachelorarbeit)

## Teaching Features

- **Century of Design Tradition**: Over 100 years of design education experience
- **Project-based Learning**: Teaching approach oriented toward real-world projects
- **Studio Culture**: Study in the Altes Zeughaus (Old Arsenal) historic building
- **Interdisciplinary Collaboration**: Joint projects with other programs and faculties
- **Practice Orientation**: Mandatory internship semester in the 5th semester
- **Strong Industry Connections**: Munich's rich design industry resources

## Facilities

- Professional workshops (Werkstätten)
- Photography, woodworking, metal, ceramics workshops
- Computer labs
- Altes Zeughaus historic building
