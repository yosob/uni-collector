# Fine and Applied Arts — Bachelor (BFA)

The Bachelor of Fine Arts at HfG Offenbach offers an interdisciplinary programme in fine and applied arts over 8 semesters (240 CP). Special feature: the "Offenbach Model" — no classes, no master classes, free access to all subject areas — including cross-departmental access to Design.

## Programme Structure

### BFA ART/DESIGN — 90 CP (3 modules)
Artistic-design subject areas. Fundamental conceptual and artistic-design skills (design, material, techniques, conception, presentation). Embedded orientation project in the 4th semester.

### BFA THEORY — 29 CP (4 modules)
Art history, philosophy/aesthetics, media theory, perception theory. Historical and systematic questions on art and design. Introduction to theoretical thinking, developing one's own arguments.

### BFA PRACTICE — 40 CP (3 modules)
Workshops and labs: Relevant artistic-design techniques and skills — analogue and digital media, materials and technologies. Experimentation and research to develop one's own representational strategies.

### BFA ELECTIVES — 61 CP (5 modules)
Individual specialisation from the full range of all module groups. Including internship (4–12 weeks, 4–12 CP), e.g. in studios, agencies, museums, art associations, galleries, media institutions, festivals, theatres. External cooperations, language courses, Free Study.

### BFA FINAL MODULE — 20 CP
Bachelor thesis.

**Total: 240 CP in 8 semesters**

## Admission

### Requirements
- University entrance qualification (Abitur or Fachhochschulreife) → portfolio as proof of artistic talent
- **Without qualification**: admission possible with exceptional artistic talent via portfolio
- Aptitude test (portfolio + entrance exam + interview)
- 6 weeks of manual internship
- Motivation letter
- Detailed CV

### Application Process
- **Portal**: [bewerbung.hfg-offenbach.de](https://bewerbung.hfg-offenbach.de) (from 1 April)
- **Submission**: 01.04.–15.04. (winter semester only)
- **Portfolio**: At least 30 works/sketches, compiled in one PDF
- **Language proof**: B2 by 15 April, C1 by 15 September
- **Entrance exams**: Mid-May (online), interviews late May/early June (in-person)
