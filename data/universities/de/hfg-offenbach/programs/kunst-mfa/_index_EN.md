# Fine and Applied Arts — Master (MFA)

The Master of Fine Arts (MFA) at HfG Offenbach offers an interdisciplinary programme in fine and applied arts over 4 semesters (120 CP). Special feature: the "Offenbach Model" — no classes, no master classes, free access to all subject areas — including cross-departmental access to Design.

## Programme Structure

### Variant 1: MFA Art
- **MFA ART/DESIGN** — 50 CP: Artistic-design subject areas, individual artistic development and project work.
- **MFA THEORY** — 20 CP: Art history, philosophy/aesthetics, media theory, perception theory.
- **MFA ELECTIVES** — 20 CP: Individual selection from the full range of all module groups.
- **MFA FINAL MODULE** — 30 CP: Master's thesis.

### Variant 2: MFA Art with greater theory component
- Recommended for doctoral aspirations (Dr. phil.)
- Extended theoretical focus
- Targeted preparation for the doctorate

**Total: 120 CP in 4 semesters**

## Admission

### Requirements
- Bachelor/Diploma (at least 210 CP) or equivalent degree
- With 180 CP: possible extension of studies
- In justified cases, professional experience (approx. 3 years) may qualify
- Proof of artistic-design aptitude
- Motivation letter indicating study focus areas and/or master's project

### Application Process
- **Portal**: [bewerbung.hfg-offenbach.de](https://bewerbung.hfg-offenbach.de)
- **Submission**: 01.04.–15.04. (winter semester only)
- **Portfolio**: At least 30 elements/documentations, compiled in one PDF
- **Language proof**: B2 by 15 April, C1 by 15 September
- **Entrance exams**: Mid-May (online), interviews late May/early June (in-person)
- **Orientation interview**: Possible before admission decision
