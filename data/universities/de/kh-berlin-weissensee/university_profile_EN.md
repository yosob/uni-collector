# Berlin Weißensee School of Art and Design — University Profile

## University Overview

**Name**: Kunsthochschule Berlin-Weißensee (KHB) / Berlin Weißensee School of Art and Design
**Location**: Berlin-Weißensee, Germany
**Founded**: 1946
**Students**: ~800
**Type**: Public Art and Design University (Kunsthochschule)
**Website**: https://kh-berlin.de

The Weißensee Kunsthochschule Berlin is a public art and design university in Berlin's Weißensee district. With approximately 800 students and 9 programs, it is one of Germany's most renowned art schools. A one-year shared foundation program (Künstlerische Grundlagen) fosters interdisciplinary exchange among all students. The school is distinguished by its strong focus on sustainability, social responsibility, and experimental approaches.

**Tuition**: Tuition-free (semester fee ~€358 including BVG transit ticket). Exception: Art Therapy MA (~€5,400/semester).

## Program Summaries

### Diplom Programs (Fine Art)

**1. Painting (Diplom Fine Art)** — *10 semesters, Winter start*
Classic Diplom program in Fine Art focusing on painting, drawing, printmaking, and contemporary art. Students develop their artistic position through intensive studio practice, individual tutorials, and theoretical seminars. Access to printmaking workshops, photography studio, and computer studio. Application deadline: ~March 15.

**2. Sculpture (Diplom Fine Art)** — *10 semesters, Winter start*
Diplom program focused on three-dimensional form, installation, and spatial art. Access to specialized workshops for bronze casting, ceramics, metal, and wood. Application deadline: ~March 15.

**3. Stage and Costume Design (Diplom Fine Art)** — *10 semesters, Winter start*
Unique Diplom program for stage and costume design in Berlin with close ties to the city's theatre and opera scene. Focus areas: scenography, theatre spatial design, costume design. Application deadline: ~March 15.

### Bachelor Programs

**4. Product Design (B.A.)** — *8 semesters, Winter start*
Sustainable, socially responsible product design with focus on industrial design, social design, and interaction design. Access to model-making workshops, digital fabrication labs, and the greenlab research platform. Application deadline: ~March 15.

**5. Fashion Design (B.A.)** — *8 semesters, Winter start*
Fashion design with strong emphasis on sustainability and experimental approaches. Students work with sewing studios, digital embroidery, dyeing labs, and weaving/knitting workshops. Application deadline: ~March 15.

**6. Textile and Material Design (B.A.)** — *8 semesters, Winter start*
Innovative textile and material design focusing on smart materials, sustainable material research, and digital textile technologies. Unique workshop infrastructure: weaving, knitting, Soft Material Technologies Lab, Soft Rapid Prototyping Lab. Application deadline: ~March 15.

**7. Visual Communication (B.A.)** — *8 semesters, Winter start*
Comprehensive visual communication program covering graphic design, typography, illustration, photography, and motion graphics. Access to computer studio, digital printing, printmaking, and XR technologies. Application deadline: ~March 15.

### Master Programs

**8. Spatial Strategies (M.A.)** — *4 semesters, Winter start*
Interdisciplinary Master's program combining art, architecture, and urban theory. Students develop site-specific projects and interventions in Berlin's urban fabric. Bilingual (German/English). Application deadline: ~March 15.

**9. Art Therapy (M.A.)** — *6 semesters, Winter start*
Continuing education Master's program combining artistic practice with therapeutic theory. Covers clinical art therapy, psychosocial counseling, and trauma therapy. **Tuition-based: ~€5,400/semester.** Dedicated campus location. Application deadline: check website.

## Distinctive Features

- **Interdisciplinary Foundation**: All students complete a shared one-year foundation program (Künstlerische Grundlagen)
- **greenlab**: Research platform for sustainable design strategies
- **eLab**: Laboratory for interactive technologies and digital art
- **Extensive Workshop Infrastructure**: 20+ workshops including bronze foundry, ceramics, printmaking, weaving, XR technologies
- **Berlin Location**: Close connections to Berlin's vibrant cultural and creative scene
- **Research Labs**: +dimensions, DiGiTal, DesignFarmBerlin, DXM, visions4people, Bauhaus 4.0
