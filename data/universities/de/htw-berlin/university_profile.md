## Overview

HTW Berlin is the largest University of Applied Sciences in Berlin and one of the largest in Germany. The university offers a wide range of programs in engineering, computer science, business, culture, and design. The design programs are concentrated in **Fachbereich 5 (FB5) — School of Culture and Design**, located at the historic Campus Wilhelminenhof in Berlin-Oberschöneweide.

## Program Highlights

### Kommunikationsdesign (BA)
The largest and most established design program at FB5. Covers visual communication, typography, editorial design, digital media, branding, and illustration. Renowned for its strong practical orientation with professional studio facilities.

### Game Design (BA + MA)
Offers both Bachelor and Master degrees. The BA focuses on game development, game art, level design, and interactive storytelling. The MA (part of System Design / Game Design) enables project-based study focused on games, playable environments, game thinking, and extended realities. Connected to the **DE:HIVE** incubator for game startups. 20 study places per year for the MA.

### Industrial Design (BA)
Focuses on product design, user-centered design, sustainability, and design of everyday objects. Emphasizes interdisciplinary collaboration and societal/technological developments. Requires German B2 language proficiency. Strong research connections.

### Public Design (MA)
A unique program focused on service design, information design, interaction design, and transformation design for the public sector. Projects are conducted with external partners. Starts in summer semester. Requires aptitude test (motivation letter, project portfolio, interview). Study advisor: Prof. Daniela Hensel.

### Modedesign (BA + MA)
Comprehensive fashion design program covering design conception, pattern making, knitwear, textile surface design, CAD, and fashion management. The MA allows specialization in knitwear design, textile surface design, wearables & performance, sustainability, or fashion/product management.

## Admission & Application

Most design programs require an **Eignungsprüfung** (aptitude test) consisting of:
- Portfolio/Mappe submission
- Homework assignment (Hausaufgabe)
- Interview or on-site test

Application typically starts in spring (February–March) for winter semester intake. Some MA programs (Public Design) start in summer semester.

## Facilities

- **DE:HIVE** — Game design incubator and studio space (Slabystraße, Schöneweide)
- Professional studios for each discipline (print, photography, 3D, sewing, knitting, CAD)
- Campus Wilhelminenhof: Historic industrial architecture combined with modern buildings, directly on the Spree river

## Tuition

No tuition fees (standard for German public universities). Semester fees apply (~€300/semester including transit ticket).
