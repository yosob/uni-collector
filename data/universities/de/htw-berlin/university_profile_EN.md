# HTW Berlin — University Profile

## University Overview

**Name**: HTW Berlin – Hochschule für Technik und Wirtschaft Berlin (University of Applied Sciences)
**Location**: Berlin, Germany
**Founded**: 1994
**Students**: ~14,000
**Type**: Public University of Applied Sciences (Fachhochschule)
**Website**: https://www.htw-berlin.de

HTW Berlin is the largest University of Applied Sciences in Berlin. Department 5 — School of Culture and Design offers a wide range of design and cultural studies programs at the historic Wilhelminenhof Campus in Oberschöneweide. The department is home to 7 Bachelor and 7 Master programs spanning game design, communication design, industrial design, fashion design, clothing technology, conservation and restoration, museology, museum management, public design, and applied landscape archaeology.

**Tuition**: Tuition-free for all students; semester fee of ~€300 (includes student services and public transport ticket).

## Program Summaries

### Bachelor Programs

**1. Communication Design (B.A.)** — *7 semesters, Winter start*
One of HTW Berlin's most renowned design programs. Covers typography, editorial design, digital media, branding, illustration, photography, and audiovisual media. Aptitude test includes an annually changing assignment (topic published last Monday in February), portfolio submission, and interview. Contact: Prof. Jürgen Huber.

**2. Game Design (B.A.)** — *7 semesters, Winter start*
Interdisciplinary training in game design, game art, game programming, and producing. Strong project focus with regular game projects in teams. Close integration with the DE:HIVE Games Incubator. Multi-stage aptitude test: assignment (announced at HIVE:FIVE in February), online registration (March–June), submission by June 15, on-site test.

**3. Industrial Design (B.A.)** — *7 semesters, Winter start*
Focuses on intuitive, sustainable, and inclusive design. Curriculum includes drawing, CAD, 3D modeling, materials science, and design methods. Aptitude test: portfolio, assignment, and introductory video. German B2 required. Deadline: ~March 23.

**4. Fashion Design (B.A.)** — *7 semesters, Winter start*
Comprehensive fashion design training from first sketch to finished product. Combines creative design with craft precision. Studios include CAD Fashion Studio, knitting lab, and textile surface design. Aptitude test with portfolio.

**5. Clothing Technology / Confection (B.Eng.)** — *7 semesters, Winter start*
Technical expertise in garment production: pattern construction, manufacturing, series production, quality management, CAD/CAM. No aptitude test required.

**6. Conservation and Restoration / Excavation Technology (B.A.)** — *7 semesters, Winter start*
Combines cultural studies with practical craft expertise in art and artifact preservation, restoration, and archaeological excavation techniques.

**7. Museology (B.A.)** — *7 semesters, Winter start*
One of the few museum-related degree programs in Germany. Covers collection management, exhibition design, museum education, cultural history, and digital methods.

### Master Programs

**1. System Design / Game Design (M.A.)** — *3 semesters, Summer start*
Project-based study with individual projects (artistic, technical, scientific, or entrepreneurship). Personal coaching by expert team. Students work in the DE:HIVE Incubator with professional studio infrastructure. 20 places. Requires exposé (3 pages) and portfolio.

**2. Public Design (M.A.)** — *4 semesters, Summer start*
Focuses on design in the public sector: service design, information design, interaction design, transformation design. Projects with external partners. Two-stage aptitude test: document review + interview. Deadline: ~January 18. Contact: Prof. Daniela Hensel.

**3. Fashion Design (M.A.)** — *4 semesters, Winter start*
In-depth specialization in knitwear design, textile surface design, wearables & performance, sustainability, and fashion/product management. Design projects with industry partners.

**4. Museum Management and Communication (M.A.)** — *4 semesters, Winter start*
Management competencies for the museum sector: critical museum theories, visual communication, multimedia, ethics and law. Practical projects with Berlin museums.

**5. Conservation and Restoration (M.A.)** — *4 semesters, Winter start*
Advanced scientific and practical training in preservation of art and cultural artifacts. Restoration methods, scientific analysis, and research projects with external partners.

**6. Applied Landscape and Field Archaeology (M.A.)** — *4 semesters, Winter start*
Advanced competencies in archaeological prospection, excavation technology, and landscape analysis. Geophysics, remote sensing, GIS, 3D scanning, and photogrammetry.

**7. Clothing Technology / Confection (M.Eng.)** — *4 semesters, Winter start*
Deepens technical and business competencies for the apparel industry: advanced production management, quality assurance, CAD/CAM, sustainability, and global supply chains.

## Application Process

### General Application Information
- **Application Portal**: HTW Berlin online application portal
- **Winter Semester**: Application deadlines vary by program (March–July)
- **Summer Semester**: January deadlines for System Design and Public Design

### Design Program Application Steps
1. Check specific requirements on the program website
2. Register for aptitude test (if required)
3. Prepare portfolio / assignment
4. Submit application materials by deadline
5. Attend on-site test or interview (if invited)

| Program | Deadline | Notes |
