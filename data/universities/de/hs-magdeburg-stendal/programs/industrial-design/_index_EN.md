# Industrial Design (B.A.) — Magdeburg-Stendal University of Applied Sciences

## Programme Profile

The bachelor's programme in Industrial Design at Magdeburg-Stendal University of Applied Sciences combines creative, technical and methodological competencies. Students learn the entire design process — from initial sketch to finished product. The focus is on developing sustainable and user-centred products for industry and society.

## Key Areas / Content

- Design fundamentals (form, colour, composition)
- Design methods and design process
- Product and industrial design
- 3D visualisation and rendering
- Prototyping and model making
- Technical products and systems
- Interface and interaction design
- UX / User Experience
- Material science and manufacturing techniques
- Simulation and digital tools
- Project work and practical projects

## Application Process (Aptitude Assessment)

The programme requires a successfully completed aptitude assessment in three stages:

1. **Institute tasks:** Complete tasks set by the institute and submit as a portfolio
2. **Home assignment:** Receive a specific task to complete and bring to the exam
3. **Exam:** Exam with 3 tasks + presentation/interview (approx. 10 minutes)

**Application deadlines:**
- Winter semester: 31.05.
- Summer semester: 30.11.

## Admission Requirements

- General university entrance qualification, entrance qualification for universities of applied sciences, or equivalent qualification
- Successful aptitude assessment (3 stages)

## Internship Semester

In the 6th semester, a compulsory internship semester (at least 16 weeks) is scheduled. Alternatively, a study abroad period is possible. Students gain practical experience in design studios, companies or agencies.

## Special Features

- Combination of creative design and technical engineering expertise
- Close connection to the IWID department
- Small study groups with intensive supervision
- Workshops and laboratories (3D printing, CNC, etc.)
- Start possible in both winter and summer semester
- Transdisciplinary projects with engineering students
