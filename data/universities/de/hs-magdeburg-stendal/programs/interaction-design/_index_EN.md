# Interaction Design (M.A.) — Magdeburg-Stendal University of Applied Sciences

## Programme Profile

The master's programme in Interaction Design imparts transdisciplinary competencies at the intersection of people, technology and society. The programme combines design, technical and societal perspectives, qualifying graduates to shape digital, physical and hybrid interaction experiences. It is aimed at bachelor's graduates from design, social sciences, computer science, psychology and electrical engineering.

## Key Areas / Content

- Human-Machine Interaction
- UX Design / User Experience Design
- Social Design and Societal Innovation
- Technology-based Design
- Extended Reality (XR): VR, AR, MR
- Tangible and Physical Interfaces
- Interaction Research and Design Research
- Critical Making and Data-Driven Design
- Design Thinking and Agile Methods
- Transdisciplinary Project Work

## Special Facilities / Labs

- **XR I LAB:** Laboratory for Virtual Reality, Augmented Reality and Mixed Reality — research and development of immersive interaction experiences
- **UX I User Experience Lab:** Usability and UX research laboratory with eye-tracking and other evaluation methods
- **Schauwerk:** Creative lab in Magdeburg city centre for open projects, collaborations and exhibitions. Address: Große Münzstraße 10, 39104 Magdeburg

## Admission Requirements

- Bachelor's degree in Design, Interaction Design, Industrial Design or related fields (also from social sciences, computer science, psychology, electrical engineering)
- At least 210 ECTS (with fewer ECTS, conditions may apply)
- Successful aptitude assessment
- Proof of sufficient German language skills

## Special Features

- Transdisciplinary approach: combining design, technology and social sciences
- Part-time study possible
- Start possible in both winter and summer semester
- Dedicated laboratories and creative spaces (XR Lab, UX Lab, Schauwerk)
- Close connection to research at the IWID department
- Societally relevant topics: sustainability, inclusion, digitalisation, demographic change
- Regular exhibitions and public events at Schauwerk
