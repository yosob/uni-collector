# Design & Computation (Master of Arts)

## Program Overview

Design & Computation M.A. — an interdisciplinary research-based master's program jointly offered by TU Berlin and UdK Berlin. Open to bachelor's graduates from design, art, engineering, natural sciences, humanities, and social sciences, the program explores pressing contemporary issues in an interdisciplinary context. 4 semesters, 120 ECTS, earning a joint degree bearing the seals of both universities.

## Background

As technology accelerates its penetration into all areas of public and private life — from the algorithmization of everyday life to the digitalization of production and labor — technological change is comprehensively reshaping social, economic, ecological, and cultural conditions. This program responds to the growing need for intersection between design, engineering, humanities, and arts disciplines in an increasingly globalized and technologized world.

## Curriculum Structure

### Semester 1: Introductory Semester (Einführungssemester)
A customizable entry semester establishing common thematic, discursive, and methodological foundations:
- **Perspectives in Design** (Wahlpflicht I)
- **Perspectives in Engineering** (Wahlpflicht I)
- **Critical Design Thinking** (Pflicht)

### Semesters 2-3: Studio Learning
The core studio "Design as Medial Practice" is the program's centerpiece:
- **Design as Medial Practice I & II** (Pflicht) — Exploring new technologies, media, materials, and methods with critical reflection and problem-oriented application
- **Modelling and Simulation** or **Structures and Systems** (Wahlpflicht II)
- **Excursions** (Pflicht)
- **Scientific Work in Computational Design** (Pflicht)
- **Free Choice** — Electives from both universities

### Semester 4: Master Thesis
- **Master Thesis** (optionally with a practical component)

## Highlights

- **Joint degree**: TU Berlin + UdK Berlin, diploma with seals from both universities
- **Fully interdisciplinary**: Art & design × engineering × humanities & social sciences
- **Research-oriented**: Project-based studio learning with annually changing themes
- **Dual university resources**: Free electives from both universities
- **International**: High proportion of international students

## Application Process

### Step 1: TU Berlin Application Portal (TuPort)
- **Portal**: [TU Berlin Master Application](https://www.tu.berlin/studierendensekretariat/bewerbung-einschreibung-master/bewerbung/bewerbung-fuer-das-erste-fachsemester)
- **Documents**: Bachelor's degree and transcripts, motivation letter, language certificate, etc.

### Step 2: Portfolio Upload
- **Form**: [Portfolio Upload Form](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLScVkg6AOv5WuwutrieG3QkQLyLb5Xw5Da0nYmpK7UGtdCt69Q/viewform)
- Any media format: essays, academic writing, art and design work, audio, film, websites, etc.

### Step 3: Interview
- Period: Mid-June - mid-August
- Format: On-site interview (special cases considered individually)
- Upon acceptance: Enroll at UdK Berlin, register at TU Berlin
