# Ausstellungsdesign und Szenografie (Exhibition Design and Scenography) — Diplom

## Program Overview

In contemporary art and performing arts, spatial work occupies a central position. Combining exhibition design and scenography in a single program reflects the fluid boundaries between spatial practices across different arts and media.

### Exhibition Design (Ausstellungsdesign)

Synthesizes artistic, curatorial, and architectural decisions, with a focus on the conception and design of contemporary art exhibitions as well as archive and thematic exhibitions. While practically developing exhibition spaces and architectures, it also provides in-depth theoretical discourse on curatorial practice.

### Scenography (Szenografie)

Understood as the practice of spatial narration, it defines a position between stage design and directing. Scenographers work in theater, film, virtual spaces, or as independent artists developing narrative spaces — installations, temporary architectures, choreographies, and performances.

### Curatorial Studies (Kuratorische Studien)

As a connecting and complementary element of both areas, it covers exhibition theory, exhibition history, theater theory, theater history, as well as curatorial and theatrical practice.
