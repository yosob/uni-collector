# Kunstwissenschaft und Medienphilosophie (Art History and Media Philosophy) — Magister

## Program Overview

The Art History and Media Philosophy department teaches the historical and theoretical foundations of art and media on a broad basis. In close exchange with the practice-oriented programs, members of this program jointly reflect on contemporary social developments and explore new fields of design and research.

### Art History (Kunstwissenschaft)

Unlike traditional art history studies, the Art History program at HfG Karlsruhe offers a systematic rather than purely historical approach. The focus is on modern and contemporary art as well as aesthetic theory. Additionally, the program is open to aesthetic phenomena beyond art, such as the visual worlds of mass media or consumer phenomena.

### Media Philosophy (Medienphilosophie)

Beyond traditional knowledge transfer, this area researches and experiments with current narrative forms of media in social and political contexts. The advantage of having philosophy within an art university is that philosophy can be analyzed and practiced as an art form. Collaboration with Media Art or Design students allows theory to be translated into practical projects.

### Cooperation and Further Studies

- Cooperation with Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT) and Heidelberg University expands the curriculum
- The study structure is equivalent to a pre-Bologna Magister degree program
- Doctoral studies (Dr. phil.) are possible after completing the Magister
