## Key Features

The Berlin University of the Arts (UdK) is one of the largest and most diverse art universities in Europe, with a history dating back to the founding of the Brandenburg-Prussian Academy of Arts in 1696. The university holds full university status, enabling it to award doctoral degrees and professorial qualifications. Its four faculties cover all artistic disciplines across fine arts, design, music, and performing arts.

### Four Faculties
- **Bildende Kunst** (Faculty of Fine Arts)
- **Gestaltung** (Faculty of Design)
- **Musik** (Faculty of Music)
- **Darstellende Kunst** (Faculty of Performing Arts)

### Teaching Philosophy
- Experimental design approach ("Unerwartetes entwerfen" — designing the unexpected)
- Interdisciplinary artistic research
- International learning environment
- Full university academic status
- Rigorous selection through portfolio review and aptitude tests

## How to Apply

### Application Deadline Summary ⚠️

Deadlines vary significantly between programs:

| Period | Programs |
|--------|----------|
| Mid-Feb – Mid-Mar | Art in Context M.A. |
| Early Feb – Early Mar | Architecture B.A./M.A. |
| Mar 1 – Apr 1 | Design, Visual Communication, Stage Design, Costume Design (B.A./M.A.), GWK B.A. |
| Apr 1 – Apr 30 | GWK M.A. ⚠️ |
| Apr 1 – May 15 | Design and Computation M.A. |
| Mid-May – Mid-Jun | Art and Media (Absolvent + Meisterschüler) ⚠️ |

### Application Process

1. **Online registration:** [my.udk-berlin.de](https://my.udk-berlin.de)
2. **Pay application fee:** 30 EUR (Architecture: 40 EUR)
3. **Submit portfolio:** Via Bemus platform (some programs by mail)
4. **Pre-selection:** Based on portfolio / application documents
5. **Aptitude test:** Written exam / interview / portfolio presentation

### Language Requirements

| Level | Certificates | Programs |
