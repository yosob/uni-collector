# Kunsthochschule Kassel (KhK)

## University Overview

The Kunsthochschule Kassel (KhK) is one of the oldest and most renowned art academies in Germany. As a partially independent institution of the University of Kassel, it combines academic tradition with contemporary artistic practice.

Founded in 1777 as an art academy, the KhK looks back on a history of over 240 years. The university is widely known for its connection to the documenta, the world's most important exhibition of contemporary art, which takes place every five years in Kassel and is closely linked to the art academy.

With approximately 800 students, the KhK offers a broad spectrum of artistic and design programs in an intimate atmosphere. The close integration of fine arts, design, visual communication, art history, and art education under one roof creates a unique interdisciplinary learning environment.

## Study Programs

### Artistic Programs
- **Bildende Kunst (Fine Arts)** — Diplom (10 semesters)
- **Design** — Diplom (10 semesters)
- **Visuelle Kommunikation (Visual Communication)** — Diplom (10 semesters)

### Teaching Degree
- **Lehramt Kunst (Art Education)** — Master of Education (4 semesters)

### Academic Programs
- **Kunstwissenschaft (Art History / Art Studies)** — B.A. (6 semesters) / M.A. (4 semesters)

## Location

Kassel is known internationally for the documenta and offers a vibrant art scene. The Kunsthochschule is centrally located with excellent studio spaces, workshops, an exhibition hall, library (Bibliothek), digital media lab (Digithek), and a canteen (Mensa).

## Key Features

- **Interdisciplinary:** Art, design, visual communication, art history, and art education under one roof
- **Class system (Klassenmodell):** Personal mentoring by renowned artists and designers
- **documenta connection:** Close ties to the world's most important contemporary art exhibition
- **Small class sizes:** Approximately 800 students across all programs
- **Workshops & facilities:** Extensive workshops for wood, metal, textile, print, photography, film, digital media, and more
- **Exhibition hall:** Regular exhibitions by students, faculty, and guest artists
