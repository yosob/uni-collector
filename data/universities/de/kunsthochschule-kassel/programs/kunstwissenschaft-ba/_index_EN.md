# Kunstwissenschaft B.A. — Art History Bachelor

## Program Overview

The Bachelor of Arts in Kunstwissenschaft (Art Science/Art History) at Kunsthochschule Kassel is a 6-semester academic program that benefits from its unique location within an art academy. Students study art history and theory in direct exchange with practicing artists and designers.

The program covers art from the Middle Ages to contemporary art, with particular strengths in modern and contemporary art history, aesthetics, and art theory.

## Subject Areas

- **Medieval & Modern Art History** — Medieval and modern art historical survey
- **Modern Art History** — Focus on modern art movements and developments
- **Classical Modernism & Contemporary Art** — From classical modernism to current art practices
- **Aesthetics & Art Theory** — Philosophical and theoretical foundations of art

## Study Structure

- **Semesters 1-2:** Introduction to art history, methods of art history, basics of visual analysis
- **Semesters 3-4:** Deepening in specific epochs and themes, art theory, aesthetics
- **Semesters 5-6:** Specialization, practical projects (museum, exhibitions), Bachelor thesis

## Special Features

- Direct exchange with art and design students at the Kunsthochschule
- Access to the Kunsthochschule's exhibitions, workshops, and events
- Connection to documenta (world-renowned contemporary art exhibition held every 5 years in Kassel)
- Practice coordination and opportunities for study abroad

## Admission

Admission requires a general higher education entrance qualification (Abitur). No artistic aptitude test required, but strong interest in art and visual culture is expected. German language proficiency at C1 level is required.
