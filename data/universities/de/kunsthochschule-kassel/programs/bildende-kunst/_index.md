# Bildende Kunst / Fine Art (Diplom)

## Program Overview

The Fine Art program at Kunsthochschule Kassel is a 10-semester artistic program leading to a Diplom degree. It follows a unique class-based system where students work closely with established artists in dedicated studio classes.

The program begins with the **Basisklasse** (foundation class) in the first year, providing a broad introduction to artistic practices and critical thinking. After the foundation year, students join one of the specialized artist classes led by renowned professors.

## Artist Classes (Klassen)

- **Klasse Al Solh** — Contemporary artistic practice with focus on social and political contexts
- **Kunst im zeitgenössischen Kontext** — Art in contemporary context, interdisciplinary approaches
- **Klasse Melhus** — Media art, video, digital culture, expanded cinema
- **Klasse Schmidt** — Contemporary art practices
- **Klasse Slotawa** — Sculptural and conceptual art, spatial practices
- **Klasse Spehr** — Artistic research, experimental practices
- **Klasse Vallejos-de Bruijne** — Transcultural and interdisciplinary art practices

## Study Structure

- **Basisklasse (Semester 1-2):** Foundation year with broad exploration of artistic media and methods. Critical discourse and experimentation.
- **Klassenstudium (Semester 3-10):** Specialized study in one of the artist classes. Individual artistic development, studio work, exhibitions, and preparation of the Diplom thesis with final artistic presentation.

## Admission

Admission requires a general higher education entrance qualification (Abitur) and successful completion of the artistic aptitude test (Eignungsprüfung). Exceptional artistic talent may qualify without formal qualifications. The aptitude test involves portfolio review and an interview.

## Application

- **Deadline:** March 15 (for winter semester)
- **Portal:** Online application portal of Kunsthochschule Kassel
- **Requirements:** Portfolio with artistic work, CV, proof of qualifications
