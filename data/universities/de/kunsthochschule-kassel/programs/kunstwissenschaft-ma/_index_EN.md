# Kunstwissenschaft M.A. — Art History Master

## Program Overview

The Master of Arts in Kunstwissenschaft (Art Science/Art History) at Kunsthochschule Kassel is a 4-semester research-oriented program that builds on the Bachelor's degree. It offers deep engagement with art history, theory, and curatorial practice within the inspiring environment of an art academy.

The program prepares students for careers in museums, galleries, cultural institutions, publishing, academia, and curatorial practice.

## Subject Areas

- **Medieval & Modern Art History** — Medieval and modern art historical survey
- **Modern Art History** — Focus on modern art movements
- **Classical Modernism & Contemporary Art** — From classical modernism to current art practices
- **Aesthetics & Art Theory** — Philosophical and theoretical foundations
- **Curatorial Practice** — Exhibition making and curatorial strategies
- **Museum Studies** — Museum theory and practice

## Study Structure

- **Semesters 1-2:** Advanced seminars in art history and theory, research methods, curatorial practice
- **Semester 3:** Research project, specialization, preparation for thesis
- **Semester 4:** Master thesis and oral examination

## Special Features

- Research-oriented study within an art academy environment
- Direct access to contemporary art production at the Kunsthochschule
- Connection to documenta and Kassel's vibrant art scene
- Option for doctoral studies (Promotion) after completion
- Practice coordination and study abroad opportunities

## Admission

Admission requires a Bachelor's degree in Art History, Art Science, or a related field with at least satisfactory grades. Evidence of adequate linguistic and methodological competencies is required. German language proficiency at C1 level.
