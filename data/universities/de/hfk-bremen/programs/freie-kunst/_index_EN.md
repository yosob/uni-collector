## Program Description

The Fine Arts Diplom program is the core fine art program at HfK Bremen, spanning 10 semesters (5 years). The program is **grade-free (notenfrei)**, meaning students' artistic work is not evaluated with grades. The Diplom degree is equivalent to a Master's degree in the German education system.

The curriculum covers painting, sculpture, time-based media, space & body concepts, performance, drawing, and intermedia photography. Students develop their personal artistic language under individual professor guidance (Korrektur), while participating in group discussions, workshops, and exhibitions. The program encourages experimental, interdisciplinary artistic practice.

## Specializations / Classes

Students can join different professor studios (Klassen), with main areas including:

- **Painting**: Oil, acrylic, mixed media
- **Sculpture**: Sculpture, installation, spatial art
- **Drawing**: Sketching, watercolor, experimental drawing
- **Time-based Media**: Video, sound, new media
- **Space & Body Concepts**: Installation, performance
- **Performance**: Performance, process art
- **Intermedia Photography**: Art photography, conceptual photography
- **Film/Video**: Experimental film, documentary, video art
- **Printmaking**: Intaglio, relief, lithography, screen printing

## Curriculum Structure

### Foundation — Semesters 1-4
- Artistic Fundamentals
- Workshop Introductions
- Art Theory and History
- Individual Professor Instruction (Korrektur)

### Main Studies — Semesters 5-8
- Free Artistic Work
- Meisterschüler Examination
- Project Work and Exhibitions
- Theory Seminars

### Diplom Semester — Semesters 9-10
- Diplom Thesis (Artistic Work)
- Diplom Examination and Exhibition

## Highlights

- 🎨 **Free creation**: Grade-free (notenfrei), centered on personal artistic development
- 👨‍🏫 **Master-apprentice model**: Professor studio system (Klassensystem)
- 🏛️ **Diplom degree**: Equivalent to Master, highly regarded in the German art school tradition
- 🔧 **Excellent workshops**: Wood, metal, plastic, textile, ceramics, printmaking, photography, video workshops
- 🌍 **International**: Students from approx. 70 countries

## Application Information

### Timeline (WS 2026/27)
- **Application Period**: February 1 — April 15
- **Portfolio Upload**: April 20–23 (until 12:00 CEST)
- **Portfolio Review**: April 29–May 4
- **Results Notification**: from May 7
- **On-site Exam**: May 19–21 (HfK, Am Speicher XI 8)
- **Final Results**: early June
- **Application Portal**: https://application.hfk-bremen.de/

### Application Process
1. Register at the application portal
2. Upload digital portfolio (photos, PDF, video, sound, etc.)
3. If shortlisted, attend on-site exam (personal dialogue)
4. Receive admission results

### Application Materials
1. **Portfolio (Digital)**: Artistic works (drawings, objects, photography, film, animation, sound, etc.)
   - Recommended: approx. 20 digital images, PDF format
   - PDF max 50 MB, total upload limit 250 MB
2. **Academic Certificate**: Abitur or equivalent (exceptions for exceptional artistic talent)
3. **Curriculum Vitae (CV)**
4. **List of Works**
5. **Language Certificate**: DSH-2 / TestDaF 4x4 / telc Deutsch C1
