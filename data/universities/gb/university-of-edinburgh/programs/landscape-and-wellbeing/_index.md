## Programme Highlights

- **UK first**: The first MSc in the UK to focus on landscape and human wellbeing
- **Interdisciplinary**: Draws on expertise from architecture, landscape architecture, health, geography, and social sciences across the University
- **OPENspace**: Connected to the internationally recognised OPENspace Research Centre
- **Site-based learning**: Takes advantage of Scotland's rich natural heritage
- **Cross-disciplinary options**: Choose from courses across the University to suit your interests

## Careers

This degree prepares graduates for work in:
- Public health
- Urban development
- Green infrastructure planning
- Human geography
- Horticulture
- Therapeutic environments

It can also deepen practice for professionals in architecture, landscape architecture, and health, and provides excellent preparation for doctoral-level research.
