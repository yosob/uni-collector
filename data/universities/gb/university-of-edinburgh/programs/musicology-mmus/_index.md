## Entry Requirements

- **Academic**: UK 2:1 honours degree or international equivalent, in music, musicology or related
- **Professional**: Relevant professional experience also considered
- **Portfolio**: Not required
- **English**: IELTS 7.0 (6.5 each), TOEFL 100 (23 each before Jan 2026), C1/C2 185 (176 each), or equivalent

## Curriculum Overview (180 credits)

### Compulsory Courses
- **Perspectives in Musicology** (20 credits) — wide range of approaches in music scholarship
- **Musicology Research Methods** (20 credits) — advanced research skills
- **Dissertation** (60 credits) — 15,000-word dissertation

### Option Courses (80 credits)
- 4 music option courses (60 credits): Music/Philosophy/Politics, Popular Music, Medieval Afterlives, Music & Human Communication, Venice 1690-1740, etc.
- 1 elective from another subject area (20 credits)

## Programme Highlights

- **World-leading researchers** — music history, jazz, analysis, audiovisual media, psychology, sociology, philosophy
- **Research collections** — University Music Instrument Collection (6,000+ objects), School of Scottish Studies Archives
- **National Library of Scotland** — one of UK's five legal deposit libraries, short walk away
- **Vibrant research culture** — regular Music Research Seminar Series, student-led MUSIC.OLOGY.ECA journal
- **Since 1838** — one of the world's oldest centres for music research
- **Ideal foundation for PhD** — advanced critical thinking and research skills

## Career Paths

- Academic careers (PhD → university teaching/research)
- Journalism
- Arts administration
- Music education
- Librarianship
