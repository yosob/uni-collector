## Programme Highlights

1. **One of the UK's largest art history departments** — Exceptionally wide range of art-historical expertise across time and place, including Africa, Latin America, China, India, Japan, and Western Europe.
2. **Display and curatorial focus** — Compulsory course on the cultures and politics of display, bridging art history with museum and exhibition practices.
3. **Work placement opportunities** — Apply for competitive placements (~40/year) in Edinburgh's museums, galleries, and archives for hands-on professional experience.
4. **Scotland's cultural landscapes** — Organised field trips to cultural institutions across Scotland at no additional cost.
5. **World Heritage City** — Study in Edinburgh, a UNESCO World Heritage Site with leading museums, galleries, rich collections, and vibrant art festivals.

## Careers

Graduates pursue careers as:
- **Museum / Gallery curator** — Curatorial roles in museums and galleries worldwide
- **Arts administrator** — Management and programming in cultural organisations
- **Cultural heritage manager** — Heritage sector roles in conservation and public engagement
- **Academic researcher / PhD** — Progression to doctoral study in art history
- **Auction house specialist** — Specialist roles in major auction houses
- **Art consultant** — Advisory roles in the art market and cultural sector
- **Publishing / Art criticism** — Writing and editorial roles in art publishing

> Alumni have secured positions at institutions including MoMA (New York), the J. Paul Getty Museum (Los Angeles), and Centre Pompidou (Paris).
