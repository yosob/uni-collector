## Entry Requirements

- **Academic**: UK 2:1 honours degree or international equivalent, in art, design, craft or related subjects
- **Professional**: Relevant professional experience also considered
- **Portfolio**: Required — up to 15 pages illustrating craft/design-led aptitude (existing work, drawings, models, sketchbooks)
- **Personal statement**: 500 words — discuss a potential craft project on one of two themes: (1) Innovative material-led practices for sustainability, (2) Contributing to debates on Craft
- **Reference**: Required
- **English**: IELTS 7.0 (6.0 each), TOEFL 100 (20 each before Jan 2026), C1/C2 185 (169 each), or equivalent

## Curriculum Overview (180 credits)

The programme is structured across three semesters:

### Semester 1 — Critical Making & Collaboration
- Explore critical making addressing craft theory and practice
- Learn to critically connect theory and practice
- Internal collaborative group project
- Focus on cooperation, compromise, authority, and ownership in making

### Semester 2 — Cross-disciplinary Research & External Engagement
- Develop critical making using cross-disciplinary research methods
- Establish critical synergy between craft and design theory
- Explore new and emerging technologies and their impact on craft
- **1-week external placement**
- External engagement/outreach project

### Final Semester — Independent Final Project
- Develop a final project through critical making
- Output can take various forms: artefacts, series of works, collection, conference/publication, research website, podcast series, etc.
- Portfolio submission (material tests, prototypes, final artefacts, project books)

## Programme Highlights

- **State-of-the-art facilities** in ceramics, glass, jewellery, silversmithing, textiles and digital fabrication
- **Cross-disciplinary approach** — work across ceramics, glass, textiles, jewellery, silversmithing plus complementary materials
- **One of the few UK programmes** with such strong disciplinary focus across multiple craft fields
- **Unique in Scotland** — situated within one of the few centres of excellence in craft in the UK
- **Industry engagement** — collaborative projects with industry partners and external placements
- **Field trips** to local and national galleries and museums

## Career Paths

Graduates pursue careers as:
- Independent designer-makers
- Craft practitioners
- Creative industry professionals
- Design studio practitioners
- Academic researchers

The programme also prepares for doctoral-level study in craft, design, or related fields.
