## Curriculum Overview (180 credits)

### Compulsory Courses
- **Approaching World Objects** (20 credits, Sem 1) — global material culture, heritage, exhibition design
- **Global Premodern Cities** (20 credits, Sem 2) — cities as global hubs; includes optional study trip to Rome/Paris
- **Dissertation** (60 credits, Summer) — 15,000-word dissertation

### Option Courses (80 credits)
- 4 option courses from art history, music, literature, history, languages, architectural history

### Key Features
- **Geographic scope**: British Empire, Europe, Mediterranean, Latin America, Middle East, India, China, Japan
- **Period**: 3rd to 18th century
- **Cultures**: Christian, Islamic, Hindu, Buddhist
- **Optional study trip**: ~4 days in Rome or Paris (~£250)
- **Internship programme**: competitive placements at national/local institutions (~40/year)

## Programme Highlights

- **One of the UK's largest concentrations** of medieval, renaissance and early modern art expertise
- **Global perspective** — diversifying art history beyond Eurocentric frameworks
- **Behind-the-scenes access** to Edinburgh's world-class galleries and museums
- **Internship programme** — hands-on experience in cultural heritage sector
- **Optional study trip** to Rome or Paris with programme team
- **Small-group teaching** — capped option course numbers ensure intimate learning

## Career Paths

- Museums and galleries
- Libraries and archives
- Auction houses
- Heritage sector (public and private)
- Media and journalism
- Academic careers (PhD research)
