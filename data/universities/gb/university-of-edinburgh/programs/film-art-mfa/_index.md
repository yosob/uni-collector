## Entry Requirements

- **Academic**: UK 2:1 honours degree or international equivalent, in a relevant subject
- **Professional**: Relevant portfolio and/or professional experience may be considered
- **Portfolio**: Required — demonstrate basic technical film skills, creative field knowledge, and interest in experimental filmmaking
- **English**: IELTS 6.5 (6.0 each), TOEFL 92 (20 each before Jan 2026), C1/C2 176 (169 each), or equivalent

## Curriculum Overview

### Year 1 — Edinburgh College of Art (120 credits)

**Semester 1 (Compulsory + Elective):**
- Cinema Lab 1 (20 credits)
- Permanent Cinema (20 credits)
- Elective choice (20 credits) from options including: Surfaces & Screens, Design Play and Games, Media and Culture, Theorising Contemporary Art, Art and Digital Culture, Contemporary Artists' Film and Video, Music on Screen, Audio Crafting

**Semester 2 (Compulsory):**
- Poetics of Cinema (20 credits)
- Film Art Development (40 credits) — thesis film development & pre-production

### Year 2 — Paris College of Art (~180 credits)

- Thesis film production, post-production, and completion
- Courses delivered at Paris College of Art
- Festival-ready thesis film as final output

## Programme Highlights

- **Dual-campus programme** — Year 1 in Edinburgh, Year 2 in Paris
- **Two renowned art schools** in European capital cities with strong cinema cultures
- **Industry-structured learning** — programme mirrors professional filmmaking workflows
- **Practising filmmakers as tutors** — directors, producers, cinematographers, editors, sound designers
- **Festival-ready thesis film** — complete a feature-length or short film project
- **Field trips funded** by the University between Edinburgh and Paris
- **Specialisation options**: fiction, experimental, or expanded filmmaking

## Career Paths

Graduates pursue careers as:
- Directors
- Artist filmmakers
- Cinematographers
- Editors
- Producers
- Sound recordists/designers
- Production/location managers
- Researchers
- Festival programmers

BAFTA and RTS award-winning alumni. Many graduates have founded production companies or work at international production firms.
