## Entry Requirements

- **Academic**: UK 2:1 honours degree or international equivalent, in architecture, urban design, planning, or related discipline
- **Professional**: Relevant professional experience also considered
- **Portfolio**: Not required
- **English**: IELTS 7.0 (6.5 writing, 6.0 other), TOEFL 100 (23 writing, 20 other before Jan 2026), C1/C2 185 (176 writing, 169 other), or equivalent

## Curriculum Overview (180 credits)

### Semester 1 — Heriot-Watt University (80 credits)
- **Sustainable Design and Development** (20 credits) — urban design principles, sustainability, development process
- **Urban Design Theory** (20 credits) — socio-economic, ecological, spatial perspectives
- **Strategic Spatial Vision Project** (20 credits) — strategic area development planning
- **Urban Design for Health and Wellbeing** (20 credits) — social, economic, environmental health

### Semester 2 — Mixed (60 credits)
- **Urban Project** (20 credits, Heriot-Watt) — applied urban design project
- **Compulsory course** (20 credits, Edinburgh ESALA)
- **Option course** (20 credits, Edinburgh) — choose from Emergent Cities, Cultural Landscapes, etc.

### Summer
- **Dissertation** (60 credits) — urban-themed, student-led, location-based

## Programme Highlights

- **Joint programme** between University of Edinburgh (ESALA) and Heriot-Watt University
- **Multi-disciplinary** — open to geography, sociology, architecture, planning, economics graduates
- **Edinburgh as laboratory** — unique urban design case study
- **Global perspective** — opportunity to focus on Global South urban transformations
- **Geddesian planning tradition** — draws on Edinburgh's historic planning heritage
- **Research-active staff** — linked to Global Urban Research Collaborative
- **Industry engagement** — guest speakers from policy and practice
- **Bridges architecture and planning** — addresses gap between design-driven and strategy-driven urban practice

Graduates pursue careers in:
