description_en: 'MA Illustration is a programme designed to develop, challenge and
  push the boundaries of what it means to be a contemporary illustrator in an international
  industry. A synthesis of theory and practice, this programme creates discourse around
  what illustration is, and what it can be.

  A studio culture opens up collaborative practice, and individual development is
  fostered through a dedicated studio with individual work spaces. You will explore
  contemporary issues through traditional image-making techniques to futuristic and
  experimental techniques.

  Postgraduate study of this subject allows for a deeper analysis of themes and texts
  and enables you to build an innovative and focused portfolio. Often, our students
  have ambitions for the professional sphere, or they may wish to pursue doctoral
  studies. In each case, there is a sustained level of research, allied to technical
  ways of working and conceptual thinking.

  Over the course of a busy year you are invited to find your own creative voice,
  fostering individual practices. You build upon this throughout the year with tutor
  support. You will write research proposals in the early weeks, describing ideas
  and contexts for individual projects, and you will also be given set projects and
  collaborative tasks at various points in the academic year.

  There is a Life Drawing studio with a series of classes and various digital clusters
  throughout the campus. Theoretical work underpins all studio practice at many levels,
  and there is a series of visiting lecturers from professional and academic worlds.

  Group projects have included exhibitions at the Scottish Parliament and Institut
  Français Écosse, postcards for the Heart of Midlothian project, and illustrations
  for charities such as Children of Scotland and Ocean Generation. Competitions and
  opportunities include Penguin, V&A, The Folio Society, 3x3, and Macmillan Children''s
  Book Competition.

## Entry Requirements

- **Academic**: UK 2:1 honours degree or international equivalent, in graphics, illustration, or art and design subjects
- **Professional**: Relevant professional experience considered without academic qualification
- **Portfolio**: Required — must demonstrate observational drawing, colour/line/composition, idea development, awareness of narrative & sequential image-making, print & digital media, and professional/cultural context of illustration
- **Personal statement + CV**: Required
- **English**: IELTS 7.0 (6.0 each), TOEFL 100 (20 each), or equivalent
