  framework. The Critical Practice Course is the foundational component, teaching
  a variety of research techniques and methods to critically assess and question assumptions.
  Students are encouraged to establish self-led design groups and engage in peer-to-peer
  feedback sessions, thinking differently, altering preconceived notions, discovering
  new ways of seeing, and developing visual awareness.

  Studio-based learning is supported by a wide range of facilities including letterpress,
  screen printing, risograph, photography, analogue and digital fabric printing, as

  critiques, skills workshops, mini-symposiums and seminars.

## Entry Requirements

- **Academic**: UK 2:1 honours degree or international equivalent, in graphics, illustration, or art and design subjects
- **Professional**: Relevant professional experience considered without academic qualification
- **Portfolio**: Required — focus on ideas and thinking behind work, not just technique. Show development process, conceptual awareness, lateral thinking, and original professionally resolved work
- **Personal statement + CV**: Required
- **English**: IELTS 7.0 (6.0 each), TOEFL 100 (20 each), or equivalent
