description_en: 'An interdisciplinary taught masters programme at the forefront of
  the University of Edinburgh''s rich expertise in the creative arts, music and digital
  media design. The programme provides knowledge, skill and understanding to thrive
  in a range of sound design situations, both commercial and experimental.

  Practice-led and strongly interdisciplinary, students learn to create innovative
  and expressive sound media including film sound, interactive game-audio design,
  sound installation and sonic art, sound software design, and creative music and
  compositional practices.

  In the first semester, the focus is on developing sound design skills, exploring
  the potential for sound recording, editing in the digital domain, processing sound
  to create new sonic structures, and considering relationships between code and sound.
  In the second semester, students consolidate knowledge through game audio technologies,
  sound for screen, and creative audio programming. The programme culminates in a
  self-directed but supervised research-led sound design project over the summer period.

  The programme has over 20 years of experience in teaching, learning and exploring
  the limits of sound design as a creative practice, academic discipline and career
  training.

## Entry Requirements

- **Academic**: UK 2:1 honours degree or international equivalent in music, sound design, media arts, sound engineering, acoustics, film, animation, or art and design
- **Professional**: Relevant professional experience also considered
- **Portfolio**: Required — at least 3 pieces (PDF with links); sound design examples, sound recordings, documentation of installations/software. Emphasis on sound design over music composition
- **Personal statement + CV**: Required
- **English**: IELTS 6.5 (6.0 each), TOEFL 92 (20 each), or equivalent
