## Entry Requirements

- **Academic**: UK 2:1 honours degree or international equivalent in physics, mathematics, computer science, or engineering. Music/music technology degrees accepted where significant math/physics component is demonstrable
- **Professional**: Relevant professional experience also considered
- **Portfolio**: Not required
- **References**: 1 reference required
- **English**: IELTS 7.0 (6.0 each), TOEFL 100 (20 each), or equivalent — **higher than most ECA programmes**
- **ATAS**: May require Academic Technology Approval Scheme clearance for visa holders

## Teaching & Assessment

- **Teaching methods**: Lectures, seminars, tutorials, practical work
- **Software training**: MATLAB (core), Python and C++ (optional courses)
- **Assessment**: Report submissions, written examinations
- **Final project**: Individual dissertation + presentation (last 3 months)

## Why This Programme

1. **Scientific rigour** — Enhanced career opportunities and possibility for further doctoral study through rigorous scientific training in acoustics and audio signal processing
2. **Creative coursework** — Availability of creative music technology coursework alongside technical training
3. **Cross-university options** — Optional modules from School of Informatics, School of Mathematics, and School of Physics and Astronomy
4. **Technical facilities** — Studios, VR/multichannel spaces, practice rooms with Steinway pianos
5. **Alumni network** — Join a large and supportive community of AMT students from the past 19 years

## Facilities

- Teaching in Alison House (Reid School of Music) for core coursework
- Some tutorials in central campus large computer labs
- Specialist facilities: studio spaces, VR/multichannel rooms, practice spaces with Steinway pianos
- Note: Reid School of Music relocating from Alison House to Lauriston Campus as part of ECA redevelopment (2024–2027)
