description_en: 'Established in 2001, the Masters of Contemporary Art Theory (MA CAT)
  is a post-studio programme that supports the research and practices of artists and
  aspiring art professionals. The MA CAT encourages practices that are speculative
  and reflective, developing artistic research on, and in, a range of media, sites
  and organisations.

  The programme supports an applied knowledge of art now, grounding schooling in the
  practices of art and contemporary art theory as well as extra-disciplinary and intermedial
  approaches. The taught curriculum consists of an innovative series of learning sprints
  designed to support the development of your artistic research and practice through
  workshops, practical partnerships with art organisations, carefully scaffolded approaches
  to collaborative inquiry (''problem-based learning''), and peer-production (''paragogy'').

  A defining aspect of the theory programme is the emphasis placed upon speculative
  scepticism — not starting from the position of knowing what post-studio practice
  is but being motivated by speculation on what it might be. The programme generates
  a dynamic, intellectually ambitious environment in which staff and students learn
  together, attracting students with very different backgrounds and experiences to
  generate diversity and richness.

  During the programme you will apply aesthetics, art theory and criticism, art historiography,
  anthropology, and conceptual personae to engage with contemporary art''s variety
  of media, technologies, images, artefacts, tactics, texts, cultural contexts and
  professional practices. The programme consists of 4 taught courses and 1 research
  capstone, each broken down into 3 or 4 ''learning sprints''.

## Programme Highlights

1. **Community-driven learning** — The MA CAT integrates peer and project approaches to learning, fostering a vibrant community where students and staff support each other.
2. **Research-led curriculum** — The curriculum is driven by staff and student curiosity, making it 'new' each year. Students become part of an evolving research project.
3. **Practical approach** — Engage in 'live' projects in collaboration with Edinburgh's numerous arts organisations. Apply artistic learning in real-world settings.
4. **World-leading research university** — Based in the University of Edinburgh, one of the world's leading research universities.
5. **Exceptional facilities** — Postgraduate study spaces, computing suites, creative industry-standard software, fabrication workshops, and an MA CAT project space.

## Careers

The programme prepares graduates for roles as:
- **Artist** — Developing a research-practice in contemporary art
- **Academic / PhD researcher** — Bridging undergraduate and doctoral research
- **Art critic / writer** — Engaging with contemporary art criticism and theory
- **Curator / Producer** — Organisational and curatorial practice in the art world
- **Art educator** — Creative and professional development in art education
