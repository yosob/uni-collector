description_en: 'Design for Change is an exciting interdisciplinary programme which
  seeks to holistically address complex global challenges — ageing populations, disruptive
  technologies, economic instabilities and inequalities, conflict and displacement,
  and environmental degradation and injustices — through design-led interventions.

  It aims to foster a new breed of designer for the 21st century, offering a particular
  blend of eco-social design. You will develop skills to research, ideate, communicate
  and deliver propositions for change that are just and equitable, and socio-culturally,
  politically and environmentally aware.

  The programme embodies and promotes ways of designing that are slow and systemic.
  You will study emplaced, relational approaches to designing for change, conscious
  of positionality, habit, ritual, practice and belief, and cultural and social difference.
  You will be guided to consider the more-than-human in your design and to explore
  alternatives to the status quo.

  Students come from all corners of the world, bringing unique perspectives and diverse
  backgrounds to enrich the studio. Together with staff, you will create a plural
  community of practice and become part of a wider design movement for change.

  '
