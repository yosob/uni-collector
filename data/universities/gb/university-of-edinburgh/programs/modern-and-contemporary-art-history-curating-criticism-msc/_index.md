## Programme Highlights

1. **Research-intensive department** — One of the largest and oldest art history departments in the UK, with world-class research output informing teaching
2. **Curatorial focus** — Core course "The Cultures and Politics of Display" addresses curating, exhibition making and the mediation of art to the public
3. **Work placement** — Competitive placements with art institutions in and near Edinburgh provide real-world professional experience
4. **Broad temporal scope** — Covers 19th to 21st century art, from historical avant-gardes to the latest contemporary trends
5. **Capital city setting** — Edinburgh's vibrant cultural landscape offers access to galleries, museums, archives and artist studios
6. **Field trips** — Organised visits to cultural institutions across Scotland at no additional cost

## Careers

The programme prepares graduates for roles as:
- **Curator** — Gallery and museum curation
- **Arts administrator** — Managing arts organisations and cultural institutions
- **Art critic/writer** — Contemporary art criticism and journalism
- **Academic/PhD researcher** — Further study in art history and visual culture
- **Exhibition organiser** — Planning and delivering exhibitions and art events
- **Cultural heritage professional** — Working with collections and public engagement
- **Art consultant** — Advisory roles in the art market and cultural sector
