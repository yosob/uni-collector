## Programme Overview

This programme encourages **speculative and reflective approaches** to art-making across all media. It provides opportunity for reflection, review and refinement of contemporary art practice towards public exhibition and professional portfolio.

The programme is structured in three phases:
1. **Autumn (Semester 1)**: Experimentation and reflection — *Art: Studio*
2. **Spring (Semester 2)**: Positioning work outside the studio/gallery for diverse audiences — *Art: Post Studio*
3. **Summer (Block 5+)**: Independent research culminating in the masters project and exhibition — *Art: Masters Exhibition*

## Career Paths

Graduates develop ambitious art practices and gain organisational knowledge for self-employment. Notable alumni achievements include:
- Bloomberg New Contemporaries
- RSA New Contemporaries
- John Kinross Scholarship (e.g. Florence residency)
- Saatchi New Sensations
- Glasgow Film Festival
- Edinburgh International Art Festival

The programme also prepares for doctoral-level study.
