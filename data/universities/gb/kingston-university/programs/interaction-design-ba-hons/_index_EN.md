# Interaction Design BA (Hons)

> Shape the way you experience and interact with the world around you. This course focuses on human experience, employing a human-centred approach to interactive storytelling and immersive experience design.

## Overview

BA (Hons) Interaction Design at Kingston School of Art explores how people connect with technology, spaces, and each other. Based within the vibrant, well-regarded Department of Illustration Animation — known for nurturing successful graduates and its human-centred approach to visual communication, design, and immersive storytelling — these key elements deeply enrich your learning experience.

The course focuses on human experience, employing a human-centred approach to interactive storytelling and immersive experience design. Students will explore augmented and virtual reality, app and screen design, physical computing, projection mapping, and creative coding. You will create and design interactions and experiences that address urgent issues and experimental ideas through innovative technologies, creative responses, and critical speculative practices.

The programme is designed for students who want to explore the intersection of design, technology, and human experience, working across digital and physical media to create meaningful interactive experiences.

### Key Features

- Based within the renowned Department of Illustration Animation at Kingston School of Art
- Human-centred design approach rooted in interactive storytelling
- Explore AR/VR, physical computing, projection mapping, and creative coding
- Strong emphasis on making and hands-on experimentation
- Opportunities for professional placement year (sandwich route)
- Located at Knights Park campus — Kingston School of Art's dedicated creative hub

## Course Structure

### Year 1 — Fundamentals

Year 1 introduces fundamental principles of design, interaction, and visual communication. Students explore how interaction design can create meaningful connections between people, technology, and environments.

| Module | Credits | Description |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| Sensing and Storytelling | 30 | Introduction to the fundamentals of interaction design through sensing and storytelling — exploring how we perceive, interpret, and communicate through designed experiences |
| Thinking through Making | 30 | Discover how making can be a powerful way of thinking and problem-solving — developing hands-on skills in material exploration and experimental fabrication |
| Professional Identity and Collaboration | 30 | Begin to develop your professional identity through collaboration and communication — working with peers on shared projects and building creative networks |
| Critical and Contextual Studies 1 | 30 | Explore the histories of illustration, animation, and interaction design — understanding the critical, cultural, and theoretical contexts that shape creative practice |

### Year 2 — Development and Application

Year 2 develops skills in interaction design practice, creative technologies, and professional development. Students begin to specialise and take increasing ownership of their creative direction.

| Module | Credits | Description |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| Spatial Interaction Design | 30 | Explore how bodies interact with space, examining how movement, proximity, and environment shape experience — designing spatial and embodied interactions |
| Prototyping and Creative Technologies | 30 | Develop skills in turning early ideas into working prototypes — exploring physical computing, virtual and augmented realities, sound design, and creative coding |
| Agency and Professional Practice | 30 | Develop growing independence and understanding of professional practice — building your agency as a designer and navigating the creative industries |
| Critical and Contextual Studies 2 | 30 | Deepen critical understanding that connects your design practice to the wider world — engaging with contemporary debates in design, technology, and society |

### Year 3 — Major Project and Professional Portfolio

Year 3 focuses on an extended self-directed project alongside professional development. Students define their own creative voice and produce a portfolio of work ready for professional practice.

| Module | Credits | Description |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| Major Project: Networks and Connection | 60 | Explore how we connect, communicate, and collaborate — undertaking an extended self-directed project that demonstrates mastery of interaction design |
| Professional Identity and Public Engagement | 30 | Position yourself critically and culturally within the world of design — developing your public profile and professional engagement strategy |
| Dissertation | 15 | Complete an extended research project on a topic of your choice — demonstrating critical thinking and scholarly writing skills |
| Future Skills | 15 | Develop future-facing skills for the creative industries — preparing for professional life after graduation |
