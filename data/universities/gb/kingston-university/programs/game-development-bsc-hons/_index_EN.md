## Overview

Game Development BSc (Hons) at Kingston University is designed for students who want to combine creative design with technical programming skills to create compelling interactive games and digital experiences. The course provides a comprehensive grounding in game design, programming, 3D modelling, and interactive media production, preparing graduates for careers in the fast-growing games and interactive entertainment industries.

Based within the Faculty of Science, Engineering and Computing, the course draws on Kingston University's expertise in both computer science and creative digital media. You will learn to programme game engines, design game mechanics, create 3D assets and environments, and understand the broader contexts of interactive entertainment — from narrative design and player psychology to the business of games publishing and distribution.

The course balances theoretical knowledge with hands-on practice. You will work with industry-standard tools and game engines (such as Unity and Unreal Engine), develop skills in C# and C++ programming, and build a portfolio of game projects that demonstrate your creative and technical abilities. Collaborative projects mirror industry practice, with students working in multidisciplinary teams to prototype, develop, and polish game experiences.

Kingston's location in London provides access to one of Europe's largest games industry clusters, with opportunities for networking, internships, and industry events. The university also has strong links with local and national game studios, supporting live briefs, guest lectures, and placement opportunities.

## Course Structure

### Year 1

Year 1 establishes the fundamental skills in programming, game design principles, and digital media creation. You will learn to code, understand how games function as interactive systems, and begin building your first playable game prototypes.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Programming for Games | 30 |
| Game Design Principles | 30 |
| 3D Modelling and Digital Visualisation | 30 |
| Mathematics and Problem Solving for Games | 30 |

**Programming for Games** — Introduces core programming concepts through the lens of game development. You will learn a modern object-oriented programming language (typically C#), working with variables, control structures, functions, and data structures. Practical exercises and mini-projects reinforce learning, culminating in the development of simple 2D game prototypes.

**Game Design Principles** — Explores the fundamental concepts behind game design, including game mechanics, player engagement, level design, and balancing. You will analyse successful and influential games, understand the principles of interactive storytelling, and apply design thinking to create your own game concepts and rule systems.

**3D Modelling and Digital Visualisation** — Introduces the creation of 3D digital assets for games. You will learn to use industry-standard modelling software to create characters, environments, props, and vehicles, developing skills in polygon modelling, texturing, UV mapping, and rendering. The module also covers the principles of visual design as they apply to game worlds.

**Mathematics and Problem Solving for Games** — Covers the essential mathematical concepts used in game development, including vectors, matrices, trigonometry, and physics simulation. You will learn to apply these concepts to solve common game development challenges such as collision detection, character movement, and camera systems.

### Year 2

Year 2 deepens technical skills and introduces more advanced topics in game development. You will work with game engines, explore artificial intelligence in games, and collaborate on larger-scale team projects that mirror professional studio practice.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Game Engine Development | 30 |
| Artificial Intelligence for Games | 30 |
| Interactive Narrative and Player Experience | 30 |
| Collaborative Game Project | 30 |

**Game Engine Development** — Develops your understanding of how game engines work and how to use them effectively. You will work with industry-standard engines (such as Unity and Unreal Engine), learning to create gameplay systems, manage assets, implement physics, and optimise performance. The module covers both using existing engines and understanding the underlying architecture.

**Artificial Intelligence for Games** — Introduces AI techniques used in modern games, including pathfinding, finite state machines, behaviour trees, and procedural content generation. You will implement AI systems for non-player characters (NPCs), create believable opponent behaviours, and explore how AI can enhance player experience.

**Interactive Narrative and Player Experience** — Examines the relationship between narrative, interactivity, and player engagement. You will study storytelling techniques adapted for interactive media, explore branching narratives, and learn how to design meaningful player choices. The module combines creative writing for games with UX design and player psychology.

**Collaborative Game Project** — A team-based module in which you work in small multidisciplinary groups to design, prototype, and develop a complete game. You will adopt professional studio roles (programmer, designer, artist), follow agile development methodologies, and present your work at a showcase event. This module mirrors the collaborative nature of the games industry.

### Year 3 (or Year 4 with sandwich placement)

Year 3 focuses on advanced game development techniques and a substantial individual project. You will specialise in areas of interest, develop professional skills, and create a polished portfolio piece.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Advanced Game Programming | 30 |
| Games Industry and Professional Practice | 30 |
| Individual Game Project | 60 |

**Advanced Game Programming** — Covers advanced programming techniques for games including networking and multiplayer systems, shader programming, procedural generation, and performance optimisation. You will work on complex technical challenges and develop specialist skills that are highly valued in the games industry.

**Games Industry and Professional Practice** — Prepares you for professional life in the games and interactive media industries. You will learn about the business of games, including publishing, funding models, marketing, and intellectual property. The module covers portfolio development, interview techniques, and career planning, with input from industry professionals.

**Individual Game Project** — The capstone project in which you conceive, design, and develop a substantial game or interactive experience. This self-directed project allows you to demonstrate your technical skills, creative vision, and project management abilities. You will produce a polished, portfolio-ready piece of work, supported by a supervisor who provides guidance and feedback throughout the development process.

## Teaching & Assessment

Teaching is delivered through a combination of:

- Practical programming workshops and lab sessions
- Game design studios and creative workshops
- Lectures and seminars on theory and context
- Team-based project work simulating industry practice
- Guest lectures from games industry professionals
- Independent study and self-directed project development

Assessment typically comprises:

- Practical coursework (game prototypes, programming assignments, 3D assets)
- Group projects and presentations
- Written reports and design documentation
- Portfolio submissions
- Individual game project (dissertation)

## Career Prospects

Graduates of Game Development BSc (Hons) are equipped with the technical skills, creative vision, and professional understanding to pursue careers in the games industry and wider creative technology sector.

Graduates go on to work in a wide range of roles, including:

- Gameplay programmer
- Game designer
- Technical artist
- Level designer
- UI/UX designer for games
- 3D modeller / environment artist
- AI programmer
- Tools programmer
- Producer / project manager
- Quality assurance (QA) tester
- Interactive media developer
- VR/AR developer

### Industry Connections

The course benefits from Kingston University's strong links with the UK games industry, particularly studios in the London and South East region. Students engage with industry through guest speakers, studio visits, game jams, and placement opportunities. Kingston's location provides access to major games industry events including EGX, Develop:Brighton, and the London Games Festival.
