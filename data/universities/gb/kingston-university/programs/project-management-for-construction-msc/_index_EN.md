## Overview

The Project Management for Construction MSc at Kingston University provides a comprehensive understanding of project management principles as applied to the construction industry. The course is designed for graduates and professionals who want to develop their expertise in managing construction projects from inception to completion.

You will study project planning, risk management, contract administration, construction technology, sustainability and health and safety. The course combines technical knowledge with management and leadership skills, preparing you for senior roles in the construction sector.

The programme is accredited by the Chartered Institute of Building (CIOB) and contributes towards membership requirements.

## Course Structure

### Semester 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Project Management Principles | 30 |
| Construction Technology and Materials | 30 |

**Project Management Principles** — Covers the core principles and methodologies of project management, including planning, scheduling, cost control, risk management and quality assurance in construction contexts.

**Construction Technology and Materials** — Develops understanding of modern construction methods, materials science, structural principles and building services in relation to project management.

### Semester 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Contract Management and Law | 30 |
| Sustainable Construction | 30 |

**Contract Management and Law** — Examines construction contracts, procurement strategies, legal frameworks, dispute resolution and the contractual responsibilities of project managers.

**Sustainable Construction** — Explores sustainability in construction, including environmental assessment, energy efficiency, waste management, green building standards and the circular economy in the built environment.

### Semester 3 (Major Project)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Dissertation / Major Project | 60 |

**Dissertation / Major Project** — Either a research dissertation on a construction management topic or a practice-based project addressing a real construction management challenge.
