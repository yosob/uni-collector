## Overview

Fashion is not an easy thing to define. It is commonly identified as being about clothes, but that cannot be all it is, because there are clothes that are unfashionable. Fashion must be the quality that makes a particular thing desirable at a particular moment in time. A huge industry has been built on the notion of fleeting desirability, a need to re-invent, re-interpret, transform. The industry requires the creative talent and imagination of those who understand the complex web of influences that shape our collective idea of what looks right at a certain time.

The BA (Hons) Fashion course at Kingston University is designed to attract those who are fascinated by change and the influences that cause it. Focusing on individual awareness and creativity, the course places emphasis on initiative, collaboration and maximising the opportunities inherent in higher education.

At Kingston School of Art, you will experiment and be encouraged to push boundaries. You'll explore sketch work, 2D to 3D, knitwear and digital print in workshops and dedicated fashion design studios. Throughout the course, you will develop your expertise in current design approaches, techniques and media, including branding, editorial and interactive design. All whilst working on core topics of sustainable and experiential design.

The course is taught primarily through studio-based design projects which are devised to promote consideration of aspects of design relevant to the module and increase in complexity as the course progresses. Contextual studies address the understanding of fashion in an intellectual, historical and cultural context. Skills and techniques such as knowledge of fabric, pattern cutting and construction, CAD and photography support project work and enable the realisation of a design idea.

As part of Kingston School of Art, students benefit from joining a creative community where collaborative working and critical practice are encouraged. Workshops and studios are open to all disciplines, enabling collaborative and multidisciplinary teamwork between students and staff. You'll also have access to the Benenden Fashion Archive, which features pieces dating from 1750 to the present day.

## Course Structure

### Year 1

Year 1 introduces design process covering all the 2D aspects of fashion design, and design product covering all 3D aspects. You will explore knitted and printed textiles, fashion styling, photography, pattern cutting and draping to create shape. Fashion illustration classes and workshops in Photoshop, pattern cutting, knitting and sewing techniques are delivered to support studio classes.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Design Process 1 | 30 |
| Design Product 1 | 30 |
| Fashion Design Process 1 | 30 |
| History of Fashion, Style and Dress | 30 |

**Design Process 1** — Complements Design Product 1, with emphasis on challenging traditional methods and innovation through research, design development and presentation, continually underpinned by drawing and the realisation of 2D sketchbook work in relation to initial 3D prototypes.

**Design Product 1** — Complements Design Process 1 in the realisation of a final toile and/or knitted piece. Projects focus on various aspects of the design resolution process leading to the making of a product using taught technical skills. Research includes studying the in-house dress archive to replicate and produce a contemporary piece.

**Fashion Design Process 1** — Introduces broader fashion skills such as textiles, imagery through photography, fashion illustration and digital design skills. Made up of projects which focus on historical research, contemporary design and social aspects. Introduces students to professional world skills such as time management and presentation skills.

**History of Fashion, Style and Dress** — Presents thematic approaches to the study of fashion as an historical subject through image-based lectures, screenings and study visits. Introduces the historical development of fashion and dress from the 1750s to the present day. Each session addresses particular ideas and practices that have shaped and constructed our contemporary understanding of fashion as both a productive profession and as a meaningful social and cultural activity.

### Year 2

In Year 2 you will participate in live, sponsored projects and international competitions. You will experience professional practice through a careers module, including talks by concept designers, retailers, stylists and recruitment agencies, plus CV writing workshops and role playing for interviews. You will explore trend through concept design and work in teams on a sustainable design project that is exhibited in London.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Design Process 2 | 30 |
| Design Product 2 | 30 |
| Professional Practice 2 | 30 |
| Critical Issues in Fashion: Research and Practice | 30 |

**Design Process 2** — Split into different projects sponsored by professional practitioners and industrial sponsors. Building on prior learning, students continue to research and design to a given brief. Projects are two-dimensionally based and assessed by the submission of a portfolio including all development work and a final presentation suitable to show to external sponsors.

**Design Product 2** — Covers the three-dimensional aspect of industrially sponsored projects. The designs in the 2D portfolio within Design Process 2 inform the realisation of a prototype (toile) garment or finalised outfit. Garments are completed in calico or appropriate fabric depending on the project brief. May include knitwear as a final 3D piece.

**Professional Practice 2** — Enables you to build on prior knowledge to assess yourself in relation to the profession of fashion design. You will develop a body of work as evidence of your achievements through group work, interview techniques and CV writing. You will begin to plan your professional portfolio as preparation for Professional Practice 3 in Level 6.

**Critical Issues in Fashion: Research and Practice** — Builds on the historical and thematic content introduced at Level 4 and emphasises the theorisation of contemporary fashion practice. Lectures, seminars, workshops, tutorials, screenings and visits inform and support your own emerging research interests and the development of independent visual and academic research skills that cross history/theory and design practice.

### Optional Year

At the end of Year 2, you have the opportunity to take a year out to study abroad and gain work experience overseas. Study Abroad programmes are located in Berlin, Amsterdam, Paris, Milan, Hong Kong, New York, Seoul and Toronto. A dedicated placement officer within the fashion department supports the placement programme. Students on the placement year receive a Certificate of Industrial Experience for satisfactory completion of the required placement module.

### Year 3 (Final Year)

The final year is about creating your own collection and portfolio and entering the British Fashion Council competitions including Mulberry, Burberry, Pringle and Chloe. All students show at the degree fashion show in Kingston; selected students show at the London press show and exhibit at Graduate Fashion Week in London.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Pre-collection & Competition | 30 |
| Major Project (Final Collection) | 60 |
| Extended Research Project | 15 |

**Pre-collection & Competition** — A combination of preparation for the Final Major Project in the form of a pre-collection project folder and the choice of a selected external competition.

**Major Project (Final Collection)** — The capstone project covering all 2D and 3D aspects relating to the development of the final collection and exit portfolio. Covers the three main stages of designing for fashion: research, design development and presentation. As a designer you will be supported by a design tutor and a pattern cutting tutor to realise the main body of work, reflecting industry practice and enabling you to engage with a team collaborating to create original work.

**Extended Research Project** — Supports an extended research project that deepens your understanding of critical issues relevant to your practice. You'll explore a topic of your choosing, framed by questions emerging from your own design work and informed by relevant theory, history or cultural context. Working independently and with tutor support, you'll undertake rigorous enquiry using appropriate research methods and sources.

**Future Skills** — A dedicated opportunity to develop Future Skills Graduate Attributes. You'll self-assess your current skills profile and determine which attributes and skills you need to develop to support your career ambitions, supported by a dedicated career coach. Activities may include short courses, enrichment activities, micro-placements and experiential learning.

### Portfolio Requirements

Please submit a portfolio in digital format, 15–20 pages in length. We're looking for you to demonstrate four key values:

- Questioning
- Curiosity
- Technical ability
- Enthusiasm

A project may be spread over several pages or take up just one. Combining your work into a PDF is recommended. You can include links to a website, Instagram, Flickr or Vimeo account.

## Teaching & Assessment

Teaching is delivered through a combination of:

- Studio-based design projects
- Live, sponsored projects with industry partners
- Lectures, seminars and tutorials
- Workshops in pattern cutting, knitting, sewing, CAD and photography
- Group critiques and peer review
- Study visits to galleries, museums and fashion events

Assessment typically comprises:

- Practical work (portfolio presentations, fashion shows, exhibitions)
- Coursework (design projects, essays, self-assessment, research folders)
