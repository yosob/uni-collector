## Overview

The Museum and Gallery Studies MA at Kingston School of Art provides a critical and practical introduction to museum and gallery work. The course explores the theory and practice of curating, collecting, exhibition design, education and management within museums and galleries.

Situated within an art school environment, the course benefits from Kingston's strong links with London's museum and gallery sector. You will engage with contemporary debates about the role of museums in society, including issues of representation, decolonisation, digital engagement and audience participation.

The course includes practical placements and project work with partner institutions, providing real-world experience in the museum and gallery sector.

## Course Structure

### Semester 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Museum and Gallery Histories and Theories | 30 |
| Curatorial Practice | 30 |

**Museum and Gallery Histories and Theories** — Explores the historical development and contemporary theory of museums and galleries. Covers topics including the politics of collecting, exhibition histories, museum ethics and the social role of cultural institutions.

**Curatorial Practice** — Introduces the practical skills of curating, including exhibition planning, interpretation, display design, cataloguing and working with artists and collections.

### Semester 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Collections Management and Conservation | 30 |
| Research Methods and Professional Practice | 30 |

**Collections Management and Conservation** — Covers the principles and practices of collections management, including cataloguing, storage, conservation, digital archiving and collections-based research.

**Research Methods and Professional Practice** — Develops research skills relevant to museum and gallery studies. Includes a professional placement or project with a partner museum or gallery.

### Semester 3 (Major Project)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Major Project | 60 |

**Major Project** — You may choose to produce either a dissertation on a museum/gallery topic or a practice-based curatorial project (such as an exhibition proposal, digital project or education programme), supported by a written critical commentary.
