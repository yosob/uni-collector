# Fine Art BA (Hons)

## Overview

The Fine Art BA (Hons) at Kingston University enables students to experiment and explore their creative potential, preparing them for a career in the visual arts or wider creative industries. Students work with a range of traditional and new technologies, developing ideas through sculpture, painting, printmaking, installation, film/photography, performance, and sound.

Kingston School of Art consistently ranks amongst the highest in the UK for Art and Design in the Guardian League Tables. Kingston is ranked No.1 in London for Art and Design (The Times Good University Guide 2023) and top five in London for Art (Guardian University League Tables 2022).

All students attend a diverse series of professional skills lecturers, practice seminars, and artist talks from staff and guest lecturers, sharing knowledge and advice to help navigate a future career in the creative industries. Students learn how to develop and present their outward-facing profile using key strategic skills for planning, showing, recording, and communicating their work.

### Key Features

- Dedicated studios and stunning technical workshops for developing individual and collaborative creative skills in traditional media and new technologies
- Professional skills modules to help identify and plan future careers, gain real-life experience, and build a showcase portfolio
- No.1 in London for Art and Design (The Times Good University Guide 2023)
- Top 5 in London for Art (Guardian University League Tables 2022)
- Located at Knights Park campus — Kingston School of Art's dedicated creative hub
- Taught by practising artists, curators and writers with national and international profiles
- Strong emphasis on exploratory approaches across a wide range of media and disciplines

## Course Content

The first year encourages an exploratory approach to fine art. Subject workshops, talks, and critiques introduce a wide range of media, technologies, and disciplines. Students undertake independent studio practice, test ideas and media use, and collaborate with peers. Critical and Historical Studies modules explore the relationship between written and spoken communications to media and materials.

### Year 1 (Level 4)

**Introducing Studio Practice**
The core studio module that introduces students to a wide range of fine art practices. Through workshops, talks and critiques, students explore sculpture, painting, printmaking, installation, film/photography, performance, and sound. The emphasis is on experimentation and discovery, developing an understanding of how ideas can be realised through different media and processes.

**Professional Skills I**
Introduces professional practices within the creative industries. Students begin to develop an understanding of career pathways, build foundational professional skills, and start constructing an outward-facing profile. Includes seminars, artist talks and workshops on planning, showing, recording, and communicating work.

**Contextualising Contemporary Practice: Fine Art (Critical and Historical Studies)**
Explores the relationship between written and spoken communications and their connection to media and materials in contemporary fine art practice. Develops critical frameworks for understanding and discussing art within broader cultural, historical and theoretical contexts.

### Year 2 (Level 5)

**Developing Studio Practice**
Builds on the exploratory foundation of Year 1, with students developing a more focused and sustained studio practice. Greater emphasis on independent work, critical self-reflection, and the refinement of ideas and processes within chosen media and approaches.

**Professional Skills II**
Further develops professional skills with a focus on gaining real-life experience. Students engage with the practical aspects of being a practicing artist — including exhibition planning, funding applications, collaboration, and audience engagement.

**Critical Issues in Fine Art: Research and Practice (Critical and Historical Studies)**
Deepens critical engagement with key issues and debates in contemporary fine art. Students develop research skills and explore the relationship between theory and their own studio practice, strengthening the connection between making and thinking.

### Year 3 (Level 6)

**Sustaining Studio Practice**
The capstone studio module in which students develop a substantial, self-directed body of work. Emphasis on independence, critical rigour, and professional presentation. Students consolidate their artistic identity and produce work for the degree show exhibition.

**Professional Skills III**
Final professional skills module focusing on career launch strategies. Covers postgraduate options, establishing a studio practice, working with galleries and curators, self-promotion, and sustaining a creative practice beyond university.

**Dissertation: Research and Reflection (Critical and Historical Studies)**
An extended piece of written research on a topic of the student's choice, related to their studio practice or wider art-historical and theoretical interests. Demonstrates the ability to conduct sustained independent research and articulate ideas in written form.

## Entry Requirements

- **UCAS Tariff:** 120–136 points
- **Level 3 qualifications:** In art and design subjects (A-levels, BTEC Diploma, Access Diploma, IB Diploma, etc.)
- **Preferred route:** Foundation Diploma in Art and Design or recognised equivalent
- **A-levels:** Art and design-related subjects preferred
- **GCSEs:** Five GCSEs at grade 4/C or above including English Language
- **Portfolio:** Digital portfolio required demonstrating creative ability and potential
- **Interview:** May be required

### International Qualifications

Equivalent international qualifications are accepted. Students whose first language is not English must meet the English language requirement.

## English Language Requirements

- **IELTS Academic:** 6.0 overall, with no band below 5.5
- **Alternative accepted tests:** TOEFL iBT, Pearson PTE Academic, Cambridge English

## Teaching & Assessment

### Teaching Methods

- Studio-based practice with dedicated workspace
- Technical workshops (sculpture, painting, printmaking, digital media, photography, film, sound)
- Lectures and seminars on critical and historical studies
- Artist talks and guest lectures from visiting professionals
- Group tutorials, critiques and peer review
- One-to-one tutorials with academic staff
- Collaborative projects and interdisciplinary opportunities

### Assessment

- Studio practice portfolio submissions
- Coursework including visual and written assignments
- Presentations and group critiques
- Dissertation (Year 3)
- Degree show exhibition (Year 3)
- No written examinations

## Career Opportunities

Graduates progress to highly regarded postgraduate and doctoral study. Alumni develop careers as artists and work across a broad range of domains and institutions including curation, teaching, and arts management. Career paths include:

- Professional artist
- Curator / gallery manager
- Art teacher / educator
- Arts administrator / manager
- Exhibition designer
- Creative director
- Art therapist (with further training)
- Community arts worker
- Writer / critic
- Further postgraduate study (MA Fine Art, MFA)

Kingston Fine Art alumni exhibit nationally and internationally. Notable alumni include Fiona Banner (Tate Britain Duveen commission 2010), Kaye Donachie, Sarah Maple, and Sarah McCrory (Director of CCA Goldsmiths, co-judge of the Turner Prize 2014). Alumni have featured in Bloomberg New Contemporaries and support their practices by assisting renowned artists including Anish Kapoor and Ryan Gander, or gain experience with organisations such as ICA, Cell Project Space, and Auto-Italia.
