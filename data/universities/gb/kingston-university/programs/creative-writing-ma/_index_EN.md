## Overview

The Creative Writing MA at Kingston University is designed for aspiring writers who want to develop their craft across a range of genres and forms. The course combines practical writing workshops with critical study of contemporary literature, helping you to develop both your creative voice and your analytical skills.

You will work with published writers and experienced tutors in a supportive workshop environment, receiving detailed feedback on your writing. The course covers fiction, poetry, scriptwriting and creative non-fiction, with opportunities to specialise in your chosen form.

The programme also addresses the professional aspects of writing, including editing, publishing, performance and the creative industries.

## Course Structure

### Semester 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Writers' Workshop | 30 |
| Contemporary Literature and Theory | 30 |

**Writers' Workshop** — A practical writing workshop module in which you develop new creative work across genres. Through weekly workshops, you will share your writing, receive constructive feedback and develop your editorial and critical skills.

**Contemporary Literature and Theory** — Provides a critical context for your creative practice, exploring contemporary literary trends, genres and theoretical approaches that inform current writing.

### Semester 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Specialist Writing Workshop | 30 |
| The Business of Writing | 30 |

**Specialist Writing Workshop** — An advanced workshop in which you focus on your chosen genre (fiction, poetry, scriptwriting or creative non-fiction), developing a sustained body of creative work towards your final portfolio.

**The Business of Writing** — Explores the professional landscape for writers, including publishing processes, literary agents, marketing, digital publishing, performance and literary festivals.

### Semester 3 (Major Project)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Major Project (Creative Dissertation) | 60 |

**Major Project** — A substantial piece of creative writing in your chosen genre, accompanied by a critical commentary. The creative element may be a novel extract, short story collection, poetry collection, screenplay or work of creative non-fiction.
