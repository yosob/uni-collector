## Overview

The Surveying MSc at Kingston University provides a route to qualification as a chartered surveyor for graduates from non-surveying backgrounds. The course covers the core disciplines of building surveying, quantity surveying and property management, providing the knowledge and skills required for professional practice.

Accredited by the Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors (RICS), the course is designed to meet the academic requirements for RICS membership. You will study building pathology, construction technology, measurement and estimation, contract administration and property law.

## Course Structure

### Semester 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Building Surveying and Pathology | 30 |
| Construction Technology | 30 |

**Building Surveying and Pathology** — Develops understanding of building defects, structural failures, damp, decay and the processes of building investigation and diagnosis.

**Construction Technology** — Covers modern and traditional construction methods, building regulations, structural principles and building services.

### Semester 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Measurement and Estimation | 30 |
| Contract Administration and Property Law | 30 |

**Measurement and Estimation** — Develops skills in measurement, quantification, cost estimation and the preparation of bills of quantities.

**Contract Administration and Property Law** — Covers construction contracts, procurement, property law, landlord and tenant legislation and dispute resolution.

### Semester 3 (Major Project)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Dissertation | 60 |

**Dissertation** — An extended piece of independent research on a surveying or built environment topic of your choice.
