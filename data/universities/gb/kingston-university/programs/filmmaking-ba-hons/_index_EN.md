## Overview

The BA (Hons) Filmmaking at Kingston School of Art is a creative and practical course that develops your skills as an independent filmmaker. The course emphasises hands-on filmmaking practice alongside critical and theoretical study of cinema, enabling you to develop a personal voice and the technical expertise to realise your creative vision.

You will explore all aspects of the filmmaking process — from directing, producing and screenwriting to cinematography, editing and sound design. The course encourages experimentation across fiction, documentary and experimental forms, helping you discover your own creative identity as a filmmaker.

At Kingston, you will benefit from studying within a vibrant art school environment where cross-disciplinary collaboration is encouraged. You will have access to professional-standard film equipment, editing suites and studio spaces, and will produce a substantial body of film work throughout the course.

The course is designed to produce graduates who are technically skilled, creatively adventurous and critically informed — prepared for careers in the film and creative industries or further study at postgraduate level.

### Key Features

- Hands-on filmmaking from day one — you will make films in every year of the course
- Covers fiction, documentary and experimental filmmaking
- Study within Kingston School of Art's creative community with access to professional facilities
- Develop skills across directing, producing, cinematography, editing and sound
- Strong emphasis on independent and creative filmmaking
- Opportunities for professional placement year (sandwich route)
- Visiting lectures from industry professionals and filmmakers

## Course Structure

### Year 1

Year 1 introduces you to the fundamental principles and practices of filmmaking. You will develop core technical skills in camera, sound, editing and directing while beginning to explore creative approaches to storytelling and visual expression. Theoretical modules provide a foundation in film language and critical analysis.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Filmmaking Practice 1 | 30 |
| Filmmaking Practice 2 | 30 |
| Film Language and Concepts | 30 |
| Key Debates in Film Studies | 30 |

**Filmmaking Practice 1** — Introduces the core technical skills of filmmaking including camera operation, lighting, sound recording and editing. Through a series of short practical exercises and projects, you will develop fundamental competencies in visual storytelling and begin to understand the creative possibilities of the medium.

**Filmmaking Practice 2** — Builds on the skills acquired in Filmmaking Practice 1, exploring narrative and creative approaches to filmmaking in greater depth. You will work on collaborative projects that develop your ability to conceive, plan, shoot and edit short film pieces.

**Film Language and Concepts** — Introduces the key concepts and vocabulary of film analysis, including mise-en-scène, cinematography, editing and sound. You will develop critical skills for understanding how films create meaning and affect audiences.

**Key Debates in Film Studies** — Explores major debates and critical approaches in film studies, including questions of authorship, genre, national cinema and representation. Provides a theoretical foundation for understanding cinema as an art form and cultural practice.

### Year 2

Year 2 deepens your filmmaking practice and expands your creative range. You will work on longer-form projects in both fiction and documentary, develop advanced technical skills, and engage with film theory at a more sophisticated level. Professional practice elements prepare you for the film industry.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Narrative Filmmaking | 30 |
| Documentary Practice | 30 |
| Film Theory and Criticism | 30 |
| Professional Practice for Filmmakers | 30 |

**Narrative Filmmaking** — Focuses on the art and craft of fiction filmmaking. You will develop skills in script development, directing actors, visual storytelling and post-production. The module culminates in the production of a short narrative film.

**Documentary Practice** — Explores the theory and practice of documentary filmmaking. You will examine different documentary modes and approaches, develop research and interviewing skills, and produce a short documentary film.

**Film Theory and Criticism** — Develops your understanding of film theory at an advanced level, engaging with critical approaches including auteur theory, genre studies, psychoanalytic film theory and contemporary debates in screen studies.

**Professional Practice for Filmmakers** — Prepares you for professional work in the film and creative industries. Covers industry structures, funding opportunities, festival strategy, freelance practice and career development. Includes input from visiting industry professionals.

### Optional Sandwich Year

Between Year 2 and Year 3, you may choose to take a sandwich year for a professional placement in the film or creative industries, or to study abroad at one of Kingston's partner institutions. The placement year provides valuable industry experience and professional networking opportunities.

### Year 3 (Final Year)

Year 3 is focused on the development and production of a major filmmaking project — your graduation film. You will also complete a research project and engage with professional practice to prepare for your career after graduation.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Major Filmmaking Project | 60 |
| Research Project | 30 |
| Professional Roles and Industry Practice | 30 |

**Major Filmmaking Project** — The capstone module of the course, in which you will conceive, develop, produce and deliver a substantial film project. You may work in fiction, documentary or experimental form, and will be supported by tutors and technical staff throughout the production process. The project demonstrates your creative vision, technical competence and ability to manage a complex production.

**Research Project** — An independent research project on a topic of your choice related to film theory, history or practice. You will engage with scholarly sources and critical debates, developing your ability to analyse and articulate ideas about cinema.

**Professional Roles and Industry Practice** — Develops your understanding of professional roles within the film industry and prepares you for career entry or postgraduate study. Covers topics including portfolio development, festival submissions, distribution and working as a freelance filmmaker.

## Teaching & Assessment

Teaching is delivered through:

- Practical filmmaking workshops and exercises
- Studio-based project work
- Lectures, seminars and film screenings
- Individual and group tutorials
- Peer review and group critiques
- Masterclasses with visiting filmmakers and industry professionals
- Location-based filming and production work

Assessment typically comprises:

- Film productions (short films, documentaries, experimental work)
- Coursework portfolios including production materials
- Written essays, analyses and critical reviews
- Research projects and presentations
- Reflective journals and production logs

## Facilities

- Professional-standard film equipment (cameras, lenses, lighting, sound recording)
- Dedicated editing suites with industry-standard software (Avid, Premiere Pro, DaVinci Resolve)
- Sound recording and post-production studios
- Green screen and studio filming spaces
- Screening room for viewing and critiquing work
- Access to Kingston School of Art's wider workshops and creative facilities

## Career Prospects

Graduates from the Filmmaking BA pursue careers across the film and creative industries, including:

- Film director
- Producer
- Cinematographer / Director of Photography
- Film editor
- Screenwriter
- Documentary filmmaker
- Sound designer
- Production manager
- Assistant director
- Film programmer / curator
- Festival organiser
- Content creator (digital and streaming platforms)
- Commercial and corporate filmmaker
- Further study at MA level (e.g. filmmaking, film studies, screen arts)

### Industry Connections

The course benefits from Kingston School of Art's extensive network of creative industry connections. Students engage with visiting filmmakers, attend industry events and film festivals, and may undertake professional placements as part of the sandwich year option.
