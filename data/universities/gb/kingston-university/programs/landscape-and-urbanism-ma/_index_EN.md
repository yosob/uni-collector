## Overview

The Landscape and Urbanism MA at Kingston School of Art explores the intersection of landscape design and urban theory, addressing the complex challenges facing cities and territories in the 21st century. The course takes a critical and speculative approach to the design and planning of urban environments.

You will engage with themes including climate change adaptation, urban ecology, social equity, spatial justice and the future of public space. The course combines design studio work with theoretical research, encouraging you to develop innovative approaches to landscape-led urbanism.

This course is ideal for graduates in landscape architecture, architecture, urban design, geography or related disciplines who want to develop a critical understanding of urban landscape issues.

## Course Structure

### Semester 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Design Studio: Urban Landscape | 30 |
| Theories of Urbanism and Landscape | 30 |

**Design Studio: Urban Landscape** — A studio-based module in which you develop design proposals for a specific urban landscape context. Emphasis on site analysis, mapping, strategic thinking and design speculation.

**Theories of Urbanism and Landscape** — Explores key theoretical frameworks for understanding the relationship between landscape and urbanism, including landscape urbanism, ecological urbanism and spatial justice.

### Semester 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Design Studio: Territory and Ecologies | 30 |
| Research Methods | 30 |

**Design Studio: Territory and Ecologies** — Extends your design exploration to larger territorial scales, engaging with ecological systems, infrastructure and regional landscapes.

**Research Methods** — Develops research skills and methodologies relevant to landscape and urbanism, supporting the development of your major project proposal.

### Semester 3 (Major Project)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Major Project | 60 |

**Major Project** — A self-directed design research project in which you investigate a landscape/urban issue of your choice, producing a design proposal supported by written research.
