## Overview

Photography plays a pivotal role in fine art, fashion, advertising, editorial and digital applications. This course is centred on the making and understanding of photographic production and critical thinking, applied to any subject area.

You'll study both analogue and digital photography. The key focus of this course is for you to develop your creative ideas alongside your professional skills, within a multi-genre approach to the medium. There will also be opportunities to work with historic manual processes, such as cyanotypes and salt prints, as well as other 'craft' processes, such as creating unique one-off photo books.

This course takes maximum advantage of Kingston's proximity to the vibrant art, photography and cultural life of London – one of the major photographic art and commercial centres of the world. It also offers the opportunity for you to prepare yourself for employment and increase your workplace skills with an optional work placement.

Industry partners have included:

- Impressions Gallery
- The Photographers' Gallery
- Magnum Photos
- British Journal of Photography
- The Association of Photographers
- Institute of Contemporary Arts (ICA)
- National Archives
- Autograph-ABP

As part of Kingston School of Art, students benefit from joining a creative community where collaborative working and critical practice are encouraged. Workshops and labs are open to all disciplines, enabling students and staff to work together, share ideas and explore multi-disciplinary making.

## Course Structure

### Year 1

In your first year, you'll become aware and critical of the range of photography practices and the possibilities of a personal photography practice. You will be encouraged to experiment with new approaches and techniques.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Photography: Histories and Contexts | 30 |
| Photography: Independent Practice 1 | 30 |
| Photography: Technical Skills | 30 |
| Photography: Moving Image and Editorial | 30 |

**Photography: Histories and Contexts** — Introduces the various contexts in which contemporary photography practices are defined, debated and displayed. Through lectures, discussions, screenings and exhibition visits, you will be introduced to the historical framework of modernity and post-modernity to understand the development and contemporary situation of the discipline. Organised as discrete but related teaching blocks that progress from broader questions of cultural practice to the more specific debates framing photography and its associated fields, including moving images and fine art.

**Photography: Independent Practice 1** — Introduces active learning, research and reflection through practical photography projects which lead from thematic and conceptual to self-initiated and developmental projects. There is an emphasis on the self-initiated development of critical photography. You will develop and independently produce (with tutorial support) a body of work that can be considered 'personal photography' arising from personal concerns — a committed and serious independent practice on reflection.

**Photography: Technical Skills** — Focuses on the attainment of an array of production and technical skills to expand and enhance the development of a personal (and professional standard) photographic language. Begins with active learning through skills-based projects, both analogue and digital. All projects encompass conceptual concerns, individual development and critical engagement with a photographic 'way of seeing' and working with light, both natural and artificial.

**Photography: Moving Image and Editorial** — Develops your independent photography further and introduces the moving image, extending your skillset and enhancing the potential for all image-based work. You will explore the implications of different modes of editorial presentation. You will self-publish photographic books and other photographic series of works for summative assessment.

### Year 2

In Year 2, you will develop your own independent and self-directed photography practice with critical support. You will continue to develop your ideas, skills and folios in more depth.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Photography: Critical Practice | 30 |
| Photography: Professional Practice | 30 |
| Photography: Critical Theories | 30 |
| Photography: Independent Practice 2 | 30 |

**Photography: Critical Practice** — Encourages and challenges you to make critical photographic projects responding to different subjects and contexts, focusing on process, audience and refinement of visual literacy. You will have the opportunity to participate in collaborative projects as well as work individually, building confidence in communication and working in partnership towards resolution.

**Photography: Professional Practice** — You will engage directly with professional practice working collaboratively and with a project partner organisation, responding to two 'live' professional briefs. You will develop and produce two new bodies of photographic work in response to the briefs, forming the core of your portfolio. You will then produce and present selected coursework as an end of year group exhibition and/or publications. Content also includes support on preparing a CV, writing supporting statements, copyright, ethics and legal issues for photographers.

**Photography: Critical Theories** — Concentrates on the theoretical frameworks in which photography is produced, consumed and interpreted today. Introducing case studies emerging from current trends in contemporary photographic practice, taught through lectures, seminars, workshops and exhibition visits. You will acquire the knowledge and analytical skills to build a framework within which to engage with the critical contexts in which photography operates, and propose an area of research for development at Level 6.

**Photography: Independent Practice 2** — You will develop portfolios of independent photography and other supporting research materials that demonstrate a growing awareness of the potential of photography across different genres and critical contexts. The development of an independent photographic practice is supported through lectures, group and peer review, workshops and individual tutorials.

### Optional Year

You can opt to study or work abroad through the University's Study Abroad programme or the Erasmus programme during your Photography degree.

### Year 3 (Final Year)

In Year 3, you will become proficient, professional, assured, innovative and outward-facing through a more fully realised and engaged personal photography practice. You will refine your photography and define your ambitions more succinctly.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Extended Research Project | 15 |
| Professional Skills | 30 |
| Major Project | 60 |
| Future Skills | 15 |

**Extended Research Project** — Supports an extended research project that deepens your understanding of critical issues relevant to your practice. You'll explore a topic of your choosing, framed by questions emerging from your own work and informed by relevant theory, history, or cultural context. Working independently and with tutor support, you'll undertake rigorous enquiry using appropriate research methods and sources.

**Professional Skills** — Concerned with the development, creation and evidencing of vital professional skills: the ability to respond with limited lead time to a photography brief; the creation of a professional work-seeker's photography portfolio; the ability to present and coherently rationalise work to an audience; the production of a relevant CV; and evidence of a web presence. The aim is to ensure you are well prepared for a career in photography and related fields upon graduation.

**Major Project** — You will produce and display a coherent body of independent photographic work. You are required to synthesise and consolidate practical skills, critical approaches and understanding of the context of contemporary photographic practice through the production and presentation of a major body of work including exhibition installation. You will develop and apply skills of editing, selection, sequencing, printing, framing and other appropriate presentation.

**Future Skills** — Gives you a dedicated opportunity to develop your Future Skills Graduate Attributes. You will self-assess your current skills profile, determine which attributes and skills you need to develop, and be supported by a dedicated career coach. Activities could include short courses, enrichment activities, micro-placements and experiential learning.

## Teaching and Assessment

Teaching is delivered through:

- Practical photography projects and workshops
- Lectures, seminars and tutorials
- Technical workshops (analogue and digital)
- Group critiques and peer review sessions
- Exhibition visits and field trips
- Live industry briefs and collaborative projects

Assessment typically includes:

- Practical coursework (portfolios, exhibitions, photo books)
- Written assignments (essays, research projects)
- Presentations and critiques
- Self-initiated projects

## Facilities

As part of Kingston School of Art, students have access to:

- Dedicated photography studios
- Analogue darkrooms
- Digital printing and scanning facilities
- Moving image equipment and editing suites
- Photographic book-making facilities
- Cross-disciplinary open workshops at Knights Park campus
