## Overview

The Graphic Design MA at Kingston School of Art is an intensive, studio-based course that encourages you to explore and expand the boundaries of graphic design practice. The course takes an experimental and critical approach, positioning graphic design as a creative discipline engaged with contemporary culture, technology and society.

You will develop your personal design voice through a combination of self-initiated projects, critical enquiry and technical experimentation. The course covers a broad range of graphic design practices including typography, branding, editorial design, digital design, motion graphics and information design.

Based at Knights Park, you will work in dedicated studio spaces with access to printmaking workshops, letterpress, digital labs and photographic facilities. The course promotes dialogue between traditional craft skills and contemporary digital practice.

## Course Structure

### Semester 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Graphic Design Practice 1 | 30 |
| Visual Communication: Critical Frameworks | 30 |

**Graphic Design Practice 1** — An intensive studio module that challenges you to develop your graphic design practice through a series of set and self-initiated briefs. Emphasis on conceptual thinking, visual experimentation and developing a distinctive design language.

**Visual Communication: Critical Frameworks** — Explores the theoretical and historical contexts of graphic design and visual communication. Engages with semiotics, design history, cultural theory and contemporary debates about the role of design in society.

### Semester 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Graphic Design Practice 2 | 30 |
| Professional Practice and Context | 30 |

**Graphic Design Practice 2** — Extends your design enquiry with greater emphasis on resolution, craft and audience engagement. You will develop work that demonstrates intellectual rigour and professional quality.

**Professional Practice and Context** — Examines the professional landscape of graphic design, including studio practice, freelancing, commissioning, publishing and the creative economy. Develops your skills in presenting, writing about and promoting your work.

### Semester 3 (Major Project)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Major Project | 60 |

**Major Project** — A self-directed project in which you produce a substantial body of graphic design work that demonstrates your personal vision, critical understanding and technical mastery. The project is presented as a final exhibition and portfolio.
