## Overview

The BA (Hons) Dance at Kingston School of Art is a dynamic and creative course that develops your skills as a dance practitioner, choreographer and thinker. The course combines practical dance training with critical and theoretical study, enabling you to develop as an articulate and versatile dance artist.

You will engage with a range of dance practices including contemporary dance, ballet, street dance and world dance forms, while developing your skills in choreography, performance and dance analysis. The course encourages you to explore the relationship between dance and wider cultural, social and political contexts.

At Kingston, you will benefit from studying within a vibrant art school environment where cross-disciplinary collaboration is encouraged. You will have access to professional-standard dance studios and performance spaces, and will participate in regular performances and collaborative projects throughout the course.

The course is designed to produce graduates who are technically skilled, creatively adventurous and critically informed — prepared for careers in the performing arts sector or further study at postgraduate level.

### Key Features

- Practical dance training from day one — you will perform and create throughout the course
- Covers contemporary dance, ballet, street dance and global dance practices
- Study within Kingston School of Art's creative community with access to professional studios
- Develop skills across choreography, performance, teaching and dance technology
- Strong emphasis on creative and experimental choreographic practice
- Opportunities for professional placement year (sandwich route)
- Regular performances and public showings of your work
- Visiting workshops from professional choreographers and dance artists

## Course Structure

### Year 1

Year 1 introduces you to the fundamental principles and practices of dance. You will develop core technical skills in a range of dance styles while beginning to explore creative approaches to choreography and performance. Theoretical modules provide a foundation in dance analysis and understanding dance in its cultural contexts.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Dance Technique and Training 1 | 30 |
| Choreography and Creative Practice 1 | 30 |
| Dance Analysis and Contexts | 30 |
| The Body in Performance | 30 |

**Dance Technique and Training 1** — Introduces core technical training in contemporary dance and complementary styles. You will develop fundamental physical skills including alignment, coordination, strength, flexibility and spatial awareness through regular technique classes and bodywork practices.

**Choreography and Creative Practice 1** — Introduces choreographic principles and creative methods for making dance. You will explore solo and group composition, improvisation, and develop your ability to generate and structure movement material. Practical workshops are supported by viewing and analysing key choreographic works.

**Dance Analysis and Contexts** — Introduces frameworks for describing, analysing and understanding dance as an art form and cultural practice. You will study key approaches to dance analysis and learn to write critically about performance, developing skills that support both your practical and theoretical work.

**The Body in Performance** — Explores the moving body from anatomical, physiological and somatic perspectives. You will develop understanding of how the body works in performance, including safe practice, injury prevention and approaches to body awareness that inform your technical and creative development.

### Year 2

Year 2 deepens your dance practice and expands your creative range. You will work on more advanced choreographic projects, develop your technical skills at a higher level, and engage with dance theory and professional practice. Opportunities to collaborate with other creative disciplines are available.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Dance Technique and Training 2 | 30 |
| Choreography and Creative Practice 2 | 30 |
| Dance Histories and Theories | 30 |
| Professional Practice in Dance | 30 |

**Dance Technique and Training 2** — Develops your technical skills at a more advanced level, with increased emphasis on performance quality, stylistic range and physical expressivity. You will engage with a broader range of techniques and approaches to movement training.

**Choreography and Creative Practice 2** — Extends your choreographic practice through more complex and ambitious projects. You will explore site-specific work, interdisciplinary collaboration, and the use of media and technology in choreographic creation. The module supports the development of your individual choreographic voice.

**Dance Histories and Theories** — Engages with key historical developments and theoretical frameworks in dance studies. You will examine the evolution of dance practices in the 20th and 21st centuries, engaging with questions of modernism, postmodernism, identity, cultural politics and global dance practices.

**Professional Practice in Dance** — Prepares you for professional work in the performing arts. Covers topics including project management, funding applications, marketing, teaching practice, community dance, and career development strategies. Includes input from visiting dance professionals.

### Optional Sandwich Year

Between Year 2 and Year 3, you may choose to take a sandwich year for a professional placement in the dance or performing arts sector, or to study abroad at one of Kingston's partner institutions. The placement year provides valuable industry experience and professional networking opportunities.

### Year 3 (Final Year)

Year 3 is focused on the development and presentation of a major choreographic or performance project. You will also complete a research project and engage with advanced professional practice to prepare for your career after graduation.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Major Dance Project | 60 |
| Dance Research Project | 30 |
| Professional Dance Practice | 30 |

**Major Dance Project** — The capstone module of the course, in which you will conceive, develop, rehearse and present a substantial piece of choreographic or performance work. You may work individually or collaboratively, and will be supported by tutors throughout the creative process. The project demonstrates your artistic vision, technical competence and ability to realise a complex performance.

**Dance Research Project** — An independent research project on a topic of your choice related to dance theory, history, practice or pedagogy. You will engage with scholarly sources and critical debates, developing your ability to analyse and articulate ideas about dance and performance.

**Professional Dance Practice** — Develops your understanding of professional contexts for dance practice and prepares you for career entry or postgraduate study. Covers topics including portfolio development, audition preparation, teaching practice, community engagement and working as a freelance dance artist.

## Teaching & Assessment

Teaching is delivered through:

- Practical dance technique classes and bodywork sessions
- Choreographic workshops and creative laboratories
- Studio-based project work and rehearsals
- Lectures, seminars and screenings
- Individual and group tutorials
- Peer review and group critiques
- Masterclasses with visiting choreographers and dance artists
- Performances and public showings

Assessment typically comprises:

- Practical performance and choreographic presentations
- Coursework portfolios including creative process documentation
- Written essays, analyses and critical reviews
- Research projects and presentations
- Reflective journals and practice-based documentation

## Career Prospects

Graduates from the Dance BA pursue careers across the performing arts and creative industries, including:

- Professional dancer / performer
- Choreographer
- Dance teacher (further training may be required for QTS)
- Community dance practitioner
- Dance movement therapist (with further training)
- Arts administrator / manager
- Dance filmmaker / digital dance artist
- Workshop leader and facilitator
- Dance critic / writer
- Events and festival organiser
- Further study at MA level (e.g. dance, choreography, performance)

### Industry Connections

The course benefits from Kingston School of Art's extensive network of performing arts connections. Students engage with visiting choreographers and dance companies, attend performances and festivals, and may undertake professional placements as part of the sandwich year option.
