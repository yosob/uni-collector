## Overview

The Art Market Appraisal MA at Kingston School of Art is a unique programme that combines art historical knowledge with the practical skills of art valuation, appraisal and the art market. The course is designed for those seeking careers in the commercial art world, auction houses, galleries, insurance and heritage sectors.

You will develop expertise in identifying, authenticating and valuing works of art across a range of periods and media. The course covers art history, the structure and operation of the art market, valuation principles, auction processes, and the legal and ethical frameworks governing the art trade.

Situated within Kingston School of Art and benefiting from London's position as a global art market hub, the course provides both academic rigour and professional relevance.

## Course Structure

### Semester 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Art History: Methods and Approaches | 30 |
| The Art Market: Structure and Operations | 30 |

**Art History: Methods and Approaches** — Develops art historical knowledge and analytical skills, covering key periods, movements and methods of art historical inquiry relevant to appraisal practice.

**The Art Market: Structure and Operations** — Examines the structure and dynamics of the global art market, including auction houses, galleries, art fairs, dealers and online platforms.

### Semester 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Principles and Practice of Valuation | 30 |
| Professional Practice and Research Methods | 30 |

**Principles and Practice of Valuation** — Covers the theory and practice of art valuation, including appraisal methods, provenance research, condition assessment, catalogue raisonnés and insurance valuations.

**Professional Practice and Research Methods** — Develops professional skills and research methods relevant to the art market, including cataloguing, attribution, legal issues (due diligence, export controls) and a professional placement.

### Semester 3 (Major Project)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Major Project | 60 |

**Major Project** — Either a dissertation on an art market/appraisal topic or a practice-based project involving the research and valuation of a collection or group of works.
