## Overview

The Real Estate MSc at Kingston University provides a comprehensive education in real estate, covering property valuation, investment, development, management and the legal and regulatory frameworks governing the property sector. The course is accredited by the Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors (RICS).

Designed for graduates seeking a career in the property industry, the course combines theoretical knowledge with practical application. You will develop the analytical, professional and technical skills required for a successful career in real estate.

## Course Structure

### Semester 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Property Valuation and Appraisal | 30 |
| Real Estate Economics and Market Analysis | 30 |

**Property Valuation and Appraisal** — Develops skills in property valuation methods, including the comparison, investment, residual and profits methods. Covers valuation of residential, commercial and industrial property.

**Real Estate Economics and Market Analysis** — Examines the economic principles underpinning real estate markets, including supply and demand, market cycles, location theory and property market analysis.

### Semester 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Property Investment and Finance | 30 |
| Property Development and Planning | 30 |

**Property Investment and Finance** — Covers the principles and practice of property investment, including portfolio theory, risk assessment, funding structures and real estate finance.

**Property Development and Planning** — Explores the property development process, including site acquisition, planning, feasibility analysis, construction and disposal. Examines the planning system and its impact on development.

### Semester 3 (Major Project)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Dissertation | 60 |

**Dissertation** — An extended piece of independent research on a real estate topic of your choice, demonstrating critical analysis and scholarly research skills.
