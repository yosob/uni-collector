## Overview

The Landscape Architecture MA at Kingston School of Art is a professionally oriented course that prepares students for practice as landscape architects. The course is accredited by the Landscape Institute (LI) and provides the educational foundation for membership of this professional body.

The programme takes a design-led approach, combining creative studio work with technical knowledge, ecological understanding and critical thinking. You will engage with a range of landscape design challenges — from urban public spaces and parks to rural landscapes, brownfield sites and environmental regeneration.

Based at Knights Park, you will work in dedicated landscape studios with access to extensive workshops, digital fabrication facilities and a living lab landscape garden. The course emphasises sustainability, climate resilience and social responsibility in landscape design.

## Course Structure

### Year 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Landscape Design Studio 1 | 40 |
| Landscape Science and Ecology | 20 |
| Landscape Design Studio 2 | 40 |
| History and Theory of Landscape | 20 |

**Landscape Design Studio 1** — Introduces the principles and methods of landscape design through a series of studio projects. You will develop skills in site analysis, conceptual design, spatial composition and visual communication.

**Landscape Science and Ecology** — Provides the scientific and ecological knowledge essential for landscape architecture, covering plant science, soil science, hydrology and ecological systems.

**Landscape Design Studio 2** — Develops your design skills through more complex projects addressing urban and rural landscape challenges. Emphasis on sustainable design strategies and technical resolution.

**History and Theory of Landscape** — Explores the historical development and theoretical foundations of landscape architecture, from historic gardens to contemporary landscape urbanism.

### Year 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Landscape Design Studio 3 (Major Project) | 60 |
| Professional Practice and Management | 20 |
| Landscape Technology | 20 |
| Dissertation | 20 |

**Landscape Design Studio 3** — The major design project in which you develop a comprehensive landscape design proposal for a real site. This self-directed project demonstrates your ability to integrate design, ecology and technology.

**Professional Practice and Management** — Covers the professional aspects of landscape architecture practice, including contracts, regulations, planning law and practice management.

**Landscape Technology** — Develops technical knowledge of landscape construction, materials, drainage, lighting and environmental systems.

**Dissertation** — An extended piece of written research on a landscape architecture topic of your choice.
