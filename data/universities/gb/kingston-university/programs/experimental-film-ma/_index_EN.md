## Overview

The Experimental Film MA at Kingston School of Art is a creative, practice-led course that explores the moving image as a form of artistic expression and critical inquiry. The course supports work across the spectrum of experimental film and moving image practice — from artist's film and video to expanded cinema, installation, performance and digital experimentation.

You will be encouraged to challenge conventional filmmaking approaches, developing your own distinctive visual language and conceptual framework. The course integrates practical production with critical study, examining the histories, theories and contemporary practices of experimental film and artists' moving image.

Based at Knights Park, you will have access to filmmaking equipment, editing facilities, gallery spaces and the interdisciplinary creative environment of Kingston School of Art.

## Course Structure

### Semester 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Experimental Film Practice 1 | 30 |
| Histories and Theories of Experimental Film | 30 |

**Experimental Film Practice 1** — Develops your practical moving image skills through experimental approaches. You will explore non-narrative structures, materialist filmmaking, found footage, abstraction and the relationship between image, sound and time.

**Histories and Theories of Experimental Film** — Examines the historical development and theoretical frameworks of experimental film and artists' moving image, from the avant-garde to contemporary practice.

### Semester 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Experimental Film Practice 2 | 30 |
| Research Methods and Professional Practice | 30 |

**Experimental Film Practice 2** — Extends your practice towards more ambitious and resolved work. You will develop a self-directed project exploring experimental approaches to the moving image, considering presentation contexts from gallery installation to single-screen projection.

**Research Methods and Professional Practice** — Develops research skills relevant to experimental film practice and covers professional contexts including festivals, galleries, commissioning and funding.

### Semester 3 (Major Project)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Major Project | 60 |

**Major Project** — A substantial piece of experimental moving image work, presented in an appropriate format (single-screen, installation, performance or online). Accompanied by a critical written commentary contextualising your practice.
