## Overview

The Interior Design MA at Kingston School of Art is a creative, research-led course that explores the design of interior environments as a critical and transformative practice. The course encourages you to question conventional approaches to interior space and develop innovative responses to the ways people inhabit, experience and interact with designed environments.

You will engage with a broad range of interior design contexts — from domestic and commercial interiors to public spaces, exhibitions, installations and temporary structures. The course integrates design practice with theoretical inquiry, considering the social, cultural and environmental dimensions of interior space.

Based at Knights Park campus, you will work in dedicated design studios with access to workshops, digital fabrication facilities and material libraries.

## Course Structure

### Semester 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Interior Design Studio 1 | 30 |
| Spatial Theory and Context | 30 |

**Interior Design Studio 1** — An intensive design studio module in which you develop a self-initiated interior design project. Emphasis on site analysis, conceptual development, spatial planning and material specification.

**Spatial Theory and Context** — Explores the theoretical frameworks that inform the design of interior space, including phenomenology, environmental psychology, social theory and the history of interior design.

### Semester 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Interior Design Studio 2 | 30 |
| Professional Practice | 30 |

**Interior Design Studio 2** — Extends your design project towards a more complex and resolved outcome. You will develop detailed design proposals including materiality, lighting, furniture and environmental systems.

**Professional Practice** — Covers the professional dimensions of interior design practice, including regulations, building technologies, project management, client relations and the design industry landscape.

### Semester 3 (Major Project)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Major Project | 60 |

**Major Project** — A self-directed design project in which you produce a comprehensive interior design proposal for a real or speculative site. The project demonstrates your ability to integrate design creativity, technical knowledge and critical understanding.
