# Illustration Animation BA (Hons)

## Overview

The Illustration Animation BA (Hons) at Kingston University — the UK's first degree in Illustration Animation — combines narrative and time-based subjects to provide complete creative freedom to engage in visual thinking, expression and communication.

Illustration has expanded from the traditional printed page to many other forms of visual media, including digital objects and interaction, spaces and environments. Animation as a time-based medium allows exploration from traditional to hybrid domains, like film and television, and virtual and augmented reality.

The course includes self-initiated and group-based assignments and presentations, with self-reflective and critical discourse encouraged. Individual practice is built and tested by peer group interaction. This breadth ensures students develop the range of skills essential to contemporary practice in all forms of applied image making.

Projects with industry, cultural and social institutions test and shape students' understanding in real-world situations. Staff practitioners and alumni networks offer insight and contacts with international creative practice, including studio visits and placements, to ensure you're industry-ready at graduation.

As part of Kingston School of Art, students benefit from joining a creative community where collaborative working and critical practice are encouraged. Workshops and labs are open to all disciplines, enabling students and staff to work together, share ideas and explore multi-disciplinary making.

### Key Features

- The UK's first Illustration Animation degree — a pioneering combined programme
- Combines two essentially narrative subjects for a diverse, exciting studio experience
- Strong emphasis on observational drawing as the foundation for creative development

## Course Content

Throughout the course you'll gain an understanding of text, image, narrative and sequence. Strong observational skills are built through drawing as a basis for your development, equipping you with the necessary techniques to realise your creative ambition.

Each level is made up of modules totalling 120 credit points.

### Year 1 (Level 4)

Year 1 encourages an open-minded and exploratory approach to illustration animation. You'll be introduced to idea development, visual research, and image and content relationships. Drawing, animation, digital crafts, presentation techniques, life drawing and location workshops are all taught.

There is an optional field trip to destinations such as New York, Berlin or Florence. Eligible students will be supported with a travel bursary.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Studio: Introductory Principles | 30 |
| Process: Materials and Methods | 30 |
| Presentation: Collaboration and Communication | 30 |
| Image & Text | 30 |

**Studio: Introductory Principles** — Introduces the nature of studio practice for Illustration Animation. Studio practice describes not only a practice of creative making but also one of cognition, thinking and reasoning. The illustrator animator's knowledge is present in their perceptive, questioning and empathetic approach to subject matter.

**Process: Materials and Methods** — Introduces the tools, processes and technologies used within illustration animation practice. Addresses how the illustrator animator interrogates subject matter through creative making, investigating how to apply tools, processes and technologies through active engagement. Visual experimentation, prototyping, testing through making and questioning of the properties and possibilities of materials.

**Presentation: Collaboration and Communication** — Introduces the importance of forming and nurturing relationships as part of a creative practice. Addresses ways that the illustrator animator can be active in the world through independent thinking, collaboration and interdisciplinarity. Students develop an understanding of audience and begin to establish a critical and cultural position.

**Image & Text** — Introduces critical thinking about the history of illustration animation, and how the knowledge produced can inform practice. Examines dominant ways histories of illustration animation have been constructed, considers diverse themes, platforms and media through different theoretical frames.

### Year 2 (Level 5)

Year 2 enables you to explore different ways of communicating your ideas. You'll learn how to critically challenge subjects and develop your personal direction. You'll work on set and self-initiated projects, developing your ability to create effective solutions.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Process: Technologies and Application | 30 |
| Presentation: Audience and Context | 30 |
| Studio: Practice and Purpose | 30 |
| Critical & Historical Studies | 30 |

**Process: Technologies and Application** — Develops and supports an individual approach to the tools, processes and technologies used within illustration animation practice. Builds on Level 4 materials and methods with deeper exploration of printing, digital publishing, screen-based platforms, and virtual realities.

**Presentation: Audience and Context** — Develops individual approaches to forming and nurturing relationships as part of creative practice. Through independent thinking, collaboration and interdisciplinarity, students develop understanding of audience and establish a critical and cultural position within the global creative industries.

**Studio: Practice and Purpose** — Develops and supports individual approaches to studio practice. Students extend and shift their works to assume new roles and responsibilities as illustrator animators, building on the foundational principles established at Level 4.

**Critical & Historical Studies** — Focuses on the theorisation of discipline-specific issues arising in the contemporary practices of animation and illustration. Develops emerging research interests and independent visual and academic research skills. Explores the development and intertextuality of visual narrative forms in the structuring of meaning, applied to contemporary case studies.

### Year 3 (Level 6)

Year 3 focuses on the development and resolution of a personal practice, with an awareness of professional contexts. A series of set and live assignments will inform your self-initiated extended project. Helping you with your individual presentation will be industry research and engagement combined with web, portfolio, showreel and curatorial workshops.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Studio: Practice and Realisation | 60 |
| Presentation: Professional Practice | 30 |
| Extended Research Project | 15 |
| Future Skills | 15 |

**Studio: Practice and Realisation** — Nurtures autonomous studio practice for Illustration Animation. The culmination of the studio strand, supporting students in developing a self-directed body of work that demonstrates creative ambition, critical awareness and professional readiness.

**Presentation: Professional Practice** — Supports self-reliance in forming and nurturing relationships as part of a creative practice. Develops understanding of audience and professional positioning within the global creative industries.

**Extended Research Project** — Supports an extended research project that deepens understanding of critical issues relevant to practice. Students explore a topic of their choosing, framed by questions emerging from their own design work and informed by relevant theory, history or cultural context.

**Future Skills** — A dedicated opportunity to develop Future Skills Graduate Attributes. Students self-assess their current skills profile, determine which attributes and skills they need to develop, and undertake a tailored series of activities and projects aligned to their career goals, supported by a dedicated career coach.

### Optional Year

You'll have the opportunity to spend a fourth year abroad, undertake a work placement, or even combine both.
