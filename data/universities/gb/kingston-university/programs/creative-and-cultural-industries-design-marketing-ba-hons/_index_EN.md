# Creative and Cultural Industries: Design Marketing BA (Hons)

> Combine design thinking with marketing strategy. This course prepares you for a career at the intersection of creativity and commerce within the cultural industries, focusing on design marketing, brand strategy and consumer engagement.

## Overview

BA (Hons) Creative and Cultural Industries: Design Marketing at Kingston School of Art is designed for students who want to work where design meets marketing in the creative industries. The course develops your ability to apply design thinking to marketing challenges while building practical skills in brand strategy, consumer insight and creative communications.

You will explore how design and marketing work together to create compelling brand experiences, influence consumer behaviour and drive cultural trends. The programme covers design marketing for branding, digital campaigns, experiential marketing and cultural promotion. You will learn to develop marketing strategies grounded in creative practice, understand audiences through design research, and create campaigns that resonate with target markets.

The course is part of Kingston's Creative and Cultural Industries (CCI) family, which shares a common first year before you specialise in Design Marketing. This structure gives you a broad understanding of the creative industries before focusing on your chosen pathway.

### Key Features

- Part of the Creative and Cultural Industries programme family at Kingston School of Art
- Common first year shared across CCI pathways before specialisation
- Focus on design-led marketing, brand strategy and consumer engagement
- Develop skills in creative marketing, audience research and cultural promotion
- Opportunities for professional placement year (sandwich route)
- Located at Knights Park campus — Kingston School of Art's dedicated creative hub

## Course Structure

### Year 1 — CCI Common Year

Year 1 is shared across all Creative and Cultural Industries pathways. Students build foundational knowledge of the creative industries, develop core creative skills and explore how ideas become cultural products.

| Module | Credits | Type |
|--------|---------|------|
| Creative Journeys | 30 | Core |
| Navigating Industry | 30 | Core |
| The Tools of Writing | 30 | Core |
| Thinking about Ideas | 60 | Core |

### Year 2 — Design Marketing Specialism

Year 2 focuses on developing design marketing skills alongside creative project management and audience understanding. Students begin to apply their knowledge through live projects and case studies.

| Module | Credits | Type |
|--------|---------|------|
| Design Marketing | 30 | Core |
| Creative Project Management | 30 | Core |
| Customer Mindfulness | 30 | Core |
| Live Case Study | 30 | Core |

### Year 3 — Advanced Practice and Major Project

Year 3 deepens design marketing practice and culminates in a major self-directed project. Students develop entrepreneurial skills and prepare for professional practice in the creative industries.

| Module | Credits | Type |
|--------|---------|------|
| Design Marketing 2 | 30 | Core |
| Culturepreneurship | 30 | Core |
| The Major Project | 60 | Core |
