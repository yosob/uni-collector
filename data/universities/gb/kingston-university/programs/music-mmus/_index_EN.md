## Overview

The Music MMus at Kingston University is a flexible programme that allows you to specialise in composition, performance, musicology or music technology. The course is designed for musicians and scholars who want to develop their practice or research to an advanced level.

You will work with experienced tutors and visiting professionals in a creative and intellectually stimulating environment. The course combines one-to-one tuition, ensemble work, seminars and independent study, enabling you to pursue your musical interests in depth.

Based at the Knights Park campus, you will have access to recording studios, practice rooms, performance spaces and music technology labs.

## Course Structure

### Semester 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Major Specialism 1 | 30 |
| Music Research Methods | 30 |

**Major Specialism 1** — Develops your principal area of musical activity (composition, performance, musicology or music technology) through one-to-one tuition and guided independent study.

**Music Research Methods** — Introduces research methodologies relevant to musical scholarship and practice, including historiography, analysis, ethnomusicology and practice-led research.

### Semester 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Major Specialism 2 | 30 |
| Option Module | 30 |

**Major Specialism 2** — Continues the development of your principal specialism at a more advanced level, working towards your final project.

**Option Module** — Choose from a range of options which may include: Composition Workshop, Ensemble Performance, Music and Technology, Film Music, Popular Music Studies, or Music Education.

### Semester 3 (Major Project)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Major Project | 60 |

**Major Project** — A substantial project in your specialist area. For composers: a portfolio of original compositions. For performers: a recital programme. For musicologists: a dissertation. For music technologists: a creative technology project.
