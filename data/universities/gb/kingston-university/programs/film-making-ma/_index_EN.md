## Overview

The Film Making MA at Kingston School of Art is an intensive, practice-based course that develops your skills in independent filmmaking. The course covers the full spectrum of film production — from development and pre-production through to shooting, post-production and distribution.

You will develop your creative voice as a filmmaker while gaining hands-on experience with professional camera, lighting, sound and editing equipment. The course supports work across fiction, documentary and experimental film, encouraging you to find your own distinctive approach.

Based at Knights Park, you will have access to dedicated filmmaking facilities including a green screen studio, editing suites, sound recording equipment and a range of professional camera kit.

## Course Structure

### Semester 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Film Making Practice 1 | 30 |
| Film Theory and Analysis | 30 |

**Film Making Practice 1** — Develops your practical filmmaking skills through a series of short film exercises and projects. You will work across roles including directing, cinematography, sound and editing, gaining hands-on experience of the full production workflow.

**Film Theory and Analysis** — Provides a critical and theoretical framework for understanding film as an art form. Engages with film history, genre theory, authorship and contemporary moving image practice.

### Semester 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Film Making Practice 2 | 30 |
| Professional Practice and Research Methods | 30 |

**Film Making Practice 2** — Extends your filmmaking practice towards a more ambitious and resolved short film project. You will develop skills in narrative construction, visual storytelling and post-production.

**Professional Practice and Research Methods** — Covers the professional dimensions of filmmaking, including funding, distribution, festival strategy and the creative industries. Research methods support your major project development.

### Semester 3 (Major Project)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Major Project | 60 |

**Major Project** — The culmination of the course, in which you produce a substantial short film (fiction, documentary or experimental). You will take a lead creative role and demonstrate mastery of filmmaking craft and artistic vision.
