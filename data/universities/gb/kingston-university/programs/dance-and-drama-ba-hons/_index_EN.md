## Overview

The BA (Hons) Dance and Drama at Kingston School of Art is a unique interdisciplinary course that combines practical training in both dance and drama. The course enables you to develop as a versatile performing artist with skills across movement, acting, physical theatre and devised performance, alongside critical and theoretical understanding of both disciplines.

You will engage with a range of practices including contemporary dance, choreography, acting, playwriting, devised theatre and physical theatre, while developing your ability to create original performance work that integrates movement and text. The course encourages you to explore the intersections between dance and drama, developing innovative approaches to contemporary performance.

At Kingston, you will benefit from studying within a vibrant art school environment where cross-disciplinary collaboration is central to the learning experience. You will have access to professional-standard dance studios, drama studios and performance spaces, and will participate in regular performances and collaborative projects.

The course is designed to produce graduates who are technically skilled, creatively versatile and critically informed — prepared for careers across the performing arts sector or further study at postgraduate level.

### Key Features

- Unique combination of dance and drama in a single degree
- Practical training in contemporary dance, acting, physical theatre and devised performance
- Study within Kingston School of Art's creative community with access to professional facilities
- Develop skills across choreography, directing, performance and workshop facilitation
- Strong emphasis on interdisciplinary and experimental performance practice
- Opportunities for professional placement year (sandwich route)
- Regular performances and public showings of your work
- Visiting workshops from professional theatre-makers and choreographers

## Course Structure

### Year 1

Year 1 introduces you to the fundamental principles and practices of both dance and drama. You will develop core technical skills in dance technique and acting, while beginning to explore creative approaches to performance-making that integrate movement and text. Theoretical modules provide a foundation in performance analysis and contextual understanding.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Dance Technique and Choreography 1 | 30 |
| Acting and Performance Foundations | 30 |
| Interdisciplinary Performance Making | 30 |
| Performance Analysis and Contexts | 30 |

**Dance Technique and Choreography 1** — Introduces core technical training in contemporary dance alongside foundational choreographic skills. You will develop physical competencies including alignment, coordination, strength and spatial awareness, while exploring creative methods for generating and structuring movement material.

**Acting and Performance Foundations** — Introduces fundamental acting techniques and performance skills. You will work on voice, movement for actors, improvisation and character development. Practical exercises and scene study develop your ability to create truthful and compelling dramatic performances.

**Interdisciplinary Performance Making** — Introduces approaches to creating performance work that integrates dance and drama. You will explore physical theatre, devised performance and collaborative creation methods, developing skills in combining movement, text and visual elements in performance.

**Performance Analysis and Contexts** — Introduces frameworks for analysing and understanding performance across dance and drama. You will study key theoretical approaches and learn to write critically about live performance, developing analytical skills that support both your practical and theoretical work.

### Year 2

Year 2 deepens your practice in both disciplines and expands your creative range. You will work on more advanced projects in dance and drama, develop interdisciplinary performance work, and engage with performance theory and professional practice.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Dance Technique and Choreography 2 | 30 |
| Theatre Performance and Production | 30 |
| Physical Theatre and Devised Performance | 30 |
| Performance Histories and Theories | 30 |

**Dance Technique and Choreography 2** — Develops your technical and choreographic skills at a more advanced level, with increased emphasis on performance quality, stylistic range and choreographic ambition. You will create more sustained and complex choreographic work.

**Theatre Performance and Production** — Extends your acting and theatre-making skills through engagement with dramatic texts, ensemble performance and production processes. You will develop skills in directing, staging and realising theatrical productions.

**Physical Theatre and Devised Performance** — Focuses on the creation of original interdisciplinary performance work. You will explore physical theatre techniques, devising methods, site-responsive performance and the integration of multimedia in live performance. The module supports the development of your individual artistic voice.

**Performance Histories and Theories** — Engages with key historical developments and theoretical frameworks in both dance and theatre studies. You will examine the evolution of performing arts practices, engaging with questions of modernism, postmodernism, identity, cultural politics and global performance traditions.

### Optional Sandwich Year

Between Year 2 and Year 3, you may choose to take a sandwich year for a professional placement in the performing arts sector, or to study abroad at one of Kingston's partner institutions. The placement year provides valuable industry experience and professional networking opportunities.

### Year 3 (Final Year)

Year 3 is focused on the development and presentation of a major interdisciplinary performance project. You will also complete a research project and engage with advanced professional practice to prepare for your career after graduation.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Major Performance Project | 60 |
| Performance Research Project | 30 |
| Professional Performing Arts Practice | 30 |

**Major Performance Project** — The capstone module of the course, in which you will conceive, develop, rehearse and present a substantial piece of interdisciplinary performance work integrating dance and drama. You may work individually or collaboratively, and will be supported by tutors throughout the creative process. The project demonstrates your artistic vision, technical competence and ability to realise complex performance work.

**Performance Research Project** — An independent research project on a topic of your choice related to performance theory, history, practice or pedagogy. You will engage with scholarly sources and critical debates, developing your ability to analyse and articulate ideas about dance, drama and performance.

**Professional Performing Arts Practice** — Develops your understanding of professional contexts for performing arts practice and prepares you for career entry or postgraduate study. Covers topics including portfolio development, audition practice, teaching, community engagement and working as a freelance performing artist.

## Teaching & Assessment

Teaching is delivered through:

- Practical dance technique classes and bodywork sessions
- Acting workshops and scene study
- Choreographic and devising workshops
- Studio-based project work and rehearsals
- Lectures, seminars and screenings
- Individual and group tutorials
- Peer review and group critiques
- Masterclasses with visiting theatre-makers, choreographers and performers
- Performances and public showings

Assessment typically comprises:

- Practical performance and creative presentations
- Coursework portfolios including creative process documentation
- Written essays, analyses and critical reviews
- Research projects and presentations
- Reflective journals and practice-based documentation

## Career Prospects

Graduates from the Dance and Drama BA pursue careers across the performing arts and creative industries, including:

- Performer (dance, theatre, physical theatre)
- Choreographer
- Director / theatre-maker
- Actor / actress
- Devised theatre practitioner
- Community arts practitioner
- Arts administrator / manager
- Workshop leader and facilitator
- Drama / dance teacher (further training may be required for QTS)
- Festival and events programmer
- Further study at MA level (e.g. performance, theatre, choreography)

### Industry Connections

The course benefits from Kingston School of Art's extensive network of performing arts connections. Students engage with visiting practitioners, attend performances and festivals, and may undertake professional placements as part of the sandwich year option.
