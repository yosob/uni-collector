## Overview

The Graphic Design and Photography MA at Kingston School of Art explores the creative intersection of graphic design and photographic practice. The course is designed for practitioners who want to work across both disciplines, developing a practice that integrates visual design with lens-based image-making.

You will explore how graphic design and photography can work together to create powerful visual communication — in editorial design, branding, book design, digital media, advertising and artistic practice. The course encourages an experimental approach, combining traditional craft skills with digital technologies.

Based at Knights Park, you will have access to photography studios, darkrooms, digital labs, printmaking workshops and dedicated studio spaces.

## Course Structure

### Semester 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Integrated Visual Practice 1 | 30 |
| Critical Frameworks: Image and Communication | 30 |

**Integrated Visual Practice 1** — Develops your practice at the intersection of graphic design and photography through a series of projects. You will explore the relationship between typography, layout, image and narrative, developing work that integrates design and photographic skills.

**Critical Frameworks: Image and Communication** — Examines the theoretical and historical contexts of visual communication, including the politics of the image, semiotics, the history of photography and design, and contemporary visual culture.

### Semester 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Integrated Visual Practice 2 | 30 |
| Professional Practice and Research Methods | 30 |

**Integrated Visual Practice 2** — Extends your practice towards a more ambitious and resolved body of work. You will develop a self-directed project that demonstrates the creative integration of design and photography at a professional level.

**Professional Practice and Research Methods** — Covers the professional landscape for graphic design and photography practitioners, including commissioning, publishing, self-promotion and the creative industries. Research methods support your major project development.

### Semester 3 (Major Project)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Major Project | 60 |

**Major Project** — A self-directed project in which you produce a substantial body of work integrating graphic design and photography. The project may take the form of a book, exhibition, digital publication, campaign or other format. Presented as a final exhibition and portfolio.
