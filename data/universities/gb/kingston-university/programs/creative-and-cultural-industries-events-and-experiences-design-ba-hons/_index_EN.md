# Creative and Cultural Industries: Events and Experiences Design BA (Hons)

> Combine spatial design thinking with experience creation. This course prepares you for a career in the events and experiential design industry, focusing on event design, immersive experiences, spatial storytelling and audience engagement.

## Overview

BA (Hons) Creative and Cultural Industries: Events and Experiences Design at Kingston School of Art is designed for students who want to create memorable events and immersive experiences within the creative and cultural industries. The course develops your ability to design spatial experiences, manage live events and craft narratives that connect audiences with brands, culture and ideas.

You will explore how physical and digital spaces can be transformed into engaging experiences — from cultural festivals and brand activations to exhibitions and immersive installations. The programme covers event design, experience architecture, spatial storytelling, audience journey mapping and the creative use of technology in live experiences. You will learn to design and deliver events that are both creatively ambitious and logistically achievable.

The course is part of Kingston's Creative and Cultural Industries (CCI) family, which shares a common first year before you specialise in Events and Experiences Design. This structure gives you a broad understanding of the creative industries before focusing on your chosen pathway.

### Key Features

- Part of the Creative and Cultural Industries programme family at Kingston School of Art
- Common first year shared across CCI pathways before specialisation
- Focus on event design, immersive experiences, spatial storytelling and audience engagement
- Develop skills in experience design, live event production and creative technology
- Opportunities for professional placement year (sandwich route)
- Located at Knights Park campus — Kingston School of Art's dedicated creative hub

## Course Structure

### Year 1 — CCI Common Year

Year 1 is shared across all Creative and Cultural Industries pathways. Students build foundational knowledge of the creative industries, develop core creative skills and explore how ideas become cultural products.

| Module | Credits | Type |
|--------|---------|------|
| Creative Journeys | 30 | Core |
| Navigating Industry | 30 | Core |
| The Tools of Writing | 30 | Core |
| Thinking about Ideas | 60 | Core |

### Year 2 — Events and Experiences Design Specialism

Year 2 focuses on developing event and experience design skills alongside creative project management and audience understanding. Students begin to apply their knowledge through live projects and case studies.

| Module | Credits | Type |
|--------|---------|------|
| Events and Experiences Design | 30 | Core |
| Creative Project Management | 30 | Core |
| Customer Mindfulness | 30 | Core |
| Live Case Study | 30 | Core |

### Year 3 — Advanced Practice and Major Project

Year 3 deepens events and experiences design practice and culminates in a major self-directed project. Students develop entrepreneurial skills and prepare for professional practice in the creative industries.

| Module | Credits | Type |
|--------|---------|------|
| Events and Experiences Design 2 | 30 | Core |
| Culturepreneurship | 30 | Core |
| The Major Project | 60 | Core |
