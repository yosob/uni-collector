## Overview

The User Experience Design MSc at Kingston University equips you with the knowledge and skills to design engaging, effective and accessible digital experiences. The course combines design thinking with technical understanding, preparing you for a career in UX design, interaction design and human-computer interaction.

You will learn to conduct user research, create wireframes and prototypes, design interfaces, and evaluate usability. The course covers both the creative and analytical aspects of UX design, from visual design and information architecture to data analysis and A/B testing.

The course benefits from Kingston's strong links with the technology sector, with input from industry professionals and opportunities for collaborative projects.

## Course Structure

### Semester 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| User Research and Design Thinking | 30 |
| Interface Design and Prototyping | 30 |

**User Research and Design Thinking** — Develops your skills in user-centred research methods including interviews, surveys, personas, journey mapping and design thinking frameworks. You will learn to translate user insights into design requirements.

**Interface Design and Prototyping** — Covers the principles and practice of designing user interfaces, including information architecture, interaction patterns, visual hierarchy and responsive design. You will develop skills in prototyping tools (Figma, Sketch, Adobe XD).

### Semester 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Usability Evaluation and Analytics | 30 |
| Advanced UX: Emerging Technologies | 30 |

**Usability Evaluation and Analytics** — Develops skills in evaluating user experience through usability testing, heuristic evaluation, accessibility assessment and web/digital analytics. You will learn to use data to inform design decisions.

**Advanced UX: Emerging Technologies** — Explores UX design for emerging technologies including voice interfaces, augmented/virtual reality, AI-driven interfaces and the Internet of Things. Examines the ethical and inclusive dimensions of UX design.

### Semester 3 (Major Project)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Major Project | 60 |

**Major Project** — A substantial UX design project in which you research, design and evaluate a digital product or service. The project demonstrates your ability to apply the full UX design process from research through to final prototype and evaluation.
