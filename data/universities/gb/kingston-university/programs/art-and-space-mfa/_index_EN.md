## Overview

The Art and Space MFA at Kingston School of Art is a unique course that focuses on the relationship between art and spatial practice. Designed for artists, architects and designers who want to explore site-specific, installation and environmental art, the course encourages you to consider how art can transform, intervene in and respond to architectural and environmental spaces.

You will develop work that engages directly with spaces and contexts — galleries, public sites, landscapes and architectural environments. The course supports a wide range of practices including installation, sculpture, intervention, performance, video and mixed-media work.

Based at Knights Park, you will benefit from dedicated studio spaces and access to the full range of workshop facilities. The course also makes use of off-site locations and external partnerships.

## Course Structure

### Year 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Art and Space Practice 1 | 40 |
| Critical Contexts: Art, Space and Place | 20 |
| Art and Space Practice 2 | 40 |
| Research Methods | 20 |

**Art and Space Practice 1** — Develops your practice in relation to site and space. Through a series of projects, you will explore the relationship between artwork, context and audience, working in studio and site-specific contexts.

**Critical Contexts: Art, Space and Place** — Examines the theoretical and historical frameworks for understanding art's relationship to space, place and architecture. Engages with phenomenology, site-specificity, land art and spatial politics.

**Art and Space Practice 2** — Extends your practice towards more ambitious spatial interventions and installations. You will develop work for specific sites, considering material, scale, duration and audience experience.

**Research Methods** — Develops research skills and methodologies relevant to spatial art practice. Supports the development of your MFA project proposal.

### Year 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Major Project | 60 |
| MFA Thesis | 20 |
| Professional Practice | 20 |
| Degree Show / Public Presentation | 20 |

**Major Project** — The culmination of the course, in which you realise a substantial body of work in relation to a specific site, space or spatial concept. The project may take the form of an installation, intervention, site-specific work or series of spatial investigations.

**MFA Thesis** — A critical written text contextualising your practice within relevant theoretical, historical and cultural frameworks.

**Professional Practice** — Covers professional development including funding, commissions, public art processes, gallery relationships and career strategy.

**Degree Show / Public Presentation** — Public presentation of your work, providing experience in realising and installing spatial work for audience engagement.
