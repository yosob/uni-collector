## Overview

The BA (Hons) Drama at Kingston School of Art is a creative and critically engaged course that develops your skills as a theatre-maker, performer and thinker. The course combines practical drama training with critical and theoretical study, enabling you to develop as an articulate and versatile theatre practitioner.

You will engage with a range of drama and theatre practices including acting, directing, playwriting, devised theatre, applied theatre and theatre for social change, while developing your ability to create original performance work. The course encourages you to explore theatre in its historical, cultural and political contexts, developing a critical understanding of the role of drama in contemporary society.

At Kingston, you will benefit from studying within a vibrant art school environment where cross-disciplinary collaboration is central to the learning experience. You will have access to professional-standard drama studios and performance spaces, and will participate in regular productions and collaborative projects throughout the course.

The course is designed to produce graduates who are creatively confident, critically informed and practically skilled — prepared for careers across the performing arts sector, arts administration, education or further study at postgraduate level.

### Key Features

- Practical drama training from day one — you will perform, create and produce throughout the course
- Covers acting, directing, playwriting, devised theatre and applied theatre
- Study within Kingston School of Art's creative community with access to professional facilities
- Develop skills across performance, directing, writing and workshop facilitation
- Strong emphasis on contemporary, experimental and socially engaged theatre
- Opportunities for professional placement year (sandwich route)
- Regular public productions and performances
- Visiting workshops from professional theatre-makers, directors and playwrights

## Course Structure

### Year 1

Year 1 introduces you to the fundamental principles and practices of drama and theatre. You will develop core skills in acting and performance while beginning to explore creative approaches to making theatre. Theoretical modules provide a foundation in theatre analysis and understanding drama in its cultural contexts.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Acting and Performance 1 | 30 |
| Theatre Making 1 | 30 |
| Theatre Analysis and Contexts | 30 |
| Staging Text | 30 |

**Acting and Performance 1** — Introduces fundamental acting techniques and performance skills. You will work on voice, movement, improvisation and character development through practical exercises, scene study and ensemble work. The module develops your ability to create truthful, engaged and compelling dramatic performances.

**Theatre Making 1** — Introduces creative approaches to making original theatre. You will explore devising methods, collaborative creation processes and strategies for generating performance material from diverse starting points. Practical workshops develop your skills as a creative theatre-maker.

**Theatre Analysis and Contexts** — Introduces frameworks for analysing and understanding theatre and performance. You will study key theoretical approaches to drama and learn to write critically about live performance, developing analytical skills that support both your practical and theoretical work.

**Staging Text** — Introduces approaches to working with dramatic texts in performance. You will study a range of play texts from different periods and traditions, exploring how meaning is created through performance. Practical workshops in directing and staging complement textual analysis.

### Year 2

Year 2 deepens your drama practice and expands your creative range. You will work on more advanced performance and production projects, develop your skills in specialised areas, and engage with theatre theory and professional practice at a higher level.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Acting and Performance 2 | 30 |
| Theatre Making 2: Writing and Devising | 30 |
| Theatre Histories and Theories | 30 |
| Applied and Community Theatre | 30 |

**Acting and Performance 2** — Develops your acting skills at a more advanced level, with increased emphasis on performance range, stylistic versatility and interpretive depth. You will work with a range of texts and performance styles, developing your ability to tackle complex and challenging roles.

**Theatre Making 2: Writing and Devising** — Focuses on the creation of original theatre through playwriting and devised performance. You will develop skills in dramatic writing, structuring narrative for performance, and collaborative devising. The module supports the development of your individual creative voice as a theatre-maker.

**Theatre Histories and Theories** — Engages with key historical developments and theoretical frameworks in theatre and performance studies. You will examine the evolution of theatrical practices, engaging with questions of naturalism, epic theatre, postdramatic theatre, feminist performance, interculturalism and global theatre traditions.

**Applied and Community Theatre** — Introduces the theory and practice of applied theatre — drama used in community, educational and social contexts. You will develop skills in facilitation, workshop design and participatory practice, exploring how drama can be used as a tool for education, social change and community engagement.

### Optional Sandwich Year

Between Year 2 and Year 3, you may choose to take a sandwich year for a professional placement in the theatre or performing arts sector, or to study abroad at one of Kingston's partner institutions. The placement year provides valuable industry experience and professional networking opportunities.

### Year 3 (Final Year)

Year 3 is focused on the development and presentation of a major performance or theatre project. You will also complete a research project and engage with advanced professional practice to prepare for your career after graduation.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Major Theatre Project | 60 |
| Theatre Research Project | 30 |
| Professional Theatre Practice | 30 |

**Major Theatre Project** — The capstone module of the course, in which you will conceive, develop, rehearse and present a substantial piece of theatre. You may work as performer, director, writer or in a combination of roles, and will be supported by tutors throughout the creative process. The project demonstrates your artistic vision, practical competence and ability to realise complex theatre work.

**Theatre Research Project** — An independent research project on a topic of your choice related to theatre theory, history, practice or pedagogy. You will engage with scholarly sources and critical debates, developing your ability to analyse and articulate ideas about drama and performance.

**Professional Theatre Practice** — Develops your understanding of professional contexts for theatre practice and prepares you for career entry or postgraduate study. Covers topics including portfolio development, audition and interview practice, theatre administration, freelance practice, funding applications and working in the creative industries.

## Teaching & Assessment

Teaching is delivered through:

- Practical acting workshops and scene study
- Devising and theatre-making workshops
- Studio-based project work and rehearsals
- Lectures, seminars and text-based study
- Individual and group tutorials
- Peer review and group critiques
- Masterclasses with visiting directors, playwrights and theatre-makers
- Public productions and performances

Assessment typically comprises:

- Practical performance and production work
- Coursework portfolios including creative process documentation
- Written essays, play analyses and critical reviews
- Research projects and presentations
- Reflective journals and practice-based documentation
- Workshop design and facilitation plans

## Career Prospects

Graduates from the Drama BA pursue careers across the performing arts and creative industries, including:

- Actor / performer
- Theatre director
- Playwright / dramatist
- Devised theatre practitioner
- Drama teacher (further training may be required for QTS)
- Community theatre practitioner / facilitator
- Arts administrator / manager
- Theatre producer
- Workshop leader and facilitator
- Drama therapist (with further training)
- Theatre critic / writer
- Events and festival programmer
- Further study at MA level (e.g. theatre, performance, directing, applied theatre)

### Industry Connections

The course benefits from Kingston School of Art's extensive network of performing arts connections. Students engage with visiting theatre professionals, attend productions and festivals, and may undertake professional placements as part of the sandwich year option. Kingston's proximity to London's theatre district provides access to a wealth of performance venues and industry events.
