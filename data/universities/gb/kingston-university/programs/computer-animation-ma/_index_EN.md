## Overview

The Computer Animation MA at Kingston University combines creative practice with technical expertise in 3D computer animation. The course is designed for graduates and professionals who want to develop advanced skills in digital animation, visual effects and computer-generated imagery.

You will work with industry-standard software and pipelines, developing skills in 3D modelling, rigging, animation, lighting, rendering and compositing. The course balances technical proficiency with creative storytelling, encouraging you to produce visually compelling and conceptually engaging animated work.

Based at the Penrhyn Road campus with access to facilities at Knights Park, you will benefit from dedicated computer labs, motion capture equipment and collaborative project spaces. The course has strong links with the animation and VFX industries, with regular input from visiting professionals.

## Course Structure

### Semester 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| 3D Modelling and Animation | 30 |
| Digital Visual Effects | 30 |

**3D Modelling and Animation** — Develops your skills in 3D character and environment creation using industry-standard software such as Maya and ZBrush. You will learn advanced modelling techniques, rigging, skinning and character animation principles.

**Digital Visual Effects** — Covers the fundamental techniques of visual effects production including compositing, particle systems, dynamics and simulation. You will work with software such as Nuke and Houdini to create photorealistic visual effects.

### Semester 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Creative Practice and Research Methods | 30 |
| Industry Practice | 30 |

**Creative Practice and Research Methods** — Develops your research skills and critical understanding of animation as a creative discipline. You will explore animation history, theory and contemporary practice while developing your major project proposal.

**Industry Practice** — Focuses on professional workflow, pipeline management and collaborative production. You will work in teams on a simulated industry brief, developing project management and communication skills essential for the animation and VFX industry.

### Semester 3 (Major Project)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Major Project | 60 |

**Major Project** — A self-directed project in which you produce a substantial piece of computer animation work. This may be a short animated film, a visual effects sequence, a game cinematic or a technical research project. You will demonstrate mastery of the tools and techniques learned throughout the course.

## Teaching & Assessment

Teaching includes:

- Practical workshops and software training sessions
- Individual and group tutorials
- Lectures and seminars on animation theory and practice
- Guest lectures from industry professionals
- Collaborative group projects

Assessment includes:

- Practical coursework (3D models, animations, VFX sequences)
- Written assignments and research proposals
- Presentations and pitches
- Major project portfolio
