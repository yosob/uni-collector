# Foundation Diploma in Art, Design & Media Practice

## Overview

The Foundation Diploma in Art, Design & Media Practice at Kingston University is an intensive one-year pre-degree programme designed to help students explore a wide range of creative disciplines before specialising at undergraduate level. Delivered within Kingston School of Art, the course provides access to outstanding workshops, studios and facilities at the Knights Park campus.

Kingston University is ranked Gold in the Teaching Excellence Framework and Top 10 in the UK for Art & Design (The Complete University Guide 2026). The Foundation Diploma received an Outstanding rating across all four areas of its Ofsted inspection.

As part of Kingston School of Art, students benefit from joining a creative community that encourages collaborative working and critical practice. Workshops and studios are open to all disciplines, enabling students and staff to work together, share ideas and explore multi-disciplinary making.

### Key Features

- Dedicated Foundation studio spaces and facilities at Knights Park campus
- Four specialist pathways: Visual Communication, 3D Design, Fashion & Fabrication, Fine Art
- Gold TEF rating and Top 10 UK for Art & Design
- Outstanding Ofsted rating across all four inspection areas
- Encourages experimentation with different materials, processes and ideas

## Course Content

The programme focuses on four main specialist areas:

- **Visual Communication** — graphic design, illustration, animation, filmmaking, photography, art direction and fashion communication
- **3D Design** — product design, furniture design, architecture, landscape architecture, interior design, prop and set design
- **Fashion & Fabrication** — menswear, womenswear, costume, textile design, pattern cutting and fashion marketing
- **Fine Art** — painting, printmaking, installation, audiovisual, sculpture, performance and photography

### Course Structure

The course runs from September to June and is divided into three parts:

#### Part 1 — Core Skills + Rotation

The course begins by introducing fundamental principles, techniques and skills important to all specialism pathways. This is followed by a 4-week rotation giving students the opportunity to experience each of the four specialist areas, helping them make an informed choice about their pathway.

#### Part 2 — Specialism

During the specialism phase, students explore their chosen subject in detail and broaden their technical and conceptual skills. Students also prepare their portfolio for degree-level application, with support from specialism tutors.

#### Part 3 — Final Major Project

In the final term, students complete an extended self-directed project using the knowledge, skills and confidence acquired throughout the year. The project culminates with a public exhibition and end-of-course celebration.

This Pearson BTEC Foundation Diploma is split into the Exploratory Phase (Rotation and Specialism) and Confirmatory Phase (Final Major Project), which share the same assessment criteria.

## Entry Requirements

- **Qualifications:** A-level, AS Level or BTEC Diploma in an art and design subject. Alternatively, an International Baccalaureate Diploma/Certificate at a minimum grade of 24.
- **GCSEs:** Five GCSEs at grade C/4, including English Language and Mathematics.
- **International qualifications:** Equivalent to UK Level 3 may also be acceptable.
- **Academic reference:** Not required for this course.
- **Portfolio:** Digital portfolio required — submitted via Dropbox link in the online application.

## English Language Requirements

- **IELTS for UKVI Academic:** 5.0 overall, with no element below 4.5.
- Nationals from majority English-speaking countries or those not requiring a student visa need to meet GCSE English Language requirements only.

## Teaching & Assessment

### Teaching Methods

- Lectures
- Seminars
- Small group tutorials
- Critiques
- Studio practice
- Workshops
- Digital labs
- Project work

Each student is assigned a personal tutor from the Foundation team who provides academic guidance and support throughout the year.

### Assessment

- **Formative assessment:** Detailed feedback through discussion and written feedback during the Exploratory Stage (two formative assessments).
- **Summative assessment:** Takes place at the end of the Confirmatory Stage and contributes to the overall qualification grade.
- Feedback is provided within 20 working days.

## Future Skills & Career Opportunities

Students complete the course with the skills, confidence and direction needed to progress to further study at undergraduate level. Recent graduates have gone on to courses such as:

- Architecture
- Fine Art
- Graphic Design
- Illustration Animation
- Fashion and Costume
- Filmmaking
- Photography
- Product and Furniture Design
- Interior Design

Many graduates choose to stay at Kingston University for their undergraduate studies.
