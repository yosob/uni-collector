## Overview

The Fine Art MFA at Kingston School of Art is an intensive, studio-based programme designed for artists who want to develop a mature, critically informed practice. As a two-year course, the MFA provides the time and space to deepen your artistic enquiry and produce a substantial body of work.

The course supports practice across all media — painting, sculpture, printmaking, photography, film, video, installation, performance and digital media — encouraging you to work across disciplines and challenge conventional boundaries. Critical dialogue is central to the course, with regular tutorials, critiques and seminars informing your studio development.

You will be part of Kingston School of Art's vibrant creative community, working alongside students in design, architecture, film and other disciplines. The Knights Park campus provides extensive workshop facilities and dedicated studio spaces.

## Course Structure

### Year 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Studio Practice 1 | 40 |
| Critical Issues in Contemporary Art | 20 |
| Studio Practice 2 | 40 |
| Research Methods for Artists | 20 |

**Studio Practice 1** — An intensive period of studio-based exploration in which you define and develop your area of artistic enquiry. Through tutorials, group critiques and self-directed work, you will begin to establish a coherent body of practice.

**Critical Issues in Contemporary Art** — Examines key debates, movements and theoretical frameworks in contemporary art. Engages with critical theory, art history and visual culture to enrich your understanding of current art practice.

**Studio Practice 2** — Continues the development of your studio practice with increasing focus and resolution. You will experiment with materials, processes and presentation strategies, culminating in a mid-point exhibition or presentation.

**Research Methods for Artists** — Introduces research methodologies relevant to artistic practice, including practice-led research, visual methodologies and auto-ethnographic approaches. Supports the development of your MFA thesis proposal.

### Year 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Studio Practice 3 (Major Project) | 60 |
| MFA Thesis | 20 |
| Professional Practice | 20 |
| Degree Show Exhibition | 20 |

**Studio Practice 3 (Major Project)** — The culmination of your MFA, in which you produce a substantial body of work for public exhibition. This self-directed module enables you to realise your artistic vision at a professional level.

**MFA Thesis** — A critical text (minimum 6,000 words) that contextualises your practice within relevant theoretical, historical and cultural frameworks. Demonstrates your ability to think and write critically about your own work.

**Professional Practice** — Prepares you for life after the MFA, covering gallery relationships, funding applications, artist statements, portfolio development and career strategies.

**Degree Show Exhibition** — The public exhibition of your MFA work, providing experience in curating, installing and presenting your practice in a professional gallery context.

- Artist-in-residence
- Art consultant
- Arts administrator

- PhD research
