## Overview

The Historic Building Conservation MSc at Kingston University provides a specialist education in the conservation, repair and adaptive reuse of historic buildings. The course is designed for architects, surveyors, planners and other built environment professionals who want to develop expertise in heritage conservation.

You will study the history and theory of building conservation, traditional building materials and construction methods, structural behaviour of historic buildings, conservation legislation and the practical techniques of building repair and restoration.

The course is accredited by the Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors (RICS) and benefits from partnerships with heritage organisations including Historic England.

## Course Structure

### Semester 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| History and Theory of Conservation | 30 |
| Historic Building Materials and Construction | 30 |

**History and Theory of Conservation** — Explores the philosophical, historical and theoretical foundations of building conservation, including the evolution of conservation philosophy, international charters and current best practice.

**Historic Building Materials and Construction** — Develops understanding of traditional building materials (stone, brick, timber, lime, metals) and historic construction methods, including their behaviour, deterioration and repair.

### Semester 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Conservation Legislation and Practice | 30 |
| Structural Conservation and Repair | 30 |

**Conservation Legislation and Practice** — Covers the legal and regulatory framework for heritage conservation in the UK, including listed building legislation, planning policy, conservation areas and heritage assessments.

**Structural Conservation and Repair** — Examines the structural behaviour of historic buildings, structural assessment methods, and the design and implementation of repair and strengthening strategies.

### Semester 3 (Major Project)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Dissertation / Conservation Project | 60 |

**Dissertation / Conservation Project** — Either a research dissertation or a practice-based conservation project involving the survey, analysis and development of a conservation strategy for a historic building.
