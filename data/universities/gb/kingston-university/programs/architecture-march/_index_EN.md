## Overview

The Architecture MArch at Kingston School of Art is a professionally accredited course leading to RIBA Part 2 exemption. It is designed for students who have completed RIBA Part 1 and wish to develop their architectural thinking and design skills to an advanced level.

The course emphasises design as a creative, research-led practice, encouraging you to explore the social, environmental and cultural dimensions of architecture. You will develop your own architectural position through a series of design investigations, engaging with real sites, communities and contemporary challenges.

Based at the Knights Park campus, you will work in dedicated design studios with access to extensive workshops, digital fabrication facilities and a supportive peer community. The course is structured around vertical design studios, each with a distinct thematic focus, allowing you to align your studies with your interests.

Kingston's architecture programmes have a strong reputation for design excellence and have been consistently recognised in national and international awards.

## Course Structure

The MArch is delivered over two years of full-time study, structured around design studio modules supported by theoretical, technical and professional studies.

### Year 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Design Studio 1 | 40 |
| Design Studio 2 | 40 |
| Technology and Environment | 20 |
| History and Theory of Architecture | 20 |

**Design Studio 1** — An intensive design module set within a specific thematic studio. You will develop design proposals in response to a brief, engaging with site analysis, programme development, spatial design and representation. Studios encourage speculative and experimental approaches grounded in real conditions.

**Design Studio 2** — Builds upon the first semester's work, extending your design investigation to a more complex and resolved architectural proposal. Emphasis on materiality, construction and environmental strategy.

**Technology and Environment** — Develops your understanding of structural systems, material properties, environmental design strategies and building services. Integrates technical knowledge with your studio design work.

**History and Theory of Architecture** — Explores contemporary and historical architectural theory, providing a critical framework for understanding and developing your own design practice.

### Year 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Design Studio 3 (Major Project) | 60 |
| Design Research | 20 |
| Professional Practice and Management | 20 |
| Dissertation | 20 |

**Design Studio 3 (Major Project)** — The culmination of the course, in which you develop a comprehensive architectural design project from initial research through to detailed resolution. This self-directed project demonstrates your ability to synthesise design, technical and theoretical concerns.

**Design Research** — Develops research methods and methodologies relevant to architectural design. You will define and pursue a research question that supports and informs your major project.

**Professional Practice and Management** — Covers the regulatory, legal, contractual and managerial aspects of architectural practice. Prepares you for the professional responsibilities of an architect.

**Dissertation** — An extended piece of written research on an architectural topic of your choice, demonstrating critical thinking, scholarly rigour and independent research skills.

## Accreditation

The course is prescribed by the Architects Registration Board (ARB) and validated by the Royal Institute of British Architects (RIBA), providing exemption from RIBA Part 2 examinations.

## Teaching & Assessment

Teaching is delivered through:

- Design studio tutorials (individual and group)
- Workshops and fabrication sessions
- Lectures and seminars
- Site visits and field trips
- Critiques and reviews with visiting critics
- Self-directed study

Assessment comprises:

- Design portfolio submissions
- Written coursework and essays
- Technical reports
- Dissertation
- Presentations and design reviews

## Facilities

- Dedicated architecture design studios
- Wood, metal and plastics workshops
- Digital fabrication lab (CNC, 3D printing, laser cutting)
- Computing labs with architectural software (AutoCAD, Revit, Rhino, Adobe Creative Suite)
- Model-making workshop
- Library with extensive architecture and design collections
