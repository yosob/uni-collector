## Overview

The Media and Communication MA at Kingston University provides a critical understanding of the role of media in contemporary society. The course combines theoretical analysis with practical skills development, examining how media industries, technologies and practices shape culture, politics and everyday life.

You will engage with key debates in media and communication studies, including digital media, social media, journalism, political communication, media ethics and global media flows. The course also develops practical skills in media production, research and analysis.

## Course Structure

### Semester 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Media Theory and Debate | 30 |
| Media Research Methods | 30 |

**Media Theory and Debate** — Examines key theoretical approaches to understanding media and communication, including media effects, political economy, cultural studies, audience research and digital media theory.

**Media Research Methods** — Develops skills in media research, including qualitative and quantitative methods, content analysis, discourse analysis and digital research methods.

### Semester 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Contemporary Media Issues | 30 |
| Media Practice or Specialist Option | 30 |

**Contemporary Media Issues** — Explores current debates and developments in media, including platform capitalism, misinformation, data governance, media representation and activism.

**Media Practice or Specialist Option** — You may choose a practical media production module or a specialist theoretical module such as Political Communication, Global Media or Digital Cultures.

### Semester 3 (Major Project)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Major Project (Dissertation) | 60 |

**Major Project** — Either a traditional dissertation or a practice-based project with a critical written component, on a media and communication topic of your choice.
