# 3D Design Innovation BA (Hons)

## Overview

The 3D Design Innovation BA (Hons) at Kingston University is a forward-thinking programme that combines traditional making skills with emerging digital technologies. Based within Kingston School of Art at the Knights Park campus, the course focuses on creative problem-solving through 3D printing, scanning, CNC machining, laser cutting and digital fabrication, while encouraging sustainability, circular design thinking and entrepreneurial practice.

The programme sits within the 3D Design Department alongside Product & Furniture Design, and shares a culture of material exploration and critical making. Students are encouraged to challenge conventional approaches to design and develop innovative solutions that respond to contemporary social and environmental challenges.

Kingston School of Art provides access to outstanding workshops, studios and digital fabrication facilities. The open-studio culture enables cross-disciplinary collaboration and peer learning across all design programmes.

### Key Features

- Combines traditional hands-on making with cutting-edge digital fabrication technologies
- Focus on sustainability, circular design and responsible innovation
- Dedicated 3D design studios and workshops at Knights Park campus
- Access to 3D printing, CNC, laser cutting and scanning facilities
- Industry-facing projects and professional practice modules
- Encourages entrepreneurial thinking and self-directed creative practice

## Course Content

The programme is structured across three levels, each building on the previous year's skills and knowledge. Students progress from foundational processes and material understanding to advanced independent design practice.

### Year 1 (Level 4)

**Design 1.1 — Process and Practice (30 credits)**
Introduces fundamental design processes, research methods and creative thinking strategies. Students explore the relationship between ideas, materials and making through a series of structured projects and workshops.

**Design 1.2 — Materiality and Making (30 credits)**
Focuses on material properties, craft techniques and hands-on fabrication. Students develop an understanding of how materials behave and how traditional making skills can inform and enhance digital processes.

**Design 1.3 — Digital Translation (30 credits)**
Introduces digital design tools and fabrication technologies including 3D modelling, 3D printing, laser cutting and CNC machining. Students learn to translate physical ideas into digital environments and back again.

### Year 2 (Level 5)

**Design 2.1 — Context and Criticality (30 credits)**
Explores the social, cultural and environmental contexts of design practice. Students develop critical thinking skills and learn to position their work within broader discourses around sustainability, ethics and responsible design.

**Design 2.2 — Professional Practice (30 credits)**
Introduces professional skills for design practice including client projects, collaborative working, presentation skills and business fundamentals. Students work on industry-facing briefs and develop their professional identity.

**Design 2.3 — Digital Futures (30 credits)**
Advances digital fabrication skills and explores emerging technologies such as parametric design, generative design and advanced 3D printing techniques. Students experiment with new possibilities for digital making.

**Critical Issues in Design: Researching Design Practice (30 credits)**
Develops research skills and critical frameworks for understanding design. Students engage with theoretical perspectives and learn to articulate their design decisions through writing and presentation.

### Year 3 (Level 6)

**Design 3.1 — Positioning and Proposition (30 credits)**
Students define their design position and develop a proposition for their Major Project. This involves extensive research, experimentation and critical reflection to establish a clear direction for self-directed work.

**Design 3.2 — Major Project (60 credits)**
The capstone project in which students develop and realise a substantial body of self-directed design work. The Major Project demonstrates the synthesis of skills, knowledge and critical understanding developed throughout the programme.

**Presentation: Set-Up (Apply) (30 credits)**
Prepares students for professional presentation of their work. Students develop a portfolio, online presence and exhibition strategy, culminating in the graduate show.

## Entry Requirements

- **UCAS Tariff:** 112–128 points
- **A-levels:** Minimum of two A-levels or equivalent qualification
- **BTEC:** Extended Diploma in a relevant subject
- **International Baccalaureate:** Minimum 24 points
- **GCSEs:** Five GCSEs at grade 4/C or above, including English Language and Mathematics
- **Portfolio:** Digital portfolio required showcasing creative work, design thinking and making skills
- **Interview:** May be required

### International Qualifications

Equivalent international qualifications are accepted. Students whose first language is not English must meet the English language requirement.

## English Language Requirements

- **IELTS Academic:** 6.0 overall, with no band below 5.5
- **Alternative accepted tests:** TOEFL iBT, Pearson PTE Academic, Cambridge English

## Teaching & Assessment

### Teaching Methods

- Studio-based design projects
- Workshop sessions (traditional and digital)
- Lectures and seminars
- Group tutorials and critiques
- One-to-one tutorials
- Peer learning and collaborative projects
- Industry briefs and live projects

### Assessment

- Coursework portfolio (design projects)
- Written assignments and critical reflections
- Presentations and critiques
- Major Project (Year 3)
- No written examinations

## Future Skills & Career Opportunities

Graduates are equipped with a versatile skill set combining creative thinking, technical making and digital fabrication expertise. Career paths include:

- Product designer / innovation designer
- 3D print specialist / digital fabricator
- Design consultant
- Creative technologist
- Entrepreneur / maker
- Spatial designer
- Design researcher
- Sustainability consultant

The course also provides a strong foundation for postgraduate study in design, innovation and related fields.
