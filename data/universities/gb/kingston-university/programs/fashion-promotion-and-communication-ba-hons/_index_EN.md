## Overview

Fashion Promotion and Communication BA (Hons) at Kingston University is designed for students who want to shape the way fashion is communicated, marketed, and experienced globally. Rather than designing garments, you will focus on the creative strategies and visual storytelling that connect fashion brands with their audiences — from editorial styling and photography to digital campaigns, social media, and event production.

Based within Kingston School of Art's Department of Fashion, the course sits alongside the renowned Fashion BA (Hons), sharing the same creative community, industry connections, and approach to critical thinking. You will develop expertise in fashion imaging, styling, trend forecasting, branding, journalism, and digital content creation, supported by workshops in photography, graphic design, film, and moving image.

The course emphasises innovation and sustainability, encouraging you to challenge conventional approaches to fashion communication and explore new platforms, technologies, and narratives. You will work on live industry briefs, collaborative projects with Fashion BA students, and self-directed projects that build a professional portfolio.

At Kingston School of Art, you will be part of a vibrant, multidisciplinary creative community. Workshops and studios are open to all disciplines, enabling collaboration with students across graphic design, photography, illustration, fine art, and more. You will also have access to the Benenden Fashion Archive, which features pieces dating from 1750 to the present day, providing invaluable research material for styling and editorial projects.

## Course Structure

### Year 1

Year 1 introduces the fundamental principles of fashion promotion and communication. You will explore fashion imaging, styling, photography, and visual communication alongside critical and contextual studies that examine fashion's role in culture and society. Workshops in digital tools, photographic techniques, and graphic layout support studio-based projects.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Fashion Communication Process 1 | 30 |
| Fashion Imaging and Styling 1 | 30 |
| Fashion Promotion Principles | 30 |
| History of Fashion, Style and Dress | 30 |

**Fashion Communication Process 1** — Introduces the core creative processes behind fashion communication. Through project-based learning, you will explore how ideas are researched, developed, and presented across media formats, building foundational skills in visual storytelling and creative thinking.

**Fashion Imaging and Styling 1** — Develops practical skills in fashion photography, styling, and art direction. You will work in studios and on location, learning to construct compelling visual narratives through garment styling, lighting, composition, and post-production techniques.

**Fashion Promotion Principles** — Introduces the fashion promotion landscape including branding, marketing, public relations, and social media strategy. You will analyse contemporary campaigns and develop your own promotional concepts through a combination of individual and group projects.

**History of Fashion, Style and Dress** — Examines fashion as a historical subject through image-based lectures, screenings and study visits. Introduces the historical development of fashion and dress from the 1750s to the present day. Each session explores the particular ideas and practices that have shaped and structured our contemporary understanding of fashion as both a productive occupation and a meaningful socio-cultural activity.

### Year 2

Year 2 deepens your creative practice and introduces professional and industry-facing projects. You will work on live briefs set by industry partners, develop advanced skills in digital content creation, and explore critical issues in contemporary fashion communication including sustainability, diversity, and ethics.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Fashion Communication Process 2 | 30 |
| Fashion Imaging and Styling 2 | 30 |
| Professional Practice 2 | 30 |
| Fashion Critical Issues: Research and Practice | 30 |

**Fashion Communication Process 2** — Builds on Year 1 with more complex, industry-facing projects. You will develop multi-platform communication campaigns, integrating photography, film, graphic design, and digital media. Projects are designed to mirror professional practice and may be set by external industry sponsors.

**Fashion Imaging and Styling 2** — Advances your technical and creative skills in fashion imaging. You will work on editorial, campaign, and lookbook production, developing a sophisticated understanding of visual language and its role in constructing brand identity and fashion narratives.

**Professional Practice 2** — Prepares you for the professional world of fashion communication. You will develop your CV, portfolio, and online presence, and engage with talks from industry professionals including PR executives, editors, stylists, and creative directors. Teamwork, interview skills, and professional networking are integral components.

**Fashion Critical Issues: Research and Practice** — Theorises contemporary fashion practice, building on the historical and thematic content introduced at Level 4. Lectures, seminars, workshops, tutorials, screenings and visits will support your emerging research interests and the development of independent visual and academic research skills across historical/theoretical and design practice.

### Optional Placement Year

At the end of Year 2, you have the opportunity to take a year out for study abroad and work experience. The study abroad programme includes opportunities in Berlin, Amsterdam, Paris, Milan, Hong Kong, New York, Seoul, and Toronto. A dedicated placement tutor within the Department of Fashion supports the internship programme. Students who complete a placement gain a Certificate of Industrial Experience.

### Year 3 (Final Year)

The final year is focused on the development of a major self-directed project and an exit portfolio that demonstrates your creative identity and professional readiness. You will produce a substantial body of work that may include an editorial publication, digital campaign, film, event, or multi-platform communication project.

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Pre-collection & Competition | 30 |
| Major Project (Final Communication Portfolio) | 60 |
| Extended Research Project | 15 |
| Future Skills | 15 |

**Pre-collection & Competition** — Preparation for the Major Project in the form of a pre-project development folder, along with the choice of a selected external competition brief.

**Major Project (Final Communication Portfolio)** — The capstone project in which you conceive, research, develop, and produce a substantial fashion communication project. This may take the form of a magazine, digital campaign, film, exhibition, event, or multi-platform narrative. You will be supported by a project tutor, working independently to realise a body of work that demonstrates your creative vision and professional capability.

**Extended Research Project** — Supports an extended research project that deepens your understanding of critical issues relevant to your practice. You will explore a topic of your choosing, framed by questions emerging from your own creative work and informed by relevant theory, history or cultural context. Working independently and with tutor support, you will undertake rigorous enquiry using appropriate research methods and sources.

**Future Skills** — A dedicated opportunity to develop Future Skills Graduate Attributes. You will self-assess your current skills profile and determine which attributes and skills you need to develop to support your career ambitions, supported by a dedicated career coach. Activities may include short courses, enrichment activities, micro-placements and experiential learning.

### Portfolio Requirements

Please submit a portfolio in digital format, 15–20 pages in length. We are looking for you to demonstrate:

- Visual curiosity and creative thinking
- Awareness of fashion communication, styling or branding
- Technical ability in photography, layout, or digital media
- Enthusiasm and personal voice

A project may be spread over several pages or take up just one. Combining your work into a PDF is recommended. You can include links to a website, Instagram, Flickr, Vimeo or blog.

## Teaching & Assessment

Teaching is delivered through a combination of:

- Studio-based creative projects
- Live briefs and sponsored projects with industry partners
- Lectures, seminars and tutorials
- Workshops in photography, film, graphic design, and digital media
- Group critiques and peer review
- Study visits to galleries, studios, fashion events and trade shows

Assessment typically comprises:

- Practical work (portfolio presentations, exhibitions, screenings)
- Coursework (creative projects, essays, self-assessment, research folders)
- Industry-facing project briefs and presentations

## Career Prospects

Graduates of Fashion Promotion and Communication BA (Hons) are equipped with the creative, strategic, and technical skills to pursue careers across the fashion and creative industries. The course's emphasis on real-world briefs and industry engagement ensures graduates are well-prepared for professional practice.

Graduates go on to work in a wide range of roles, including:

- Fashion PR and communications manager
- Fashion stylist
- Creative director
- Fashion editor / journalist
- Social media and digital content manager
- Brand strategist
- Fashion photographer
- Visual merchandiser
- Event and show producer
- Trend forecaster
- Fashion marketing executive

### Industry Connections

The course benefits from Kingston School of Art's extensive industry network and the Department of Fashion's reputation for producing industry-ready graduates. Students work on live briefs with leading fashion brands, publications, and agencies throughout their studies.

Notable alumni from Kingston's fashion programmes work across the global fashion industry in creative, strategic, and communications roles at brands, media companies, and agencies worldwide.
