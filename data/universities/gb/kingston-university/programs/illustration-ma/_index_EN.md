## Overview

The Illustration MA at Kingston School of Art is a studio-based course that encourages you to develop a distinctive personal voice in illustration. The course takes an expansive view of illustration, embracing traditional and emerging forms of visual communication — from editorial and book illustration to sequential art, animation and experimental image-making.

You will be encouraged to challenge and expand the boundaries of what illustration can be, developing your practice through drawing, printmaking, digital media and mixed approaches. Critical thinking and contextual studies are integrated with studio practice, helping you to situate your work within broader cultural and professional contexts.

Based at Knights Park campus, you will have access to dedicated studio spaces, printmaking workshops, digital labs and the broader creative community of Kingston School of Art.

## Course Structure

### Semester 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Illustration Practice 1 | 30 |
| Visual Storytelling and Narrative | 30 |

**Illustration Practice 1** — Develops your illustration practice through a series of set and self-initiated projects. Emphasis on experimentation with media, process and visual language to develop your personal illustration style.

**Visual Storytelling and Narrative** — Explores the relationship between image and narrative, examining how stories can be told through sequential images, visual metaphors and the interplay of text and image. Engages with historical and contemporary approaches to visual storytelling.

### Semester 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Illustration Practice 2 | 30 |
| Professional Practice and Research Methods | 30 |

**Illustration Practice 2** — Extends and deepens your illustration practice with greater emphasis on professional-quality output and resolved outcomes. You will develop a sustained body of work that demonstrates conceptual depth and technical accomplishment.

**Professional Practice and Research Methods** — Develops your understanding of the professional contexts of illustration, including commissioning, publishing, self-promotion and the creative industries. Research methods support the development of your major project proposal.

### Semester 3 (Major Project)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Major Project | 60 |

**Major Project** — A self-directed project in which you produce a substantial body of illustration work. This may take the form of a book, series of prints, animated sequence, visual essay or other format. The project demonstrates your personal vision and professional capability.
