## Overview

The Sustainable Design MA at Kingston School of Art explores how design can contribute to a more sustainable and equitable future. The course examines the environmental, social and economic dimensions of sustainability, challenging you to develop design solutions that address the urgent challenges of climate change, resource depletion and social inequality.

You will engage with approaches including circular design, design for social innovation, systems thinking, participatory design and design for behaviour change. The course combines design practice with critical thinking and research, encouraging you to develop projects that have real-world impact.

The course is interdisciplinary, welcoming students from design, architecture, engineering, social sciences and other backgrounds who want to apply design thinking to sustainability challenges.

## Course Structure

### Semester 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Sustainable Design Principles and Practice | 30 |
| Design Research for Sustainability | 30 |

**Sustainable Design Principles and Practice** — Introduces the core principles, frameworks and methodologies of sustainable design. You will explore lifecycle thinking, material flows, social design and systems approaches through lectures, seminars and design projects.

**Design Research for Sustainability** — Develops research skills and methods for understanding sustainability challenges, including ethnographic research, systems mapping, participatory methods and impact assessment.

### Semester 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Design Studio: Sustainability in Context | 30 |
| Collaborative Project | 30 |

**Design Studio: Sustainability in Context** — A studio-based module in which you develop a design project addressing a specific sustainability challenge. You will work with real stakeholders, sites and contexts to develop a meaningful design response.

**Collaborative Project** — An interdisciplinary group project in which you work with students from other programmes to address a complex sustainability challenge, developing teamwork, communication and project management skills.

### Semester 3 (Major Project)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Major Project | 60 |

**Major Project** — A self-directed design research project in which you investigate a sustainability issue of your choice, producing a design proposal or intervention supported by a written research report.
