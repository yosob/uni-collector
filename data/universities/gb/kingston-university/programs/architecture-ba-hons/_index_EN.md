# Architecture BA (Hons)

## Overview

The Architecture BA (Hons) at Kingston University is a professionally validated programme that forms an integral part of the RIBA parts 1, 2 and 3 sequence leading to registration as an architect. The course is validated by the Royal Institute of British Architects (RIBA) and prescribed by the Architects Registration Board (ARB), giving graduates RIBA Part 1 exemption — the first step towards becoming a qualified architect.

Based in the Department of Architecture and Landscape at Kingston School of Art, the programme is located on the Knights Park campus, directly adjacent to other creative disciplines. It is ranked Gold in the Teaching Excellence Framework and is consistently recognised as one of the top architecture schools — named in Domus magazine's top 50 architecture schools in Europe and ranked top 3 in London for Architecture (Complete University Guide 2026).

The course is built around a "thinking through making" ethos. Studio projects form at least 50% of the course, with an emphasis on hands-on design exploration alongside rigorous intellectual development. Students have access to purpose-built architecture studios on the upper floors of the New Extension Building and to outstanding workshops on the lower floors, including 3D workshops, digital media suites, printing and photography facilities.

### Key Features

## Course Content

The programme is structured across three levels, each building progressively on knowledge and skills. Students develop expertise in architectural design, representation, history and theory, professional practice, and making. RIBA has introduced new Themes and Values for Architectural Education with a further focus on climate literacy, health and life safety, ethical practice, and research literacy — the modules within the course work within this framework.

### Year 1 (Level 4)

**Reading and Representing: Architecture Starts With Seeing (30 credits)**
Focuses on the acquisition and consolidation of architectural representation and drawing skills. Students learn to observe, analyse and represent the built environment through a range of media including casting, pencil and charcoal rendering, large-scale model making, computer-based graphics and CAD drawing.

**Professional Practice and Making: Architecture is for Others (30 credits)**
Introduces students to the professional dimension of architecture and the collaborative nature of design practice. Includes a real-scale built project that develops hands-on making skills and an understanding of materials and construction.

**Studio: Architecture is Slow (30 credits)**
The core design studio module featuring site-based projects that address the principles and context of architectural design. Students develop foundational design skills through iterative making, drawing and critical discussion.

### Year 2 (Level 5)

**Reading: Architecture Is Seen by Each of Us Differently (30 credits)**
Deepens understanding of architectural history and theory. Students engage with diverse perspectives on the built environment and develop critical frameworks for analysing architecture from multiple viewpoints.

**Professional Practice: Architecture Is Shaped by Society (30 credits)**
Explores how architecture is shaped by and responds to social, cultural and economic forces. Students examine the architect's role within broader societal contexts and develop awareness of professional responsibilities.

**Studio: Architecture Is Formed by Climate and Comfort (30 credits)**
The Year 2 design studio focuses on the relationship between architecture, climate and human comfort. Students develop design responses that address environmental performance, sustainability and the experience of building occupants.

**Making & Representing: Architecture Is Found in Play (30 credits)**
Encourages creative experimentation through making and representation. Students explore playful, iterative design processes that push the boundaries of conventional architectural production.

### Year 3 (Level 6)

**Reading: Architecture Is a Common Ground (30 credits)**
Advanced critical reading and research module. Students develop sophisticated theoretical positions and engage with architecture as a shared cultural and spatial practice.

**Professional Practice and Making: Architecture Is a Matter of Ethics and Competence (30 credits)**
Addresses the ethical dimensions of architectural practice alongside advanced professional and making skills. Students confront the responsibilities of the architect in relation to society, environment and professional standards.

**Architecture Is... (60 credits)**
The thesis design project — the capstone of the programme. Students develop and realise a substantial self-directed architectural design project that demonstrates the synthesis of all skills, knowledge and critical understanding acquired throughout the course.

## Entry Requirements

- **UCAS Tariff:** 128–144 points
- **A-levels:** Minimum of two A-levels or equivalent qualification (General Studies and Key Skills not accepted)
- **BTEC:** Extended Diploma in a relevant subject
- **Foundation Diploma:** In Art and Design accepted
- **International Baccalaureate:** Minimum points accepted
- **GCSEs:** Five GCSEs at grade 4/C or above, including English Language and Mathematics
- **Portfolio:** Digital portfolio required showcasing creative and architectural work
- **Interview:** May be required

### International Qualifications

Equivalent international qualifications are accepted. Students whose first language is not English must meet the English language requirement.

## English Language Requirements

- **IELTS Academic:** 6.0 overall, with no band below 5.5
- **Alternative accepted tests:** TOEFL iBT, Pearson PTE Academic, Cambridge English

## Teaching & Assessment

### Teaching Methods

- Design studio projects (at least 50% of the course)
- Workshop sessions including model making, casting, digital fabrication
- Lectures and seminars on history, theory and professional practice
- Group tutorials and design critiques
- One-to-one tutorials
- Site visits and real-scale built projects
- Peer learning and collaborative design

### Assessment

- Design studio portfolios and project submissions
- Coursework including drawings, models and visual presentations
- Written assignments and critical essays
- Presentations and design critiques
- Thesis design project (Year 3)
- No written examinations

## Career Opportunities

Graduates find employment in architectural and multidisciplinary built environment practices. Career paths include:

- Architect (following RIBA Part 2 and Part 3 completion)
- Architectural assistant / graduate architect
- Urban designer
- Interior architect
- Environmental and planning consultant
- Public sector design advisor
- Design researcher
- Further academic study (MA Architecture, MArch)

Kingston architecture students regularly feature in prestigious awards including the AJ Student Prize and the RIBA President's Medals. Many graduates work in London and the south-east, across the UK and internationally.
