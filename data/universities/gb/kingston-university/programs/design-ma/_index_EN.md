## Overview

The Design MA at Kingston School of Art is a multidisciplinary design course that supports students in developing an advanced, research-informed design practice. The course encourages you to explore design as a critical and creative activity that can address complex social, environmental and cultural challenges.

You will have the freedom to define your own design direction, whether in product design, furniture, spatial design, service design or emerging areas such as speculative and critical design. The course emphasises making, experimentation and material exploration, supported by critical thinking and research.

Based at the Knights Park campus, you will have access to extensive workshops including wood, metal, ceramics, digital fabrication and printmaking. The course promotes a studio culture of collaboration, peer learning and creative risk-taking.

## Course Structure

### Semester 1

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Design Practice 1 | 30 |
| Design Research Methods | 30 |

**Design Practice 1** — An intensive studio-based module in which you develop a self-initiated design project. Through making, testing and iterative development, you will refine your design process and begin to define your area of enquiry.

**Design Research Methods** — Introduces a range of research methodologies relevant to design, including user-centred research, material investigation, design ethnography and practice-led research. Supports the development of your project proposal.

### Semester 2

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Design Practice 2 | 30 |
| Professional Context | 30 |

**Design Practice 2** — Extends and deepens your design project, moving towards a more resolved and ambitious outcome. Encourages engagement with external contexts, users and stakeholders.

**Professional Context** — Explores the professional landscape of design, including entrepreneurship, commissioning, sustainability, ethics and the creative economy. Develops skills in presenting, writing and communicating your work to diverse audiences.

### Semester 3 (Major Project)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Major Project | 60 |

**Major Project** — The culmination of the course, enabling you to produce a substantial body of design work that demonstrates your personal design vision, technical skill and critical understanding. The project is supported by tutorials, critiques and technical workshops.
