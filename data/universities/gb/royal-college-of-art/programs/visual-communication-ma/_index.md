## Visual Communication MA — Royal College of Art

The Visual Communication MA at the RCA is a one-year full-time (45-week) postgraduate programme within the **School of Communication**. It supports designers and image-makers to develop thoughtful, collaborative and socially engaged practices — creating transformative movements that invite change.

The programme centres on **critical making and thinking-in-making**, encouraging students to question and challenge their understanding of visual communication while developing their own creative process. You'll explore the discipline through diverse approaches including illustration, graphic design, moving image, experimental publishing, and typography.

### Key Features

- **Critical practice**: Engage with visual communication as a site of social, political and cultural inquiry — developing work that is both conceptually rigorous and formally inventive.
- **Diverse media**: Work across illustration, graphic design, moving image, typography, experimental publishing, and digital media.
- **Collaborative culture**: Learn alongside a global cohort of practitioners informed by different cultures, politics, traditions and future aspirations.
- **Interdisciplinary opportunities**: The AcrossRCA unit enables collaboration with students from other RCA programmes across all four schools.
- **Industry connections**: Participate in a range of industry and academic events throughout the programme.

### Programme Structure

**Term 1**: Encounters unit (45 credits) — testing, questioning and challenging your understanding of visual communication. AcrossRCA interdisciplinary unit (15 credits).

**Term 2**: Making Worlds with Others school-wide unit (15 credits). Affinities unit (30 credits) — focused project-based work deepening your practice. Core Skills unit (15 credits). Begin developing the Independent Research Project proposal.

**Term 3**: Independent Research Project (60 credits) — a self-directed body of work culminating the year's study, curated for public presentation.
