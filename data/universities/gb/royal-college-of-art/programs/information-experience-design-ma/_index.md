## Information Experience Design MA — Royal College of Art

The Information Experience Design (IED) MA at the RCA is a one-year full-time (45-week) postgraduate programme within the **School of Communication**, currently located at the White City campus (relocating to Battersea in 2027). It is a unique transdisciplinary programme that focuses on the creative practice of intervening in, designing, and generating experiences of complex ideas and phenomena using data and experience design.

### Programme Philosophy

Information Experience Design (IED) is the creative practice of designing and generating experiences of complex ideas and phenomena using data and experience design. The shared medium in IED is **information**: students work with both "cold" data (quantifiable numeric data) and "warm" data (the data of interrelationship). This enables the building of exciting personal practice in living systems and computational systems.

Pieces created in IED include large-scale installations, immersive digital experiences, radical performances, and living artefacts and ecosystems. The programme aims to inspire experimental works that generate transformation, seek better futures through compelling experiences, and compose new ways of relating to and being in the world.

### Key Features

- **Transdisciplinary approach**: No specific disciplinary boundary — students draw from games, interaction design, critical theory, anthropology, speculative design, posthumanities, philosophy, ecology, and more.
- **Experimental practice**: Bold, playful experimentation across digital and analogue, material and immaterial, "high" and "low" art forms.
- **Student-driven technical skills**: Technical instruction is based on a plan designed by the student with technician support, resulting in a unique composite skill set.
- **Post-digital focus**: Working at the intersection of computational and living systems, spanning VR, AR, sound, light installation, wearables, and more.
- **Interdisciplinary opportunities**: AcrossRCA unit enables collaboration with students from other RCA programmes.

### Programme Structure

**Term 1 — Form & Method (45 credits) + AcrossRCA (15 credits)**: Immersion in foundational and state-of-the-art practice and theory in experience design and intervention. Launch into a series of experiments in rapid making. By the end, propose aims, draft concept summary and technical summary.

**Term 2 — Situated Practice (30 credits) + Making Worlds with Others (15 credits) + Core Skills (15 credits)**: Refine and develop signature practice through focused making. Develop audience awareness, collaboration skills, and in-depth understanding of how work fits into contemporary art. Making Worlds with Others is a school-wide collaborative unit.

**Term 3 — Independent Research Project (60 credits)**: Integrate practice into larger critical discourse, reflect on significance, and generate knowledge through a research piece and presentation. Focus on transformation — emphasising wider relevance and exploring how practice can generate evolution or revolution in the world.
