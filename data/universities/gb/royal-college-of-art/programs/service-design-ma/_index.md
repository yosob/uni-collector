## Service Design MA — Royal College of Art

The Service Design MA at the RCA is a one-year full-time (45-week) postgraduate programme within the **School of Design**. It equips students with cutting-edge thinking and tools to provide human insight, engage with complex systems, and create and prototype new futures to become effective changemakers in the modern world.

The programme is predominantly project-based, with students developing their practice through team-based projects. This includes collaborations with commercial, public sector or social organisations who bring real issues and opportunities for future disruption or innovation. Students move through the design process of deep research, issue reframing, creative concept generation, prototyping and user testing, while taking a critical and reflective view of Service Design as a discipline.

### Key Features

- **Studio-based learning**: Learn by doing real-world projects addressing health inequality, the green transition, designing for cities, emerging technology and AI.
- **Industry collaborations**: Work on live briefs with commercial, public sector and social organisations each year.
- **Cross-disciplinary engagement**: AcrossRCA unit and the School-wide Grand Challenge connect students across all RCA programmes.
- **Critical & reflective practice**: Develop confidence in contributing to the evolution of Service Design as a practice and subject.
- **Independent Research Project**: A self-directed major project in Term 3, developing strategic visions with practical deliverable design interventions, culminating in a public event.
- **Diverse cohort**: Welcomes applicants from design, computing, technical disciplines, entrepreneurship, and the public sector.

### Programme Structure

**Term 1**: *Service Design Foundation* — theories and concepts underpinning service design practice, with a small-scale analytically focused team project. *Service Design Practice* — applying tools and methods to a live project with industry partners in areas such as health, sustainability, business innovation or social inclusion. *AcrossRCA* (15 credits) — interdisciplinary collaborative unit.

**Term 2**: *Service Design in Context* — developing the IRP proposal by examining the relationship between practice and context, and the impact of service design on economic, technological, social, cultural and environmental systems. *Grand Challenge* — School-wide cross-disciplinary team project addressing a 'wicked' design problem, potentially with international partners. *Core Skills* (15 credits) — academic and professional skills development.

**Term 3**: *Independent Research Project (IRP)* — self-directed project to establish yourself as a professional service designer, developing strategic visions with practical deliverable design interventions to create new services and systems. The project forms the core public output of the programme and contributes to public discourse on the value of services and interactions.

- **Home deposit**: £1,000 (non-refundable, offset against tuition fees)

- **Progression discount**: £1,000 (GradDip → MA), £2,000 (MA → another Master's)

### English Language Requirements

- IELTS 6.5 overall with minimum 5.5 in each component
- Equivalent qualifications accepted (TOEFL, PTE, Duolingo)
- Students achieving 6.0 with 5.5 in Test of Written English may be eligible for English for Academic Purposes course
- Exempt if you have a 2.1 degree or above from a majority English-speaking nation within the last five years

### Application

- **Portal**: [RCA Applicant Portal](https://applicant-portal.rca.ac.uk/)
- **Portfolio required**: Yes — evidence of challenging projects, user research, empathy, creative ideas, crafting solutions, implementation understanding, and shaping services/experiences
- **Personal statement**: 300 words (interests/motivations, background, future goals)
- **Video**: Max 2 minutes (motivation, favourite service experience, passionate aspect of Service Design, favourite portfolio project)
