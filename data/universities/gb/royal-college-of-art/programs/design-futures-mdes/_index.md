## Design Futures MDes — Royal College of Art

The Design Futures MDes at the RCA is a one-year full-time (45 weeks) or two-year part-time (90 weeks) postgraduate programme within the **School of Design**. Flexibility and choice are at the heart of the offer, with a combination of Core and Elective units enabling students to create a bespoke programme of studies that best suits their approach, context and interests. The programme addresses global environmental, cultural and societal challenges through interdisciplinary design practice.

The programme welcomes students from a very diverse range of backgrounds — from design or any other disciplines or professions — including mid-career designers and career changers.

### Key Features

- **Interdisciplinary by design**: Develop the role of Design within an interdisciplinary context to address complex global challenges.
- **Flexible elective structure**: Choose from School of Design electives and RCA Cross-College electives across both terms to shape a bespoke learning journey.
- **Burst learning model**: Intense on-campus activity delivered in blocks, supplemented by online seminars, group tutorials and peer-led asynchronous activities.
- **Futures methodologies**: Engage with diverse forms of knowledge, stakeholders and perspectives to imagine and realise alternative futures.
- **Systems thinking**: Understand complex systems behaviour and how elements interact and disrupt outcomes.
- **Independent Research Project**: A major self-directed project (60 credits) in Term 3, individual or collaborative, with professional public dissemination.

### Programme Structure

**Term 1**: *Research Methodologies* (15 credits) — introduces research and futures methodologies to support the imagination and realisation of alternative futures by engaging with diverse forms of knowledge, stakeholders and perspectives. *Complex Systems* (15 credits) — introduces the behaviour of systems, how elements interact and disrupt outcomes. Plus one School-wide elective and one College-wide elective.

**Term 2**: *Envisioning Futures* — combines futures methods with advanced digital and analogue envisioning methods bringing to life near and deep futures from a multi-species perspective. *IRP Proposal* (15 credits) — developing a research proposal to demonstrate the need for research on a chosen topic. Plus one School-wide elective and one College-wide elective.

**Term 3**: *Independent Research Project* (60 credits) — self-directed project, individual or collaborative, drawing on programme learning to devise work addressing key ambitions of research and professional practice, with professional public dissemination.

- **Home deposit**: £1,000 (non-refundable, offset against tuition fees)
- **Overseas deposit**: £2,000 (non-refundable, offset against tuition fees)
- **Progression discount**: £1,000 (GradDip → MA), £2,000 (MA → another Master's)

### English Language Requirements

- IELTS 6.5 overall with minimum 5.5 in each component
- Equivalent qualifications accepted (TOEFL, PTE, Duolingo)
- Students achieving 6.0 with 5.5 in Test of Written English may be eligible for English for Academic Purposes course
- Exempt if you have a 2.1 degree or above from a majority English-speaking nation within the last five years
