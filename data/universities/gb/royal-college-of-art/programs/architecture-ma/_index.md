## Architecture MA — Royal College of Art

The Architecture MA at the RCA is a two-year full-time postgraduate programme within the School of Architecture, based at the Kensington campus. It is one of the world's leading architecture programmes, known for its speculative, research-driven approach to architectural design.

The programme is structured around **Architectural Design Studios (ADS)**, each led by a dedicated studio tutor team with distinct research agendas. Students work within their ADS on design projects that engage with contemporary architectural, social, environmental, and cultural challenges.

### Key Features

- **Studio-based learning**: Each student joins an ADS with a specific thematic focus, enabling deep exploration of architectural ideas through design.
- **Critical practice**: The programme integrates critical theory, historical research, and speculative design methods.
- **Technical studies**: Dedicated tuition in structures, technology, and fabrication (led by Luis Sacristán Murga and Manja van de Worp).
- **Thesis project**: Year 2 culminates in a substantial individual design thesis.
- **Location**: Kensington campus, London.

### Programme Structure

**Year 1**: Design research methods, studio projects, technical studies, critical practice seminars, and workshops.
**Year 2**: Individual design thesis developed within the ADS framework, supported by tutorials, critiques, and final exhibition.
