## Animation MA — Royal College of Art

The Animation MA at the RCA is a one-year full-time (45-week) postgraduate programme within the **School of Communication**, currently located at the White City campus (relocating to Kensington in 2027). It is one of the world's most celebrated animation programmes, known for its artistic excellence, risk-taking, and director-led approach.

The programme fosters **critical and creative artistic investigation** through a diverse range of practices spanning analogue, digital, and hybrid forms. Whether you've worked in animation for several years or are just starting out, the programme challenges you to push the boundaries of what's possible.

### Key Features

- **Director-led approach**: Students develop their authorial voice within contemporary discourse and the field of expanded animation.
- **Diverse techniques**: Experiment with motion capture, XR, pre-cinematic, classical, stop motion, VR, AR, installation, and projection mapping.
- **Global cohort**: Work alongside students informed by different cultures, politics, traditions, and future aspirations.
- **Industry connections**: Participate in a range of industry and academic events throughout the programme.
- **Interdisciplinary opportunities**: AcrossRCA unit enables collaboration with students from other RCA programmes.

### Programme Structure

**Term 1**: Studio practice, critical explorations seminars, technical workshops, and the AcrossRCA interdisciplinary unit (15 credits).

**Term 2**: Making Worlds with Others school-wide unit (15 credits), Critical Non/Fiction and Experimental/Expanded Practices (30 credits), Core Skills unit (15 credits). Begin developing the Independent Research Project proposal and production plan.

**Term 3**: Independent Research Project (60 credits) — a self-directed programme of study resulting in a public-facing exhibition. Includes Professional Practice curriculum and events.
