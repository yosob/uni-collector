## Digital Direction MA — Royal College of Art

The Digital Direction MA at the RCA is a one-year full-time (45-week) postgraduate programme within the **School of Communication**, based at the White City campus. It addresses the rapid expansion of digital technology in storytelling and media production, exploring how technologies like VR, AR, AI, games engines, spatial sound and mobile platforms impact diverse storytelling practices, traditions, cultures and audiences.

The programme foregrounds **new models of critical, cooperative and experimental practice** in digital storytelling, focusing on the future of narrative and immersive experience. It is designed for practitioners and researchers who want to push the boundaries of how stories are told and experienced through emerging technologies.

### Key Features

- **Critical storytelling**: Explore foundational and experimental approaches to narrative across diverse media ecologies.
- **Immersive technologies**: Work hands-on with VR, AR, spatial sound, games engines, AI, and mobile platforms.
- **Cooperative practice**: Engage in collaborative, cross-disciplinary projects through AcrossRCA and Making Worlds with Others units.
- **Industry context**: Situate creative work within an expanding media landscape encompassing digital platforms, broadcast, gallery, and public spaces.
- **Interdisciplinary opportunities**: AcrossRCA unit enables collaboration with students from other RCA programmes.

### Programme Structure

**Term 1**: **Critical Stories** (45 credits) — foundational approaches to storytelling practice through critical, cooperative and experimental methods, exploring media ecologies. **AcrossRCA** interdisciplinary unit (15 credits).

**Term 2**: **Making Worlds with Others** school-wide unit (15 credits). **Immersive Enquiries** (30 credits) — studio-based creative research engaging with immersive technologies. **Core Skills** unit (15 credits) — develops specific technical skills in relevant tools and platforms.

**Term 3**: **Independent Research Project (IRP)** (60 credits) — a self-directed programme of study resulting in a public-facing presentation. Supported by tutorials, crits, and professional practice events.
