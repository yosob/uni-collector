## Art & Design Graduate Diploma — Royal College of Art

The **Art & Design Graduate Diploma** (GradDip) at the RCA is a 30-week full-time pre-master's programme that prepares students for postgraduate study at MA level. It is the only programme of its kind at the RCA and spans all four schools — **Architecture, Arts & Humanities, Communication, and Design** — making it a truly interdisciplinary experience.

The programme is designed for students who need to develop their portfolio, strengthen their academic skills, or transition between creative disciplines before committing to an MA. With five specialist pathways — **Communication, Design, Fashion & Textiles, Fine Art, and Research** — students can tailor the programme to their individual goals while building foundational creative and academic competencies.

### Key Features

- **Pre-master's pathway**: Specifically designed to prepare students for MA-level study at the RCA and beyond.
- **Guaranteed MA progression**: Students who achieve a Distinction (70%+) are guaranteed a place on an RCA MA programme.
- **Five specialist pathways**: Communication, Design, Fashion & Textiles, Fine Art, and Research.
- **Multiple delivery modes**: Available on-campus (White City), fully online (distance learning), or blended.
- **Three intakes per year**: January, May, and September entry points.
- **Cross-school access**: Drawing on expertise and resources from all four RCA schools.
- **English language progression**: Graduates are exempt from RCA MA English language requirements upon successful completion.
- **~60% progression rate**: Approximately 60% of graduates are accepted for further study at the RCA.

### Programme Structure

The 30-week programme (120 credits at FHEQ Level 6) is structured around specialist pathway practice combined with core academic skills development:

- **Specialist Practice**: Studio-based creative work within the chosen pathway (Communication, Design, Fashion & Textiles, Fine Art, or Research), including tutorials, workshops, group critiques and independent project development.
- **Academic Skills**: Research methods, critical writing, presentation skills and portfolio building to prepare for MA-level academic demands.
- **Interdisciplinary Engagement**: Opportunities to work across disciplines and engage with the broader RCA community.

### Specialist Pathways

| Pathway | Focus |
|---|---|
| **Communication** | Visual communication, animation, digital media, graphic design |
| **Design** | Product design, service design, interdisciplinary design practice |
| **Fashion & Textiles** | Fashion design, textile design, material exploration |
| **Fine Art** | Painting, sculpture, printmaking, photography, contemporary practice |
| **Research** | Critical theory, research methodologies, academic writing for creative disciplines |

### Delivery Modes

| Mode | Format | Fee (Home) | Fee (Overseas) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **On-campus** | In-person at White City campus | £10,400 | £22,650 |
| **Online (Distance)** | Fully remote delivery | £7,300 | £15,850 |
| **Blended** | Mix of on-campus and online | £10,400 | £22,650 |

### Fees (September 2026 Entry)

- **Deposit**: £2,000 (non-refundable, offset against tuition fees)
- Fees vary by delivery mode (see table above)
- **Note**: White City campus relocating to Kensington from 2027

### English Language Requirements

- **IELTS 5.5** overall with minimum 5.5 in each component
- Lower requirement than RCA MA programmes (IELTS 6.5), reflecting the programme's preparatory nature
- **English exemption**: Graduates who complete the GradDip are exempt from RCA MA English language requirements

### Application

- **Portal**: [RCA Applicant Portal](https://applicant-portal.rca.ac.uk/)
- **Portfolio**: Creative portfolio (up to 10 slots)
- **Personal statement**: 300 words
- **Multiple intakes**: January, May, September
- **Application deadline** (September 2026): 7 August 2026
