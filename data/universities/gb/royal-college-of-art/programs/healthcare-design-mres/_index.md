## Healthcare & Design MRes — Royal College of Art

The Healthcare & Design MRes at the RCA is a unique interdisciplinary part-time programme that draws on the complementary expertise from the RCA's **School of Design** and **Helen Hamlyn Design Centre**, and the healthcare expertise from **Imperial College London**'s medical faculty and the **Helix Centre** based at St Mary's Hospital in Paddington, London.

> **Note**: Healthcare & Design MRes is no longer accepting applications, and teaching of the current cohort will finish in the Summer term 2026. If you'd like to stay updated on future programme developments, contact healthcare-design@rca.ac.uk.

Students develop an awareness of the role and value of design in solving healthcare challenges, as well as a multi-professional perspective on problem-solving and the importance of clinical/design collaboration. The programme uses a range of research techniques, tools and frameworks in the fields of design and innovation, and offers an environment in which to develop design-led skills to rapidly research, synthesise and prototype solutions to healthcare challenges.

### Key Features

- **Interdisciplinary collaboration**: Co-delivered by the RCA and Imperial College London, bridging design and clinical expertise.
- **Clinical–design integration**: Work at the intersection of healthcare and design, based at the Helix Centre at St Mary's Hospital.
- **Part-time format**: 2-year part-time study ideal for working professionals, with one-week intensive teaching sessions per module.
- **Research-focused**: Major personal design research project conducted throughout both years, assessed via viva and thesis.
- **Real-world impact**: Projects addressing genuine healthcare challenges, including work with NHS trusts and healthcare organisations.
- **Behaviour change expertise**: Specialist focus on designing for behaviour change in health contexts.

### Programme Structure

**Year 1** — Four taught modules delivered in one-week intensive sessions:
- *Design Led Innovation in Healthcare* — applying design innovation methodologies to healthcare contexts
- *Principals of Design and Innovation* — foundational theories and frameworks
- *Health Research Toolkit* — research techniques and tools for healthcare design
- *Helix Design Dash* — intensive team-based design challenge in a clinical setting

Teaching includes interactive lectures, seminars, tutorials, case-based discussion, technology demonstrations, and practical team-based workshop projects.

**Year 2** — Four further modules:
- *Innovation* — advanced innovation strategies and frameworks
- *Designing for Behaviour Change* — applying behaviour change theory through design
- *Health Business Toolkit* — business and entrepreneurial skills for healthcare innovation
- *Leadership* — leadership skills for clinical-design collaboration

Each module concludes with an assessable presentation and a piece of coursework.

**Design Research Project** — A major personal design research project conducted throughout the two years. Supervision is led by the RCA with support from Imperial College. Students can undertake the project in their workplace (with employer support) or select a self-directed theme. Assessed via a viva with design projects and a thesis.

### Staff

- **Anna Wojdecka** — Senior Tutor and Academic Lead for Healthcare & Design MRes. Innovation Design Engineer with interdisciplinary expertise in Human Centred Design, Engineering and Disruptive Technology.
- **Professor Ashley Hall** — Postgraduate Research Lead. Professor of Design Innovation in the School of Design at the RCA.

### Facilities

The School of Design is based across the RCA's Battersea and Kensington sites. Students have access to the College's workshops with traditional facilities for woodworking, metalworking, plastics and resins, including bookable bench spaces, computer-driven subtractive milling equipment, and additive rapid prototyping. The programme also has a dedicated Healthcare & Design Studio.
