## Fashion MA — Royal College of Art

The Fashion MA at the RCA is a one-year full-time (45-week) postgraduate programme within the **School of Design**. It challenges the dominant fashion paradigm, pivoting from the traditional model of making clothes (line-up, to collection, to fashion show) toward a practice that responds to environmental concerns, social responsibility, and geopolitical and economic challenges.

The programme creates an environment where students reform fashion for a state of change — open to new and emerging thinking, guided by **diversity, inclusivity and cultural awareness**. Students are encouraged to perceive systems and structures, expressing fashion identities with awareness of how values are embedded in creative outputs.

### Key Features

- **Critical fashion practice**: Reform the dominant fashion paradigm through environmental, social and economic responsiveness.
- **Change Agent provocations**: Engage with urgent territories including Waste Lands, Inclusion & Accessibility, and Digital Dignity.
- **Cross-disciplinary collaboration**: AcrossRCA unit enables working with students from all RCA programmes; the school-wide Grand Challenge tackles major strategic design issues in cross-disciplinary teams.
- **Identity-centred approach**: Reflect upon, discuss and strengthen your own authentic fashion identity while helping others do the same.
- **Independent Research Project**: A self-directed major project in Term 3, mentored throughout, culminating in professional expert critique and public exhibition.
- **Diversity & inclusion**: The programme explicitly welcomes and values diversity in gender identity, supporting visibility and expression across the full spectrum of identity.

### Programme Structure

**Term 1**: *Change Agent* — provocations by disruptors and advocates exploring Waste Lands, Inclusion & Accessibility, and Digital Dignity. *Change Perspective* — reforming the fashion paradigm toward environmental/social responsiveness through community work, co-creation and iterative prototyping. *AcrossRCA* (15 credits) — interdisciplinary collaborative unit.

**Term 2**: *Advanced Practice* — articulating your unique perspective, system and aesthetic for fashion, considering non-hierarchical thinking, trans & post-humans, all gender identities, and inclusive economics. *Grand Challenge* — school-wide cross-disciplinary team project on a 'wicked' design problem. *Core Skills* (15 credits) — academic and professional skills development.

**Term 3**: *Independent Research Project* (60 credits) — a self-directed project with your own brief, mentored throughout, with a 'Final Engagement' involving professional expert critique and a public exhibition.
