## City Design MA — Royal College of Art

The City Design MA at the RCA is a one-year full-time postgraduate programme (180 credits, 45 weeks) within the School of Architecture, based at the Kensington campus. It offers an interdisciplinary approach to creating new spaces, positioning city design as more than designing physical structures — it is about how we live together, work, and move.

The programme is led by **Dr Dubravka Sekulić** and emphasises **justice-oriented thinking**, **transformative design practice**, and the use of **digital design, analysis and visualisation tools** including moving image, animation, augmented and immersive design.

### Key Features

- **Studio-based teaching**: Design-led and site-based pedagogy working with real urban contexts and non-academic partners (NGOs, planning authorities, community organisations).
- **Justice-oriented approach**: Focus on environmental and spatial justice, advocating for equitable urban futures.
- **Digital innovation**: Emphasis on digital visualisation, simulation tools, film, animation, and immersive media as drivers of speculative innovation.
- **Interdisciplinary collaboration**: Connect with architects, social scientists, policy makers, and grassroots organisations.
- **Practice mentorship**: Innovative mentorship scheme connecting students with leading figures in City Design in London and internationally.
- **Location**: Kensington campus, London.

### Programme Structure

**Term 1** — Design Studio I: Conditions (15 credits): Introduction to the programme's design-led, site-based pedagogical model, working in partnership with non-academic urban actors and organisations.

**Term 2** — Design Studio II: Propositions (30 credits): Developing design research addressing specific urban conditions at neighbourhood, street and block scale. Seminar II: Transformations (15 credits): Contemporary urban transformation theories. School-wide elective (15 credits): choose from Investigations, Media Studies II, Re-use, or Take Back the Land. Core Skills (15 credits).

**Term 3** — Independent Research Project (60 credits): Culminating project submitted by project (1,500 words + portfolio) or by thesis (10,000 words + portfolio), including a public presentation and exhibition.

- Apply in Round 1 or 2 for scholarship consideration

### English Language Requirements

- IELTS 6.5 overall with minimum 5.5 in each component
- Students with 6.0 overall (5.5 in Test of Written English) may be eligible for the College's English for Academic Purposes course
