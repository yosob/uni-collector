## Design Practice MArch — Royal College of Art

The MArch in Design Practice at the RCA is an innovative programme that engages the social and political systems underlying the climate crisis by design. Based in the School of Design, it equips students to understand, analyse and contend with these systems, supporting the development of propositions that creatively and constructively bring about change.

The programme is aimed both at postgraduate students and mid-career professionals, offering a balance between core knowledge, elective and collaborative work, and a self-defined research project. Flexibility and choice are at the heart of the offer, with a combination of core and elective units enabling students to create a bespoke programme of studies.

### Key Features

- **Climate-focused architecture**: The programme centres on the climate crisis and the built environment, engaging carbon economies, material processes, risk, and just transitions.
- **Interdisciplinary welcome**: Open to architects, designers, spatial practitioners, and those from other backgrounds (local government, NGOs, journalism, activism).
- **Forensic design approach**: The unique Detailing Risk unit uses forensic methods to study design failures and their social implications.
- **Design Policy Proposals**: Students produce actionable policy proposals in the Just Transition unit.
- **Cross-College electives**: Access to a wide range of electives across all RCA schools, enabling interdisciplinary exploration.

### Programme Structure (Full-time, September entry)

**Autumn term**: Material Processes (15 cr) · Carbon Economies (15 cr) · 2 Cross-College electives (15 cr each)

**Spring term**: Detailing Risk (15 cr) · Just Transition (15 cr) · 2 Cross-College electives (15 cr each)

**Summer term**: Independent Research Project (60 cr)

The full-time programme is delivered over 45 weeks. January entry follows a Spring → Summer → Autumn sequence.

### Part-time Option

Delivered over 90 weeks across two years, with 2 core units and 1 elective per term, plus 30-credit IRP in each summer term.
