## Design Products MA — Royal College of Art

The Design Products MA at the RCA is a one-year full-time (45-week) postgraduate programme within the **School of Design**. It explores how design can address real-world challenges through experimentation, critical thinking, and making, positioning graduates at the forefront of the design products landscape.

The programme encourages students to develop a **strong and adaptive personal creative process**, building a portfolio of projects that range from experimental prototypes to fully resolved design outcomes. It embraces a broad spectrum of design practice — from methods and materials to technology, manufacturing, futures, and social/participatory design.

### Key Features

- **Experimental approach**: Expand the range and ambition of design practice through various forms of experimentation — methods, materials, technology, making, manufacturing, futures, and social/participatory design.
- **Cross-disciplinary collaboration**: AcrossRCA unit enables working with students from all RCA programmes; the school-wide Grand Challenge tackles major strategic design issues in cross-disciplinary teams.
- **Industry engagement**: Opportunities to participate in commercially sponsored projects with external partners.
- **Independent Research Project**: A self-directed major project in Term 3 synthesising all learning, supported by targeted supervision.
- **Diverse backgrounds welcomed**: Applicants from engineering, architecture, communication, medical/health, economics/business, material science, technology, UX and beyond.

### Programme Structure

**Term 1**: *Locating Practice* — positioning your practice within the design products landscape. *Experimental Design* — expanding range and ambition through experimentation. *AcrossRCA* (15 credits) — interdisciplinary collaborative unit.

**Term 2**: *Advanced Practice* — deepening creative methods and application. *Grand Challenge* — school-wide cross-disciplinary team project on a 'wicked' design problem. *Core Skills* (15 credits) — academic and professional skills development. Commercially sponsored project opportunities.

**Term 3**: *Independent Research Project* (60 credits) — a self-directed, high-resolution design project that argues for a new design space, supported through supervision.
