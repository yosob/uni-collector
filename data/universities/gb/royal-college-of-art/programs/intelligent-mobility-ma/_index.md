## Intelligent Mobility MA — Royal College of Art

The Intelligent Mobility MA at the RCA is a one-year full-time (45-week) postgraduate programme within the **School of Design**, hosted by the **Intelligent Mobility Design Centre**. It extends traditional vehicle and transport design into a visionary, transformative and connective approach to future mobility.

The programme challenges students to rethink the future of movement — from autonomous vehicles and electrification to how people, places and technology converge in the next generation of mobility systems. Students develop design excellence through project-based learning, combining deep human insight with advanced technological understanding to create desirable, sustainable and socially relevant mobility solutions.

### Key Features

- **Intelligent Mobility Design Centre**: The programme is hosted by RCA's dedicated research centre, connecting students with cutting-edge industry and academic research in mobility design.
- **Future-focused curriculum**: Explore autonomous driving, electrification, sustainable design, emotional technology and future mobility typologies.
- **Industry engagement**: Live projects and collaborations with leading automotive and mobility companies.
- **Cross-disciplinary learning**: AcrossRCA unit and School-wide Grand Challenge connect students across all RCA programmes.
- **Independent Research Project**: A self-directed major project in Term 3, addressing a defined research question that is both socially relevant and technically challenging, culminating in public exhibition.
- **Portfolio-driven admission**: Selection based on creativity, imagination, design excellence and demonstrable understanding of 3D form.

### Programme Structure

**Term 1**: *People and Places* — understanding how future mobility fits into a changing global landscape and how people and place connect through mobility, highlighting the need for design to meet broader infrastructural and social requirements. *Humanising Technology* — exploring how transformational mobility technologies such as autonomy and electrification can be interpreted to provide new and desirable human interactions, gaining a critical understanding of emerging technologies. *AcrossRCA* (15 credits) — interdisciplinary collaborative unit.

**Term 2**: *Automotive Transitions* — reimagining how technology and social trends will influence future automotive design, with emphasis on advanced manufacturing, contemporary socio-cultural issues and future business models. *Grand Challenge* — School-wide cross-disciplinary team project addressing a 'wicked' design problem, potentially with international partners, providing unique networking opportunities. *Core Skills* (15 credits) — academic and professional skills development.

**Term 3**: *Independent Research Project (IRP)* — the final Master's project requiring students to identify, develop and address a defined research question which is both socially relevant and technically challenging. Work must be documented at all stages for public dissemination, culminating in a physical and/or digital exhibition. Students are responsible for the intellectual and technical evolution of the project, from identifying stakeholders and research methodology to delivering a fully resolved vision in appropriate media.

### Teaching & Learning Methods

- Personally directed study guided by tutors through regular tutorials
- Personal tutorials to explore and challenge attitudes and knowledge
- Group tutorials to debate issues across cohorts
- Group seminars providing contextual and ideological knowledge
- Group workshops and skills training (e.g. specialist CAD, research skills)
- Individual interim project presentations and reviews (non-examined)
- Final examinations by an examination board

- IELTS 6.5 overall with minimum 5.5 in each component
- Students achieving 6.0 with 5.5 in Test of Written English may be eligible for English for Academic Purposes course
- Exempt if you have a 2.1 degree or above from a majority English-speaking nation within the last five years
