## Interior Design MA — Royal College of Art

The Interior Design MA at the RCA is a one-year full-time postgraduate programme within the School of Architecture, based at the Kensington campus. It is one of the world's leading interior design programmes, recognised for its forward-thinking, research-driven approach to the design of interior environments.

The programme explores emergent ideas and issues concerning distinct aspects of the design of the interior, treating it as an **interface between occupants and the built environment** and as an **agent for social change**. Students engage with the diversity of human occupation across numerous environments, from the room to the city scale.

### Key Features

- **Three superPLATFORMS**: Students work within one of three thematic platforms — **superREUSE** (adaptive reuse and reprogramming), **superMATTER** (materiality, making and fabrication), and **superFUTURES** (speculative and future-facing approaches) — each led by specialist tutors.
- **Studio-based learning**: Project-driven studio practice with an emphasis on speculation, analysis, and rigor in thinking and making.
- **Socially engaged design**: The programme positions the interior as a catalyst for addressing climate change, social injustice, and other pressing contemporary issues.
- **Cross-disciplinary dialogue**: Opportunities for collaboration across the School of Architecture and the wider RCA.
- **Location**: Kensington campus, London.

### Programme Structure

The programme is structured around the three superPLATFORMS. Students are assigned to a platform and engage in a series of design projects, workshops, seminars, and critical discussions throughout the year. The year culminates in a major design project and exhibition.
