## Textiles MA — Royal College of Art

The Textiles MA at the RCA is a one-year full-time (45-week) postgraduate programme within the **School of Design**. It challenges students to develop a strong foundation in textile practice through research, experimentation and exploration of colour, material and process — pushing the boundaries of what textiles can be.

The programme spans **print, knit, mixed media and weave**, encouraging students to critically engage with their choice of materials and processes. It explores textiles across multiple contexts — from sustainable and future materials to fine art, soft systems, and smart technologies.

### Key Features

- **Material-led practice**: Develop expertise in colour, material and process through hands-on experimentation in print, knit, mixed media and weave.
- **Critical engagement**: Current debates on sustainability, gender & politics, materials & craft, future materials, mixed realities and soft systems are embedded in the curriculum.
- **Cross-disciplinary collaboration**: AcrossRCA unit enables working with students from all RCA programmes; the school-wide Grand Challenge tackles major strategic design issues in cross-disciplinary teams with international partners.
- **Industry collaborations**: Opportunities to work with external industry partners on live projects.
- **Independent Research Project**: A self-directed major project in Term 3, culminating in a collective Textiles Festival with public dissemination.
- **Textiles Festival**: All students participate in a public festival that enables critique of work in relation to the wider discipline.

### Programme Structure

**Term 1**: *Textile Alchemy* (45 credits) — establishing practice through research, experimentation and exploration of colour, material and process. *AcrossRCA* (15 credits) — interdisciplinary collaborative unit responding to urgent contemporary themes.

**Term 2**: *Textile Make* (30 credits) — deepening understanding and skill capability in chosen practice area, covering current textile debates. *Grand Challenge* — school-wide cross-disciplinary team project on a 'wicked' design problem with potential international partners. *Core Skills* (15 credits) — academic and professional skills development.

**Term 3**: *Independent Research Project* (60 credits) — a self-directed body of work demonstrating Masters-level expertise, supported by tutorials, seminars and workshops, culminating in the Textiles Festival.

- IELTS 6.5 overall with minimum 5.5 in each component
