## Innovation Design Engineering MA/MSc — Royal College of Art × Imperial College London

The Innovation Design Engineering (IDE) MA/MSc is a unique **double Master's programme** run jointly between the Royal College of Art and Imperial College London for over 40 years. This 2-year full-time programme (360 credits) within the **School of Design** integrates design and engineering with science and enterprise to produce a new type of innovator capable of tackling complex challenges faced by society and the planet.

IDE is about **transformation** — of practices that transcend disciplinary boundaries, of understanding how past, present and future can identify opportunities to create lasting impact, and of you as an individual into a creative leader. The programme is fiercely curious, open-minded, empathic, and analytical.

Past IDE students have explored everything from medical devices that manage health conditions to systems for protecting the environment against pollution. Notpla, co-founded by IDE students, won the Earthshot Prize for creating a solution to the global plastic packaging problem. IDE students have won the National James Dyson Award three years in a row.

### Key Features

- **Dual degree**: Graduate with both an MA from the RCA and an MSc from Imperial College London.
- **Transdisciplinary approach**: Integrate design, engineering, science and enterprise across all phases.
- **Industry collaboration**: Work with real organisations on live innovation challenges.
- **Powerful alumni network**: Join the "IDE family" — a supportive community spanning decades.
- **Award-winning outcomes**: Students and alumni regularly win national and international awards including the James Dyson Award and Earthshot Prize.
- **Biomaterials to AI**: Learn from academics making an impact across the full spectrum of innovation.

### Programme Structure (Three Phases, Six Terms)

**Phase 1 — Foundations (Terms 1 & 2)**
A series of units: *IDE Fundamentals; Transdisciplinary Practices; Cyber Physical Systems; Regenerative Materials; Structures & Aesthetics; Impact*. Each unit focuses on a particular aspect of innovation, design and engineering through design-led projects to briefs — sometimes set and sometimes self-devised. Emphasis on imaginative ideas, development and validation through testing prototypes, simulation and feedback from expert stakeholders.

**Phase 2 — Consolidation (Terms 3 & 4)**
Two units: *Sustainable Systems* and *Agency & Implementation*. Develop your individual and professional agency, identity and pathways through engagement with organisations, your own ventures or within research fields. Phase 2 culminates in defining and planning your team and individual projects for Phase 3.

**Phase 3 — Mastery (Terms 5 & 6)**
A team-based project, individual projects and elective modules. Strengthen your professional agency, leadership, knowledge and skills. The thematic areas explored can be diverse and are supported by a broad range of expert tutors. Creates a substantial springboard into ventures, research and careers.

### English Language Requirements

- IELTS Academic: 6.5 overall (minimum 5.5 in every component)
- Exempt if you have a 2.1 degree or above from a majority English-speaking nation within the last five years
- Student Visa holders must also meet Home Office minimum requirements for entry clearance

### Application Requirements

- **Portfolio**: Single PDF (max 20 pages) — show excellence of expertise, innovation/design/engineering interests, creative abilities
- **Personal statement**: 300 words covering your introduction, background, and future aspirations
- **Video**: 2 minutes, responding to at least 3 prompts (inspirations, aspirations & IDE fit, unique skills you bring)
- **Academic transcripts** and **references**
- **English proficiency** proof

### Career Outcomes

IDE graduates head into diverse creative careers as consultants, innovators, entrepreneurs, researchers, freelancers or within corporations. The dual degree and interdisciplinary training create uniquely positioned professionals at the intersection of design, engineering and enterprise.
