# Applied Storytelling MA

## Programme Overview

The MA Applied Storytelling is a practical, hands-on course designed for those who want to use storytelling in real-world settings. Whether you're interested in working in **community projects**, **education**, **the creative industries**, or **policy work**, this course will equip you with the skills to apply storytelling for social impact, personal growth, and professional development.

This innovative Master's degree builds on Loughborough's **international reputation** for research in applied storytelling relating to health, the environment, education and social justice. Students work alongside researchers on existing projects in the UK and overseas.

The programme is taught through the **Storytelling Academy** — Loughborough's internationally renowned centre for applied storytelling research and practice — and is affiliated with **UNESCO's first ever Chair in Storytelling Education for Sustainability**.

## Entry Requirements

- **Academic:** A 2:1 honours degree (or equivalent international qualification) in **any subject**. Equivalent professional experience/qualifications may also be considered.
- **English Language:** IELTS 6.5 overall with 6.0 in each component.

## Programme Structure

The programme consists of **180 credits** across two semesters and a summer project period.

### Semester 1 (60 credits — Compulsory)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Digital Storytelling for Creative Practice | 15 |
| Performative Writing | 15 |
| Research Methods for Storytelling | 30 |

### Semester 2 (60 credits — Compulsory)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Translating Theory into Practice | 30 |
| Co-creative Community Practices | 30 |

### Summer (60 credits — Choose one)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Dissertation | 60 |
| Major Project | 60 |

## Teaching & Assessment

The programme features a variety of teaching and learning methods including **lectures, seminars, tutorials, field trips, independent study, group work, supervision, workshops, and practical sessions**.

Assessment is through a combination of **practical exercises, essays, presentations, project portfolios** and a **major project/dissertation**.

## Career Prospects

Graduates are equipped for a wide range of careers in applied storytelling and related fields:

- **Community Arts Practitioner** / Storytelling Facilitator
- **Education Specialist** / Creative Learning Officer
- **Heritage and Museum** Storyteller / Interpretation Officer
- **NGO Communications** and Advocacy Officer
- **Healthcare Storytelling** Practitioner
- **Creative Industries** Consultant
- **Policy and Social Impact** Storyteller
- **Digital Content** and Narrative Strategist
- **PhD Study** & Academic Research

The programme's connections to the **UNESCO Chair** and the **Storytelling Academy's** extensive network of partners provide unique pathways into international development, sustainability and social justice work.

## Facilities

The programme is based in the School of Design and Creative Arts, housed in a purpose-built **design building** featuring innovative teaching spaces, workshops, studios, and project display areas. Students have access to digital storytelling equipment, performance and rehearsal spaces, and the full resources of the **Storytelling Academy**.
