# User Experience Design MA/MSc

## Programme Overview

Our User Experience Design programme will develop your critical awareness of major issues in interaction and user experience design. The programme aims to educate designers who are champions of user-centred and inclusive design, fostering an understanding of human needs and user empowerment.

The programme examines how to design effective user experiences across interactive products, services, and systems. You will gain practical and theoretical knowledge in human-centred design, usability evaluation, and design research methods. The programme offers both **MA and MSc pathways** — the MA focuses on creative practice through a design major project, while the MSc emphasises research through a UX research project.

The programme is **accredited by the Chartered Institute of Ergonomics and Human Factors (CIEHF)** as a Technical Degree Course, ensuring alignment with professional standards.

## Entry Requirements

- **Academic:** A 2:1 honours degree (or equivalent international qualification) in a discipline relevant to User Experience Design, including Art and Design, Computer Science, Media, Psychology, or Business.
- **English Language:** IELTS 6.5 overall with 6.0 in each component.

## Programme Structure

The programme consists of **180 credits** across two semesters and a summer project period.

### Semester 1 (60 credits — Compulsory)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Experience Design | 30 |
| Usability, Principles and Practice | 15 |
| Understanding People, Behaviours and Context | 15 |

### Semester 2 — Compulsory

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Industry Project | 15 |
| Generative Research and Prototyping for MA Design Major Project | 15 |
| Evaluative Research and Prototyping for MSc Design Research Project | 15 |

### Semester 2 — Optional (choose one)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Interaction and Immersive Futures | 30 |
| Disruptive Interaction Design | 30 |

### Summer (60 credits — Compulsory)

| Module | Pathway | Credits |
|--------|---------|---------|

## Teaching & Assessment

The programme features a variety of teaching and learning methods including lectures, seminars, tutorials, field trips, independent study, group work, supervision, workshops, practical sessions, online tutorials, and virtual learning. Assessment is through coursework, including design projects, group and individual presentations.
