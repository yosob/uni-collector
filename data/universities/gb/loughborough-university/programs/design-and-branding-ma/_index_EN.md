# Design and Branding MA

## Overview

The MA Design and Branding programme at Loughborough University London equips students with a critical understanding of the strategic role of design and branding within businesses, organisations and society. The programme combines theory and practice of design methodologies, enabling students to work both individually and in diverse teams.

Students develop collaborative skills through active learning and teamwork, analysing and evaluating problems and responding to challenges in real time. The programme culminates in a design and branding dissertation that draws on design, branding and organisational theories to develop analytical and research skills.

## Entry Requirements

- **Degree:** A 2:2 honours degree (or equivalent international qualification) in design (e.g. engineering design, fashion design, art and design), innovation, business, media, technology or a related subject.
- **Non-design backgrounds:** Applicants from non-design backgrounds must have achieved 55% or above in their final year.
- **English Language:** IELTS 6.5 overall with 6.0 in each component.

## Modules

### Semester 1 — Compulsory (45 credits)

| Module | Credits | Description |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| Branding & Identity | 15 | Explores foundational concepts of branding, design and social identity including identity, socialisation, and representation, and their application to brand design practice |
| Design Innovation | 15 | Introduces design approaches and tools for understanding and addressing human-centred innovation challenges |
| Grand Challenges | 15 | Examines the grand challenges facing global society with reference to the UN Sustainable Development Goals and explores the role of design in developing sustainable solutions |

### Semester 1 — Optional (choose 1, 15 credits)

| Module | Credits | Description |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| Strategy and Market Analysis | 15 | Explores competitive positioning and the strategies organisations use to gain and maintain competitive advantage |
| The Age of Promotion | 15 | Examines strategies for winning hearts and minds, promoting products, services and ideas through promotional culture |
| Creative Producing in the Digital Economy | 15 | Develops understanding of creative production and collaboration within the digital experience economy |

### Semester 2 — Compulsory (60 credits)

| Module | Credits | Description |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| Design Research | 15 | Covers design research history and a range of qualitative and quantitative methodologies |
| Design for Experiences | 15 | Focuses on user experience design, innovation management and the creation of meaningful interactions |
| Collaborative Project | 15 | Project-based module emphasising teamwork and collaboration with external partners |
| Branding and Strategy | 15 | Core module exploring branding, communication and strategic design within organisational contexts |

### Summer (60 credits)

| Module | Credits | Description |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| Dissertation | 60 | An in-depth research project drawing on design, branding and organisational theories to develop analytical and research skills |

## Teaching & Assessment

### How You'll Study

- Lectures
- Seminars
- Tutorials
- Independent study
- Group work

As well as regular timetabled teaching, students have the chance to take part in guest lectures and projects on a range of topics.

### How You'll Be Assessed

You can expect to complete essays and reports of varying lengths, as well as presentations, projects and exams.

## Your Future Career

This programme prepares you for careers in design, branding, marketing, strategy and innovation. Graduates will be equipped to work as brand strategists, design managers, creative directors and innovation consultants within agencies, consultancies and organisations across a wide range of sectors.
