# Graphic Design and Visualisation MA/MSc

## Programme Overview

Our Graphic Design and Visualisation Masters offers an innovative and creative approach to graphic design, combining practice-based work with critical thinking and research. The programme is designed for students who want to develop advanced skills in graphic design, visualisation, and related creative fields.

You will explore new and emerging technologies, develop your creative practice, and build a strong understanding of design research methods. The programme offers both **MA and MSc pathways**, allowing you to tailor your degree to your career aspirations — the MA focuses on practical creative outcomes, while the MSc emphasises technical and research skills.

## Why Choose This Programme?

- **2nd in the UK** for Graphic Design (Guardian University Guide 2026)
- **Top 4 in the UK** for Art and Design (Complete University Guide 2026)
- **Top 50 in the World** for Art and Design (QS World University Rankings by Subject 2025)
- Access to state-of-the-art design studios and digital fabrication facilities
- Dual MA/MSc pathways for career flexibility
- Strong industry connections and professional practice focus

## Entry Requirements

- **Academic:** A 2:1 honours degree (or equivalent international qualification) in Art and Design or a closely related subject.
- **English Language:** IELTS 6.5 overall with 6.0 in each component.

## Programme Structure

The programme consists of **180 credits** across two semesters and a summer project period.

### Semester 1 (60 credits — Compulsory)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Modelling and Deployment | 15 |
| Design Teams | 15 |
| Research in a User's World | 15 |
| Design Research Methods | 15 |

### Semester 2 (60 credits — Compulsory)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Design Challenges: Management and Entrepreneurship | 15 |
| International Design Practices | 15 |
| Defining a Specialist Project | 15 |
| Specialist Project Materials and Processes | 15 |

### Summer (60 credits — Compulsory, choose one)

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|
| Final Project: Practical Perspectives | 60 |
| Final Project: Theoretical Perspectives | 60 |

## Teaching & Assessment

The programme features a variety of teaching and learning methods including lectures, seminars, workshops, studio-based practice, group critiques, and individual tutorials. Assessment is continuous and based on an appraisal of practice, written material, related research, and professional and entrepreneurial skills.

The universal demand for graphical knowledge and increasing emphasis on visualising complexity across diverse fields provides a vast range of career choices for graduates:

- Photography & Art Direction
- Graphic Design & Publishing
- Illustration & Comic Illustration
- Video Production, Television & Film
- Branding & Marketing
- Exhibition & Display Design
- App Design & UX
- PhD Study & Academic Research
- Freelance Design Practice
