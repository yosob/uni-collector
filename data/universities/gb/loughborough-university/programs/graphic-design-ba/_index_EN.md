curriculum_summary: 'Year 1 (All Compulsory — 8 modules):

  - Creative Production — Core digital competencies for visual outputs; industry-standard
  digital production skills

  - Graphic Design Context — Core ideas and concepts of graphic design within critical,
  historical, and theoretical context; creative design processes emphasising research,
  iterative development, experimentation, and criticality

  - Type and Language — Practical, critical, historical, and theoretical introduction
  to typography and writing systems

  - Visual Thinking — Applied visual methods and processes for creative enquiries;
  digital and analogue image-making techniques

  - Writing for Practice — How writing frames art and design practices; academic writing
  and argumentation

  - Visual Practices — Applied image-making methods and procedures; articulating concepts
  across contexts and media

  - Branding and Strategy — Branding and strategy design methods; storytelling, tone
  of voice, brand personality; graphic systems for visual outputs

  - Applied Storytelling for Sustainability — Narrative arc as a creative device;
  participatory storytelling techniques; video filming, editing, and post-production


  Year 2 (6 Compulsory + Optional):

  Compulsory:

  - Social Design — Practical and theoretical introduction to social design; responsible
  approaches to graphic design practice

  - Visualisation — Data visualisation, knowledge visualisation, network visualisation,
  system mapping, and information graphics

  - Narrative and Sequence — How graphic design utilises narrative and sequence; technical,
  practical, and conceptual skills

  - Collaboration — Collaborative and participatory design processes; co-design, stakeholder
  engagement, and consultation

  - Audience and Environment — Targeted understanding of audiences and environments;
  analytical design methods; unbounded design briefs

  - Design Research Methods — Variety of design research methods

  Optional (sample):

  - Digital Moving Image

  - Branding and Identity

  - Typography in Use

  - Arts Management

  - Responsible Practice: Making your Manifesto

  - Creative Dissent: Protest, Activism and Art

  - Creative Placemaking

  - Drawing Characters: Representation and Identity

  - Story Design for Creative Industries

  - Fashion to Function: Designing Clothing and Wearable Products

  - The Ethics and Aesthetics of Generative AI in Design

  - Phantom Threads: Fashion, Costume and Culture in Film


  Final Year (2 Compulsory + Optional):

  Compulsory:

  - Negotiated Project — High-level graphic design practice in a subject specialism;
  independent, creative, intellectual, and professional enquiry

  - Major Project — Substantial body of expert practice; professionally presented
  portfolio ready for exhibition

  Optional:

  - Dissertation — Independent research on a topic relevant to creative practice

  - Industrial Report — Industry-focused research in an area related to creative practice
