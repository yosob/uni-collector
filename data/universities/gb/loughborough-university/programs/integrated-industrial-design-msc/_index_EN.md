# Integrated Industrial Design MSc

## Programme Overview

Industrial Design is the professional practice of designing products, devices, objects and services used by millions of people around the world every day. Industrial designers traditionally focus on the physical appearance, functionality, and manufacturability of a product, but they are now involved in far more during a development cycle, extending to the overall lasting value and experience a product or service provides for end-users.

Our MSc Integrated Industrial Design degree will further develop your critical awareness of major industrial design practice, increasing input capability and value to employers. The programme encompasses the entire design process, from a design problem or opportunity through to a complete product that is fit for mass or batch production.

It is suitable for both **recent graduates** and **current practitioners** who are looking to advance their core design knowledge and specialist skills to a higher level.

## Entry Requirements

- **Academic:** A 2:1 honours degree (or equivalent international qualification) in Industrial Design, Product Design, Engineering Design or closely related subject.
- **Portfolio:** Evidence of experiential learning and/or practice, with clear evidence of physical prototyping capability at an appropriate standard is required.
- **English Language:** IELTS 6.5 overall with 6.0 in each component.

## Programme Structure

The programme consists of **180 credits** across two semesters and a summer project period.

| Module | Credits | Type |

| Module | Credits |
|--------|---------|

|--------|---------|

## Teaching & Assessment

Teaching and learning is conducted using a combination of lectures, seminars, tutorials, independent study, workshops, and practical sessions. Assessment is through coursework only, including design projects, reports, group and individual presentations — there are **no written examinations**.

## Career Prospects

Graduates are equipped for a wide range of careers in industrial design and product development:

- Industrial Designer / Product Designer
- Design Consultant / Design Manager
- New Product Development Manager
- Design Engineer / CAD Specialist
- Creative Director / Innovation Consultant
- UX Designer / Design Researcher
- Entrepreneur / Freelance Designer
- PhD Study & Academic Research

The IED accreditation provides a pathway to professional registration, further enhancing career prospects.

## Facilities

The programme is based in a purpose-built **£21 million design building** featuring state-of-the-art workshops, laboratories, design studios, computer suites, prototyping facilities, and project display areas. Students have access to industry-standard equipment including 3D printers, CNC machines, laser cutters, and advanced CAD software.
