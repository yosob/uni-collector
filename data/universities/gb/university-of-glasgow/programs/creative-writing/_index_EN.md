overview: The Creative Writing MLitt at Glasgow is designed for those already engaged
  in writing. The programme's clear three-part structure — focused on creative, critical
  and practical issues — distinguishes it from others offered in the UK. The programme
  has an excellent reputation amongst writers, agents and publishers, and has helped
  launch the careers of successful writers including Anne Donovan, Louise Welsh and
  Rodge Glass. Students benefit from guest speakers including authors, poets, journalists,
  publishers, editors, literary judges and playwrights, strong links with literary
  agents and publishers, and Glasgow's vibrant literary culture as the UK's top cultural
  and creative city.
admission_requirements: Applicants will normally have a 2.1 Honours degree (or equivalent),
  though this is not a pre-requisite. The primary basis for admission is the appraisal
  of a portfolio of creative work. A maximum of 20 pages (one side only, double spaced
  throughout) per submission will be considered, containing prose, verse, script,
  or a combination. One reference letter is also required, ideally from an academic
  or creative referee.
