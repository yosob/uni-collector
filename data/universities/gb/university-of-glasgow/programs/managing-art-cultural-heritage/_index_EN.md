overview: The Erasmus Mundus International Masters in Managing Art & Cultural Heritage
  in Global Markets (MAGMa) explores contemporary issues affecting the market for
  art and cultural heritage utilising historical, theoretical and experiential models,
  with the primary aim of better equipping the art and cultural heritage professional
  with the skills necessary to work within a highly complex and growing field worldwide.
  A consortium of five European universities — University of Glasgow, Institut d'Etudes
  Supérieures des Arts (IESA) in Paris, School of Arts and Humanities of the Universidade
  de Lisboa and University Institute of Lisbon (ISCTE), and Erasmus University Rotterdam
  — delivers a unique multidisciplinary and cohesive approach combining theory with
  practice, enabling an integrated approach to understanding the social, economic,
  legal and cultural issues affecting the art and cultural heritage sectors.
admission_requirements: A 2.1 Honours degree or non-UK equivalent in History, History

  Museum and Cultural Heritage Studies, Social and Economic History, Cultural Management,
  Conservation, Archaeology, Anthropology, Architecture, Classics, Law, Arts and Cultural
  Tourism, Film and Cinema Studies, or Journalism. Qualifications below 2:1 considered
  if supported by substantial experience in Arts or Heritage fields.

  - Management Foundations

  Online (University of Glasgow): Interdisciplinary Methodologies in Art & Cultural

  - Collecting and Heritage Histories

  - Arts Law & Digitalisation OR Museum and Heritage Law & CSR & Cultural Heritage

  Online (University of Glasgow): Art & Cultural Heritage in a Globalised World


  Summer: Optional Summer School


  Semester 3 (Sep–Jan) — Erasmus University Rotterdam: Two Study Tracks — Economics
  of Heritage OR Tourism & Heritage

  - Cultural Economics Perspective and Research Workshop

  Online (University of Glasgow): Provenance and Restitution
