overview: The MLitt Curatorial Practice (Contemporary Art) is a one-year taught postgraduate
  programme established in 2014 and jointly run by The Glasgow School of Art and the
  University of Glasgow, with the degree awarded by the University of Glasgow. The
  programme is aimed at graduates wishing to develop a curatorial career within the
  field of contemporary visual arts. It offers students the opportunity to study curatorial
  practice in Glasgow, a city with one of the most vibrant and distinctive international
  art scenes in Europe. The programme is embedded within the city, drawing on its
  community of artists, curators, institutions and international connections. Throughout
  the year students are supported to identify and undertake individually motivated
  curatorial projects and research as the basis for the development of a self-sustaining
  practice. Students work closely with artists and a range of organisations, consolidating
  and articulating their practice within an international context of theory and practice.
  There is a balance between theoretical exploration and critique, where writing and
  concept-formation are challenged and developed by practical application and experimentation.
  Students have the opportunity to work both individually and collaboratively on projects
  that extend beyond exhibition-as-form, to include performance-based work, publications,
  digital platforms and other discursive modes of curation.
admission_requirements: Normally a UK Bachelors honours degree (or its international
  equivalent) in a relevant or related subject that gives appropriate grounding for
  postgraduate study. Applicants who do not meet the standard academic entry requirements
  may still be considered on the basis of their portfolio and/or relevant professional
  experience. Applicants must submit a personal statement of no more than 500 words
  reflecting on why they are interested in pursuing a career in curating, their key
  areas of interest, and why this programme is suited to their future development.
  An interview is also required. The programme seeks self-motivated students who are
  determined critical thinkers and professionally orientated.

curriculum_summary: 'Semester 1 (60 credits):

  - Curatorial Practice 1 (40 credits, core) — independent and self-directed curatorial
  exploration with flexible structure responding to programming and exhibition opportunities

  - Research Methods and Methodologies in Practice (20 credits, core) — delivered
  by The Glasgow School of Art, shared research methods course


  Semester 2 (60 credits):

  - Curatorial Practice 2 (40 credits, core) — continuation of curatorial practice
  with live project opportunities and placement possibilities

  - Optional course (20 credits) — choose from a range of postgraduate electives across
  The Glasgow School of Art and the College of Arts & Humanities, University of Glasgow


  Semester 3 (60 credits):

  Select one of three routes:

  - Master''s Project in Curatorial Practice 1 (60 credits) — 100% curatorial project

  - Master''s Project in Curatorial Practice 2 (60 credits) — 50% curatorial project
  and 50% extended essay

  - Dissertation (60 credits) — 100% written dissertation


  Total: 180 SCQF credits. Assessment through peer and staff review, essays, critical
  reviews and practice-based coursework with continuous tutorial feedback.
