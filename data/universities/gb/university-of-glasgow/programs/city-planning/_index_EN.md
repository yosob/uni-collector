## Programme Overview

The Masters in City Planning will help you develop a critical understanding of cities. You will explore the positive impact that evidence-based and innovative decision-making can have on human settlements. Cities lie at the heart of the many challenges human societies face today, including climate change, infrastructure delivery, affordable housing, and social inequality. These challenges ensure that professional city planners around the world are in high demand. You will be equipped with the knowledge and skills you need to enter the planning profession and become sustainable place makers of the future.

## Entry Requirements

- **Academic**: 2.1 Honours degree (or non-UK equivalent) in a relevant Social Science or Humanities subject such as Urban/City/Regional Planning, Geography, Architecture, Landscape Architecture, Land Economy, Land Resource Management, Real Estate, Environmental Design, Public Policy, Law, Philosophy, Politics, History, Art History, Economics, Sociology. Applicants with a 2.2 Honours degree may sometimes be considered.
- **English Language**: IELTS 6.5 overall with no subtest less than 6.5, or equivalent (TOEFL iBT 93-94 overall, PTE Academic 65 overall, Cambridge CPE/CAE 176 overall)
- **Work Experience**: Relevant work experience may also be considered. Please upload a CV.
- **Personal Statement**: Required if qualification is below 2:1 or in a non-listed subject.

## Curriculum

**Core Courses — Part 1: Principles:**
- Economics of Planning and Development Lab (URBAN5037)
- Integrative City Planning Lab (URBAN5156)
- Planning Systems and Procedures (URBAN5157)
- Planning Theory and Values (URBAN5158)

**Core Courses — Part 2: Practice:**
- Applied Built Environment Research (URBAN5155)
- Designing Resilient Places (URBAN5010)
- Sustainable Spatial Strategies Lab (URBAN5046)

**Part 3: Project:**
- Real Estate and Planning Dissertation (URBAN5054P) OR Real Estate and Planning Major Research Project (URBAN5150P)

**Specialist Pathways (choose one):**
- Housing Pathway — Housing, Inequality and Society; International Housing Policy; Sustainable Housing Development
- Place & Design Pathway — Community Empowerment and Engagement; Place Adaptation and Green Infrastructure; Urban Conservation
- International Pathway — Global Urban Challenges; Migration Policy Making
- Transport Pathway — Fundamentals of Transport Studies (core); Place Adaptation and Green Infrastructure; Principles and Applications of GIS
- Energy & the Environment Pathway — Place Adaptation and Green Infrastructure; Sustainable Energy Landscapes
- Digital Cities Pathway — Big Data, AI & Urban Analytics; Digital Planning Systems and Tools; Principles and Applications of GIS; Smart Cities and Infrastructure

**Teaching methods**: Lectures, workshops, seminars, field visits, walking tours, site visits, optional field trip to London.
