## Programme Overview

The Masters in City Planning & Real Estate Development will prepare you for working either as a planner or real estate surveyor. You will develop an in-depth appreciation of how different stakeholders in the planning and real estate sectors make decisions about future places. Cities lie at the heart of the many challenges human societies face today, including climate change, infrastructure delivery, affordable housing, and social inequality. These challenges ensure that the professions of city planning and real estate development are in high demand around the world.

This unique degree programme brings together the disciplines of city planning and real estate development in a concentrated year of study. The philosophy of the programme is to break down the professional barriers between two built environment disciplines that make better place-based decisions when they work together. The skills and knowledge this degree delivers are crucial in our rapidly changing world, particularly in the context of rapid urbanisation and climate change.

## Why This Programme

- Dual accredited by the Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors (RICS) and the Royal Town Planning Institute (RTPI), significantly enhancing employment prospects.
- Teaching is strongly engaged in policy and practice, with close links to the Scottish Government, local authorities and other agencies.
- Rooted in place-based learning through tackling complex urban sites across Greater Glasgow — an exciting city to study planning and real estate.
- Opportunity to participate in an optional field trip to London (past visits include The Shard, King's Cross, Battersea Power Station and grassroots community projects).
- Underpinned by two substantial research centres: the Centre for Housing Evidence (CaCHE) and the Urban Big Data Centre (UBDC).

## Career Prospects

Chartered town planner, chartered surveyor, or both. Graduates work in major international planning and real estate consultancies (ARUP, WSP, JLL, Savills), local and national government (Glasgow City Council, Scottish Government), third sector and community development organisations, urban regeneration, housing policy, transport planning, property development and investment. Dual RICS and RTPI accreditation provides a pathway to chartered membership of both professional bodies.
