## Education in Museums & Heritage (Erasmus Mundus International Master) IntM

### Quick Reference

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Degree** | IntM (International Master) |
| **Duration** | Full-time: 24 months |
| **Start** | September |
| **Campus** | Glasgow: Gilmorehill campus + International (multiple European locations) |
| **College** | College of Social Sciences (School of Education) |
| **Credits** | 120 ECTS |
| **Apply** | University of Glasgow Online Application |

### Tuition (2026-27)

| Fee Category | Amount (per year) |
|-------------|-------------------|
| UK/EU & Programme Countries | £8,640 / €10,167 |
| All Other Countries | £18,480 / €21,746 |

> Deposit: £2,000 required upon offer. Students cover their own travel between partner institutions.

### Entry Requirements

- **Academic**: 2.1 Honours degree or equivalent (e.g. GPA 75%) in a relevant arts, humanities or social science subject
- **Portfolio**: Not required
- **English Language**: IELTS 6.5 (no subtest below 6.0), TOEFL iBT 90, or PTE Academic 60

### Programme Structure

An Erasmus Mundus Joint Master spanning 4 semesters across multiple European universities:

- **Semester 1 (Glasgow)**: Museum Education — Access & Inclusion, Critical Enquiry, Museums Education & Curriculum Development, The Museum as a Source of Learning
- **Semester 2**: Choose University of Tartu (Intangible Cultural Heritage) or University College Cork (Digital Cultures & Humanities)
- **Summer**: Optional non-credit summer school (European Museum Academy or Mexico)
- **Semester 3**: Choose University of Malta (Museum & Heritage Education) or Radboud University (Arts & Culture)
- **Semester 4**: Research Dissertation at chosen partner university

### Programme Highlights

- Erasmus Mundus Joint Master funded by the European Commission
- Study at 3-4 universities across Europe over 2 years
- Consortium: Glasgow, Tartu, Radboud, Malta, Cork
- Optional summer school with the European Museum Academy or in Mexico
- Language learning and museum placements included
- Education at Glasgow ranked 6th in the UK (CUG 2025)

### Career Prospects

Graduates will be equipped with academic knowledge, research skills and practical employability skills through this jointly developed and delivered programme. Career opportunities exist across the cultural heritage sector — museums, galleries, historic houses, heritage sites and consultancy — in roles ranging from education and outreach to collections management, curation, marketing and management.
