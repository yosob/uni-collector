### Entry Requirements

- **Academic**: 2:1 Honours degree or non-UK equivalent in any subject
- **Chemistry**: GCSE/Higher/international equivalent required (or pass in 'Chemistry for Conservators' distance-learning course)
- **Portfolio**: Required at interview stage — must demonstrate excellent hand-stitching skills, attention to detail and high standard of finish
- **Personal Statement**: Approximately 500 words — must demonstrate understanding of and interest in textile conservation as a profession
- **English Language**: IELTS 6.5 (no sub-test less than 6.0)
- **Interview**: Applicants meeting requirements will be invited to interview
- **Experience**: Previous conservation or museum experience desirable but not essential

### Programme Structure

Two-year programme with integrated practical training:
- **Year 1**: Core skills + developing practical conservation skills + preventive conservation + textile technology + summer placement
- **Year 2**: Advanced skills + conservation projects + research dissertation

Combines hands-on conservation practice with theoretical study, a professional placement, and a research dissertation.

### Career Prospects

The great majority of graduates work in museums and cultural institutions worldwide. Graduates work in nearly 40 countries and hold senior positions internationally. Notable placement institutions include the British Museum, National Museum Scotland, Historic Royal Palaces, the Smithsonian, and the Abegg-Stiftung.
