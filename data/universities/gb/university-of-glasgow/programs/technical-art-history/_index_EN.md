### Entry Requirements

- **Academic**: 2:1 Honours degree or non-UK equivalent in Art History, Fine Art, Conservation, or related discipline
- **Portfolio**: Not required
- **English Language**: IELTS 6.5 (no sub-test less than 6.0)

### Programme Structure

An interdisciplinary programme combining art history, science and conservation:
- **5 core courses** + **work placement** + **dissertation (object-based research)**
- Semester 1: Research Methods and Skills, Art in the Making: Historical Techniques, Art in the Making: Modern & Avant-Garde Techniques
- Semester 2: The Authentic Art Work, Testimonies On Artists' Practice, Work Placement
- Summer: Dissertation research and writing

### Career Prospects

The programme prepares graduates for careers in cultural heritage organisations, technical art history, curation, collections care, art authentication, and academic research. It also provides excellent preparation for further postgraduate study in conservation.
