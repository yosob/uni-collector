### Entry Requirements

- **Academic**: 2.1 Honours degree (or non-UK equivalent) in any subject
- **Alternative**: 2.2 Honours degree considered with relevant experience; substantial directly relevant experience may replace formal qualifications
- **Portfolio**: Not required
- **English Language**: IELTS 6.5 overall (no sub-test less than 6.0), TOEFL iBT 90, or PTE Academic 65

### Programme Structure

The programme combines academic study with practical professional skills:

- **Semester 1**: 3 core courses — Introduction to Museology, Museum Skills and Professional Practice, Managing and Using Collections (60 credits)
- **Semester 2**: Research Design and Methods (core) + optional courses including Exhibition Development (The Hunterian), Work Placement, and Digital Cultural Heritage (60 credits)
- **Summer (MSc)**: Dissertation or Applied Dissertation (work-based project, 60 credits)
- PgDip students complete 120 credits without the dissertation

### Why This Programme

- Only CILIP-accredited Museum Studies MSc in the UK
- Co-taught with heritage professionals from The Hunterian, Glasgow Museums, and the National Library of Scotland
- Privileged access to Glasgow's world-class museum collections
- Optional work placement in a museum or heritage organisation
- Applied Dissertation option for practice-based research

### Career Prospects

Graduates pursue careers across the museum and heritage sector — in museums, galleries, historic houses, heritage sites, and consultancy. Roles span front-of-house, learning and engagement, digital engagement, collections management, curation, marketing, and management.
