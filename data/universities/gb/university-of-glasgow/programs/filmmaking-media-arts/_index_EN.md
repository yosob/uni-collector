## Programme Overview

The Masters focuses on low-budget and experimental filmmaking and media art. It is designed to provide the necessary practical and critical skills required for current and 'future' screen-based production. This programme crosses between practice and theory of film, moving image and new media arts, and provides an open space for experimentation, while advancing skills and creative practice.

## Focus Areas

Filmmaking, Media Arts, Experimental Film, Moving Image, Documentary, New Media Production

## Entry Requirements

- **Academic**: 2.1 Honours degree (or non-UK equivalent) in Film and TV Studies, New Media Arts, Visual Arts or another relevant subject, with at least 30% of credit-bearing modules in theory or practice oriented modules in Filmmaking and Media Arts at an average grade of pass.
- **English Language**: IELTS 6.5 (no subtest less than 6.0) or equivalent
- **Portfolio**: Applicants should demonstrate relevant creative practice experience in filmmaking or media arts.

## Curriculum

**Semester 1 (Core):**
- Practical Workshop 1: Project Preparation (FTV5048)
- Experimental Art and Media (FTV5049)

**Semester 2 (Core + Optional):**
- Practical Workshop 2: Pilot Project (FTV5047)
- One optional course from: Advanced Topics in Film Studies, Advanced Topics in Television Studies, Festivals, Genders, Making Time

**Summer (Compulsory):**
- Practice Led Dissertation (FTV5046P): Self-directed film or media arts project with 3,000-word critical explanation

**Teaching methods**: Lectures, practical workshops, seminar discussions, screenings, one-on-one tutorials.
