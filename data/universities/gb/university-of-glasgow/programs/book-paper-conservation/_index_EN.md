### Entry Requirements

- **Academic**: 2:1 Honours degree or non-UK equivalent in any subject
- **Chemistry**: GCSE/Higher/international equivalent required (or pass in 'Chemistry for Conservators' distance-learning course)
- **Portfolio**: Required at interview stage — must demonstrate attention to detail and quality of finish
- **Personal Statement**: Required — must indicate chosen specialism (Book & Archival Materials Conservation or Paper Conservation)
- **English Language**: IELTS 6.5 (no sub-test less than 6.0)
- **Interview**: Applicants meeting requirements will be invited to interview

### Programme Structure

Two-year programme with two specialist tracks:
- **Year 1**: Core skills + developing practical conservation skills + preventive conservation + placement
- **Year 2**: Advanced skills + conservation projects + research dissertation (15,000 words)

The programme combines practical workshops throughout both years with theoretical courses, a professional placement, and a research dissertation.

### Career Prospects

Graduates pursue careers as practising specialist conservators, cultural heritage researchers, and in collections management, archives, and arts administration roles.
