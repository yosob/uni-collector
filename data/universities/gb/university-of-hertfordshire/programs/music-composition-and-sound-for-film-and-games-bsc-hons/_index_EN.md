# Music Composition and Sound for Film and Games BSc (Hons)

## Overview

BSc (Hons) Music Composition and Sound for Film and Games is one of five courses in the Undergraduate Music Programme at the University of Hertfordshire. The degree provides an insight into the important role the soundtrack plays in shaping narrative across film, computer games and interactive media. Merging both technology and creativity, this course embraces a forward-thinking approach with a strong emphasis on preparing students for the demands of the modern film and game workplace. Students collaborate extensively within the School of Creative Arts, connecting with peers in the fields of Film, Digital Animation and Game Design, enriching skills in composing, producing and implementing all layers of the soundtrack.

- **UCAS Code:** W392
- **Duration:** 3 years (4 years with sandwich year)
- **Study Abroad Option:** Yes
- **Work Placement Option:** Yes
- **Campus:** College Lane

## Entry Requirements

112–120 UCAS points from at least 2 A-levels (or equivalent qualifications such as BTEC Extended Diploma, International Baccalaureate, or Access to HE Diploma). GCSE Mathematics and English Language at grade 4/C or above (or equivalent). Applicants with relevant professional experience may also be considered.

## English Language Requirements

- IELTS 6.0 (with no less than 5.5 in any band) or equivalent
