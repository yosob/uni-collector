## Course Overview

You'll gain two vital media skills by combining journalism and media. This exciting course will develop your practical, professional writing skills, shed new light on how the media works and get you some hands-on experience that employers are really looking for.

## Year 1

You'll learn the basics of the journalism industry. You'll explore factual storytelling, through written copy, blogs, podcasting and video. You'll learn about journalism ethics, the relationship between journalism and government, and how the media industry works. You'll learn how to communicate to audiences in the globalised media.

## Year 2

You'll build on your first year by expanding your skillset. You'll learn about broadcasting, as well as creating a magazine concept. You'll also learn about journalism history, news reporting, branding, and magazine design. You'll explore the publishing industry too, while creating a short video documentary.

## Year 3 (Final Year)

By your final year, you'll have developed your knowledge of international reporting and will create your own website. You'll also learn about work experience, freelancing and media business. You'll study writing for different audiences, as well as PR, advertising, and media and identity. You'll explore celebrity and media, a media project for a client, and designing for the web.

## Professional Accreditations

The course is accredited by PRCA (Public Relations Consultants Association) and BSME (British Society of Magazine Editors), ensuring industry recognition and professional standards.

## Career Opportunities

Our graduates have an excellent record of gaining employment in media, publishing, teaching and information management where their skills in analysis, communication and evaluation are highly valued. Many of our graduates work in journalism, PR, advertising, and digital content creation.
