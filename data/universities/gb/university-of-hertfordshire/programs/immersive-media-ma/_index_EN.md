# Immersive Media MA

## Overview

The MA Immersive Media course enables you to explore the creation of immersive content for extended reality (XR), virtual reality (VR), augmented reality (AR), and mixed reality platforms. You will develop advanced creative and technical skills to design and produce interactive, immersive experiences that push the boundaries of storytelling and audience engagement.

The programme is designed for creative practitioners who want to work at the cutting edge of immersive technology. You will explore the full pipeline of immersive content creation, from conceptualisation and design through to real-time implementation and user experience testing. The course covers 360-degree video, spatial audio, interactive narrative, virtual production, and real-time 3D environments.

The course connects creative practice with critical media theory, enabling you to understand the cultural and social implications of immersive technologies while developing your artistic vision. You will work with industry-standard tools including game engines (Unreal Engine, Unity), 3D modelling software, and dedicated VR/AR development platforms.

Students also have the opportunity for unique collaboration with students on other pathways, working with games artists, filmmakers, creative AI practitioners, and digital media artists.

- **Study Mode:** Full Time / Part Time
- **Duration:** 1 year (full time) / 2 years (part time)
- **Campus:** College Lane

## Entry Requirements

A degree, ideally in a relevant subject (2.2 minimum), plus a portfolio demonstrating recent engagement and creative activities in your intended study area. Accreditation of professional experience may be possible for practitioners without a first degree.

## English Language Requirements

- IELTS 6.5 (with no less than 5.5 in any band) or equivalent

## Portfolio Requirements

A portfolio demonstrating recent engagement and creative activities in the intended study area of immersive media or a related creative discipline. The portfolio should evidence creative practice, technical proficiency with digital tools, and engagement with interactive or immersive content creation.
