# Fashion and Fashion Business BA (Hons)

## Course Overview

This BA (Hons) Fashion and Fashion Business course merges creative innovation with business strategy, perfect for aspiring entrepreneurs in fashion. As one of the few fashion courses in the UK to blend creative design with strategic business expertise, you'll immerse yourself in professionally equipped studios. You'll learn about design, product development, and the fundamentals of running a fashion business — from buying and merchandising to e-commerce, digital strategies, sales, branding and promotion.

In recent years, global fashion brands including Adidas, Puma, Ellesse, F&F, Ted Baker, Maria Grachvogel, and many others have chosen to work with the course, offering invaluable insight into the real-world of fashion business. The programme has a history of training award-winning graduates who are highly sought-after by industry, with alumni going on to work for Acne, Superdry, UGG, ASOS, Liberty, and many more.

**Key highlights:**
- 9th in the UK for art (Daily Mail University Guide, 2025)
- Over 90% of undergraduates are in work/study (Graduate Outcomes, 2025)
- Member of the British Fashion Colleges Council
- Located just 20 minutes from London, one of the world's fashion capitals
- Annual participation in Graduate Fashion Week in London
- Access to global competitions, live industry projects, and work placements

## Curriculum

### Level 4 (Year 1)

| Module | Credits | Type |
|--------|---------|------|
| Principles of Global Fashion Design (L4) | 30 | Compulsory |
| Fashion: Critical Contexts (L4) | 30 | Compulsory |
| Global Fashion Business Principles (L4) | 30 | Compulsory |
| Fashion Design Skills and Technology (L4) | 30 | Compulsory |

**Year 1 focus:** Core fashion design and business skills, hand rendering and technical drawings, 2D and 3D visualisation, fabrics and materials, global supply chains, business strategy, research and analysis, report writing, design proposals, presentation and portfolio skills.

### Level 5 (Year 2)

| Module | Credits | Type |
|--------|---------|------|
| Strategic Sales, Marketing and E-Comm (L5) | 30 | Compulsory |
| Buying and Merchandising (L5) | 15 | Compulsory |
| Advanced Fashion Product Development (L5) | 15 | Compulsory |
| Concept to Customer for Global Markets (L5) | 30 | Compulsory |
| Fashion Critical Contexts (L5) | 30 | Compulsory |

**Year 2 focus:** Product development and creativity, live assignments from industry partners, portfolio development, customer psychology and buying behaviour, case study of a global fashion company, work placement opportunities.

### Level 6 (Year 3)

| Module | Credits | Type |
|--------|---------|------|
| Fashion Business Case Study (Major Project) (L6) | 60 | Compulsory |
| Design Culture Inquiry (L6) | 30 | Compulsory |
| Fashion Branding and Professional Portfolio (L6) | 30 | Compulsory |

**Year 3 focus:** Independent business case study based on dream career, company strategy analysis, design culture essay, fashion branding, visual identity development, professional portfolio for Graduate Fashion Week.

## Entry Requirements

- **UCAS Points:** 112–120
- **A Level:** BBC–BBB (preferably including Art & Design or a creative subject)
- **BTEC Extended Diploma:** DMM–DDM (Art & Design or related)
- **GCSE:** Grade 4/C in English Language and Mathematics
- **Access to HE Diploma:** Pass with 45 credits at Level 3, including 24 at Merit or above
- **Interview:** Required (all suitable applicants are invited to interview)
- Applicants with relevant professional experience or a strong portfolio may also be considered

## English Language Requirements

- **IELTS:** 6.0 overall with no less than 5.5 in any band
- Equivalent qualifications accepted (TOEFL iBT, PTE Academic, etc.)

## Portfolio Requirements

Portfolio of creative work required for interview. Should demonstrate:
- Visual thinking and design process
- Fashion illustration and drawing skills
- Creative flair and originality
- Examples of sketchbooks, development work, and finished pieces
- Work across a range of media and techniques

Portfolio advice: [Undergraduate Art and Design Portfolio Advice](https://www.herts.ac.uk/courses/undergraduate-courses/art-design-and-fashion/undergraduate-art-and-design-applicant/undergraduate-art-and-design-portfolio-advice)
