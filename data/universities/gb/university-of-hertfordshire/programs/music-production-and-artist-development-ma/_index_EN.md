# Music Production and Artist Development MA

## Overview

The MA Music Production and Artist Development course enables you to study music production at an advanced level while developing your artistic identity and professional practice. You will explore the creative, technical, and business dimensions of music production, developing skills in recording, mixing, mastering, songwriting, and artist development.

The programme is designed for musicians, producers, and creative practitioners who want to advance their music production skills while building a sustainable artistic career. You will work in professional-standard recording studios and production facilities, developing expertise in a range of genres and production styles. The course encourages creative experimentation while building a strong foundation in music theory, audio engineering, and music business practice.

The course connects creative practice with critical media theory, enabling you to understand the broader cultural, technological, and economic contexts of contemporary music production. You will explore topics including music technology innovation, digital distribution, live performance, and the creative industries landscape.

Students also have the opportunity for unique collaboration with students on other pathways, working with songwriters, audio engineers, and digital media artists.

- **Study Mode:** Full Time / Part Time
- **Duration:** 1 year (full time) / 2 years (part time)
- **Campus:** College Lane

## Entry Requirements

A degree, ideally in a relevant subject (2.2 minimum), plus a portfolio demonstrating recent engagement and creative activities in your intended study area. Accreditation of professional experience may be possible for practitioners without a first degree.

## English Language Requirements

- IELTS 6.5 (with no less than 5.5 in any band) or equivalent

## Portfolio Requirements

A portfolio demonstrating recent engagement and creative activities in the intended study area of music production. The portfolio should evidence music production practice, songwriting or composition ability, and technical proficiency with music production tools (such as Logic Pro, Ableton Live, Pro Tools, or equivalent).
