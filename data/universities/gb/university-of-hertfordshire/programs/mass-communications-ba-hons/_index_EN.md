# Mass Communications BA (Hons)

## Course Overview

This BA (Hons) Mass Communications course combines media theory with practical skills, preparing you for a career in the fast-moving media industry. You'll explore how media shapes culture, society, and politics while developing professional skills in content creation, journalism, film, and digital media.

The programme is accredited by the PRCA (Public Relations and Communications Association), ensuring your degree is recognised by industry professionals. With optional work placement and study abroad opportunities, you'll gain real-world experience that sets you apart in the graduate job market.

**Key highlights:**
- Accredited by PRCA (Public Relations and Communications Association)
- Optional sandwich year for industry work placement
- Study abroad opportunity at partner universities worldwide
- Strong focus on practical media production alongside critical theory
- Access to professional-standard media facilities at College Lane campus
- Located just 25 minutes from London, a global media hub

## Curriculum

### Level 4 (Year 1)

| Module | Credits | Type |
|--------|---------|------|
| Media Communications and Digital Contexts | 30 | Compulsory |
| Media Storytelling Skills | 30 | Compulsory |
| Film Studies – Theory into Practice | 30 | Compulsory |

**Optional modules (choose 30 credits from):**
- Creativity, Originality and GenAI in the Media Industry (30 Credits)
- Journalism, Media and Society (30 Credits)
- Books That Changed the World (30 Credits)
- Writing for Stage and Screen (30 Credits)
- Language modules (French, German, Spanish, Japanese, Mandarin, Korean)

**Year 1 focus:** Understanding media landscapes, digital contexts, storytelling fundamentals, film theory, and building a foundation in media analysis and production.

### Level 5 (Year 2)

| Module | Credits | Type |
|--------|---------|------|
| Research Methods and Critical Practice in Media Communication | 30 | Compulsory |
| Media Brands – Layouts and Identities | 30 | Compulsory |
| Contemporary Film and TV: US and UK | 30 | Compulsory |

**Optional modules (choose 30 credits from):**
- Documentary Film and Podcasts (30 Credits)
- Multiplatform Journalism (30 Credits)
- Texts and Screens: Literature, Film and TV Adaptation (30 Credits)
- Professional Writing (30 Credits)
- Language modules at Stage 2

**Year 2 focus:** Advanced media research, brand identity, contemporary film analysis, documentary production, journalism, and professional writing.

### Level 6 (Year 3)

| Module | Credits | Type |
|--------|---------|------|
| Mass Communications Major Project | 30 | Compulsory |
| Media Enterprise and Innovation | 30 | Compulsory |
| Digital Media and Creative Enterprise | 30 | Compulsory |

**Year 3 focus:** Independent major project, media entrepreneurship, digital creative enterprise, portfolio development, and professional readiness for the media industry.

## Entry Requirements

- **UCAS Points:** 112–120
- **A Level:** BBC–BBB
- **BTEC Extended Diploma:** DMM–DDM
- **GCSE:** Grade 4/C in English Language and Mathematics
- **Access to HE Diploma:** Pass with 45 credits at Level 3, including 24 at Merit or above
- Applicants with relevant professional experience may also be considered

## English Language Requirements

- **IELTS:** 6.0 overall with no less than 5.5 in any band
- Equivalent qualifications accepted (TOEFL iBT, PTE Academic, etc.)
