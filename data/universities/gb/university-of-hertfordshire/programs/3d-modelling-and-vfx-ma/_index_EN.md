# 3D Modelling and VFX MA

## Overview

The MA 3D Modelling and VFX course enables students to study a modular structure that has produced professionals across the screen industries. Students can enter or progress in 3D modelling, animation and VFX industries, focusing on the forefront of developments in technology while exploring a range of processes, matched with space and tuition to accelerate their practice and career.

More and more of what we see on our cinema and TV screens — in films, episodic TV, commercials and even historical drama — is planned, created and integrated by creatives using powerful software and inventive pipelines. 3D characters, creatures, assets and environments are built and integrated or composited into high resolution film to enable new stories to burst into life.

The course exposes students to critical and media theories that they can connect to their emerging practice to grow their repertoire of skills and stand out in the marketplace. Students will complete the course having created 3D and/or compositing VFX work of a high quality, gaining an understanding of the potential advances in 3D modelling, VFX compositing and their impact on a wide range of production processes across the screen industries.

Students also have the opportunity for unique collaboration with students on other pathways, working with animators, games artists, concept artists and digital comics illustrators.

- **Study Mode:** Full Time / Part Time
- **Duration:** 1 year (full time) / 2 years (part time)
- **Campus:** College Lane

## Entry Requirements

A degree, ideally in a relevant subject (2.2 minimum), plus a portfolio demonstrating recent engagement and creative activities in your intended study area. Accreditation of professional experience may be possible for practitioners without a first degree.

## English Language Requirements

- IELTS 6.5 (with no less than 5.5 in any band) or equivalent

## Portfolio Requirements

A portfolio demonstrating recent engagement and creative activities in the intended study area of 3D modelling and VFX. The portfolio should evidence creative practice, technical proficiency in 3D software, and engagement with visual effects or digital art techniques.
