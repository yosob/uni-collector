# Music Production and Promotion BSc (Hons)

## Overview

BSc (Hons) Music Production and Promotion is one of five exciting courses in the Undergraduate Music Programme at the University of Hertfordshire. The degree is designed to blend music production skills and sound technology with the key promotional skills required for aspiring music producers. Students develop their own musical style whilst learning how to record, produce and master products so that they are industry ready for marketing and promotion.

There are no formal exams — the focus is placed on practical work. As creative technologists, students use their own music as coursework submissions, with the aim of building up an impressive portfolio of work that is release ready. Students collaborate extensively within the School of Creative Arts, connecting with peers in the fields of Songwriting, Audio Engineering, Live Sound, Lighting and Performance Technology, reflecting how they will work in industry.

Many staff within the Music department are active in the music industry — as producers, songwriters, composers, remixers, sound designers or technology journalists — and bring their experience and expertise into the teaching space. The course benefits from strong industry links via the Music Academic Partnership (MAP), a groundbreaking initiative that unites educational institutions with members of the UK music industry.

GCSE Mathematics and English Language at grade 4/C or above (or equivalent). Applicants with relevant professional experience may also be considered.
