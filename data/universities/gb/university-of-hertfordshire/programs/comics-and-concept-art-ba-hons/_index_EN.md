# Comics and Concept Art BA (Hons)

## Overview

This innovative course is designed for aspiring comic illustrators and concept artists who dream of creating characters and the worlds they inhabit. You'll develop the design skills to visualise characters and environments not just as images but as compelling narratives — for print, digital publications, animation, games and film.

You'll learn from staff whose credits include Avengers, The Hobbit and Thor, and join the No.1 Animation School in Europe (and No.2 in the World) as acclaimed by The Rookies Awards 2024. The digital animation programme as a whole is accredited by The Rookies, SideFX and CILECT, ensuring industry recognition for training the artists of the future.

The course blends project work, software and art-based workshops, and lectures. You'll be assessed through group and individual project work, portfolio submissions, sketches, storyboards, digital artwork and presentations. You'll also develop analytical thinking, critical curiosity and essay writing skills.

The design, illustration and storytelling skills you'll gain are in high demand by the sector, along with transferable skills in pitching ideas, project management, self-promotion and problem solving. Career options include freelance comic artist, independent author, webcomic creator, and concept artist for animation, games and film industries.

## Modules

### Level 4 (Year 1)

In your first year, you'll benefit from common modules within the wider animation programme, gaining valuable knowledge of the digital media industry. You'll study alongside peers on 2D and 3D animation, games and visual effects, expanding your network from day one. You'll learn digital illustration techniques, practice life drawing, design characters and environments, and explore comics, animation and film history.

| Module | Credits | Type |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Introduction to CG | 30 | Compulsory |
| Applying CG | 30 | Compulsory |
| Creativity, Concept & Story | 30 | Compulsory |
| Animated Thinking | 30 | Compulsory |

### Level 5 (Year 2)

In your second year, you'll develop your illustrative style. You'll use your design skills to create characters, creatures, props and environments. You'll produce comic narratives, tailoring your approach to match the needs of print and digital publications. At the end of the year, you'll showcase your individual flair and design expertise in a major comic or concept art project.

| Module | Credits | Type |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Progressing Character & Concept Art | 30 | Compulsory |
| Creative Project | 30 | Compulsory |
| Comics & Professional Practices | 30 | Compulsory |
| Moving Visions | 30 | Compulsory |
| Year Abroad | 0 | Optional |

### Level 6 (Year 3)

In your final year, you'll write your dissertation on an area of popular culture linked to comics and concept art. You'll develop an ambitious project, using expert design techniques and digital illustration and 3D software to bring your original ideas to life. You'll take a project from initial concept to production, proving your project management and leadership qualities, and graduate with an impressive professional portfolio, showreel and website.

## Career Prospects

Graduates pursue a wide range of careers within the creative industries, including comic artist, concept artist, freelance illustrator, independent author, webcomic creator, character designer, environment artist, and roles in animation, games and film studios. Alumni have gone on to work at ILM on Star Wars films, Epic Games on Fortnite, Blue Zoo animating cartoon series, and Sony as concept artists. The programme has strong industry links with regular contact with professionals and excellent placement opportunities.
