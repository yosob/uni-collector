# Concept Art MA

## Overview

The MA Concept Art course enables you to study concept art at an advanced level, developing your creative and technical skills for visual storytelling across the screen industries. You will build a strong portfolio of concept art work that demonstrates your ability to visualise characters, environments, creatures, and props for film, television, games, and animation.

The University of Hertfordshire is recognised as a leading institution for concept art education, ranked **1st in the UK for Best Concept Art School** (The Rookies Top Global Creative Schools, 2024) and **9th in the UK for Art** (Daily Mail University Guide, 2025). This reputation is built on a curriculum that blends traditional drawing skills with cutting-edge digital techniques and industry-aligned briefs.

The course connects creative practice with critical media theory, enabling you to develop a distinctive artistic voice while understanding the broader cultural and industry contexts in which concept art operates. You will work with both 2D and 3D tools, developing skills in visual development, storytelling, and world-building that are essential to the entertainment industries.

Students also have the opportunity for unique collaboration with students on other pathways, working with games artists, animators, 3D modellers and VFX artists.

- **Study Mode:** Full Time / Part Time
- **Duration:** 1 year (full time) / 2 years (part time)
- **Campus:** College Lane

## Entry Requirements

A degree, ideally in a relevant subject (2.2 minimum), plus a portfolio demonstrating recent engagement and creative activities in your intended study area. Accreditation of professional experience may be possible for practitioners without a first degree.

## English Language Requirements

- IELTS 6.5 (with no less than 5.5 in any band) or equivalent

## Portfolio Requirements

A portfolio demonstrating recent engagement and creative activities in the intended study area of concept art. The portfolio should evidence strong drawing and visualisation skills, character and environment design ability, and proficiency with digital painting tools (such as Photoshop, Procreate, or equivalent).
