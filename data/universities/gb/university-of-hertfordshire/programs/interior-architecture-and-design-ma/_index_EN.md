# Interior Architecture and Design MA

## Overview

The MA Interior Architecture and Design course enables you to study interior architecture at an advanced level, developing your creative, technical, and professional skills to design innovative and sustainable interior spaces. You will engage with contemporary design challenges, exploring the relationship between people, spaces, and environments through a combination of studio practice and critical inquiry.

The programme is designed for graduates and practitioners who wish to deepen their understanding of interior architecture and push the boundaries of spatial design. You will explore adaptive reuse, sustainable design strategies, material innovation, and the integration of technology within interior environments. The course encourages you to develop a personal design philosophy grounded in research and critical practice.

You will work with a range of design tools and technologies, from hand drawing and physical model-making to advanced digital design software and VR visualisation. The course connects creative studio practice with theoretical and contextual studies, preparing you for professional practice in interior architecture and design.

Students also have the opportunity for unique collaboration with students on other pathways, working with architects, product designers, and digital media artists.

- **Study Mode:** Full Time / Part Time
- **Duration:** 1 year (full time) / 2 years (part time)
- **Campus:** College Lane

## Entry Requirements

A degree, ideally in a relevant subject (2.2 minimum), plus a portfolio demonstrating recent engagement and creative activities in your intended study area. Accreditation of professional experience may be possible for practitioners without a first degree.

## English Language Requirements

- IELTS 6.5 (with no less than 5.5 in any band) or equivalent

## Portfolio Requirements

A portfolio demonstrating recent engagement and creative activities in the intended study area of interior architecture and design. The portfolio should evidence spatial design thinking, technical drawing and visualisation skills, and engagement with materials, construction, and environmental design.
