# Creative AI for Screen MA

## Overview

The MA Creative AI for Screen introduces you to the creative application of artificial intelligence technologies for screen-based content creation. You will explore the intersection of AI and creative practice, developing skills to leverage machine learning, generative AI, and computational tools within film, television, games, and digital media production.

This pioneering programme addresses the rapidly growing demand for creative professionals who understand both the potential and limitations of AI in screen industries. You will develop practical skills in AI-assisted content creation while critically engaging with the ethical, cultural, and industry implications of these transformative technologies.

The course combines hands-on technical workshops with critical media theory, enabling you to develop AI-enhanced creative workflows that are both innovative and responsible. You will work with a range of AI tools and platforms, exploring applications in visual effects, character generation, environment creation, automated editing, and interactive storytelling.

Students also have the opportunity for unique collaboration with students on other pathways, working with filmmakers, games artists, animators, and immersive media practitioners.

- **Study Mode:** Full Time / Part Time
- **Duration:** 1 year (full time) / 2 years (part time)
- **Campus:** College Lane

## Entry Requirements

A degree, ideally in a relevant subject (2.2 minimum), plus a portfolio demonstrating recent engagement and creative activities in your intended study area. Accreditation of professional experience may be possible for practitioners without a first degree.

## English Language Requirements

- IELTS 6.5 (with no less than 5.5 in any band) or equivalent

## Portfolio Requirements

A portfolio demonstrating recent engagement and creative activities in a related area. The portfolio should evidence creative practice, technical proficiency with digital tools, and an interest in the application of AI or computational methods to creative screen-based work.
