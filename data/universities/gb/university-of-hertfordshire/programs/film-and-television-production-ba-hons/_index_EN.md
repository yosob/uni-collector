# Film and Television Production BA (Hons)

## Overview

A ScreenSkills and CILECT accredited course that provides an environment in which students can grow into confident television and film practitioners. Ranked among the top 20 for film production nationally (The Guardian), the course delivers industry-ready graduates into the workplace. Students learn professional skills from the outset, managing and producing their own location and studio-based shoots, and experiencing a variety of key roles on set and in post-production. The course has industry partnerships with Warner Bros., the Production Guild, Halo Post-Production, and the ENVY Academy. UCAS Code: W602.

## Entry Requirements

112–120 UCAS points. A Level: BBC–BBB. BTEC: DMM (Extended Diploma). IB: 112–120 points from a minimum of 2 HL subjects at H4 or above (with remaining points from a combination of HL, SL and Core). Access Course: Diploma with 45 Level 3 credits at Merit. GCSE: Grade 4/C in English Language and Mathematics.

## Focus Areas

- multi-camera television studio production

- directing

## Facilities

- Mike Newall Sound Stage TV Studio — large HD broadcast standard facility with railed light rig, pedestal cameras with autocue, green screen and vision mixer
- Doug Milsome Cinematography Lab — technical training and small-scale filming space
- 40-seat Walter Murch Post-Production Lab — Avid Media Composer, Pro Tools, Baselight, DaVinci Resolve, Final Draft
- 120TB EditShare shared storage — collaborative editing workflow (Broadcast Tech Awards 2021 finalist)
- Camera fleet: Blackmagic URSA 4.6K digital cinema, Canon C100, Canon XF305, Panasonic 371 with Zeiss and Samyang lenses
- Lighting: Kino Flo and Arri lamp systems
- Audio: Sennheiser, SQN and Zoom microphones and recorders
- Grip: Steadicam rigs, jibs, dollies
- Dedicated colour grading suites, ADR and dubbing studios
- Library of 23,000+ sound effects and 4,000+ music tracks

## Career Perspectives

Graduates initially go on to become runners, junior researchers, assistant producers, assistant directors, editors and camera assistants, and freelancers. Thereafter, they may expect to progress to senior roles such as producer, director, senior editor, head of department.

Notable graduate credits include:
- Archivist and Script Editor, EastEnders (BBC)
- Producer, The Grand Tour (Amazon)
- Production Assistant, Wonder Woman, The Mummy, Tomb Raider, Solo: A Star Wars Story, Spider-Man Far From Home
- Post-Production Runner, Gogglebox (Channel 4)
- Producer/Director, Jamie Oliver's FoodTube (YouTube)
- Video Assistant, Ready Player One, Justice League, Murder on the Orient Express, Christopher Robin, Mary Poppins Returns
- Visual Effects Production Assistant, Star Wars: The Last Jedi
- Camera Operator, Victorian Bakery (BBC)
- Assistant Producer, Strictly Come Dancing (BBC)
- Marketing Assistant, Warner Brothers
