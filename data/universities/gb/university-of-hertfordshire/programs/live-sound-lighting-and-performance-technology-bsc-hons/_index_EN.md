# Live Sound, Lighting and Performance Technology BSc (Hons)

## Overview

BSc (Hons) Live Sound, Lighting and Performance Technology sits within the overarching Music & Sound Technology Programme at the University of Hertfordshire. The course delves into a wide array of sound principles, including acoustics and capture, sound reinforcement, music studio design, signal theory, processing, sound synthesis, audio electronics, networked audio, multichannel sound, and storage. Once students master the fundamentals, the focus shifts to venue-based audio systems, covering everything from concert halls to vibrant festivals.

Students learn core principles of live sound and lighting technology, working with industry-standard equipment including Midas Pro Series audio consoles and Avolites lighting consoles. The programme develops techniques and approaches using a range of sound technologies, fostering professional and industrial contextual immersion and awareness. Within the wider School of Creative Arts, students collaborate with peers from animation, film and TV, building a broad network of creative industry connections.

There are four degree awards within the Music & Sound Technology Programme: BSc (Hons) Music Production, BSc (Hons) Music and Sound Design Technology, BSc (Hons) Audio Recording and Production, and BSc (Hons) Live Sound, Lighting and Performance Technology. All courses share the same ethos, with opportunities to work alongside music composition and music industry management students.

## Focus Areas

- live sound reinforcement and mixing
- lighting design and programming (Avolites)
- audio system design for venues and festivals
- digital audio console operation (Midas Pro Series)
- acoustics and psychoacoustics
- audio electronics and signal routing
- networked audio systems (Dante/Rednet)
- event management and production
- broadcast audio
- projection mapping
- music technology and DAW proficiency
