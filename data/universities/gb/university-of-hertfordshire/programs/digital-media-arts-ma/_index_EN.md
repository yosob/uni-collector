# Digital Media Arts MA

## Overview

The MA Digital Media Arts course enables you to study digital media arts practice at an advanced level, developing your creative and technical skills across a broad range of digital media forms. You will explore the intersection of art, technology, and culture, creating innovative digital work that engages with contemporary media practices and critical discourses.

The programme is designed for creative practitioners who want to expand their practice into new digital territories. You will work across multiple media including interactive media, digital imaging, moving image, sound art, web-based art, and physical computing. The course encourages experimentation and critical reflection, enabling you to develop a distinctive creative voice within the digital arts landscape.

The course connects creative practice with critical media theory, enabling you to situate your work within broader cultural, social, and technological contexts. You will engage with questions of digital culture, interactivity, participation, and the evolving relationship between humans and technology.

Students also have the opportunity for unique collaboration with students on other pathways, working with games artists, filmmakers, immersive media practitioners, and creative AI specialists.

- **Study Mode:** Full Time / Part Time
- **Duration:** 1 year (full time) / 2 years (part time)
- **Campus:** College Lane

## Entry Requirements

A degree, ideally in a relevant subject (2.2 minimum), plus a portfolio demonstrating recent engagement and creative activities in your intended study area. Accreditation of professional experience may be possible for practitioners without a first degree.

## English Language Requirements

- IELTS 6.5 (with no less than 5.5 in any band) or equivalent

## Portfolio Requirements

A portfolio demonstrating recent engagement and creative activities in the intended study area of digital media arts. The portfolio should evidence creative digital practice, technical proficiency with relevant tools and technologies, and engagement with contemporary digital art and media practices.
