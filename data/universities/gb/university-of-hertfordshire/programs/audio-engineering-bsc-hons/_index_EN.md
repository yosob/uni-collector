# Audio Engineering BSc (Hons)

## Overview

BSc (Hons) Audio Engineering is one of five exciting courses in the Undergraduate Music Programme at the University of Hertfordshire. A technical and varied course covering a range of topics including acoustics, sound recording and capture, reinforcement, and signal processing as well as advanced sound signal theory, sound synthesis and processing, audio electronics (including making your own sound tools), multichannel and immersive mixing, mastering, re-mastering and restoration.

During this course, guided by supportive academics and industry experts, students discover the diverse opportunities in the world of audio engineering. By pushing boundaries and embracing new experiences and technologies, students produce a professional portfolio of recordings, mixes, remixes, and masters, which will benefit their future career. Students also develop a thorough knowledge of how sound is controlled, transmitted, and received through the lens of acoustics, signal processing, technology specification and networked audio systems.

Students collaborate extensively within the School of Creative Arts, connecting with peers in the fields of Music Production, Songwriting, Music Composition for Film and Games, and Live Sound, Lighting and Performance Technology, reflecting how they will work in industry.

Many staff within the Music department are active in the music industry — as producers, songwriters, composers, remixers, sound designers or technology journalists — and bring their experience and expertise into the teaching space. The course benefits from strong industry links via the Music Academic Partnership (MAP), a groundbreaking initiative that unites educational institutions with members of the UK music industry.

## Entry Requirements

112–120 UCAS points from at least 2 A-levels (or equivalent qualifications such as BTEC Extended Diploma, International Baccalaureate, or Access to HE Diploma). GCSE Mathematics and English Language at grade 4/C or above (or equivalent). Preferred subjects include Music, Music Technology, Mathematics, or Physics. Applicants with relevant professional experience may also be considered.
