# MArch Architecture and Urbanism

## Overview

The MArch Architecture and Urbanism programme is designed to enable you to develop your own critical and creative position in architecture. The course provides a platform for advanced architectural study, integrating design studio work with research in architecture and urbanism, and preparing you for professional architectural practice.

The programme encourages you to engage with contemporary architectural debates, exploring themes of sustainability, urban regeneration, social equity, and technological innovation. You will develop your design skills through a series of studio-based projects that address real-world challenges, supported by theoretical and contextual studies that inform and enrich your creative practice.

The course is prescribed by the Architects Registration Board (ARB) and validated by the Royal Institute of British Architects (RIBA) at Part 2 level, providing the academic qualification needed for progression towards professional registration as an architect in the UK.

You will work with a range of design tools and technologies, from traditional drawing and model-making to advanced digital design software, BIM, and environmental simulation tools. The course fosters a culture of critical enquiry, experimentation, and collaboration.

- **Study Mode:** Full Time
- **Duration:** 2 years (full time)
- **Campus:** College Lane

## Entry Requirements

A degree in Architecture (ARB/RIBA Part 1 prescribed), typically with a 2.2 or above, plus a portfolio demonstrating your architectural design abilities. Accreditation of professional experience may be possible for practitioners without a first degree.

## English Language Requirements

- IELTS 6.5 (with no less than 5.5 in any band) or equivalent

## Portfolio Requirements

A portfolio demonstrating architectural design ability, creative thinking, and technical competence. The portfolio should evidence design studio work, spatial thinking, and engagement with architectural theory and practice.
