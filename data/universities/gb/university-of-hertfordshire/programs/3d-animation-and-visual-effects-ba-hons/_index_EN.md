# 3D Animation and Visual Effects BA (Hons)

## Overview

Whether it's a feature film, cartoon series, commercials or idents, this course prepares you for a career in the exciting world of 3D animation and visual effects. From character animation, cinematography and modelling to rigging and compositing, you'll master the skills needed to thrive in a demanding and creative industry.

You'll join a prestigious course at the No.1 Animation School in the UK and Europe (and No.3 in the World) as acclaimed by The Rookies Awards 2020. The digital animation programme is accredited by The Rookies, SideFX and CILECT, meaning you'll be joining a department that has gained international recognition for training the animators of the future. The course is ranked 2nd in the world for production excellence and sits in the top 7 for 3D animation courses in Europe. Herts is also the only UK university to be shortlisted at the Visual Effects Society Awards in Los Angeles in the past 18 years.

You'll immerse yourself in our animation studios, which are kitted out with everything you need to bring your creations to life. Our Learning Resource Centre contains clones of our animation labs (software and hardware) so you can continue your animation studies late at night or during the weekend. Plus, you can use our motion capture suite, digital photography and filmmaking equipment to work on your own projects.

In your **first year**, you'll benefit from common modules within the wider animation programme, gaining valuable knowledge of the digital media industry. You'll study alongside peers on comics and concept art, 2D animation and games, expanding your network from day one. You'll practice life drawing, designing characters and environments. You'll explore animation and film history, bringing cultural context to your craft. You'll gain experience as a 3D artist, building confidence in modelling, texturing and shaders, as well as specialised VFX skills. At the end of this year you'll have the choice to switch to a different animation degree course if your passion has led you in a new direction.

In your **second year**, you'll choose from a range of elective projects. This is your chance to focus on what interests you — whether it's cinematography or character concept art, you'll start turning your passion into expert knowledge. Start packing out your CV with specialist skills including matte painting, rigging and coding. Gain a thorough understanding of FX simulations, working with destruction techniques, hard and soft body collisions and pyrotechnics. Whether you want to hone your skills in compositing 3D renders into live action plates or filming live action, this is the year to experiment. You'll work on live industry projects and make your first short 3D or VFX film as a team project.

In your **third year**, you'll write your dissertation on an area of popular culture linked to 3D animation and visual effects that fascinates you. You'll choose your area of specialism and immerse yourself in real-world practices. You'll get stuck into an ambitious final film project, guiding your idea through every stage of production to showcase your talent, skills and potential to succeed. Your final film project will be screened at our Animation Exposé show where you can enter it for different awards, judged by a panel of industry experts. We'll also encourage you to enter your work into international competitions like The Rookies. You'll graduate with a showreel and professional website to launch your career.

## Entry Requirements

112–120 UCAS points. A Level: BBC–BBB (mandatory Art & Design or creative subject involving drawing). BTEC: DMM–DDM (Art & Design, Games Art, Games Art and Animation, Animation, Level 3 Extended Diploma in Games/Animation/VFX Skills (NextGen), or UAL Extended Level 3 Diploma in Creative Media Production). T Levels accepted in various subjects (grade M plus A Level in Art & Design or creative subject with drawing, grade B). Access Course: Diploma with 45 Level 3 credits at Merit. GCSE: Grade 4/C in English Language and Mathematics. Interview required.

## Career Prospects

The digital animation programme prepares you for a range of careers within the industry. These include 3D animator, 3D generalist, FX technical director, compositor, environment artist, character artist, concept artist, 2D animator, matte painter, lighting TD, rigger and a huge variety of roles in between.

Our graduates have gone on to work at ILM on Star Wars films, to Epic Games to work on Fortnite, to Blue Zoo to animate cartoon series, and to Sony to work as concept artists. Many graduates also work in the visualisation industries, using their design skills in the fields of architecture, product design and automotive design. As a result, we have animation graduates employed by Foster and Partners, Taylor James and McLaren F1. Many start their own companies, some of whom have become leaders in their fields.

Notable graduate credits include work on Oscar-winning films and blockbusters such as Star Wars, Gravity, Jungle Book, Lion King, Inception, Avatar, the Harry Potter films, The Chronicles of Narnia, Game of Thrones, and more.
