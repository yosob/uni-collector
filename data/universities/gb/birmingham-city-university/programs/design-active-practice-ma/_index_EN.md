# Design: Active Practice MA

## Overview

MA Design: Active Practice at Birmingham City University offers a unique opportunity to rethink conventional design models through the integration of research and practice. The course empowers students to critically engage with the purpose and value of artefacts, systems, and processes, equipping them to address complex social, political, and technological challenges through innovative design solutions. By combining critical inquiry with hands-on studio work, students develop the skills and confidence to become leaders driving transformative change in the design field.

With a strong emphasis on social change, the course centres on learning by doing. Active design processes — such as making and dialogue — are central to developing competencies in design research, analysis, creative problem-solving, and prototyping. Key themes of Design Cultures, Connections, and Challenges guide practice-based learning through individual and collaborative projects, enhancing teamwork and communication skills via real-world design briefs and live assignments.

Housed in the historic Birmingham School of Art, the programme thrives within a vibrant research culture that welcomes practitioners from diverse backgrounds. The dynamic studio environment fosters individual growth and collaboration, empowering students to build an impressive portfolio and expand their professional networks through participation in design festivals and workshops across Europe.

## Course Structure

The programme is structured around three core thematic pillars: **Design Cultures**, **Design Connections**, and **Design Challenges**, followed by a Major Project. These themes guide students from contextual understanding through to applied practice and independent research.

**Core Themes and Modules:**

- **Design Cultures** — Explores the cultural, social, and political contexts of design practice. Develops critical perspectives on how design shapes and is shaped by cultural narratives, value systems, and power structures.
- **Design Connections** — Focuses on collaborative and interdisciplinary practice. Students work on real-world briefs and live assignments, building competencies in design research, stakeholder engagement, and participatory design methods.
- **Design Challenges** — Addresses complex social, political, and technological challenges through innovative design solutions. Emphasises prototyping, testing, and iterative design processes.
- **Major Project** (60 credits) — An in-depth, practice-based research project in which students develop a substantial body of work that demonstrates critical inquiry, creative problem-solving, and professional-level design practice.

### Professional Placement Route (Optional)

This course offers an optional professional placement, allowing students to spend 20 weeks with an employer following completion of the taught modules. The placement provides an excellent opportunity to enhance employability and evidence professional skills, attitudes and behaviours at the point of entry to the postgraduate job market.

Students are responsible for finding and securing a suitable placement, with support from the University's extensive network of local, regional and national employers and the Careers team. Placements are confirmed following a competitive, employer-led selection process.

### Assessment

Assessment is primarily through coursework, portfolio submissions, presentations, and the Major Project. There are no formal written examinations.
