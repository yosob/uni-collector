# Architecture with Foundation Year BA (Hons) (4 years)

## Overview

Our Architecture with Foundation Year BA (Hons) course begins with a foundation year designed to develop the core skills, knowledge and confidence needed to progress onto the BA Honours Architecture degree. The foundation year introduces fundamental principles of architectural thinking, including drawing and visual communication, spatial design, model-making, and digital skills. Students explore architecture's cultural, social and environmental context, develop creative problem-solving abilities, and build essential academic skills such as research, critical reflection and portfolio development.

After completing the foundation year, students progress to the RIBA-validated and ARB-prescribed BA (Hons) Architecture course, which offers RIBA Part 1 exemption. The course frames architecture as a radical act of care, nurturing disciplinary disruptors, future leaders, innovators and thinkers.

Taught in the £62 million Parkside Building at our City Centre Campus, the course is validated by the Royal Institute of British Architects (RIBA). Birmingham City University is highly respected for its Architecture provision, having delivered the subject since 1909.

## Course Structure

The course is structured as a foundation year followed by three years of the BA Architecture programme, each with progressive themes: Foundation, Principles (Year 1), Process (Year 2), and Identity (Year 3). All modules are assessed through 100% coursework.

### Foundation Year
*120 credits total*

The foundation year introduces fundamental principles of architectural thinking:
- **Drawing and Visual Communication** — Core skills for architectural representation
- **Spatial Design** — Introduction to spatial thinking and design processes
- **Model-making** — Physical and digital model-making techniques
- **Digital Skills** — Introduction to CAD and digital design tools
- **Architecture's Cultural, Social and Environmental Context** — Understanding architecture in its broader context
- **Creative Problem-solving** — Developing design thinking abilities
- **Academic Skills** — Research, critical reflection and portfolio development

### Year 1 — Theme: Principles
*120 credits total*
- **History of Architecture and Design** (20 credits) — Overview of key design ideologies of the 20th century and world architecture through the centuries
- **Structures, Environment & Technical Studies** (20 credits) — Materials, structures, environmental design strategies for robust and sustainable architecture
- **Design Apparatus** (20 credits) — Broad range of skills for architectural communication and representation including hand and digital skills (drawing, CAD, Adobe packages)
- **Design Resolution 1** (60 credits) — Design projects exploring the experience and mapping of the city, leading to various scaled designed interventions

### Year 2 — Theme: Process
*120 credits total*
- **Architectural Theory and Research Methods** (20 credits) — Exploration of theoretical ideas in architecture; how theory informs formation, analysis and making of buildings
- **Design Methods** (20 credits) — Research methods using qualitative/quantitative methodologies and design-led research to develop independent design strategies
- **PRAXIS (Management Practice and Law)** (20 credits) — Professional practice, management, law, and a two-week assessed work placement in an architecture practice
- **Design Resolution 2** (60 credits) — Design projects at intermediate level building on Year 1 principles

### Final Year (Year 3) — Theme: Identity
*120 credits total*
- **Critical Study in Architecture** (20 credits) — Detailed critical study of a case study based on social, cultural and political topics in contemporary architectural discourse
- **Design Exploration** (20 credits) — Opening creative exploration of a selected studio approach for a year-long design process on a single project
- **Systems in Architecture** (20 credits) — Inquiry-based approach to technical integration; fabrication, assembly and critical technical analysis
- **Design Resolution 3** (60 credits) — Comprehensive design proposal resolving theoretical, spatial or aesthetic concepts at strategic, building and detailed levels

- **100% coursework** — No practical or written exams (after foundation year)
- Assessment mirrors real-world architectural practice
- Portfolio-based assessment building your 'passport to practice'
- Lectures, seminars, studio-based design projects, workshops
- Individual and group tutorials
- Cross-disciplinary Co.LAB projects with external clients
