## Programme Overview

The MA Landscape Architecture at Birmingham City University is a progressive and forward-thinking programme that empowers students to advance their professional journey as landscape architects while taking the initial strides toward becoming Chartered Landscape Architects.

The programme enables students to address pressing global challenges such as **climate change** and **biodiversity decline**, while contributing to the creation of sustainable, equitable, and resilient landscapes, places and communities. The course stimulates a vibrant **design studio culture** that promotes rigorous research, innovative design approaches, creative thinking, and strong individual ownership of concepts.

It prioritises current professional practices and real-world projects to facilitate the thoughtful integration of theory and practice across various scales — from landscape planning to detailed design solutions.

### Design Themes

- Landscape health and wellbeing
- Habitat creation and biodiversity
- Climate change adaptation
- Settlement design and food security
- Large infrastructure schemes (e.g. HS2, West Midlands National Park)
