# Performance MMus / PgDip / PgCert

## Overview

The principal aim of our Master of Music (MMus) Performance course is to provide you with knowledge, skills, and confidence to succeed in your chosen branch of the music profession, giving you a head-start in addressing the challenges involved in becoming a professional musician. The course is suitable for both recent graduates and suitably qualified mature students.

### Performance Pathways

- **Instrumental Performance** (MMus, PgDip, PgCert) — Prepares you for a solo, chamber or orchestral career. Available to students pursuing orchestral instruments, keyboard instruments and other solo instruments such as recorder, guitar, and saxophone. Also welcomes instrumentalists and singers developing fusions of folk music with other genres, songwriters, and woodwind players pursuing a multi-instrumentalist career in musical theatre.
- **Vocal Performance** (MMus, PgDip, PgCert) — Advanced-level training in singing. You're encouraged to increase your experience as a performer and broaden your repertoire, with opportunities to specialise. Regular opportunities to sing as a soloist and in ensembles.
- **Orchestral Performance (Strings)** (MMus, PgDip) — Developed in partnership with the City of Birmingham Symphony Orchestra (CBSO). For advanced string players considering professional orchestral auditions. You will spend significant rehearsal time in the CBSO alongside dedicated host players as mentors, with opportunities to play at the back of the section.
- **Jazz** (MMus, PgDip, PgCert) — High quality advanced-level tuition enabling you to specialise as a Jazz performer or instigator. Regular individual specialist tuition from professional jazz musicians, emphasis on improvisation and regular opportunities with small and large jazz ensembles.

### What's Covered in This Course?

- Regular individual tuition from leading UK and internationally renowned performers, practitioners, teachers, and researchers
- Bespoke support classes in musicianship and repertoire
- Opportunities to participate in a wide variety of creative projects, including interdisciplinary collaborations
- Frequent opportunities to perform within Departmental activities and concerts
- Access to forums, seminars and masterclasses with distinguished visiting artists
- Access to the Conservatoire's broad range of ensemble activities
- Professional Development modules tailored to your career aspirations

## Entry Requirements

- UK students should normally hold an honours degree, ideally but not necessarily in Music
- Non-UK students should hold a Bachelor's degree or similar degree-equivalent diploma
- **Audition:** Live audition required for most Principal Study areas
- **IELTS:** 6.0 overall with no less than 5.5 in each band (international/EU students)

## Course Structure

### PgCert (60 credits)
- **Principal Study 1: Instrumental and Vocal Performance** (60 credits)

### PgDip (120 credits)
- **Principal Study 1** (60 credits) — In your chosen performance pathway
- **Career Development** (20 credits) — Self-promotion, self-management, funding and wellbeing
- **Professional Development Option** (40 credits)

### MMus (180 credits)
- All PgDip modules plus:
- **Principal Study 2** (60 credits) — Advanced performance project in your specialist pathway
