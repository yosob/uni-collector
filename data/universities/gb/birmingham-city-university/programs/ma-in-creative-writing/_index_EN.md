# MA in Creative Writing

## Overview

The MA in Creative Writing at Birmingham City University is delivered through the **Institute of Creative and Critical Writing (ICCW)** within the School of English. The course offers an intensive programme of creative writing development, supported by published writers and visiting speakers who help students develop their creative practice across multiple genres.

Students explore fiction, poetry, screenwriting, and creative non-fiction while developing critical skills through the integration of creative practice and literary analysis. The programme is distinctive in its **all-core, no-optional** structure, ensuring comprehensive skills development across all forms of creative writing. The course culminates in a **Major Project**: an extended piece of creative writing in the student's chosen form, developed with supervisory support from established writers.

Located in Birmingham — a UNESCO City of Design with a thriving literary scene — the programme benefits from strong connections to the city's vibrant cultural and literary communities.

## Course Structure

### Core Modules (180 credits — all core)

| Module | Code | Credits | Semester |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reading into Writing | EGL7xxx | 20 | 1 |
| Specialist Workshops – Creative Writing | EGL7xxx | 40 | 1–2 |
| The Writer's Craft | EGL7xxx | 40 | 1–2 |
| Literature in Practice | EGL7xxx | 20 | 2 |
| Major Project: Creative Writing | EGL7230 | 60 | 3 |

**Reading into Writing** (20 credits) — Explores the relationship between reading and creative writing, developing critical and creative skills through close reading and analysis of contemporary texts across multiple genres.

**Specialist Workshops – Creative Writing** (40 credits) — Intensive workshop-based module allowing students to develop their writing in specialist areas under guidance from published writers. Covers fiction, poetry, screenwriting and creative non-fiction.

**The Writer's Craft** (40 credits) — Develops writing craft through workshops and critical analysis of technique across multiple genres. Students refine their voice and technique through sustained creative practice and peer feedback.

**Literature in Practice** (20 credits) — Examines contemporary literary practice and theory, connecting critical reading with creative output. Develops understanding of the relationship between literary criticism and creative production.

**Major Project: Creative Writing** (60 credits) — An extended piece of creative writing in the student's chosen form, developed with supervisory support from established writers. Represents the culmination of the MA programme.
