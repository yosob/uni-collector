# Acting BA (Hons) | Royal Birmingham Conservatoire

## Programme Overview

Apply your passion and complete your training at Royal Birmingham Conservatoire — a vocational actor training course offering drama school level training to mould you into a versatile actor and help you launch your career. The course is designed to develop you practically, personally and professionally, preparing you for this challenging industry.

You will receive specialist training in voice, movement and singing, graduating with the skill and aptitude to take on the exciting and dynamic world of the dramatic arts. According to the Guardian League Table 2026, Royal Birmingham Conservatoire's acting and performance provision is ranked in the Top 10 in the UK for Drama and Dance.

Classes are mainly studio-based, focusing on the practical application of acting, voice, movement and singing. During the final year, you will have the opportunity to make a show reel, perform showcases to industry professionals, and be cast in professional productions staged in external theatres.

## Course Structure

### Year One

A laboratory-style year in a safe, focused environment at Ruskin Hall, building core skills and confidence.

**Core Modules (120 credits):**

- **Acting Theory and Practice 1** (40 credits) — Introduction to core principles of creating and communicating a character, using contemporary practitioners' strategies. Develops imaginative powers, narrative skills, improvisation and text analysis.
- **Voice and Movement Practices 1** (40 credits) — Study of voice, singing and movement techniques. Specialised teaching to help release habitual patterns and connect truthfully to emotional and physical content of roles.
- **Rehearsal Exercise** (40 credits) — Practice of essential technical skills within a rehearsal environment. Students rehearse with a director in part-year groups, with one project per term from a variety of historical, cultural and genre sources.

### Year Two

Advancing and integrating techniques across stage, screen and radio media.

**Core Modules (120 credits):**

- **Acting Theory and Practice 2** (40 credits) — Consolidates and advances Year 1 work. Explores texts and rehearsal techniques in detail across stage, screen and microphone. Includes green screen, motion capture, voiceover experience.
- **Voice and Movement Practices 2** (40 credits) — Further develops voice, singing and movement disciplines. Addresses acoustic demands of performance spaces and the requirements of characterisation, physical and vocal transformation.
- **Performance Exercise** (40 credits) — Integration of acting, voice, movement and singing into holistic characterisation. Provides at least 3 live performance opportunities for each exercise, developing a personal working system for professional practice.

### Year Three (Final Year)

Professional-level productions, screen work and industry preparation.

**Core Modules (120 credits):**

- **Production** (60 credits) — Rehearsal and performance of up to three productions (two in Autumn, one in Spring) in professional external venues. Simulates professional working practices within the theatre industry.
- **Recorded Media** (40 credits) — Practical experience in preparation, rehearsal and recording of both screen and radio pieces. Work with ensemble, director and technical personnel. Two separate assessments (screen and radio), equally weighted.
- **Professional Development** (20 credits) — Preparation for industry entry: showcase performances to agents, casting directors and artistic directors (normally in Birmingham and London). Portfolio of audition material. Freelance business skills for self-employed actors.
