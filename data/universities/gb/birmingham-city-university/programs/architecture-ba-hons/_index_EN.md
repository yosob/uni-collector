# Architecture BA (Hons) (3 years)

## Overview

Our Architecture BA (Hons) course is validated by the Royal Institute of British Architects (RIBA) and prescribed by the Architects Registration Board (ARB), offering RIBA Part 1 exemption. This provides distinct support when applying for placement in practice.

The course provides a broad foundation in architecture within the context of the 'urbanised field', exploring the physical and ideological relationship between architecture, landscape architecture and the city. You will develop creative flair and technical skills through 100% coursework assessment, with no practical or written exams — mirroring the way you would work in the real world.

The programme is structured around progressive themes across three years: Principles (Year 1), Process (Year 2), and Identity (Year 3). Cross-disciplinary practice is facilitated through the Co.LAB initiative, enabling engagement with design and production practices across subjects and with external clients.

## Course Structure

The course is structured around progressive themes: Principles (Year 1), Process (Year 2), and Identity (Year 3). All modules are core and assessed through 100% coursework.

### Year 1 — Theme: Principles
*120 credits total*
- **History of Architecture and Design** (20 credits) — Overview of key design ideologies of the 20th century and world architecture through the centuries
- **Structures, Environment & Technical Studies** (20 credits) — Materials, structures, environmental design strategies for robust and sustainable architecture
- **Design Apparatus** (20 credits) — Broad range of skills for architectural communication and representation including hand and digital skills (drawing, CAD, Adobe packages)
- **Design Resolution 1** (60 credits) — Design projects exploring the experience and mapping of the city, leading to various scaled designed interventions

### Year 2 — Theme: Process
*120 credits total*
- **Architectural Theory and Research Methods** (20 credits) — Exploration of theoretical ideas in architecture; how theory informs formation, analysis and making of buildings
- **Design Methods** (20 credits) — Research methods using qualitative/quantitative methodologies and design-led research to develop independent design strategies
- **PRAXIS (Management Practice and Law)** (20 credits) — Professional practice, management, law, and a two-week assessed work placement in an architecture practice
- **Design Resolution 2** (60 credits) — Design projects at intermediate level building on Year 1 principles

### Optional Professional Placement Year
Between Year 2 and Final Year, students may undertake an optional placement year in industry.

### Final Year (Year 3) — Theme: Identity
*120 credits total*
- **Critical Study in Architecture** (20 credits) — Detailed critical study of a case study based on social, cultural and political topics in contemporary architectural discourse
- **Design Exploration** (20 credits) — Opening creative exploration of a selected studio approach for a year-long design process on a single project
- **Systems in Architecture** (20 credits) — Inquiry-based approach to technical integration; fabrication, assembly and critical technical analysis
- **Design Resolution 3** (60 credits) — Comprehensive design proposal resolving theoretical, spatial or aesthetic concepts at strategic, building and detailed levels

## Teaching & Assessment

- **100% coursework** — No practical or written exams
- Assessment mirrors real-world architectural practice
- Portfolio-based assessment building your 'passport to practice'
- Lectures, seminars, studio-based design projects, workshops
- Individual and group tutorials
- Cross-disciplinary Co.LAB projects with external clients

## Accreditation

- **RIBA (Royal Institute of British Architects)** — RIBA Part 1 exemption, supporting placement in practice
- **ARB (Architects Registration Board)** — Prescribed qualification, regulated by Parliament since 1997

- Michael Hopkins and Partners
- MAKE
- Glenn Howells Architects
- Grimshaw Architects
- Hawkins Brown
- Populus
- Gensler
- Associated Architects

Many students gain summer paid work in practices as a result of the placement.
