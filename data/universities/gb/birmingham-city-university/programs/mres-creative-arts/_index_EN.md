# Creative Arts MRes (Masters by Research)

## Overview

The Masters by Research (MRes) Creative Arts course is a flexible research-based course designed to provide graduates with a foundation for a career in research, the public/private sector, the arts or academia (i.e. PhD studies). The purpose is to offer high quality postgraduate training in methods and practice of research and relevant transferable skills that offer a unique learning experience to advance knowledge within your specialist area of the arts or humanities.

The MRes offers a foundation of advanced knowledge, specialist research skills and transferable attributes to prepare for academic and professional research careers. It seeks to develop reflexive independent researchers who can operate at the forefront of their academic discipline and of professional practice.

### What's Covered in This Course?

This course gives you the flexibility to choose your own specialist area to focus on within the Creative Arts, from either a practice or academic perspective. Broadly, we can accommodate projects in:

- Art and Design
- English and Creative Writing
- Fashion and Textiles
- Film
- Photography
- Jewellery
- Media and Cultural Studies

You will be taught by experts from across the Creative Arts whose research is internationally excellent. You will work alongside other Creative Arts postgraduate research students and join a research cluster relevant to your discipline.

### Why Choose Us?

- Part of a rich research tradition stretching back to 1843 as a Government School of Design
- Research characterised by collaboration and interdisciplinarity
- 100% of BCU's research in communication, culture, media, music, drama, performing arts, film and screen studies rated four-star or three-star for impact (REF 2021)
- Taught by academics with international reputations producing globally significant research
- Explicitly prepares students for further doctoral study

## Entry Requirements

- Minimum 2:2 UK degree or international equivalent in an arts, humanities or related subject area
- Written Personal Statement
- Project proposal (max one side of A4) outlining what you want to explore and why
- Practice-based applicants should also provide a link to an online portfolio
- **IELTS:** 6.5 overall with 6.0 minimum in all bands (international/EU students)

## Course Structure

The MRes culminates in an independent research project, either by dissertation or by practice, supported by expert supervision from leading researchers. Students develop a research proposal and reflection on professional development and career ambitions.

## Employability

### Progression

The MRes explicitly prepares students for further doctoral study (PhD) in their area of expertise.
