## Overview

Future Media MA at Birmingham City University is a forward-thinking postgraduate programme that combines advertising agency methods with content production skills and digital marketing strategies. Running since 2012, it has a proven track record of launching graduates into successful careers in the creative media industry.

The course focuses on strategic and creative roles, equipping students with expertise in client account handling, creative direction, brand development, copywriting, content creation, web analytics, and social media monitoring. Students tackle real-world briefs in small class sizes of around 20, working alongside industry partners including top advertising and marketing agencies.

The programme begins with **Birmingham Agency Week**, where students visit leading digital marketing and advertising companies in the region. Teaching is practice-led and theory-applied, with all assignments rooted in industry briefs. Topics explored include sustainable futures, creative innovation, online safety, ethical practice, generative AI implications, and inclusivity in digital media.

## MA Pathway Focus

The MA pathway concentrates on:

- Client account handling and creative direction
- Creative brief writing, concept development, visualisation and copywriting
- Customer relationship management, task models, user journeys and wireframe development
- Creative skills development in design, brand development and guardianship
- Web analytics, search and social media monitoring
- Video content creation, production and delivery for deployment

## Modules

### Semester 1

| Module | Description |
|---|---|
| **Creative Advertising and Brand Strategy** | Client account handling and creative direction; creative brief writing, concept development, visualisation and copywriting |
| **Digital Content Creation** | Video content creation, production and delivery across digital platforms; creative skills in design and brand guardianship |
| **Digital Marketing Analytics and Strategy** | Web analytics, search and social media monitoring; strategic decision-making for digital marketing campaigns |

### Semester 2

| Module | Description |
|---|---|
| **User Experience and Customer Engagement** | Customer relationship management, task models, user journeys and wireframe development |
| **Professional Practice in Digital Media** | Industry briefs with real-world clients; multichannel digital campaign planning and execution with agency partners |

### Final Project

| Module | Description |
|---|---|
| **Major Project** | A three-month self-directed final project focused on personal professional development. Many students leverage this as a mentored conduit to employment |

## Entry Requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Degree** | Minimum 2:2 honours degree (or equivalent) |
| **IELTS** | 6.0 overall, no band below 5.5 |
| **Personal Statement** | 700–1,000 words explaining motivation for studying Future Media and choosing the MA pathway |
| **Other** | GCSE Level 2 Maths and English; no specific media/marketing background required |

Relevant background disciplines include Marketing, Business Studies, English/Linguistics, Economics/Statistics, Multimedia/Digital Content Creation, Art and Design, Media and Communications, Journalism/PR, or Psychology.

## Employability

The course produces **"T-skilled"** professionals — proficient across disciplines with specialist expertise. MA graduates typically pursue strategic roles:

- **Account Manager** — client liaison
- **Brand Strategist** — brand strategy and consumer insight
- **Creative Producer** — content and campaign production
- **Content Curator** — editorial and digital content strategy
- **Digital Marketing Executive** — campaign execution
- **Marketing Campaign Developer** — multichannel campaign design

## Industry Connections

- **Birmingham Agency Week** — visits to major digital marketing and advertising companies
- Industry partners provide mentorship and potential employment opportunities
- All assignments rooted in real-world briefs
- Small class sizes (~20) with professional practitioners
