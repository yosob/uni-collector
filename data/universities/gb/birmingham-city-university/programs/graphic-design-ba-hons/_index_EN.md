# Graphic Design BA (Hons)

## Overview

BA (Hons) Graphic Design at BCU is an exciting, engaging, and stimulating course that equips you with the skills, knowledge, and confidence needed to enter the creative industries. You will develop a variety of design approaches and creative problem-solving techniques across areas including branding, packaging, motion design, editorial, typography, UX/UI, art direction, and advertising, as well as emerging technologies such as VR/AR and 3D rendering. The course encourages a multi-disciplinary approach and active collaboration with students from illustration, photography, and digital marketing.

## Course Structure

### First Year

Your first year introduces fundamental skills and the critical framework within Graphic Design, covering composition, line, form, narrative, type, imagery, colour, motion design, UI and interactive design.

**Core Modules (100 credits):**

- **Fundamental Practice: Graphics** (40 credits) — Introduction to Graphic Design as a practice and profession, developing core visual communication principles and hands-on/digital skills.
- **Professional Futures: Communication** (20 credits) — Explores the role of effective communication in emerging design practice through theoretical frameworks and practical exercises.
- **Design at Play** (40 credits) — Immersive exploration of brand design across channels — visual identities, packaging, and digital platforms.

**Optional Modules (20 credits):**

- **Design for Change** (20 credits) — Interdisciplinary module on design for positive social and environmental impact, regenerative design, and human-centred design.

### Second Year

Develop your personal direction through theory and experiment, with opportunities for placement, live briefs, and interdisciplinary collaboration.

**Core Modules (100 credits):**

- **Emerging Technologies** (40 credits) — AR, VR, 3D modelling, projection mapping, and generative coding in graphic design.
- **Professional Futures: Collaboration** (20 credits) — Interdisciplinary teamwork and real-world collaborative project.
- **Defining Practice: Graphics** (40 credits) — Define your area of practice through three phases; develop an industry-standard showreel.

**Optional Modules (choose 20 credits):**

- **Work Placement** (20 credits) — 70-hour work placement with CV and interview preparation.
- **Independent Creative Entrepreneur** (20 credits) — Entrepreneurial skills for a portfolio creative career.
- **Industry Project** (20 credits) — External professional brief with sustainability focus.

### Professional Placement Year (Optional)

Spend a full year with an employer after Year 2. Fees are 20% of the full-time course fee for that year.

### Final Year

Establish your distinctive work identity and work autonomously on self-generated projects to a professional standard.

**Core Modules (120 credits):**

- **Advancing Practice** (40 credits) — Increasingly autonomous development of your individual creative direction.
- **Independent Enquiry** (20 credits) — Written assignment contextualising your work through primary and secondary research.
- **Major Project** (40 credits) — Sustained, in-depth research project — written dissertation or practice-based portfolio.
- **Professional Futures: Presentation** (20 credits) — Essential skills and strategies for navigating the design industry.
