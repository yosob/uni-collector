# Architecture MArch (2 years)

## Overview

The MArch (RIBA Part 2) is the second stage of your professional training to become an architect. You'll be inspired by innovative architecture teaching from academic and industry professionals and develop your critical ability, skills and creativity.

Benefiting from our links with more than 100 architecture practices, our graduates design and create the buildings of the future. They have secured successful architecture jobs with well known practices such as Associated Architects, Hawkins\Brown, Hopkins Architects and Glenn Howells Architects.

### What's covered in this course?

Recognised by the Architects Registration Board (ARB) and RIBA for exemption from Part II examination, the course is stage two of your professional development programme to become an architect.

The programme goes beyond the professional body criteria, giving you a stimulating and dynamic educational experience of architecture at postgraduate level. You'll study in a design studio-led research environment and you will be able to create architectural designs that meet necessary technical requirements while still having aesthetic appeal.

There are fantastic international opportunities — our students have undertaken fieldwork in Reykjavik, Istanbul, Amsterdam and Barcelona. There's also an annual New York study visit sponsored by Ibstock Brick.

You'll have opportunities to take part in research projects and our connections will help your work get noticed. MArch graduate Thomas Cotton had his final thesis project published on New York's Museum of Modern Art website for the exhibition Uneven Growth.

Birmingham School of Architecture and Design has strong alumni and connections to the city's creative and professional networks both through visiting tutors and critics.

Our Co.LAB is a collaborative architecture and design initiative based within the School that gives you the chance to work on live projects with our partner organisations including schools and charities. Our students recently worked on a project with 1930s listed structures at Dudley Zoological Gardens in collaboration with other students from the School of Art.

### Why Choose Us?

- You'll benefit from our international network of architectural practices and creative organisations to gain opportunities in transdisciplinary projects and industry-focussed modules.
- Birmingham City University is highly respected for its Architecture provision, having delivered the subject since 1909.
- The School has a long history of delivering live projects where you can work with industry partners and real communities through our Co\LAB initiative.
- The course prioritises a response to the climate emergency with a focus on material literacy, embodied carbon, circular design and input from our Experimental Sustainability Studio.
- Our students have received international recognition in prestigious awards, including commendations in the SPAB Phillip Web Award, AJ Student Award for Sustainability, and the Deutsche Bank Award for Creative Entrepreneurship.
- You'll participate in innovative modules where you can select your project to prioritise collaborative design approaches and research themes.

## Entry Requirements

### Essential Requirements
- **Degree:** Minimum 2:1 honours degree in Architecture (or equivalent)
- **RIBA Part 1:** Required (exceptions considered in exceptional circumstances with prior equivalent academic and practical experience)
- **Professional experience:** At least 9 months of post-undergraduate professional practice experience
- **Portfolio:** Required — demonstrating diverse design and technical work
- **Critical self-appraisal:** Required — covering design process, studio work, postgraduate candidacy, relevant academic/work experience
- **Interview:** May be required if not all essential requirements are met

### Portfolio Requirements
Applicants are expected to demonstrate a diverse design and technical portfolio with undergraduate, professional and other supporting work, along with personal statement/critical self appraisal.

## Course Structure

The course is structured across two stages, with studies interconnected across areas of design, research and theory, technology and collaborative practices. Design modules are completed through vertical Studio Units, each with focused theoretical frameworks.

### Stage 1 — 120 credits
*All modules are core*

| Module | Credits | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Advanced Praxis** | 40 | Develops advanced design and collaboration skills within professional practice context. Covers architect's responsibility to society, legal/legislative context, business management, and collaborative detailed architectural design. |
| **Research Principles** | 20 | Introduction to Masters level history and theory of architecture. Develops research, enterprise and practice themes through critical reading, interpretation and dialogue. |
| **Material Practices** | 40 | Promotes engagement with research through learning by making. Develops strategic ability to actively make and critique work through direct material engagement and collective action. |
| **Co.Lab II** | 20 | Interdisciplinary design-research connecting with Birmingham's professional context and creative scene through alumni, tutors, events and live projects. |

### Stage 2 — 120 credits
*All modules are core*

| Module | Credits | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Architectural Thesis** | 60 | Culmination of critical and creative studies. Research-by-design pedagogy with value and impact beyond the course. Year-long study building on the Material Practices unit work. |
| **Negotiated Practice** | 20 | Extends the Architectural Thesis through a 'negotiated artefact'. Includes opportunities for public dissemination and the final MArch exhibition. |
| **Special Study** | 40 | Major original critical work — either Extended Practice (researched design/production) or a dissertation. Can inform and be informed by all other course modules. |

### Studio Units

Design modules are delivered through vertical Studio Units with focused theoretical frameworks:

- **Extinction Rebellion Architecture** — Using XR's call to action to progress with radical sustainable environments and communities
- **ARENA** — Exploring architecture as a cultural practice, viewing the city as a performance to disrupt
- **Modern Gazetteer** — Taking a close reading of the city to create considered interventions through an economy of means

## Teaching & Assessment

- Modules assessed through a mix of reviews, portfolios, presentations and written submissions
- Studio-based design projects with individual and group tutorials
- Specialist tuition in technology to support each studio unit
- Visiting tutors and critics from local, national and international architectural practices
- 100% coursework — no formal examinations

## Employability

### Enhancing Employability Skills
Upon completing the MArch, you'll be able to produce complex design proposals showing an understanding of current architectural issues. You'll have sound knowledge of procurement, building production and legislation. The course gives you a thorough understanding of the links between design and technology and how architects work with other construction professionals.

Working on live projects and with industry partners will give you a portfolio of projects that engage with collaborative practice, transdisciplinary explorations and highly developed technological resolution.
