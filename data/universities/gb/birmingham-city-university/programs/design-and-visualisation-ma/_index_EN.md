## Overview

The MA Design and Visualisation at Birmingham City University is built to help students thrive in a changing design profession by combining cutting-edge technical expertise with the human qualities that AI cannot replace. Design and visualisation plays a key role in many of today's businesses and organisations — from designing complex animations, brand identity, 3D products, virtual interiors, landscape, architectural visualisation and new conceptual designs.

Students can choose to study from a variety of cross-disciplinary MA modules in which to learn how to apply design visualisation techniques and strategies, choosing from areas such as product design, interior design, graphic design and various other specialisations. The course challenges conventional design presentation techniques and prepares graduates for the future demands of a rapidly evolving design industry. Students engage with Live Projects within the curriculum, offering unique opportunities to work on real projects for real clients.

## Module Details

### Design Visualisation (20 credits)
Explores an iterative-design-centred approach to prototyping design artefacts within a contextual setting. Develops methodologies of visualisation in traditional techniques (2D sketching, technical illustration) and workshop-based scaled model making using high-level 3D processes. Also develops skills in animated and advanced computer-based visualisation techniques such as augmented reality for presenting interactive media.

### Design Realisation (40 credits)
Students choose between a **Product brief** or an **Interior brief**. The Product Option provides a broader understanding of typical product design outputs and associated design processes, drawing from established theories and case studies on product evolution. The Interior Option engages at an advanced level with commercial markets, design processes and models of design management through theoretical and practical analysis.

### Competition or Collaboration (40 credits)
Students choose between a **Product brief** or an **Interior brief**, engaging with externally generated briefs. The Product Option explores alternative concept generation through deconstructing and reconstructing design narratives for future product design sectors. The Interior Option involves advanced critical analysis for mapping design futures of interior products and spaces.

### Co.LAB — Live Project Postgraduate (20 credits)
An opportunity to learn and critically reflect on collaboration skills vital to the Creative Industries. Students work on annually determined elective project briefs from Product, Interior, Architecture and Landscape. May involve live projects with other students or collaboration with staff on research projects.

### Major Project (60 credits)
A sustained, in-depth and theoretically informed research project exploring an area of personal interest. The outcome may take the form of a written dissertation or a practical outcome with accompanying reflective, critical and contextual material.

## Career Prospects

Graduates are equipped for a wide range of roles in the design industry:

- **Design Practice (in-house & consultancy):** TV characters/environments/motion graphics, advertising, films/VFX/PreVis, web design, product configurators, product design, architecture, video games, AR, VR, marketing/communications, infographics/HUDs, navigation systems
- **Design Entrepreneurship:** Self-employment and freelance practice
- **Design Advisory Services:** Digital training manuals, pathfinding, interfaces/GUIs
- **Education/Training/Research:** Teaching design-related topics
- **Medical Research:** e.g., keyhole surgery simulators
- **Design Research:** MPhil/PhD study
