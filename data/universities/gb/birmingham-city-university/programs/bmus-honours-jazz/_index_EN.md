# Jazz BMus (Hons)

## Overview

The BMus (Hons) Jazz course at Royal Birmingham Conservatoire is a specialist course for performers in jazz. Its main aim is to prepare students for a career in the music profession in the field of jazz. The launch of the Eastside Jazz Club has helped consolidate the integral role that Royal Birmingham Conservatoire has to play in a major UK city that enjoys a thriving jazz scene.

Our Jazz Department is based around the Club, where student musicians share a platform and get invaluable side-by-side performance experience with the best on the scene. This course places emphasis on practical work, with the majority of time dedicated to one-to-one lessons, small group coaching and private practice. You'll study modules that have been specifically and skilfully designed for jazz musicians, and we encourage you to explore in depth the relationship between improvisation and composition, which is central to developing your individual voice.

You will graduate as a versatile, professional musician who is knowledgeable about the entrepreneurial opportunities available to you, and aware of the commercial realities for musicians joining today's UK jazz scene as a portfolio musician.

## Course Structure

### Year One (Level 4)

- **Principal Study 1** (40 credits) — One-to-one tuition in jazz performance, standard repertoire, group improvisation
- **Praxis 1** (40 credits) — Composition, jazz harmony, keyboard skills and ear training
- **Listening and Context 1** (20 credits) — Historical development of jazz, listening and analytical skills
- **Professional Portfolio 1: Community Engagement** (20 credits) — Music activities in community/education settings, conducting, workshop facilitation

### Year Two (Level 5)

- **Principal Study 2** (40 credits) — Advanced jazz performance, expanded repertoire
- **Praxis 2** (40 credits) — Further composition and improvisation proficiencies
- **Listening and Context 2** (20 credits) — In-depth knowledge of key moments in jazz history
- **Professional Portfolio 2: Pedagogy and Practice** (20 credits) — Pedagogical skills for the music profession

### Year Three (Level 6)

- **Principal Study 3** (40 credits) — Refining performance identity
- **Praxis 3** (40 credits) — Advanced composition and improvisation
- **Professional Portfolio 3: Collaborative Project** (20 credits)
- **Plus 20 credits from option modules**

### Year Four (Level 7)

- **Final Recital** (60 credits) — Major performance project
- **Professional Portfolio 4** (60 credits) — Career preparation and professional development
