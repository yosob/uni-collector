# Media and Cultural Studies PhD

## Overview

Our Media and Cultural Studies PhD research degree will help you achieve a career in media research or academia. The PhD enables research into a broad range of interests within the fields of media, communication and cultural studies. Your research will be delivered over three years (full-time) or four years (part-time or distance learning).

We support both traditional PhD research presented through an academic thesis and PhD research through practice.

### What's Covered in This Course?

- Individual programmes of research across many areas of interest
- Opportunity to contribute world-leading insight to the Birmingham Centre for Media and Cultural Research (BCMCR)
- On completion, you will have contributed new knowledge or extended existing theory and understanding
- Research-based programme (does not include taught modules)

### Why Choose Us?

- Member of the thriving research culture at BCMCR
- PhD research organised as a programme of professional and academic development
- Individual support and guidance from a team of supervisors
- Strong sense of community among media students and supervisors
- Regular research seminars, conferences and symposia
- Dedicated workspace for PhD research activity
- Opportunities for studentships through Midlands4Cities DTP
- 100% of BCU's research in this area rated four-star or three-star for impact (REF 2021)

- **Start Date:** September 2026 / February 2027
- **Faculty:** Faculty of Arts, Design and Media
- **Campus:** City Centre Campus (Parkside Building)
- **Open to International Students:** Yes (also available via Distance Learning)

## Entry Requirements

- Normally hold or expect to be awarded a Masters degree in a relevant subject area
- Exceptional candidates without a Masters but with a first-class Bachelors degree in media studies may be considered
- Also welcome enquiries from those with appropriate professional experience
- Research proposal required (submitted with application)
- Initial PhD enquiry (max 500 words) required before full application
- **IELTS:** 7.0 overall with no band below 6.5 (international/EU students)

## Research Clusters (BCMCR)

The Birmingham Centre for Media and Cultural Research engages in collaborative work across nine research clusters:

1. **Cultural Activism and Alternative Politics**
2. **Equality, Diversity and Inclusion in the Arts**
3. **Film and the Digital Image**
4. **Game Cultures**
5. **Gender and Sexuality**
6. **History, Heritage and Archives**
7. **Media, Journalism and Place**
8. **Popular Music Studies**
9. **South Asian Media**

## Current Research Interests

We particularly invite enquiries in: amateur/informal cultural production · austerity and the media · comedy · community media and media activism · creative industries, cultural policy and cultural work · cult cinema · cultural entrepreneurship · diasporic and transnational media · film and television audiences · gaming cultures · heritage and archives · journalism · media and cultural history · media ecology · media industries · popular music · race, ethnicity and media · radio studies · screen industries · social media · sport and the media · youth media and culture
