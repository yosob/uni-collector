## Overview

Textile Design explores the potential of fabrics and materials to create innovative designs for fashion, interiors and the creative industries. This four-year programme includes a Foundation Year that introduces you to fundamental creative processes before you progress to the full BA (Hons) Textile Design programme.

You will develop skills in print, knit, weave and embroidery, combining traditional craftsmanship with digital technologies. The course has a strong emphasis on sustainability and responsible design practice.

## Modules

### Foundation Year
- Developing a Narrative — 20 credits
- Understanding Fundamental Processes — 20 credits
- Introducing Trends — 20 credits
- Brand Development — 20 credits
- Signature Project — 40 credits

### Year One
- Creative Industries: Connections — 20 credits
- Textiles for Fashion: Print & Knit — 20 credits
- Textiles for Fashion: Embroidery & Weave — 20 credits
- Textiles for Interiors: Pattern & Digital Processes — 40 credits

### Year Two
- Sustainable Design: Principles & Practice — 20 credits
- Creative Industries: Industry Futures — 20 credits
- Design for Industry — 40 credits
- Textile Craftsmanship — 40 credits

### Year Three
- Work Placement — 20 credits
- Industry Project — 20 credits
- Independent Creative Entrepreneur — 20 credits
- Creative Industries: Research Development and Enquiry — 20 credits
- Textile Collection — 40 credits
- Textile Showcase — 20 credits
- Next Step — 40 credits


## Entry Requirements

80 UCAS Tariff points. A-level, BTEC or equivalent qualifications. IELTS 6.0 with no less than 5.5 in each band for international students. Portfolio may be required.

## Career Prospects

Textile designer, fabric developer, print designer, knitwear designer, interior textiles designer, colourist, design consultant, sustainable design specialist.
