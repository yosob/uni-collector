# Acting MFA | Master of Fine Arts

## Programme Overview

The MFA Acting course at Royal Birmingham Conservatoire is designed for graduates who wish to undertake advanced studies to further their knowledge and widen their experience of acting in order to take up a professional acting career upon graduation. Specifically designed with international students in mind, this full-time programme is delivered intensively over two years.

The delivery is based on the conservatoire model, requiring intensive taught delivery of a minimum of thirty to forty hours per week over a minimum of thirty weeks per year. Its focus is practice-based learning through which students develop the requisite intellectual, imaginative and physical skills of the professional actor. During the course, students form an acting company and each exercise, workshop or production is designed to challenge the student actor and progressively develop their imaginative, intellectual and creative skills.

The course seeks to create independently minded practitioners who are able to apply a variety of methodologies to their creative work. Its aim is to inculcate forward thinking practitioners able to create real employment opportunities for themselves, both in finding work and creating work.

The focus of work is European, with an emphasis on texts from within the British tradition. From Shakespeare to the pantomime, Restoration comedy to contemporary situation comedy, the British tradition of acting focuses on the importance of narrative, depth, dimension and consistency of character, technical precision, and the actor's relationship with the audience.

## Course Structure

### Year One

Your first year follows an integrated learning mode, investigating through practice the key developmental stages of British theatre. In the first two terms, MA and MFA students work together in classes and workshops. In the third term, MFA students form their own company and undertake an extended Shakespeare workshop, investigating the demands of classical acting in greater depth.

**Modules:**
- **Acting Theory and Practice** — Core principles of character creation and expression, exploring Psychological Realism and British acting methodologies
- **Voice and Movement / Practical Voice** — Development of voice, movement and physical skills for the actor
- **Performance Exercise** — Rehearsal-based practice integrating acting, voice and movement
- **Extended Shakespeare Workshop (MFA specific, Term 3)** — Deep investigation of classical acting demands
- **Pedagogy and Placement 1** — Introduction to pedagogical approaches and professional placement

**Note:** MFA students do not take the Research Project module in Year 1 (completed in Year 2).

### Year Two

In the second year, MFA students deepen their learning and further develop their abilities. They undertake a Major Project providing opportunities for personal evaluation and creative development. They also perform in three productions, each selected to challenge and further develop the ensemble and individual work.

**Modules:**
- **Major Project (Research Project)** — Personal research with opportunities for creative development and evaluation
- **Production (x3)** — Three theatrical productions, each selected to challenge the ensemble and individual development
- **Singing OR Voice and Text** — Students choose one (not both)
- **Practical Voice** — Retake with assistant teaching role (supervised by module tutor)
- **Pedagogy and Placement 2 and 3** — Advanced pedagogical study and professional placements
- **Professional Portfolio** — Development of professional materials and career preparation
- **Acting for Camera and Voice Over** — Screen acting and voiceover techniques

### Career Directions

Graduates are equipped with the full range of skills required of the contemporary professional actor — able to engage critically and creatively in their work, achieving professional standards in stage, screen and broadcast roles.
