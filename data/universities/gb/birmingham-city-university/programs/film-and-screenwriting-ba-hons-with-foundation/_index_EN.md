## Overview

Do you have a passion for writing, creating and filmmaking? This Film and Screenwriting with a Foundation Year course offers a unique opportunity to develop your skills in both film production and screenwriting. The Foundation Year prepares you for degree-level study before you progress to the main programme.

You will learn to write compelling scripts, understand film language and storytelling, and develop practical production skills. The course combines creative writing craft with technical filmmaking knowledge.

## Modules

### Foundation Year
- Media Audiences — 20 credits
- Professional and Academic Skills — 20 credits
- Media Production Practice — 20 credits
- Collaborative Media Production — 20 credits
- Media Concepts and Investigation — 20 credits
- Media Project — 20 credits

### Year One
- Screenwriting for Film Genres — 40 credits
- Film and the Principles of Storytelling — 20 credits
- Language of Film — 20 credits
- Screenwriting Primer — 20 credits
- Film Primer — 20 credits

### Year Two
- Writing for TV — 40 credits
- Script Development — 20 credits
- Collaborative Practice — 20 credits
- Adaptation for the Screen — 20 credits
- Cinema of the Seventies — 20 credits
- Pre-Production: Script to Set — 20 credits

### Year Three
- Horror Narratives — 20 credits
- Professional Practice — 40 credits
- Major Project — 60 credits


## Entry Requirements

80 UCAS Tariff points. A-level, BTEC or equivalent qualifications. IELTS 6.0 with no less than 5.5 in each band for international students.

## Career Prospects

Screenwriter, script editor, film director, film producer, script reader, development executive, content writer, film critic.
