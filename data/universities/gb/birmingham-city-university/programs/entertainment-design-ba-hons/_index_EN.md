curriculum_summary: 'Level 4 (Year 1 — 120 credits): What is Entertainment Design?
  (20 credits) — explores four foundational principles: storytelling, space, figure,
  and audience; Tools & Techniques: Meaning (40 credits) — hands-on experimentation
  with analogue and digital processes including drawing, sculpting, modelling, fabrication,
  digital creation, and AI; The Intersection: In Practice (40 credits) — explores
  how the four guiding principles interact, combine, and challenge each other; Exploring
  Direction (20 credits) — reflection on personal values, creative instincts, and
  emerging areas of interest.

  Level 5 (Year 2 — 120 credits): The Intersection: as Discovery (40 credits) — building
  on foundational understanding of how storytelling, space, figure, and audience create
  meaningful experiences; Tools and Techniques: Communication (20 credits) — refining
  visual, verbal, digital, and physical presentation skills; Identifying Direction
  (40 credits) — sustained enquiry into personal design direction within the entertainment
  industry. Plus one optional module (20 credits): Industry Project, Independent Creative
  Entrepreneur, or Work Placement (70-hour placement).

  Level 6 (Year 3 — 120 credits): Tools and Techniques: Refined (40 credits) — pushing
  beyond established skills, focusing on excellence, innovation, and critical thinking;
  Framing Direction (20 credits) — developing the intellectual and creative foundation
  for the final major project; The Intersection: Resolved (60 credits) — self-directed
  final major project demonstrating personal vision and contribution to the future
  of entertainment design.

  Core principles: storytelling, space, figure, and audience. Industry-informed briefs
  and collaborations with partners including Royal Shakespeare Company, Boomtown Festival,
  Katapult, Scruffy Dog Creative Group, Blackhurst and Co, and Digbeth Loc. Studios.'
