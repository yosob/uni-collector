# Conservatoire Research Degrees PhD

## Overview

A PhD at Royal Birmingham Conservatoire will help you create opportunities to develop research skills that support performance, composition or a scholarly career. Whether you are interested in music (including composition, music technology, musicology, performance and jazz) or performing arts (theatre, acting, directing, applied drama, dance and interdisciplinary performance practice), you will be supervised by leading experts in the field.

Choosing to study in a conservatoire environment allows you access to top-level performers, enabling you to situate your research in an environment of practical music making and acting.

### What's Covered in This Course?

- Self-directed, independent study supported by experienced supervisors
- Regular opportunities to present and share your work with other research students
- Access to top-level performers and state-of-the-art £57 million building
- Flexibility to present research in various formats: fully text-based (80,000 words) or practical (composition, performance or scholarly edition) with a written element

### Why Choose Us?

- Thriving research community with particular specialisms in composition, music technology, musicology, performance and performing arts
- Close links with the city's musical and theatrical organisations: CBSO, BCMG, The Rep Theatre, Midlands Arts Centre, Royal Shakespeare Company
- Music students eligible to audition for Conservatoire ensembles
- Both Music and Performing Arts students can take part in practical activities and audit lectures

- Normally hold or be working towards a Masters in a relevant subject area
- Exceptional candidates without a Masters but with a strong undergraduate degree may be considered
- Also welcome enquiries from those with appropriate professional experience
- Research proposal required
- **IELTS:** 7.0 overall with no band below 6.5 (international/EU students)

## Research Specialisms

### Composition
Alternatives to the traditional concert hall · Composer-led collectives · Queerness and Othering · Interdisciplinary composition · Post-genre composition · Composition with minimal means · Community engagement as artistic practice · Composition and environmental concerns

### Musicology
French Music (17th-18th century, Messiaen, Dutilleux) · Late Medieval and Renaissance Music · Performance Practices · Early Music Theory · Music and Historical Soundscapes

### Music Technology
Human-Computer Interaction in music · Electroacoustic composition · Soundscape Composition and Ecomusicology · Ethics of AI in Music Creation · Accessible Digital Musical Instruments

### Performance as Research
Research questions arising from performance practice · Historical performance practice · Contemporary practice including jazz · Mixed mode submission (recordings, live performance, written thesis)

### Performing Arts
Contemporary performance pedagogies · Interdisciplinary and multimedia performance · Theatre making and devising · Dance performance · EcoSomatics and ecological performance practices · Applied theatre · Theatre, politics and activism
