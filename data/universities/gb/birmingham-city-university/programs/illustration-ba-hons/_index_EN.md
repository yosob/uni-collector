# Illustration BA (Hons) (3 years)

> 🎨 **Industry-Connected:** Strong live project engagement with clients including Emma Press, Birmingham City Council, and the Greeting Card Association. Students regularly secure paid commissions through course collaborations.

## Overview

The BA (Hons) Illustration course at Birmingham City University's School of Visual Communication develops your creative and professional illustration practice across a broad range of contexts. From editorial and publishing to fashion and digital media, the course equips you with the skills to succeed in the fast-changing creative industries.

With support from staff, you will develop a real depth of knowledge of illustration practice, as well as other design-related areas. The course covers **editorial illustration, children's books, fashion illustration, sequential illustration, narrative storytelling, surface pattern design, printmaking and digital illustration**.

The programme emphasises **live projects, industry engagement and real-world briefs** from Year 2 onwards, with strong connections to publishers, agencies and commercial clients.

## Course Structure

The programme is structured across three progressive years, each comprising 120 CATS credits (360 total). Strong emphasis on live projects, industry engagement and professional development throughout.

### Year 1 — Foundations of Illustration Practice
*120 credits total*
- **Fundamental Practice: Illustration** (40 credits) — Explore core principles and techniques of illustration through research, sketchbook documentation, creative iteration and making processes. Begin with a dynamic transitional project emphasising individual development and creative thinking.
- **Professional Futures: Communication** (20 credits) — Develop initial career skills through understanding effective communication in the context of emerging design practice. Engage with theoretical frameworks and practical exercises.
- **Moving Stories** (20 credits) — In-depth understanding of narrative processes in illustration. Theory sessions and technical workshops on visual storytelling, with individual and collaborative group projects delivered in various formats.
- **Discuss and Distribute** (40 credits) — Reflective exploration of contemporary illustration through critical analysis and creative production. Develop a unique voice as an independent image-maker while producing work for a public-facing outcome.

### Year 2 — Deepening Practice & Industry Engagement
*120 credits total*
- **Audience & Context** (40 credits) — Deepen knowledge in contemporary illustrative practice. Choose from live, simulated or competition briefs reflecting real industry demands. Develop visual language and direction.
- **Professional Futures: Enterprise & Employability** (20 credits) — Develop professional skills, understanding of enterprise, self-promotion and building a sustainable creative career.
- **Visualising Narratives** (20 credits) — Develop ability to visualise narratives through a range of media and formats with increasing sophistication.
- **Developing Practice: Illustration** (40 credits) — Focus on a specific area of illustration practice with a specially assigned tutor. Supported by seminars and lectures from industry experts.

### Year 3 — Independent Practice & Graduation
*120 credits total*
- **Critical Practice** (40 credits) — Sustained, in-depth research project exploring an area of personal interest. Outcome as written dissertation or practice-based portfolio.
- **Professional Futures: Presentation** (20 credits) — Essential skills and strategies for navigating the design industry. Focus on presentation and employability.
- **Major Project** (40 credits) — Self-directed, sustained research project relevant to illustration. Guided by lecturers with relevant expertise.
- **Negotiated Practice** (20 credits) — Self-directed study with critical analysis to shape ongoing development as a successful creative.

### Optional Professional Placement Year
After Year 2, students may undertake a year-long professional placement (extending the course to four years). Placement year fees are 20% of the full-time course fee.

## Live Projects & Industry Engagement

- **Emma Press** — Vibrant independent publisher running live projects with students; students have been commissioned as a result of this collaboration
- **Birmingham City Council** — Students illustrated an annual report; five pieces of published paid work resulted from this ongoing collaboration
- **Greeting Card Association (GCA)** — Yearly collaborative project culminating in student placements and publishing
- **Duplicate Publishing Fair** — Annual celebration of printed zines, comics, artists' books and alternative publishing. Students organise, market and sell work alongside established artists
