# Digital Animation BA (Hons)

*Formerly: Animation BA (Hons)*

## Overview

BA (Hons) Digital Animation at BCU is a creative, practice-based course that develops your skills in 2D and 3D animation, character performance, and storytelling. You will learn to create original animated content using industry-standard tools and techniques, whilst developing your own unique creative voice. The course covers the full animation production pipeline — from concept development, storyboarding, and visual design through to character animation, compositing, and final output. Students benefit from access to dedicated animation studios equipped with industry-standard software (including Maya, Toon Boom Harmony, and the Adobe Creative Suite), motion capture facilities, and rendering workstations. The course is designed to prepare you for the animation industry, with opportunities for live projects, collaboration with students across the Faculty of Arts, Design and Media, and an optional professional placement year.

## Course Structure

### First Year (Level 4)

Your first year provides the foundational skills and knowledge for animation practice. You will be introduced to 2D and 3D animation techniques, storyboarding, visual communication, and the principles of character performance, alongside developing critical awareness of animation in context.

**Core Modules (120 credits):**

- **2D Fundamentals of Animation** (20 credits) — Explore the fundamental principles of animation through 2D techniques such as drawn animation and cut-out animation. Develop skills in timing, spacing, weight, and overlap, using industry-standard 2D animation software.
- **3D Fundamentals of Animation** (20 credits) — Discover the principles of 3D animation, modelling and texturing using industry-standard software (e.g., Maya). Learn the fundamentals of 3D character rigging, lighting and rendering.
- **Fundamentals of Storyboarding and Sequential Art** (20 credits) — Develop visual storytelling skills through storyboarding and sequential art. Learn to plan animated sequences, structure narratives visually, and create animatics.
- **Visual Communication** (20 credits) — An introduction to the fundamental principles of visual communication, colour theory, composition, and design thinking as they apply to animation practice.
- **Principles of Animation and Character Performance** (20 credits) — Explore the 12 principles of animation and apply them to character performance, creating believable and expressive animated characters.
- **Animation in Context** (20 credits) — Study the history and theory of animation from early experimental work through to contemporary digital practice. Develop critical awareness of animation as an art form and industry.

### Second Year (Level 5)

In your second year you will advance your technical skills in both 2D and 3D animation, develop short film concepts, and deepen your understanding of character performance and critical discourse. Collaborative practice prepares you for teamwork in industry.

**Core Modules (120 credits):**

- **Collaborative Practice** (20 credits) — Work in interdisciplinary teams on a collaborative project, developing teamwork, communication, and project management skills within a creative production environment.
- **3D Animation** (20 credits) — Advance your 3D animation skills with more complex character animation, environments, and effects. Develop proficiency in industry-standard 3D pipelines.
- **Short Film Development** (20 credits) — Develop original concepts for short animated films, from initial idea through to pre-production, including scripting, storyboarding, and visual development.
- **Character Animation and Performance** (20 credits) — Deepen character animation skills with a focus on performance, emotion, dialogue, and creating nuanced, believable animated performances.
- **2D Animation** (20 credits) — Advance 2D animation techniques with more complex sequences, character animation, and experimental approaches to 2D moving image.
- **Animation Discourse** (20 credits) — Engage with critical debates, theories, and discourses around animation as a creative practice. Develop research and analytical skills.

### Final Year (Level 6)

Your final year focuses on producing a substantial animation project and a polished final animated film. You will apply all skills developed throughout the course, supported by research methodologies and professional pre-production and production practices.

**Core Modules (120 credits):**

- **Major Project** (60 credits) — Produce a substantial, self-directed animation project that demonstrates your creative identity, technical skills, and professional competence. This is the culmination of your studies.
- **Research and Editing** (20 credits) — Develop research methodologies and editing techniques for animation, supporting your Major Project and professional development.
- **Film Pre-Production** (20 credits) — Plan and prepare for your final film production, including concept art, storyboarding, animatics, design, and production scheduling.
- **Film Production** (20 credits) — Execute the production of your final animated film, applying all skills developed throughout the course to produce a polished, professional-standard animated short.

### Professional Placement Year (Optional)

An optional professional placement year is available between Year 2 and Final Year, providing the opportunity to gain industry experience with an animation studio, games company, or creative agency.
