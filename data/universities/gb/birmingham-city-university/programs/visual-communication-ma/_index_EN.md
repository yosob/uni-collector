# Visual Communication MA

## Overview

Visual Communication MA at BCU is a dynamic and progressive course that challenges the way we look at images and text in a present-day context. In a world saturated with visual stimulus, students are trained to navigate through this complexity, finding ways to impart new information through graphic design, typography, moving image, illustration and photography. The course has existed for over 50 years, embracing technological advances to equip students with the essential knowledge and skills needed for the new emerging creative industries.

Students learn in studios, workshops, and through partnerships with organisations and industry. The course offers two specialist pathways — **Graphic Communication** and **Illustration** — studied under a single MA Visual Communication award. Students are expected to explore, experiment, and push the boundaries of design and image making.

## Specialist Pathways

### Graphic Communication

Focuses on graphic design, typography, and visual communication through print and digital media. Develops advanced skills in design thinking, visual language and creative problem-solving.

### Illustration

Focuses on illustration, image-making, and visual storytelling across various media platforms. Develops a personal visual language and critical approach to contemporary illustration practice.

## Course Structure

### Core Modules (160 credits)

- **Advanced Critical Practice** (40 credits) — Equips you with essential skills and knowledge for your future career. Explores the role of effective communication in emerging design practice through theoretical frameworks and practical exercises to develop contextual understanding.
- **Research through Practice** (20 credits) — Introduces creative research methods and methodologies used in arts and design production. Focus on research through practice where practice itself serves a research purpose.
- **Contextual and Professional Practice** (40 credits) — Develops deeper analytical and evaluative skills, promoting critical awareness within your specialism, advancing professional relationships through externally generated professional briefs and/or self-directed negotiated briefs.
- **Professional Project** (60 credits) — An in-depth and theoretically informed research project exploring an area of personal interest. Viewed as a first step in professional progression rather than a final step in the degree.

### Optional Modules (choose 20 credits)

- **Industry Project** (20 credits) — Apply knowledge and skills to an external, professional brief set in negotiation with an external client/agency/community. Collaborate across disciplines.
- **Independent Creative Entrepreneur** (20 credits) — Develop entrepreneurial skills for a portfolio creative career. The art of promoting and pitching ideas for an independent creative journey.
- **Work Placement** (20 credits) — A 70-hour work placement (concentrated block or spread out). Develop professional attributes and subject-specific skills with support from academic staff and ADM Careers+.

### Professional Placement Route (Optional)

Extends the course to 18 months with an integrated placement component.
