## Overview

Welcome to the BA Fashion Business and Promotion course — this course opens doors to a multitude of roles in the fashion industry. Explore Marketing, Buying, Public Relations, Trend Forecasting, Event Experience and much more. Get ready to dive deep into the world of fashion — your gateway to a world of endless possibilities in the fashion industry.

The first two years of the course allow you to explore the multi-faceted disciplines that are integral to the way that the fashion business operates. The third year then allows you to refine your expertise through negotiated projects and tailor your work to become an expert in your chosen field.

Guest speakers have included buyers from George and River Island, social media and PR experts from Missguided, marketing and PR industry managers and fashion bloggers. You'll gain commercial awareness through a series of live industry projects, industrial placements and opportunities for competitions.

### Why Choose Us?

- We utilise our industry connections to bring your live briefs from the likes of Named Collective, Next, Gymshark, DRPG, Sephora and Selfridges.
- Interested in the practical side of Fashion? You have full access to all our studios and workshops whilst you are a student here.
- Our course team have all worked in the industry for brands such as Alexander McQueen, Armani, George, Debenhams, French Connection and Harrods, and bring their huge wealth of knowledge into all our projects.
- We offer weekly academic tutorials to support your progress and ensure you are getting the most out of the course.
- We support our graduates with our alumni programme, sharing industry jobs and exciting opportunities.

## Curriculum

### Year One (Level 4) — 120 credits

All modules are **core**:

| Module | Credits |
|---|---|
| Creative Industries: Connections | 20 |
| Fashion Business Context | 20 |
| Fashion Activism: A Catalyst for Change | 20 |
| Buying & Merchandising for the Future | 40 |
| Global Fashion Spaces: Experiential Retail | 20 |

**Creative Industries: Connections** — Explore the connections between creative fashion and textile industries in the past, and how this informs present and future trends. You will be introduced to the way individuals have influenced culture, freedom of expression and experimentation, and how this pushes boundaries and explores identity.

**Fashion Business Context** — A comprehensive overview of the diverse sectors within the fashion industry, introducing the critical analysis business model of P.E.S.T.L.E factors (Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, and Environmental). Explore industry sectors from luxury to budget and metaverse.

**Fashion Activism: A Catalyst for Change** — Explore the powerful intersection of fashion and activism. Empowered with knowledge and skills to contribute to the role of fashion in societal and environmental shifts — from historical movements to contemporary campaigns addressing ethical practices, sustainability, diversity, and body positivity.

**Buying & Merchandising for the Future** — Introduction to buying and merchandising principles, the roles and responsibilities of the product team, and the buying cycle in relation to the fashion industry. Includes a live industry project developing a strategy for a new range concept.

**Global Fashion Spaces: Experiential Retail** — Explore new and emerging formats for retail across fashion and lifestyle brands on a global scale. Delve into the evolution of retail spaces, consumer driven demand, technological advancements and the impact of socio-cultural factors on the retail landscape.

### Year Two (Level 5) — 120 credits

**Core modules (100 credits):**

| Module | Credits |
|---|---|
| Creative Industries: Industry Futures | 20 |
| Marketing Strategies | 40 |
| Experience Economy | 40 |

**Optional modules (choose 20 credits):**

| Module | Credits |
|---|---|
| Work Placement | 20 |
| Industry Project | 20 |
| Independent Creative Entrepreneur | 20 |

**Creative Industries: Industry Futures** — Equips students with essential skills and knowledge for a successful transition into industry work placement and beyond. Focuses on professional practices, industry expectations, and personal development strategies through theoretical knowledge, practical exercises, case studies, and industry guest lectures.

**Marketing Strategies** — Immerses students in the dynamic world of promoting and positioning fashion brands. Explores fundamental principles of marketing and public relations tailored to the fashion business — from strategic planning to creative execution.

**Experience Economy** — Delves into the transformative realm of the experience economy, emphasising the creation and delivery of compelling and immersive consumer experiences. Positioned at the intersection of marketing, business strategy, and consumer behaviour.

**Work Placement** — Develop professional attributes and subject skills through experience in the workplace, with critical reflection on learning in that context. Students arrange their own placement with support from academic staff and BCU Careers.

**Industry Project** — Apply knowledge and skills to an external, professional brief. The brief is set by or in discussion with an external client/agency/community, considering sustainability and relevant UN Sustainable Development Goals.

**Independent Creative Entrepreneur** — Build skills to promote and 'pitch' ideas to others. Develop a portfolio career making good use of creative skills — producing creative artefacts, performing, offering a service or consultancy.

### Professional Placement Year (Optional) — 120 credits

In order to qualify for the award of BA (Hons) Fashion Business and Promotion with Professional Placement Year, a student must successfully complete the Professional Placement Year module.

### Year Three (Level 6) — 120 credits

All modules are **core**:

| Module | Credits |
|---|---|
| Creative Industries: Research Development and Enquiry | 20 |
| Major Concept Strategy | 20 |
| Creative Realisation | 40 |
| Self Promotion and Commercialisation | 40 |

**Creative Industries: Research Development and Enquiry** — Further develop research and inquiry skills within the context of the fashion and textile industry. Undertake a range of research methods on a topic of personal interest, critically analyse findings, and present conclusions and recommendations in a proposal that feeds into an independent project.

**Major Concept Strategy** — Showcase knowledge, critical thinking, and practical skills acquired throughout the academic journey. Develop and execute a major project reflecting individual interests and aspirations within the landscape of fashion business, incorporating fashion design, business strategy, marketing, sustainability, and experiential concepts.

**Creative Realisation** — Focus on execution of creative outcomes from the previous Major Project business plan. Conceptualize and implement a project demonstrating a solution for a professional world, applying innovation, sustainability, and market relevance to address real-world challenges.

**Self Promotion and Commercialisation** — Transition from academic study to the professional arena. Reflect on academic study and work experience skills, develop self-promotion skills for a chosen career path, and set out an action plan for the future. Optimize promotion through relevant digital platforms.

## Employability

### Enhancing Your Employability Skills

The course aims to:

- Produce industry-ready graduates who can creatively contribute to fashion and its related industries in local, UK or global markets.
- Provide opportunities to explore, practice and challenge the inter-relationships between the commercial environment, creative thinking and technical practice.
- Develop student confidence and professionalism through fluency in verbal communication, as well as coherent written and visual presentation skills.
- Stimulate commercial awareness through project management, live and team projects, industrial liaison and visits, industrial placement and competition.
- Instil cooperation, competition and an entrepreneurial approach in all student achievements.

### Placements

Students have the opportunity to undertake **72 hours of work placement** at the end of the second year, supported by the Careers team and a series of workshops. A **Professional Placement Year** option is also available for longer-term placements.

Previous placements include working with leading brands like **Superdry, Selfridges, Next, Primark, DUNE London**, taking on roles in blogging, social media, PR and buying.
