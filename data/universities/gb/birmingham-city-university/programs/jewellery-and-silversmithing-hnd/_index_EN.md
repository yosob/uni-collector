## Overview

Birmingham City University's Higher National Diploma course has an excellent reputation within the jewellery industry and is aimed at those who are looking for a practical, industry-focused qualification in jewellery and silversmithing.

Based in the heart of Birmingham's famous Jewellery Quarter, you will learn fundamental hand skills, silversmithing, production processes, gemmology, and CAD for bespoke production. The course provides a strong foundation for professional practice or progression to a full degree.

## Modules

### Year One
- Fundamental Hand Skills — 40 credits
- Fundamental Silversmithing Skills — 20 credits
- Fundamental Production Processes — 40 credits
- Fine Jewellery — 20 credits

### Year Two
- Introduction to Gemmology — 20 credits
- CAD for Bespoke Production — 20 credits
- Silversmithing and Objects — 20 credits
- Advanced Production Processes — 40 credits
- Professional Context — 20 credits
- Specialist Practice — 40 credits
- Live Project — 20 credits


## Entry Requirements

64 UCAS Tariff points. A-level, BTEC or equivalent qualifications. IELTS 6.0 with no less than 5.5 in each band for international students. Portfolio may be required.

## Career Prospects

Jewellery maker, silversmith, production jeweller, CAD technician, bench jeweller, gemmologist, jewellery designer, workshop manager.
