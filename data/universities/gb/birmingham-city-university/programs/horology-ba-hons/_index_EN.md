# Horology BA (Hons)

## Overview

BA (Hons) Horology at BCU is one of the only courses of its kind in the UK, combining traditional craftsmanship with cutting-edge technology to study the art and science of time measurement. You will develop practical and analytical skills to identify and resolve complex faults in clocks and watches, using both fine hand skills and machining techniques. The course covers the theory of horology alongside valuable practical restoration skills that are in high demand within the industry, including Computer Aided Design (CAD) using Solidworks, rapid prototyping, and traditional repair and conservation techniques.

You will study at the internationally-renowned School of Jewellery in Birmingham's famous Jewellery Quarter, benefiting from outstanding industry partnerships with luxury brands such as Richemont (Cartier, IWC, Panerai, Piaget, Jaeger-LeCoultre, Montblanc), LVMH (Tag Heuer, Zenith, Bulgari, Hublot), and Christopher Ward, which offer work placements, mentoring, and commercial experience. The course has a strong employability record with graduates working for brands such as Cartier, Rolex, Tag Heuer, and the British Museum.

## Course Structure

### First Year

Develop foundational horological knowledge and practical skills. You will learn the fundamentals of clock and watch mechanisms, production techniques, and specialist horological hand skills.

**Core Modules (100 credits):**

- **Introduction to Horology** (HOR4010, 20 credits) — Foundation module introducing the principles of horology, the history of timekeeping, and fundamental clock and watch mechanisms.
- **Production Techniques** (HOR4011, 40 credits) — Core practical module developing machining and manufacturing skills essential for horological practice, including use of lathes, milling machines, and other workshop equipment.
- **Specialist Horological Skills** (HOR4012, 40 credits) — Develop specialist hand skills for clock and watch servicing, repair, and restoration, building proficiency in fine mechanical work.

**Optional Modules (choose 20 credits):**

- **CAD for Bespoke Production** (JEW4023, 20 credits) — Introduction to Computer Aided Design using Solidworks for creating bespoke horological components.
- **Introduction to Gemmology** (GEM4007, 20 credits) — Introduction to the study of gemstones, covering identification, properties, and their relevance to luxury goods industries.

### Second Year

Advance to commercial-level servicing skills and production techniques. Develop professional repair capabilities alongside optional modules in commercial awareness, luxury branding, work placement, and live projects.

**Core Modules (80 credits):**

- **Commercial Servicing Skills** (HOR5012, 40 credits) — Develop professional-standard servicing and repair skills for commercial clock and watch work, meeting industry quality benchmarks.
- **Advanced Production Techniques** (HOR5013, 40 credits) — Build on Year 1 production skills with advanced manufacturing techniques, including complex component fabrication and precision machining.

**Optional Modules (choose 40 credits):**

- **Commercial Awareness** (HOR5014, 20 credits) — Develop understanding of business and commercial issues relevant to the horological industry.
- **Luxury Jewellery Branding** (JEW5027, 20 credits) — Explore branding, marketing, and commercial strategies within the luxury jewellery and watch industries.
- **Work Placement** (ADM5000, 20 credits) — Undertake a work placement in a relevant horological or luxury goods industry setting.
- **Live Project** (ADM5001, 20 credits) — Work on a real-world brief set by an industry partner.

### Professional Placement Year (Optional)

Spend a full year with an employer after Year 2. Fees are 20% of the full-time course fee for that year.

### Final Year

Focus on a major horological project of your own design and develop comprehensive employment skills for entry into the professional industry.

**Core Modules (120 credits):**

- **Major Project: Horology** (HOR6009, 60 credits) — Design and manufacture a substantial horological piece (clock or watch) demonstrating technical mastery, creative vision, and project management skills.
- **Employment Skills** (HOR6008, 60 credits) — Develop comprehensive professional and employment skills, building a portfolio that demonstrates professional capabilities to prospective employers.
