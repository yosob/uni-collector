# Digital Film Production BSc (Hons)

## Overview

BSc (Hons) Digital Film Production at Birmingham City University is a practice-led, industry-focused programme that prepares you for careers across the film, television, and digital media industries. As an **ARRI Certified Film School**, BCU provides direct access to industry-standard cinema cameras and professional production workflows.

Ranked **Top 10 in the UK for Film Production and Photography** (Guardian League Tables 2024, 2025, 2026), the course covers the complete filmmaking pipeline — from script development and pre-production through to shooting, post-production, and distribution. You will gain hands-on experience in cinematography, directing, producing, editing, sound design, visual effects, and colour grading.

The course includes live briefs with industry clients, collaborative projects with Curzon Street Studios, and an optional professional placement year.

## Course Structure

### Year One (Level 4)

Students build foundational knowledge in filmmaking principles, visual storytelling, and technical production skills. Core concepts include cinematography fundamentals, editing techniques, sound recording for film, and introduction to screenwriting. Students begin working with professional camera systems and editing software.

**Core Modules (120 credits)** — Module names require verification from the BCU course specification.

### Year Two (Level 5)

Students progress to advanced production techniques including directing, producing, colour grading, visual effects compositing, and multi-camera studio production. Collaborative projects simulate real-world film production environments, including live briefs with external clients. Advanced post-production workflows and industry-standard delivery formats are covered.

**Core Modules (120 credits)**

### Professional Placement Year (Optional)

Spend a full year working in the film, television, or digital media industry after Year 2. Fees are 20% of the full-time course fee for that year. Previous placement hosts include leading production companies, broadcasters, and post-production houses.

### Final Year (Level 6)

Students specialise in their chosen area of filmmaking and complete a major individual or collaborative film project. Advanced topics include motion control cinematography (BOLT JR+), HDR workflows, immersive media, and professional portfolio development. Focus on career preparation, industry networking, and festival submission strategies.

**Core Modules (120 credits)**
