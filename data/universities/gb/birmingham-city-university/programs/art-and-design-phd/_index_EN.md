# Art and Design PhD

## Overview

A PhD in Art and Design at Birmingham City University will help you create opportunities to develop research skills that support professional practice, research and/or academic careers within the fields of art, design and the creative industries. You will study in a world-class research environment which enables both practice-led and/or entirely text-based PhD research to be developed to the highest level.

A PhD enables you to follow a programme of self-directed, independent study, supported by experienced supervisors who are themselves experts in their area. You will also be supported by the wider research community in Art and Design and will have regular opportunities to attend research seminars, conferences and symposia.

### Why Choose Us?

- You will become a member of the thriving research culture in Art and Design across the Schools of Architecture and Design, Art, Fashion and Textiles, Jewellery and Visual Communication
- World-class research environment supporting both practice-led and text-based research
- We support a range of thesis submissions, including a combination of written and practice elements as well as a traditional written thesis
- Dedicated workspace for research students in the contemporary Parkside building, or historic School of Art and School of Jewellery buildings
- Eligible for PhD studentships via the Midlands4Cities Doctoral Training Partnership

## Research Clusters

### Art Activisms
The political dimensions of contemporary cultural production, art as activism, intersections with civil society, political movements and social practices.

### Design Ecologies
Exploring the relationship between designed artefacts, systems and environments in the context of climate crisis, biodiversity loss and resource depletion.

### Made and Worn
Investigating how crafted, manufactured and worn artefacts operate as vehicles of meaning, at the intersection of craft/material cultures and the study of fashion and dress.

### Material Encounters in Art and Design
Exploring aspects of materiality: tangibility, touch, digital materiality, language and form, uncertainty, temporality, movement, health, medium specificity, image.

### Urban Cultures
Interdisciplinary research from architecture, landscape architecture, conservation, design management, and inclusive design. Exploring designed and natural environments and their impact on health and wellbeing.
