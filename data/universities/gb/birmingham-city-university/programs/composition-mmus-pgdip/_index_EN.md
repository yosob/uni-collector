# Composition MMus / PgDip / PgCert

## Overview

The principal aim of our Master of Music (MMus) Composition course is to provide you with knowledge, skills, and confidence to succeed in the music profession as a composer, giving you a head-start in addressing the challenges involved in becoming a professional musician. The course is suitable for both recent graduates and suitably qualified mature students.

Our composition route actively celebrates the diversity of today's musical genres, and fosters artists who challenge, innovate, and transform notions of artistic practice today. You'll spend most of your time writing music for professional and student ensembles, whether you choose to respond to our set briefs or work on your own projects. You'll be encouraged to express yourself in multiple musical and artistic genres.

By studying composition within a thriving conservatoire setting, you'll have excellent opportunities to collaborate with performers and to have your works performed.

### What's Covered in This Course?

- Regular tailored, one-to-one tuition from leading UK and internationally renowned composers, performers, practitioners, teachers, and researchers
- Group classes: Postgraduate Forum, Media, Analysis for Composers, and Interdisciplinary and Collaborative Practice
- Opportunities to participate in a wide variety of creative composition projects, including interdisciplinary collaborations
- Frequent opportunities to have your music performed
- Access to forums, seminars and masterclasses with distinguished visiting artists, composers, and performers
- Access to the Conservatoire's broad range of ensemble activities, including chamber music, operatic productions, Brass Band, Wind Orchestra, Symphony Orchestra, Thallein (Contemporary Music) Ensemble, and the Folk Ensemble
- Wide-ranging Professional Development modules to tailor your course to your individual needs
- Networking with leading professionals from around the world

### 12-Month Route

A 12-month intensive MMus route may be proposed for candidates who demonstrate an exceptional level of achievement and a strong academic record. Contact conservatoire.admissions@bcu.ac.uk for eligibility.

## Entry Requirements

- UK students should normally hold an honours degree, ideally but not necessarily in Music
- Non-UK students should hold a Bachelor's degree or similar degree-equivalent diploma
- **Audition/Portfolio:** Submit a portfolio of compositions; live audition may also be required
- **IELTS:** 6.0 overall with no less than 5.5 in each band (international/EU students)

## Course Structure

### PgCert (60 credits)
- **Principal Study 1: Composition** (60 credits) — Advanced training in composition: practical skills (instrumentation, voice leading, rhythm, melody, harmony, presentation) and critical/intellectual skills (creative thinking, aesthetic awareness, self-reflection)

### PgDip (120 credits)
- **Principal Study 1: Composition** (60 credits)
- **Career Development** (20 credits) — Self-promotion, self-management, funding and wellbeing for the 21st-century musician
- **Professional Development Option** (40 credits) — Choose from a broad menu of optional modules

### MMus (180 credits)
- **Principal Study 1: Composition** (60 credits)
- **Career Development** (20 credits)
- **Professional Development Option** (40 credits)
- **Principal Study 2: Composition** (60 credits) — Further advancement and a major composition portfolio

### Optional Professional Development Modules
A wide menu of options is available to help tailor your course to your career aspirations.

### Professional Placement Year (Optional)

An optional 20-week Professional Placement towards the end of your Masters degree enables you to further improve your employability skills and evidence your professional skills at the point of entry to the postgraduate job market.
