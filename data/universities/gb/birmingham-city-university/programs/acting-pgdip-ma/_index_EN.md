# Acting PgDip/MA | Royal Birmingham Conservatoire

## Programme Overview

Do you already have a drama or theatre arts-based degree? Do you want to take the next step towards a career in acting? The postgraduate Acting course at Royal Birmingham Conservatoire is designed to help you further your knowledge and widen your experience of acting, putting you in a position to launch a professional acting career when you graduate.

The aim is to support you in becoming an independently minded performer and practitioner who can apply a variety of methodologies to creative work. You will leave with a forward-thinking mindset, able to create real employment opportunities for yourself — both in finding work and creating work.

You will have opportunities to enhance your experience and understanding of acting by focusing on the British and European acting traditions. Forming an acting company alongside other postgraduate students, each exercise, workshop or production is designed to challenge you and develop the imaginative, intellectual and creative skills required for acting. The focus of work within the repertoire is European initially and widens to include repertoire from World theatre as the course progresses. You will pay particular attention to the ensemble, the tools of psychological realism, and the demands of classical texts, professional preparation, and personal research.

## Course Structure

The course is based on the conservatoire model which requires an intensive approach to teaching and learning. Focus is on practice-based learning, through which you develop the intellectual, imaginative and physical skills required of professional actors.

As a Master's student, you will be expected to take a proactive attitude toward study with timetabled classes as well as time for self-directed study. Throughout the course, you will be taught through a series of modules covering different aspects of the acting field, teaching you a diverse, yet specialised combination of knowledge and skill.

### PgDip Modules (120 credits)

In order to complete the PgDip, students must successfully complete all core modules totalling 120 credits.

### MA Modules (180 credits)

In order to complete the MA, students must successfully complete all core modules totalling 180 credits, including a research project.

### Key Areas of Study

- **British and European Acting Traditions** — Psychological realism, classical texts
- **Ensemble Work** — Forming an acting company with fellow postgraduate students
- **Voice and Movement** — Physical and vocal skills for the professional actor
- **Classical Texts** — Shakespeare and beyond
- **Solo Performance Project** — Unique to this programme, performed as part of the yearly MA FEST
- **Professional Preparation** — Industry readiness and career development

## Career Prospects

### Career Directions

Graduates are equipped to launch professional acting careers, having developed the full range of skills required of the contemporary professional actor. The course develops forward-thinking practitioners who can create employment opportunities for themselves.

### Industry Connections

Royal Birmingham Conservatoire staff have vast experience, both within the UK and internationally. Staff have worked with well-known individuals, projects and companies such as the BBC, the RSC, the NHS, British repertory and festival theatres.

### Notable Alumni

- **Nicola Coughlan** — Star of *Bridgerton* and *Derry Girls*

### MA FEST and Beyond

One of the most unique features of the programme is the solo performance project, performed as part of the yearly MA FEST. These performances go on to venues such as:
- Edinburgh Fringe
- Camden/Brighton Fringe
- Birmingham Comedy Festival
- Strawberry One Act Play Festival (NYC) — where former students have won major acting awards
