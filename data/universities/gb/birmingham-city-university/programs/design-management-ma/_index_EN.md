# Design Management MA

## Overview

MA Design Management at Birmingham City University is a transformative programme that bridges the gap between design and business. It equips students with the strategic, leadership and entrepreneurial skills needed to manage design processes, lead creative teams, and drive innovation within organisations. Open to applicants from any discipline — designers and non-designers alike — the course develops creative thinking, design research capabilities, and business acumen.

Students benefit from industry-engaged projects with companies such as GKN (FTSE 100), a six-week optional placement, and a curriculum focused on real-world design management challenges. The programme is delivered by the Birmingham School of Architecture and Design and offers multiple study modes including full-time, part-time, work-based learning, and a January start option.

## Course Structure

### Full-Time (September Start) — Semester Structure

**Semester 1:**
- **Design Research** (20 credits) — Develops creative research methods and methodologies used in design and arts production. Focus on research through practice.
- **Professional Development** (20 credits) — Equips students with essential professional skills for careers in design management.
- **Design Strategy and Innovation** (20 credits) — Explores strategic approaches to design management and innovation processes.

**Semester 2:**
- **Global Perspectives** (20 credits) — Examines design management in an international and cross-cultural context.
- **Leadership and Design Entrepreneurship** (40 credits) — Develops leadership skills for managing creative teams. Covers entrepreneurship, intellectual property law, finance, sustainability, marketing, and technology.

**Semester 3:**
- **Major Project: Design Management** (60 credits) — An in-depth research project integrating research skills, strategic thinking, and professional practice.

### Professional Placement Route (Optional)

Extends the course to 18 months with an integrated placement component (60 additional credits at Level 6), totalling 240 credits.
