# Fashion Design BA (Hons)

## Overview

Our BA Fashion Design course will provide you with the fundamental skills needed to work within the fashion industry, including fashion theory, commercial and conceptual design, trend research and garment development. You will learn design skills such as CAD, live fashion drawing, fashion illustration, fashion flats, styling and photoshoots. The course benefits from a £340 million investment in state-of-the-art facilities at the City Centre Campus.

In your final year, you will design and produce a collection of garments plus associated portfolio work for your major project, with a focus on either the menswear or womenswear market sectors.

## Course Structure

### Year One

In your first year you are introduced to a broad range of fashion studies theory and technical skills, building a sound base for further study.

**Core Modules (120 credits):**

- **Creative Industries: Connections** (20 credits) — Explore connections between creative fashion and textile industries past and present, and how this informs future trends.
- **Fundamental Fashion Design Skills** (20 credits) — Unleash your creativity and explore the role of the fashion designer through a multidisciplinary journey of garment crafting and design.
- **Fashion Design Technology** (20 credits) — Explore traditional flat pattern cutting techniques including draping, stand manipulation, and Lectra technology.
- **Fashion Systems** (40 credits) — Systems thinking for creating impactful designs, exploring fashion archives, historical garments and contemporary trends.
- **Fashion Design Manifesto** (20 credits) — AI-integrated problem-solving approach to design, developing samples for your design pack.

### Year Two

Develop advanced research, design, presentation and technical skills, preparing for industry work experience.

**Core Modules (100 credits):**

- **Creative Industries: Industry Futures** (20 credits) — Essential skills for successful transition into industry work placement.
- **Fashion Design Tailoring** (40 credits) — Traditional bespoke tailoring techniques including padding, pitch, fit, hand and pad stitch, and moulding.
- **Fashion Design Technology** (40 credits) — Experimental module merging digital design, advanced technical skills, and sustainability commitment.

**Optional Modules (choose 20 credits):**

- **Work Placement** (20 credits)
- **Industry Project** (20 credits)
- **Independent Creative Entrepreneur** (20 credits)

### Professional Placement Year (Optional)

Between Year 2 and Year 3, you can opt to take a professional placement year to gain valuable industry experience.

### Year Three

Design and produce a collection of garments for your major project.

**Core Modules (120 credits):**

- **Creative Industries: Research Development and Enquiry** (20 credits) — Advanced research and inquiry skills within fashion and textiles.
- **Fashion Design Pre-Collection (FMP)** (40 credits) — Develop a personalised design methodology and showcase your unique creative perspective.
- **Fashion Design Collection Construction** (20 credits) — Meticulous construction of a signature collection aligned with industry standards.
- **Fashion Design Industry Portfolio** (40 credits) — Create your own Lookbook photoshoot and develop presentation and curation skills.
