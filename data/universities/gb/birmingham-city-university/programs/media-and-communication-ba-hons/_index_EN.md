## Overview

How media is made, who makes it, how it is consumed, and whose interests it serves are key questions for our time. Our BA (Hons) Media and Communication degree will help you develop the critical understanding and practical skills needed to navigate and shape this rapidly changing landscape.

You will develop skills in media analysis, content creation, and communication strategy while gaining a deep understanding of media industries, audiences, and the cultural impact of media.

## Modules

### Year One
- Popular Culture — 20 credits
- Understanding Texts, Industries and Audiences — 20 credits
- Investigating Texts, Industries and Audiences — 20 credits
- Image and Sound — 20 credits
- Creating Media Content — 20 credits
- Community and Industry Collaboration — 20 credits

### Year Two
- Cultural Theory — 20 credits
- Creative Industries — 20 credits
- Identity and the Media — 20 credits
- Work Placement — 20 credits
- Live Project — 20 credits
- Collaborative Practice — 20 credits

### Year Three
- Media for Social Change — 20 credits
- Major Project - Preparation — 20 credits
- Major Project - Dissertation — 40 credits
- Major Project - Hybrid — 40 credits
- Major Project - Production — 40 credits


## Entry Requirements

112 UCAS Tariff points. A-level, BTEC or equivalent qualifications. IELTS 6.0 with no less than 5.5 in each band for international students.

## Career Prospects

Media analyst, communications officer, content strategist, PR executive, social media manager, media researcher, digital marketing executive, brand consultant.
