## Overview

User Experience Design MSc at Birmingham City University is a **conversion course** that provides students with the skills and experience required to work professionally in the User Experience (UX) sector. The programme covers the full UX design process — from user research and interaction design through to front-end development and immersive technologies.

Students learn to create digital products and services that are user-centred, accessible and visually compelling. The course combines theoretical knowledge with hands-on practical skills, including human-centred design methodologies, UX development techniques, visual interface design, research and user testing, accessibility standards, and advanced immersive technologies such as AR and VR.

Based at the **Curzon Building** on the City Centre Campus, students have access to industry-standard facilities and benefit from Birmingham's growing digital and tech sector. The programme is designed for graduates from **any discipline** looking to transition into UX roles. An **optional Professional Placement year** is available, extending the course to two years full-time.

## Employability

The UX sector is one of the fastest-growing areas in the digital economy. Graduates are prepared for roles including:

- **UX Designer** — user-centred product design
- **UI Designer** — visual interface design
- **UX Researcher** — user research and usability testing
- **Interaction Designer** — interaction patterns and behaviours
- **Product Designer** — end-to-end product design
- **Front-End Developer (UX-focused)** — implementing UX designs
- **Accessibility Specialist** — inclusive digital experiences
- **Service Designer** — holistic service design
