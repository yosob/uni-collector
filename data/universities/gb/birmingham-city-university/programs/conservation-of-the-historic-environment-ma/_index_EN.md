## Overview

The Conservation of the Historic Environment MA at Birmingham City University is a practice-oriented postgraduate programme designed for professionals and graduates who want to develop specialist knowledge and skills in the conservation and management of historic buildings and environments. Delivered on a part-time basis over two years, the course combines academic rigour with practical, hands-on experience through workshops, site visits, and real-world project work.

Based at the City Centre Campus in the Parkside Building, the programme is run by the Birmingham School of Architecture and Design — one of the largest and most established architecture schools in the UK. The course benefits from strong links with heritage organisations, conservation professionals, and local historic environments, providing students with invaluable networking and professional development opportunities.

The programme is available at three award levels: MA (180 credits), PgDip (120 credits), and PgCert (60 credits), allowing students to tailor their study commitment to their professional circumstances.

## Entry Requirements

- A good honours degree (minimum 2:1) in a relevant subject, plus some built environment experience or practical construction experience in heritage and conservation
- Applicants with a 2:2 degree and significant professional experience in a conservation-related field may also be considered
- International applicants must demonstrate English language proficiency (IELTS 6.0 overall, with no band below 5.5)
- Applications from professionals with extensive relevant experience but without a first degree may be considered on an individual basis

## Course Structure

Students study one module per term, with three terms per academic year over two years. The programme is structured to build knowledge progressively — from foundational conservation principles to advanced practice and research.

### Year 1

**Term 1: Conservation Philosophy and Practice**
This module introduces the philosophical and theoretical foundations of conservation, exploring key concepts including authenticity, significance, and values-based approaches. Students examine the evolution of conservation thought from the 19th century to the present day.

**Term 2: Heritage Legislation and Policy**
Students explore the legal and policy frameworks that govern heritage conservation in the UK, including listed building legislation, conservation area controls, and international charters and conventions.

**Term 3: Building Investigation and Analysis**
This practical module develops skills in investigating and analysing historic buildings, including methods of surveying, recording, and assessing building fabric, construction techniques, and materials.

### Year 2

**Term 4: Conservation Technology and Materials**
An in-depth study of traditional building materials and techniques, including stone, brick, timber, lime, and metalwork. Students learn about the causes of decay and deterioration, and appropriate repair and conservation methods.

**Term 5: Heritage Management**
This module examines the broader context of heritage management, including heritage asset management, funding and regeneration, community engagement, and sustainable approaches to the historic environment.

**Term 6: Major Project / Dissertation (MA only)**
Students undertake a substantial research project or dissertation on a topic of their choice related to the conservation of the historic environment. This allows students to apply their learning to a specific area of interest and demonstrate advanced research skills.

## Teaching & Learning

- Lectures and seminars by academic staff and visiting conservation professionals
- Practical workshops in traditional building craft skills
- Guided site visits to historic buildings and conservation projects
- Peer learning and group discussion
- Self-directed study and research
- One module per term, allowing working professionals to balance study with employment

## Facilities

- Dedicated studios and workshops at the Parkside Building
- Access to specialised conservation materials and resources
- Well-equipped library with extensive heritage and architecture collections
- Digital fabrication and scanning equipment

## Career Opportunities

Graduates of this programme go on to work in:
- Local authority conservation and planning departments
- Heritage organisations (e.g., Historic England, National Trust, CADW)
- Private conservation and heritage consultancy practices
- Architectural practices with specialist conservation teams
- Building contracting firms specialising in historic building repair
- Independent conservation advisory roles
