# Art and Design with Creative Technologies BA (Hons) (3 years)

> A hybrid course for the future — exploring the intersection of art, design, and creative technologies to shape careers in the evolving creative industries.

## Overview

Art and Design with Creative Technologies BA (Hons) is a new hybrid undergraduate programme at Birmingham City University designed for makers, thinkers, futurists, coders, performers, technologists, conservationists, and designers. The course explores the intersection of art and design to prepare students for careers in the rapidly evolving creative industries.

The programme is structured around a series of **Labs** — Thought Lab, Materials Lab, Digital Lab, Performance Lab, and Nature Lab — that progressively build creative, technical, and critical skills. From Year 2, students engage with live local, national, and global briefs with real clients, and in Year 3, they develop an independent practice through a major research project and body of work.

The course emphasises collaboration, professional practice, and real-world employability, with opportunities to work with organisations such as Midlands Art Centre, Boomtown Music Festival, Oval Real Estate, and international partners including Kyiv National University of Technology and Design and the University of Chicago.

**Visiting Professor:** Jason Bruges, Founder of Jason Bruges Studio — internationally renowned for innovative installations and interactive spatial design.

## Course Structure

The course is structured around three progressive years, each comprising 120 credits. Assessment is through coursework — no written exams.

### Year 1 — Foundation Labs (120 credits, all core)

Students rotate through interdisciplinary labs, each building core creative skills:

| Module | Credits | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Thought Lab | 20 | Critical thinking, self-awareness, creative design thinking, verbal and visual communication, writing skills. Engages global, diverse voices. |
| Materials Lab | 20 | Exploring material connectivity — fusion, knotting, welding, embedding, joints, breaking. Collaborative testing and prototyping. |
| Digital Lab | 20 | Fundamentals of creative digital technologies — coding, digital modelling, drawing, projection, filming, VR/AR, sound, and light. |
| Performance Lab | 20 | Narrative creation through movement, performance, spoken word, animation, costume, or set design. |
| Nature Lab | 20 | Making with found natural materials — foraging, growing, building, stone carving, nesting, thatching, collecting, observing. |
| Speculative Lab | 20 | Year culmination — self-organised speculative proposals for Birmingham's wider community, ending with an outward-facing exhibition. |

### Year 2 — Live Projects (120 credits)

Students apply skills to real-world briefs at local, national, and global scales:

**Core modules (100 credits):**

| Module | Credits | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Live Project: Local | 20 | Professional brief set by a local external client or community. Real-life problem-solving with interdisciplinary collaboration. |
| Live Project: National | 40 | Nationally located live brief with employability focus. Building professional communication and interdisciplinary design practice skills. |
| Live Project: Global | 40 | Globally located live brief emphasising Global Citizenship, cross-cultural understanding, and international working practices. |

**Optional modules (choose 20 credits):**

| Module | Credits | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Collaborative Practice | 20 | Interdisciplinary project with students from complementary disciplines or academic staff. Develops collaboration as a key employability skill. |
| Work Placement | 20 | Professional experience in a relevant workplace, developing subject skills and professional attributes. |

### Professional Placement Year (Optional)

Students can opt for a sandwich year of professional placement between Year 2 and Year 3. Fees during the placement year are equivalent to 20% of the full-time course fee.

### Year 3 — Independent Practice (120 credits)

Students diverge to follow their own pathway, navigating the Labs individually:

- Undertake a **sustained, in-depth, theoretically informed research project** exploring an area of personal interest
- Create a **body of work** (individual or collaborative)
- Integrate with **professional companies and the creative landscape** to identify a future career path and develop a **professional identity**
- Students may focus on one Lab, a combination, or create new Labs of their own

### Go Abroad

Students can apply to study on an exchange programme for one semester at a partner university (typically in the second semester of Year 2). Summer volunteering and study opportunities at partner institutions are also available in locations such as France, Spain, Brazil, Fiji, India, Mexico, South Korea, and the USA.

### Trips and Visits

International trips have included the Venice Biennale, New York, Berlin, and Barcelona. National trips include London, Liverpool, and Bristol.

## Career Prospects

The course develops transferable skills including adaptability, creativity, collaboration, problem-solving, communication, analytical reasoning, critical thinking, and writing. The Digital Creative sector is the largest growth sector of the creative industries.

Potential career paths include:
- Immersive and interactive art and design
- Experiential and spatial design
- Production design
- Product design
- Costume design
- Games design
- Creative technologist
- Freelance artist / Creative practitioner
- Creative industries entrepreneur
- Community artist / Art educator
- Art director
