# Fashion and Textile Design MA

## Overview

The MA in Fashion and Textile Design at Birmingham City University is a practice-based, research-driven programme that supports creative practitioners to develop advanced skills in fashion design, textile innovation, and surface pattern. It offers specialist pathways in **Fashion Design** and **Textile and Surface Design**, and is structured around three key pillars: **Material and Process Exploration**, **Cultural and Sustainable Contexts**, and **Industry Application**.

The programme encourages experimentation with both digital and hand-crafted techniques, exploring the tactile, structural, and visual potential of materials. Students investigate how cultural narratives, material innovation, and sustainability inform textile and surface design. Through live projects, prototyping, and cross-disciplinary collaborations, students translate creative research into professional outcomes.

Students join this specialism from a wide range of backgrounds: textiles, fashion, fine art, graphic design, illustration, product design, or as a maker, artist, or creative practitioner seeking to evolve their material practice or develop their own studio or brand.

## Course Structure

### Full-Time — Semester Structure

**Semester 1:**
- **Advanced Research Skills** (20 credits) — Develops creative research methods and methodologies used in design and arts production. Focus on research through practice.
- **Material, Process and Ideation** (40 credits) — Encourages experimentation with digital and hand-crafted techniques, exploring the tactile, structural, and visual potential of materials.

**Semester 2:**
- **Professional Futures: Collaborative Challenge** (20 credits) — Live project work and cross-disciplinary collaboration with industry partners.
- **Innovation and Enterprise** (40 credits) — Develops entrepreneurial skills and industry-focused creative practice.

**Semester 3:**
- **Project Resolution and Collection Launch** (40 credits) — A major creative project culminating in a resolved collection or body of work.
- **Reflection** (20 credits) — Critical reflection on creative practice, contextualising work within broader cultural and theoretical frameworks.

### Professional Placement Route (Optional)

Extends the course to 18 months with an integrated placement component (60 additional credits at Level 6), totalling 240 credits.

### Assessment

Entirely coursework-based — no written examinations. Assessment methods include portfolio submissions, design projects, research presentations, and reflective reports.
