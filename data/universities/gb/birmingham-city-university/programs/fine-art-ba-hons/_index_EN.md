# Fine Art BA (Hons) (3 years)

> 🏛️ **Historic Venue:** Based in the Grade I-listed Margaret Street School of Art — the first municipal art school in the UK.

## Overview

The BA (Hons) Fine Art course at Birmingham City University is designed to develop your art practice and creative thinking. Through the skilful manipulation of materials, processes, and ideas, you will offer creative insights that shape who you are and how you exist in the world. These are valuable skills in the fast-changing and interconnected web of ecological, political, socio-economic, and philosophical contexts.

The course will help you embrace important traditional making skills such as **painting, drawing, printmaking, sculpture, installation, digital media, and performance**, while also encouraging you to fuse these with new developments in creative practice. This hybrid approach, along with discipline-specific studios and specialist workshops, will equip you to navigate the evolving landscape of the Creative Industries into the future.

The course is based on **four key principles**:
- **Making Skills** — Develop technical skills in purpose-built studio spaces and specialist workshops
- **Making Public** — Explore art practice in the public realm through exhibiting and sharing outcomes with broad audiences
- **Making Communities** — Build meaningful relationships between art practice and society
- **Making a Living** — Focus on personal development and building a sustainable creative career

## Course Structure

The course is structured around three progressive years, each comprising 120 CATS credits (360 total). The programme is built around four key principles: Making Skills, Making Public, Making Communities, and Making a Living.

### Year 1 — Foundations of Practice
*120 credits total*
- **Making Studio** (40 credits) — Develop your art practice through exploration of painting, sculpture, printmaking, photography, film, sound, performance, installation, drawing and digital processes. Begin to identify your own voice as an artist.
- **Making a Living 1: Developing Skills** (20 credits) — Introduction to the creative economy, professional skills development, and career trajectory exploration.
- **Making Meaning** (40 credits) — Develop critical understanding of the art world and its contexts. Introduction to key concepts and critical frameworks relevant to contemporary art practice.
- **Making Public** (20 credits) — Explore how art can be communicated and shared publicly through exhibition, publication, online platforms, performance and collaboration.

### Year 2 — Deepening Practice
*120 credits (100 core + 20 optional)*
- **Making Practice** (40 credits) — Develop your art practice with increasing independence and ambition. Identify and develop your own artistic concerns and creative methodologies.
- **Making a Living 2: With Communities** (20 credits) — Focus on working with communities and the public realm. Develop understanding of participatory and socially engaged art practices.
- **Making Critical** (40 credits) — Deepen critical and theoretical engagement with contemporary art. Develop your ability to articulate your own position as a practitioner.
- **Work Placement** (20 credits, optional) — Develop professional attributes through direct workplace experience.
- **Live Project** (20 credits, optional) — Engage with external partners and organisations on a live project brief.

### Year 3 — Independent Practice and Graduation
*120 credits total*
- **Making Research** (40 credits) — Undertake research-led art practice with full independence, developing a self-directed body of work.
- **Making a Living 3: Professional Practice** (20 credits) — Build and grow a sustainable creative career. Establish professional context and networks.
- **Making Exhibitions** (60 credits) — Capstone module: produce and present a major body of work for public exhibition in the graduate show.

### Optional Professional Placement Year
After Year 2, students may undertake a year-long professional placement (extending the course to four years). You will be able to draw on the University's extensive network of local, regional, and national employers. Placement year fees are 20% of the full-time course fee.
