# Experimental Performance MMus / PgDip / PgCert

## Overview

The Experimental Performance pathway at Royal Birmingham Conservatoire is designed for emerging arts practitioners, providing you with a unique opportunity to undertake independent artistic projects in a fully supportive environment. Practitioners from a variety of disciplines — including instrumental/vocal performance, composition, dance, choreography, theatre, visual and performance art, and creative writing — come together with like-minded people.

The core of your study will be formed by your own artistic ideas, which are developed, through research and dialogue, into professional practical projects. This course has been created by artists who actively work with an interdisciplinary mind-set. Working in an interdisciplinary way means that you don't settle for a particular artistic genre but are interested in finding the best way of expressing an artistic idea.

### What's Covered in This Course?

- Regular individual tuition from leading UK and internationally renowned performers, practitioners, teachers, and researchers
- Bespoke support classes in musicianship and repertoire
- Opportunities to participate in a wide variety of creative projects, including interdisciplinary collaborations
- Frequent opportunities to perform, both within Departmental activities and in concerts
- Access to forums, seminars and masterclasses with distinguished visiting artists, composers, and performers
- Access to the Conservatoire's broad range of ensemble activities
- Wide-ranging Professional Development modules
- Networking with leading professionals from around the world

### Why Choose Us?

- Created by artists who actively work with an interdisciplinary mind-set
- We are interested in your ideas and will help you think them through and realise them professionally
- You may perform text on stage, dance, compose, or combine multiple art forms
- Collaborative environment with like-minded practitioners across disciplines

## Entry Requirements

- UK students should normally hold an honours degree, ideally but not necessarily in Music
- Non-UK students should hold a Bachelor's degree or similar degree-equivalent diploma
- **Audition/Portfolio:** Submit a portfolio and/or attend a live audition
- **IELTS:** 6.0 overall with no less than 5.5 in each band (international/EU students)
