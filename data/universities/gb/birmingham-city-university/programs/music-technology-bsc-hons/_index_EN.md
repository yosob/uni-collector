# Music Technology BSc (Hons)

**Birmingham City University**

## ⚠️ Programme Discontinued

This programme has been discontinued and is no longer accepting new students. The closely related **Sound Engineering BSc (Hons)** is the current equivalent offering from Birmingham City University.

**Replacement Programme:** [Sound Engineering BSc (Hons)](https://www.bcu.ac.uk/courses/sound-engineering-bsc-hons-2026-27)

---

## Overview

The BSc (Hons) Music Technology at Birmingham City University was aimed at the technical music producer, training students to use technology to create, record and distribute music. The programme combined creative production with deep technical understanding of audio systems, acoustics, and digital signal processing.

## What You'll Learn

The programme focused on the technical aspects of music production, including:

- **Audio Engineering:** Recording, mixing, and mastering techniques
- **Digital Signal Processing:** Mathematical foundations and real-time audio processing
- **Music Production:** Creative use of DAWs (Logic Pro, Pro Tools) and hardware
- **Acoustics:** Room acoustics, psychoacoustics, and sound reinforcement
- **Software Development:** Audio programming with MATLAB, Python, and C++
- **Sound Design:** Synthesis, sampling, and electronic music creation

## Facilities

Students had access to:

- World-class recording studios at **Royal Birmingham Conservatoire**
- **STEAMhouse** SoundLab facilities
- Solid State Logic (SSL) recording consoles
- Industry-standard PA systems
- Neumann, Brauner and AKG microphones
- Pro Tools, Logic, MATLAB, Python, C++ workstations
