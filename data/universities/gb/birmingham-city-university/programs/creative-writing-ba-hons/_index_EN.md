## Overview

Our BA (Hons) Creative Writing course develops contemporary creative writers across a variety of forms, genres, and platforms, including prose fiction, poetry, creative non-fiction, screenwriting, audio drama, podcasting, and digital media. The course provides the time, space and support to explore your creative voice and develop your writing practice.

You'll study with established writers and industry professionals, learning the craft of writing while developing your individual creative identity. The course combines practical workshops with critical analysis, encouraging you to experiment with form and genre while developing your technical skills.

## Modules

### Year One
- Voices of Birmingham — 20 credits
- Prose Writing — 20 credits
- Adaptation — 20 credits
- Key Critical Concepts — 20 credits
- Craft of Writing — 20 credits
- Genre — 20 credits

### Year Two
- Writing Short Stories — 20 credits
- The Novel — 20 credits
- Creating Compelling Content — 20 credits
- Screenwriting — 20 credits
- Digital Storytelling — 20 credits
- Work Placement — 20 credits

### Year Three
- Writing Poetry — 20 credits
- Writing Creative Nonfiction — 20 credits
- Major Project Preparation — 20 credits
- Audio Drama and Podcast Writing — 20 credits
- Major Project: Creative Writing — 40 credits


## Entry Requirements

112 UCAS Tariff points. A-level, BTEC or equivalent qualifications. IELTS 6.0 with 6.0 in writing and 5.5 in other bands. Portfolio of creative writing may be required.

## Career Prospects

Author, screenwriter, copywriter, journalist, content writer, editor, publisher, scriptwriter for TV/film/radio, digital content creator, communications officer.
