# Interior Architecture and Design BA (Hons) (3 years)

## Overview

Our Interior Architecture and Design degree is about people — developing environments where people can grow and thrive. You'll learn the skills and tools to design and shape spaces, and engage with users, clients, communities, consultants, and stakeholders. This course gives you the skills to transform or give new life to spaces, teaching you how to plan and design creative interiors.

You'll study the adaptation and re-use of space within new and existing building stock, looking at every aspect from aesthetics to human needs, from materiality and atmosphere to character, colour, light and shade. You'll also discover new interior environments across hospitality, retail, leisure and residential sectors.

Taught by experienced practising designers, this course mixes practice with theory, so you can create links between concepts and materials while strengthening your design skills across key industry sectors. First year projects focus on key principles, such as lighting, texture, and colour, applied first to surface and then to objects in space.

## Course Structure

The course is structured across three years, each building progressively on the previous. All modules are assessed through 100% coursework.

### Year 1 — Foundation of the Discipline
*120 credits total — all core modules*

- **Re-ordering Space** (20 credits) — Explore spatial activities and space planning to design creative and innovative interiors by investigating spatial concepts, design and representation. Focus on relationships between adjacent spaces/activities, spatial hierarchies, and the ephemeral qualities of light, shade, and colour.
- **Manipulating Space** (20 credits) — Develop understanding of human physicality and the manipulation of space. Explore physical, psychological and emotional factors influencing user responses to environments and actions in everyday life.
- **Transforming Space 1** (40 credits) — Develop an interior design project within a given brief, approaching the design of a single-function space. Undertake research on an unfamiliar programme and integrate it within a given site.
- **Twentieth Century Design Cultures** (20 credits) — Engage with key design ideologies of the 20th Century through lectures and seminars. Learn about design history and the social, political and cultural context that gave rise to specific design movements.
- **Design Devices** (20 credits) — Develop deeper awareness of industry-relevant communication methods, expanding visualisation language. Explore portfolio techniques toward becoming a design-aware and industry-aware student.

### Year 2 — Defining Your Design Identity
*100 credits core + 20 credits optional*

Core modules:
- **Interior Strategies** (40 credits) — Approach challenges of Interior Architecture and Design from an interdisciplinary perspective. Drawing on architecture, arts, literature, film, photography, fashion and more.
- **Transforming Space 2** (40 credits) — Develop an interior design project for a mix-use space. Make critical choices amongst several options involving multiple sites and spatial functions.
- **Design Communication** (20 credits) — Develop advanced CAD skills (SolidWorks) and use them in relation to the design process, utilising industry-recognised production methods and techniques.

Optional modules (choose one):
- **Experience & Employment 1** (20 credits) — Work placement or live project
- **Experience & Employment 2** (20 credits) — Part-time work/volunteering
- **Architecture and Design Year Out (Sandwich)** (20 credits) — Year-long industry placement

### Year 3 — Defining Your Professional Persona
*120 credits total*

Projects focus on the near future of the discipline, engaging with work placement, professional bodies, and specialists to define your major project aligned with early career ambitions.

- **Design Integration** (40 credits) — Explore design feasibility across 2-3 projects within Interior Architecture and Design from a professional perspective. Take outline briefs alongside specific existing sites, objectively interrogate them by exploring the potential of design, technology, structure and professional practice.
- **Major Project** (60 credits) — Undertake a sustained, in-depth and theoretically informed research project exploring an area of personal interest. The outcome may take the form of a written dissertation or a practice-based portfolio.
- **Professional Practice** (20 credits) — Develop necessary skills for professional practice in interior architecture and design, with the ability to self-manage individual activities.

## Career Prospects

Graduates are well-prepared for careers in interior architecture and design, with skills in spatial thinking, design communication, and professional practice. Career paths include:

- Interior architect
- Interior designer
- Architectural design consultant
- Retail and workplace designer
- Exhibition and events designer
- Set designer (theatre/film)
- Spatial designer
- Design project manager

Recent graduate projects demonstrate the breadth of the discipline:
- Material investigation: developing and prototyping biomaterials for interior applications
- Developing LGBTQI+ spaces through community engagement
- Exploring the influence of graphic design on spatial design
- Questioning the relationship between material and immaterial in the re-use of heritage buildings
