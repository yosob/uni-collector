# Interior Architecture and Design with Foundation Year BA (Hons) (4 years)

## Overview

Our Interior Architecture and Design with Foundation Year BA (Hons) course begins with a foundation year designed to develop the core skills, knowledge and confidence needed to progress onto the BA Honours Interior Architecture and Design degree. The foundation year introduces fundamental principles of design thinking, including drawing and visual communication, spatial awareness, model-making, and digital skills. Students explore interior architecture's cultural, social and environmental context, develop creative problem-solving abilities, and build essential academic skills such as research, critical reflection and portfolio development.

After completing the foundation year, students progress to the full BA (Hons) Interior Architecture and Design course — a programme about people, developing environments where people can grow and thrive. You'll learn the skills and tools to design and shape spaces, and engage with users, clients, communities, consultants, and stakeholders. The course gives you the skills to transform or give new life to spaces, teaching you how to plan and design creative interiors.

You'll study the adaptation and re-use of space within new and existing building stock, looking at every aspect from aesthetics to human needs, from materiality and atmosphere to character, colour, light and shade. You'll also discover new interior environments across hospitality, retail, leisure and residential sectors.

Taught by experienced practising designers in the Parkside Building at our City Centre Campus, this course mixes practice with theory, so you can create links between concepts and materials while strengthening your design skills across key industry sectors.

## Course Structure

The course is structured as a foundation year followed by three years of the BA Interior Architecture and Design programme, each with progressive themes. All modules are assessed through 100% coursework.

### Foundation Year (Year 0) — Building Foundations
*120 credits total*

The foundation year introduces fundamental principles of design thinking and interior architecture:
- **Drawing and Visual Communication** — Core skills for design representation
- **Spatial Awareness and Design** — Introduction to spatial thinking and design processes
- **Model-making** — Physical and digital model-making techniques
- **Digital Skills** — Introduction to CAD and digital design tools
- **Interior Architecture's Cultural, Social and Environmental Context** — Understanding design in its broader context
- **Creative Problem-solving** — Developing design thinking abilities
- **Academic Skills** — Research, critical reflection and portfolio development

### Year 1 — Subject Foundations
*120 credits total — all core modules*

- **Spatial Re-organisation** (20 credits) — Explore spatial activity and spatial planning, designing creative interior spaces through the study of spatial concepts, design and representation. Focus on relationships between adjacent spaces/activities, spatial hierarchy, and sensory qualities of light, shadow and colour.
- **Spatial Manipulation** (20 credits) — Understand the relationship between anthropometrics and spatial manipulation. Explore the physiological, psychological and emotional factors that influence users' environmental reactions and everyday behaviour.
- **Spatial Intervention 1** (40 credits) — Develop an interior design project within a given brief, working on a single-function space design. Research unfamiliar proposals and integrate them into a designated site.
- **20th Century Design Culture** (20 credits) — Learn about the key design ideologies of the 20th century through lectures and seminars. Study the history of design and the social, political and cultural contexts that gave rise to specific design movements.
- ** Design Methods** (20 credits) — Develop an in-depth understanding of industry-relevant design communication methods, expanding visual language. Explore portfolio techniques to become a student with design awareness and industry awareness.

### Year 2 — Defining Design Identity
*100 credits core + 20 credits optional*

Core modules:
- **Interior Strategies** (40 credits) — Tackle challenges in interior architecture and design from an interdisciplinary perspective. Draw from architecture, art, literature, film, photography, fashion and more.
- **Spatial Intervention 2** (40 credits) — Develop mixed-function interior design projects. Make critical choices among options involving multiple sites and spatial functions.
- **Design Communication** (20 credits) — Learn advanced CAD skills (SolidWorks), using industry-recognised production methods and techniques.

Optional modules (choose one):
- **Experience and Employment 1** (20 credits) — Work placement or live project
- **Experience and Employment 2** (20 credits) — Part-time work/volunteering
- **Architecture and Design International Year (Sandwich)** (20 credits) — Full-year industry placement

### Final Year (Year 3) — Defining Professional Identity
*120 credits total*

Projects focus on the future direction of the discipline, with placements, professional bodies and specialists helping to define major projects aligned with early career goals.

- **Design Synthesis** (40 credits) — Explore the design feasibility of 2-3 interior architecture and design projects from a professional perspective. Objectively analyse design proposals, technology, structures and professional practice within specific sites.
- **Major Project** (60 credits) — Undertake a sustained, in-depth theoretical research project exploring an area of personal interest. Outcomes can be a written dissertation or a practice-based portfolio.
- **Professional Practice** (20 credits) — Develop necessary skills for professional practice in interior architecture and design, with the ability to self-manage individual activities.
