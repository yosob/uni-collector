## Overview

Gemmology MSc at Birmingham City University is a research-focused postgraduate programme that equips students with the tools and techniques necessary to undertake research at the forefront of the gemmological field. Based in custom-designed facilities within Birmingham's Assay Office, in the historic Jewellery Quarter, students have access to equipment more commonly seen in research laboratories, and through the university's extensive network of contacts, potential access to an even greater range of analytical tools.

The course encourages investigation of gemmological questions and theories through literature-based peer-reviewed research and practical testing. Students are supported by experienced tutors who are all recognised industry professionals, benefiting from the extensive network of gemmologists through contacts in various organisations across the globe, including laboratories, museums, auction houses and other related sectors.

## Course Description

This Masters programme gives you the tools and techniques necessary to undertake research at the forefront of your chosen gemmological area. You will be supported throughout your studies by experienced tutors, with a wealth of knowledge and experience between them — all nationally and internationally recognised industry professionals.

As well as learning important skills for your future career development from the practical modules, you will learn valuable research techniques and analytical skills which will stand you in good stead with future potential employers. The course encourages you to investigate gemmological questions and theories, by way of literature-based peer-reviewed research and practical testing.

Birmingham School of Jewellery is celebrating 130 years of involvement in the jewellery trade in Birmingham. The gemmology department has relocated into custom-built facilities within the Assay Office, ensuring close industry contact and a dedicated environment for study. You will benefit from interacting with a dedicated cohort of gemmology students, from those studying at undergraduate level upwards, enabling you to build a network of contacts from a varied range of backgrounds.

## Entry Requirements

- **Degree:** A minimum of a 2:2 honours degree, or equivalent, in Gemmology or a related subject (e.g. mineralogy or geology).
- **IELTS:** 6.0 overall (no band below 5.5)
- We also welcome applications from individuals who may not satisfy the normal entry requirements, but have a high level of educational, industrial or professional experience. If you do not meet all of the essential entry requirements, you will also be required to complete an interview.

## Employability

You will learn a range of skills which will be key to enhancing your future employability:

- Research skills, both in terms of literature and numerical data
- Analytical techniques for data analysis and presentation
- The ability to operate a range of research-level analytical tools, based on various forms of spectroscopy
- The ability to work independently and collaboratively
- To be a self-motivated, organised, effective learner
- Effective time management and project management skills

**Key industry links include:**

- **UK:** AnchorCert Lab, Gem-A SGA, Birmingham Assay Office, IIDGR, Christie's, Sotheby's, Bulgari, Bentley & Skinner
- **International:** SSEF, GIA, AGL, AGIL, Alrosa

## Facilities

Gemmology students are based in new, state-of-the-art premises at Moreton Street in the Jewellery Quarter, within the Assay Office Birmingham building. The purpose-built workshops are spacious and superbly well-equipped with equipment, samples and books. There is access to some of the highest specification equipment available for gemmological education in the world. Students also have access to the advanced equipment housed within the Assay Office's AnchorCert laboratory.

The Research Lab includes a range of spectroscopic analysis equipment, along with the latest diamond screening tools.
