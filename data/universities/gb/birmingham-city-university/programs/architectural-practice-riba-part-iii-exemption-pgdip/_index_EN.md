## Overview

Successful completion of the programme gives you recognition for RIBA Part 3, registration with the Architects Registration Board (ARB) and the ability to call yourself an architect.

The course covers professional practice, building procurement, contract administration, and professional experience. It is designed for graduates who have completed RIBA Part 1 and Part 2 and who wish to complete their professional qualification.

## Modules

### Programme Modules
- Professional Practice and Management — 20 credits
- Building Procurement and Contract Administration — 20 credits
- Professional Experience and Development — 40 credits
- Case Study — 40 credits


## Entry Requirements

RIBA Part 1 and Part 2 qualifications (or equivalent). ARB prescribed qualifications. Normally a minimum of 24 months professional practical experience. IELTS 6.5 with no less than 6.0 in each band for international students.

## Career Prospects

Registered architect, architectural project manager, design manager, construction manager, building consultant, principal designer.
