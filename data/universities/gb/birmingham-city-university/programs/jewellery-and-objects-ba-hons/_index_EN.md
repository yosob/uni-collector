# Jewellery and Objects BA (Hons)

**Birmingham City University · School of Jewellery**

## Overview

Experiment with materials, techniques and processes on a highly respected jewellery design degree course. You are encouraged to pursue innovation, challenge conventions and push the boundaries of the discipline.

This practical degree is an internationally-respected jewellery design course. It gives you the freedom to develop your own creativity by encouraging you to experiment with a variety of ideas, materials, processes and techniques. You'll be supported and encouraged to create innovative designs to a high standard of professionalism, and have the chance to enter competitions and awards.

Individuality is fundamental to your creative development, personal philosophy and direction. The School of Jewellery building is uniquely positioned in the heart of the Jewellery Quarter enabling you to be fully immersed in the industry from the first day of your studies.

## Why Choose Us

- **Internationally renowned** School of Jewellery, the largest in Europe, founded in 1890

## Course Structure

### Year One
Develop traditional processes and experimental materials investigation.

**Core Modules (100 credits):**
- Jewellery and Objects in Context (20 credits)
- Specialist Techniques (40 credits)
- Contextual Exploration (40 credits)

**Optional Modules (choose at least 20 credits):**
- Surface and Ornament (20 credits)
- Fine Jewellery Techniques (20 credits)
- Introduction to Gemmology (20 credits)

### Year Two
Explore specialist pathways, employability and collaborative practice.

**Core Modules (80 credits):**
- Contextual Specialism (40 credits)
- Jewellery and Objects in Depth (40 credits)

**Optional Modules (choose at least 40 credits):**
- Narrative of Objects (20 credits)
- Material Exploration (20 credits)
- Luxury Jewellery Branding (20 credits)
- Live Project (20 credits)
- Work Placement (20 credits)
- Study Abroad (60 credits)

### Year Three
Undertake major personal projects and develop professional practice.

**Core Modules (120 credits):**
- Major Project: Jewellery and Objects (40 credits)
- Studio Perspectives (40 credits)
- Professional Practice (20 credits)
- Studio Development (20 credits)

This course offers an optional placement year between Year 2 and Year 3, allowing you to spend a full year with an employer. Placement year fees are 20% of the full-time course fee.
