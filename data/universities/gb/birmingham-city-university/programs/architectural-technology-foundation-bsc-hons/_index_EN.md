# Architectural Technology with Foundation Year BSc (Hons) (4 years)

## Overview

Our Architectural Technology with Foundation Year BSc (Hons) degree is where the science of construction meets the art of design. The course focuses not only on the creation of the built environment, but also the structuring of space in and around it. The Foundation Year provides a broad study programme that underpins the follow-on degree, enabling you to study over an extended full-time duration of four years.

After completing the foundation year, you will develop your ability to appreciate and analyse existing design as well as create new ones, while keeping buildability, technology, procurement, contractual relations and the people involved in the process in mind. You will also explore the social, regulatory, technical, sustainable and aesthetic context of the UK and global development industry.

Much of your learning activity will be hands-on, with access to our strong industry links. You'll be provided with the latest CAD software, meaning you'll be well equipped to make an impact in an important industry. This course is accredited by both CIAT (Chartered Institute of Architectural Technologists) and CIOB (Chartered Institute of Building).

Studying at our Millennium Point campus in Birmingham's City Centre, you'll be immersed in a creative environment with the chance to be inspired by the urban architecture of Birmingham — a city undergoing major regeneration with the Big City Plan and HS2 on the doorstep.

## Progression Requirement

In order to progress to the next year of your degree, it is necessary to achieve a pass in all of the modules of the Foundation Certificate (year one of four).

The Foundation Year is designed for students who do not yet meet the standard entry requirements for the BSc Architectural Technology course. It provides an alternative route into the degree, with a broad study programme that underpins the follow-on years.

## Course Structure

The course is structured as a foundation year followed by three years of the BSc Architectural Technology programme. Each year comprises CORE modules totalling 120 credits. The programme is modelled on the practice of the architectural technologist in industry, developing a broad range of technical and built environment knowledge.

### Foundation Year
*120 credits total*

The Foundation Certificate provides a broad study programme that underpins the follow-on degree:
- **Introduction to the Built Environment** — Fundamental concepts of construction and design
- **Mathematical and Scientific Principles** — Core technical skills underpinning architectural technology
- **Communication and Study Skills** — Academic writing, research and presentation techniques
- **Introduction to Design Technology** — Basic CAD and design software skills
- **Construction Materials and Methods** — Overview of key construction principles
- **Project-Based Learning** — Hands-on introduction to architectural technology practice

### Year 1 (First Year of Degree)
*120 credits total*

Develops fundamental skills and knowledge in the residential area of the built environment:
- **Architectural Technology 1** — Introduction to residential construction technology
- **Architectural Graphics** — 2D and 3D drawing and representation techniques
- **Design Studio 1** — Residential design projects with a focus on spatial planning
- **Architectural History and Theory 1** — Contextual understanding of architecture and the built environment
- **Architectural Structures** — Structural principles and their application in residential design
- **Introduction to BIM** — Building Information Modelling concepts and software (Revit, Navisworks)

### Year 2 (Second Year of Degree)
*120 credits total*

Develops specialist skills with more challenging project experiences:
- **Design Studio 2** — Commercial-scale design projects
- **Architectural Technology 3** — Advanced construction technology for commercial buildings
- **Building Regulations and Control** — UK building law, regulations and compliance
- **Professional Studies 1** — Procurement, contracts and project management
- **Advanced CAD and BIM** — 3D modelling environments and collaborative working
- **Elective modules** — Specialist options across the built environment

### Final Year (Third Year of Degree)
*120 credits total*

Refines skills and prepares for professional practice:
- **Design Studio 3 (Major Project)** — Comprehensive design project demonstrating full competence
- **Architectural Technology 4** — Advanced specification and technical integration
- **Professional Studies 2** — Industry practice, ethics and professional development
- **Research Project** — Independent research into a built environment topic
- **Integrative Project** — Cross-disciplinary collaborative project
