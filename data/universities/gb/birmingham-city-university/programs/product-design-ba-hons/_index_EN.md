# Product Design BA (Hons)

## Overview

Our BA (Hons) Product Design course provides an enriching experience, offering you the essential knowledge, skills, and understanding to create relevant products and furniture. With a strong emphasis on 'learning through making', our approach integrates innovation, user-centred design, social awareness, sustainability, and commercial understanding.

Contemporary product design spans industries such as consumer electronics, furniture, medical devices, automotive and more. It combines creativity, functionality, and technical knowledge to create innovative, sustainable products that meet user needs and are feasible to produce. Today's designers must navigate rapid technological advancements, sustainability challenges, and user experience to create functional and socially responsible designs.

## Course Structure

### Year One

In your first year you are introduced to the fundamentals of product and furniture design, building essential skills in design thinking, making, communication and contextual understanding.

**Core Modules (120 credits):**

- **Design Essentials** (40 credits) — Develop fundamental design skills through hands-on making and iterative exploration, including sketching, model-making, prototyping and material understanding.
- **Professional Futures: Communication** (20 credits) — Develop professional communication skills including visual communication techniques, presentation skills, and digital tools used in the design industry.
- **Design for Production** (20 credits) — Explore the relationship between design and manufacturing, learning about materials, production processes, and designing for scalability.
- **Design in Context** (40 credits) — Understand the broader context in which design operates, exploring design history, theory, social responsibility, sustainability, and the role of the designer in society.

### Year Two

In your second year you develop more advanced design skills, working on industry-focused projects that deepen your understanding of product and furniture design in professional contexts.

**Core Modules (100 credits):**

- **Design for Industry** (40 credits) — Work on industry-linked design projects developing commercially viable products with real briefs, client requirements, and market considerations.
- **Professional Futures: Collaboration** (20 credits) — Develop collaborative working skills through multidisciplinary team projects, learning to communicate effectively and manage projects.
- **Design Focus** (40 credits) — Deepen your expertise in a specific area of product or furniture design through focused research, experimentation, and a resolved design outcome.

**Optional Modules (choose at least 20 credits):**

- **Industry Project** (20 credits)
- **Independent Creative Entrepreneur** (20 credits)
- **Work Placement** (20 credits)

### Professional Placement Year (Optional)

Between Year 2 and Year 3, you can opt to take a professional placement year to gain valuable industry experience, build connections, and enhance your employability.

### Year Three (Final Year)

In your final year you undertake a major design project from research through to final presentation, demonstrating your ability to work at a professional level.

**Core Modules (120 credits):**

- **Professional Futures: Presentation** (20 credits) — Develop advanced presentation and portfolio skills to showcase your work professionally to different audiences.
- **Major Research Project – Discover/Define** (40 credits) — Conduct in-depth research to define your major project direction through user research, market analysis, and contextual study.
- **Major Design Project – Develop/Deliver** (60 credits) — Execute your major design project from concept to final delivery, producing a fully resolved product or furniture design outcome.
