# Fashion Communication BA (Hons)

## Programme Overview

The BA (Hons) Fashion Communication at Birmingham City University will transform you into a fashion-focused visual designer. You'll combine art direction, styling, graphic design, film, animation, photography, journalism, events, and trend forecasting for fashion. You'll learn to brand, style, write, shoot, package, and direct — as an interdisciplinary practitioner, communicating to consumers in both the real world and the metaverse, driven by creative storytelling.

You won't be a mere follower; you'll become a pioneer, embracing a forward-thinking mindset that positions you at the forefront of contemporary fashion communication. Rooted in a love of tradition and craft, the programme is equally committed to pushing the boundaries of innovation and sustainability for a better world.

You'll be taught by a team of industry specialists with a proven track record of working with top brands and influential industry practitioners. The course is an award-winning programme that shapes fashion communicators who can work in teams, as individuals, while nurturing entrepreneurs and future-proofers.

## Course Structure

### Year One

Year one introduces the foundational skills and knowledge of fashion communication across print, digital, and emerging platforms.

**Core Modules (120 credits):**

- **Creative Industries: Connections** (20 credits) — Explore connections between creative fashion and textile industries past and present, examining how individuals have influenced culture, freedom of expression and experimentation.
- **Art Direction for Print and Journalism** (20 credits) — Immerse yourself in art direction within the fashion publication industry. Learn core graphic design principles (type, image, layout), produce a contemporary fashion magazine in both digital and printed formats, and develop copywriting skills.
- **Future Thinking and Idea Generation** (20 credits) — Learn the foundations of idea generation and future thinking methodology. Develop concepts informed by wider cultural and social trends relevant to fashion communication.
- **Visual Storytelling** (40 credits) — Master the art of visual storytelling for fashion. Learn to connect location, wardrobe, casting, props, hair and makeup to communicate compelling fashion narratives.
- **Fashion Metaverse** (20 credits) — Explore digital fashion film and experimental fashion communication. Examine the metaverse landscape, sustainable digital art approaches, AR, VR, and digital marketing platforms.

### Year Two

Year two builds upon knowledge and skills gained in year one, applying them to a more external focus including industry competitions and internships.

**Core Modules (100 credits):**

- **Creative Industries: Industry Futures** (20 credits) — Essential skills and knowledge for successful transition into industry work placement. Develop professional practices, understand industry expectations, and build personal development strategies.
- **Art Direction for Branding and Promotion** (40 credits) — Explore branding creative concepts, products, and services using digital and physical production skills. Analyse markets and assess commercial viability through research and visual exploration.
- **Fashion Film with Styling** (40 credits) — Fashion filmmaking and visual storytelling with emphasis on creative direction. Explore still and moving images, animation, and motion graphics, integrating styling into fashion photography and videography.

**Optional Modules (choose 20 credits):**

- **Work Placement** (20 credits) — Develop professional attributes through workplace experience (70 hours).
- **Industry Project** (20 credits) — Apply knowledge to an external professional brief, addressing sustainability and UN Sustainable Development Goals.
- **Independent Creative Entrepreneur** (20 credits) — Develop entrepreneurial skills for diverse creative career paths.

### Professional Placement Year (Optional)

Spend a whole year with an employer after completing your second year. Placement year fees are 20% of the full-time course fee. Previous placement partners include GQ, InStyle, Grazia, Suitcase, Selfridges, ASOS, Topshop, and Puma.

### Year Three (Final Year)

Focus on the area of the fashion industry you intend to work in. Tailor final-year projects to your career ambitions.

**Core Modules (120 credits):**

- **Creative Industries: Development and Enquiry** (20 credits) — Further develop research and inquiry skills within the fashion and textile industry context. Undertake research methods on a personal topic of interest and present conclusions and recommendations.
- **Final Major Project** (40 credits) — Dive deep into a specific realm aligned with future career goals. Craft a substantial body of visually compelling communication design as the cornerstone of your portfolio.
- **Craft & Make** (20 credits) — Translate design concepts into reality in a professional studio environment, bringing developed ideas to fruition.
- **Commercialisation & Portfolio Prep** (40 credits) — Develop self-promotion assets and prepare a strategy to progress into an industry role. Solidify work for a seamless transition into fashion, communication, lifestyle, or media fields.
