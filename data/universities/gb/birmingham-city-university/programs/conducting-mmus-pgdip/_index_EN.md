# Conducting MMus / PgDip

## Overview

The principal aim of our Master of Music (MMus) Conducting course is to provide you with knowledge, skills, and confidence to succeed as a professional conductor. The course is suitable for both recent graduates and suitably qualified mature students, with a range of support to ensure that you leave Royal Birmingham Conservatoire ready to pursue a conducting career.

### Conducting Pathways

We offer three specialist conducting pathways:
- **Brass Band Conducting**
- **Choral Conducting**
- **Orchestral Conducting**

All our conducting pathways offer the technical training, collaborative opportunities, and podium time necessary to help you prepare for a career as a professional conductor. You will spend considerable time developing your directing techniques in rehearsals and will also have opportunities to demonstrate your skills in performance. In addition to attending and participating in scheduled ensemble activities, you will be encouraged to explore other opportunities to engage as conductor or observer. Students across all three pathways will also attend classes/workshops of their peers studying other conducting specialisms.

### What's Covered in This Course?

- Regular individual tuition from leading UK and internationally renowned practitioners, teachers, and researchers
- Bespoke support classes in musicianship and repertoire
- Opportunities to participate in a wide variety of creative projects, including interdisciplinary collaborations
- Frequent opportunities to perform, both within Departmental activities and in concerts
- Access to forums, seminars and masterclasses with distinguished visiting artists
- Access to the Conservatoire's broad range of ensemble activities
- Professional Development modules tailored to your individual needs
- Networking with leading professionals from around the world

## Entry Requirements

- UK students should normally hold an honours degree, ideally but not necessarily in Music
- Non-UK students should hold a Bachelor's degree or similar degree-equivalent diploma
- **Audition:** Live audition required, demonstrating conducting ability
- **IELTS:** 6.0 overall with no less than 5.5 in each band (international/EU students)

## Course Structure

### PgDip (120 credits)
- **Career Development** (20 credits) — Self-promotion, self-management, funding and wellbeing for professional musicians
- **Principal Study 1** (60 credits) — Choose one pathway:
  - Principal Study 1: Choral Conducting — Developing gestural techniques, administration, management, communication, interpretation and musicianship; weekly rehearsals of Conservatoire's Camerata chamber choir; masterclasses from visiting experts
  - Principal Study 1: Orchestral Conducting — Conducting techniques in rehearsal and performance, rehearsal management, musicianship skills, score reading, programming, stylistic awareness and interpretation; experience with Symphony Orchestra, Wind Orchestra, Thallein Ensemble
  - Principal Study 1: Brass Band Conducting — Conducting techniques, rehearsal management, musicianship, score reading, programming and stylistic awareness in the brass band context
- **Professional Development Option** (40 credits)

### MMus (180 credits)
- All PgDip modules plus:
- **Principal Study 2** (60 credits) — Advanced conducting project and further development in your specialist pathway
