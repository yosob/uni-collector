# Arts and Education Practices MA

## Overview

The MA Arts and Education Practices at Birmingham City University is the **only Arts Education MA in the UK based in a School of Art**. Rooted in the principle of education as a "practice of freedom" (Bell Hooks, 1994), the course develops artist educators who work across community projects, artist-led spaces, galleries, schools, colleges, and universities.

Students engage with critical pedagogy through facilitation, mentoring, collaboration, dialogue, and mutual enquiry. The course draws on Birmingham School of Art's rich heritage and its partnerships with leading cultural organisations, offering a unique environment where art practice and education theory converge.

The course has an international reputation, with alumni working in over **20 countries** worldwide. It is designed for those who wish to develop their practice as artist educators, whether in formal educational settings, galleries, community contexts, or independent practice.

## Course Structure

### Core Modules (160 credits)

| Module | Code | Credits | Semester |
|---|---|---|---|
| Narratives of Art and Design Education Practices | ART7906 | 40 | 1 |
| Research in Practice | ART7902 | 20 | 2 |
| Global Innovations in Arts Practice Education | ART7868 | 40 | 2 |
| Major Project | ADM7912 | 60 | 3 |

**Narratives of Art and Design Education Practices** (40 credits) — Explores the histories, theories and narratives that shape art and design education. Develops critical understanding of how educational practices are constructed and communicated.

**Research in Practice** (20 credits) — Introduces creative research methods and methodologies. Includes a placement within an arts education context, connecting theory with professional practice.

**Global Innovations in Arts Practice Education** (40 credits) — Examines innovative approaches to arts education across global contexts. Develops understanding of international perspectives and contemporary practices in the field.

**Major Project** (60 credits) — An in-depth independent research project exploring an area of personal interest within the expanded field of arts education. Viewed as a first step in professional progression.
