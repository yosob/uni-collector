## Programme Overview

The MA Interior Architecture and Design programme at Birmingham City University explores the distinctive qualities associated with the interiors of inhabited architecture. The course offers you the opportunity to tailor your own professional path — whether in more conventional areas within interiors (residential, retail, health, transportation, etc.) or in related fields (exhibition design, installation design, etc.).

The programme enables you to learn about recent transformations in the industry, with a particular focus on **sustainability**, **new technologies** (including but not limited to AI), and **user-led design**. You will develop a communication and marketing toolkit that effectively connects you with your audience, making good use of social media platforms, networking, and job-hunting opportunities.

You will be encouraged to engage with on-campus facilities — including workshops where you can experiment with advanced methods of fabrication using laser cutting, digital printing, and digital carving technologies. Sessions are recorded to support flexible learning alongside other commitments.

Studying at postgraduate level offers a unique opportunity to hone your research skills, considerably improving your writing and communication to enhance career prospects. The programme partners with distinctive players in the industry and presents attractive internship opportunities. Previous graduates have been employed at leading international practices as architects, interior designers, or design lecturers, with the pathway to PhD study also opened up.

### Key Themes

- Interior architecture and inhabited spaces
- Sustainability and environmental design
- New technologies, including AI and digital fabrication
- User-led and human-centred design
- Professional practice and industry engagement
- Design communication and marketing

## Module Structure (180 CATS)

### Trimester 1 — PG Certificate Stage (60 Credits)

| Module | Credits | Type |
|--------|---------|------|
| Design Cultures | 20 | Subject (shared) |
| Design Systems | 40 | Core |

This initial stage tackles the context and current conditions affecting the Interior Architecture and Design Industry Sector, examining the impact on design commissions, concepts, constraints, and communication.

### Trimester 2 — PG Diploma Stage (60 Credits)

| Module | Credits | Type |
|--------|---------|------|
| Co.LAB (Live Project – PG) | 20 | Optional |
| Appreciative Design Inquiry | 40 | Core |

The second stage addresses how creative and commercial design processes can elevate interior design facilities and spaces to new levels of experience, performance, and perception.

### Trimester 3 — MA Stage (60 Credits)

| Module | Credits | Type |
|--------|---------|------|
| Major Project: Interior Architecture and Design | 60 | Core |

This final stage allows students to determine a self-generated Signature Brief — a creative interior design project tailored to the unique skills, interests, and professional or academic aspirations of the MA student.

### Optional: Professional Placement Year (60 Credits, Level 6)

Extends the course to 18 months with an integrated professional placement module (PLA6003, 60 credits).

## Learning Outcomes

### Knowledge and Understanding
- Evaluate evidence, arguments and assumptions to make sound judgments within a structured discourse relating to interior architectural culture, theory, and design
- Apply knowledge of the global context of the Interior Architect and construction industry for decision-making in complex and unpredictable circumstances
- Understand how knowledge is advanced through research to produce clear, logically argued, and original written work

### Practical and Professional Skills
- Determine your own strategic 'Praxis' — becoming the designer who owns their approach, practice, and process
- Apply a range of communication methods and media to present creative explorations and proposals effectively to distinct audiences
- Operate independently or collaboratively to identify individual learning needs and prepare for employment as a creative design practitioner
