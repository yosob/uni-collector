# Fine Art MA (1 year full-time)

> 🏛️ **Historic Venue:** Based in the Grade I-listed Margaret Street School of Art — the UK's first municipal art school, part of a revolutionary movement to make art available to all.

## Overview

The MA Fine Art at Birmingham City University brings together a diverse community of international creative visual thinkers and makers with the aim of cultivating innovative, imaginative, and self-reflexive approaches to making contemporary art. The course encourages a plurality of visions based on individual, proposal-led study, whilst encouraging a range of media exploration.

From **drawing, painting and print, to sculpture, digital media and performance**, the programme allows you to experiment with a variety of artistic techniques and develop your unique voice through analogue and digital processes. You will explore the material, political, cultural, and theoretical factors shaping contemporary fine art, gaining hands-on experience in professional production, advanced research methods, contextual understanding and effective dissemination of your work.

> "My MA in Fine Art enabled me the time and space to reach a new level of critical engagement with my practice. Continual discussion with my peers from a diverse range of disciplines pushed my work into new territories and encouraged crucial challenges that gave rise to bold developments." — Betsy Bradley, MA Fine Art Graduate

## Entry Requirements

### Academic
- A minimum of a **2:2 honours degree**, or equivalent, in **Fine Art, Art and Design**, or another Arts-based subject
- Equivalent **professional or life experience** also considered
- **Portfolio** required — applicants must submit a good portfolio of work

### English Language (International)
- **IELTS:** 6.0 overall with no less than 5.5 in each band, or equivalent
- Pre-sessional English courses available via BCUIC (Birmingham City University International College)

## Course Structure

### Core Modules (160 credits total)

| Module | Credits | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Studio Practice** | 40 | Self-directed practical module. Develop individual/collaborative arts practice through a study proposal. Reorientate practice through imaginative practical experimentation and research into contemporary art, philosophical, and transcultural contexts. |
| **Research Through Practice** | 20 | Introduction to creative research methods and methodologies. Focus on research through practice where practice itself serves a research purpose. Deepen understanding of your own work through analysis and synthesis of research tools. |
| **Advanced Studio Practice and Dissemination** | 40 | Advance the speculative and experimental nature of art making. Further extend and deepen integration of practical artistic development with theoretical and contextual research. Develop work through inter-disciplinary and technical experimentation. |
| **Professional Project** | 60 | Sustained, in-depth and theoretically informed research project exploring an area of personal interest. Outcome may take the form of a written dissertation, practical outcome with accompanying reflective/critical material, or negotiated equivalent. |

### Optional Modules (choose 20 credits)

| Module | Credits | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Work Placement** | 20 | 70-hour work placement (block or spread over the module). Develop professional attributes and subject-specific skills. Prepare CV, attend interviews, source own placement with support from academic staff and ADM Careers+. |
| **Industry Project** | 20 | Apply knowledge and skills to an external, professional brief set in negotiation with an external client/agency/community. Apply discipline-specific skills broadly, collaborating with fellow students and stakeholders. |
| **Independent Creative Entrepreneur** | 20 | Focus on creative portfolio careers, promoting and pitching ideas. Develop skills for crafting artistic artifacts, performing, providing services, or offering consultancy as an independent creative practitioner. |

### Professional Placement (optional, 60 additional credits)

Students choosing the Professional Placement option undertake a **20-week credit-bearing placement** with an employer, extending the programme to **18 months full-time** (240 total credits). The placement is designed to evidence professional skills, attitudes and behaviours at the point of entry to the postgraduate job market.

Please note: Placements are confirmed following a competitive, employer-led selection process and cannot be guaranteed.

## Career Prospects

Graduates develop transferable skills including **creative problem solving, communication, presentation, adaptability, independence, teamwork, and time management**, as well as technical and digital skills in a range of workshop practices.

Career paths include:
- Practicing Artist
- Curator / Gallery Manager
- Arts Administrator
- Academic / Researcher
- Creative Project Founder
- Gallery Space Founder
- Art Educator / Teacher
- Cultural Programme Manager
- Arts Consultant

Alumni have been successful in diverse sectors including education, cultural industries, and arts organisations in both the public and private sectors at national and international level.
