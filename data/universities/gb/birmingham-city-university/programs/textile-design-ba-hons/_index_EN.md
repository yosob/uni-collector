# Textile Design BA (Hons) (3 years)

> 💡 **Note:** The former "Surface Pattern and Textile Design BA (Hons)" has been merged into this programme.

## Overview

The Textile Design BA (Hons) at Birmingham City University explores the potential of fibres, yarns and processes to create cloth and materials with distinctive qualities and properties. It is a highly creative field with the potential to impact and enrich all aspects of our lives. Knowledge in this area is key in making informed choices that will reduce the environmental and social impact of products and can be used artistically to raise awareness and inspire behavioural change.

The course nurtures fresh thinking and industry-ready designers and practitioners with expertise in the craftsmanship of Textile Design across the disciplines of **Print, Weave, Knit, Embroidery, and Material Innovation**. The exciting potential of cross-disciplinary practice is supported and encouraged, giving students the option to either specialise or combine disciplines, embracing both digital technologies and traditional craft skills.

Through experimentation in dedicated textile workshops equipped with cutting-edge industry facilities, students develop the specialist skills, knowledge and experiences required to thrive in a growing field of industry roles and opportunities. Creative play and problem-solving are at the heart of learning. Students are guided by passionate and experienced staff as they discover their own personal expression through the language of design — **colour, pattern, surface, texture and image**.

**Birmingham** is a vibrant and multi-cultural city providing exposure to diverse perspectives and opportunities. The inclusive creative community prioritises and celebrates individuality, empowering and supporting students to embrace and embed this within their practice.

## Course Structure

The course is structured around three progressive years, each comprising 120 CATS credits (360 total). The programme centres on the five core textile disciplines of **Print, Weave, Knit, Embroidery, and Material Innovation**, with cross-disciplinary practice supported throughout.

### Year 1 — Exploration and Foundation
*120 credits total*
- **Introduction to Textile Processes: Print, Weave, Knit, Embroidery** (40 credits) — Develop foundational skills across all core textile processes
- **Design Fundamentals: Colour, Pattern, Surface, Texture** (20 credits) — Master the core elements of design language
- **Material Innovation and Experimentation** (20 credits) — Explore material properties and innovative applications
- **Contextual and Professional Practice 1** (20 credits) — Industry awareness and professional direction
- **Creative Play and Problem-Solving** (20 credits) — Develop design thinking through experimentation

### Year 2 — Specialisation and Industry Engagement
*120 credits total*
- **Specialist Textile Practice** (40 credits) — Deepen skills in a chosen discipline or cross-disciplinary pathway
- **Industry Live Briefs and Professional Collaboration** (20 credits) — Collaborate with real industry clients on design projects
- **Integrated Work Placement** (20 credits) — Complete industry placements within the first two years
- **Contextual and Professional Practice 2** (20 credits) — Sustainable design and circular economy principles
- **Sustainable and Responsible Design Concepts** (20 credits) — Drive the industry's transition to a circular sustainable economy

### Year 3 — Autonomous Practice and Graduation
*120 credits total*
- **Final Collection (Autonomous Practice)** (60 credits) — Complete a self-directed textile design graduation collection
- **Next Step: Bridging Study and Industry** (20 credits) — Plan your career trajectory with university network support
- **Professional Portfolio Development** (20 credits) — Tailored professional portfolio preparation
- **Contextual and Professional Practice 3** (20 credits) — Final positioning and industry readiness

### Optional Professional Placement Year
After Year 2, students may undertake a year-long professional placement (extending the course to four years). Placement year fees are 20% of the full-time course fee.
