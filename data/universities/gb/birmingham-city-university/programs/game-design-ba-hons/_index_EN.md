# Game Design BA (Hons)

## Overview

BA (Hons) Game Design at Birmingham City University offers the perfect platform for aspiring game designers to turn their creative visions into reality. You will learn the art of crafting engaging game narratives, mechanics, and world-building in a creative and supportive environment. The course provides a balance between artistic vision and technical proficiency, covering game design theories, modelling, animation, immersive technologies, and collaborative team projects that simulate real-world game studio production.

You will have access to industry-standard design software and tools, including Adobe Creative Suite, Maya, and ZBrush, as well as developer kits from PlayStation, Xbox, and Nintendo. The course benefits from partnerships with Unity and Unreal Engine, and is TIGA accredited.

## Course Structure

### Year One (Level 4)

Students acquire a solid foundation in game programming, design theories, and the technical aspects of game development. Through lectures, practical workshops, and group projects, you will learn core concepts in game design such as game mechanics and storytelling. You will develop basic programming and artistic skills essential for creating engaging games and apply your knowledge in practical scenarios to design simple game prototypes.

**Core Modules (120 credits)** — Module names require verification from the BCU course page or course specification document as they are loaded dynamically.

### Year Two (Level 5)

Students delve deeper into sophisticated design techniques and work on more complex projects, simulating real-world game development environments. This phase includes advanced modules on interactive narrative development and user interface design. There is a strong emphasis on teamwork and collaborative projects.

**Core Modules (120 credits)**

### Professional Placement Year (Optional)

Spend a full year with an employer after Year 2. Fees are 20% of the full-time course fee for that year.

### Final Year (Level 6)

Final year students complete advanced modules and a major project, consolidating their skills for industry readiness. Focus on professional portfolio development and real-world project execution.

**Core Modules (120 credits)**
