# Landscape Architecture with Foundation Year BA (Hons) (4 years)

## Overview

Our Landscape Architecture with Foundation Year BA (Hons) course begins with a foundation year designed to develop the core skills, knowledge and confidence needed to progress onto the BA Honours Landscape Architecture degree. The foundation year provides an opportunity to explore the exciting world of contemporary design through various scales and develop creative solutions, encapsulating Landscape Architecture, Architecture, Interior Architecture and Design, and Product and Furniture Design.

It provides a supported learning environment to explore conceptual ideas through to detailed design, mapping and modelling and is assessed through mini design-focussed projects and a specialised final project in a chosen field. The foundation year is ideally suited to students who need to develop their design, drawing, technical and presentations skills before entering a BA course, or for those who have not yet decided which specific design discipline or career to pursue.

After completing the foundation year, students progress to the full BA (Hons) Landscape Architecture course — fully recognised and accredited by the Landscape Institute (LI). The course embraces a creative studio culture, which mirrors professional working practices and is supported by work placements to prepare you for your future career. Students are encouraged and supported in competing for industry awards.

During your study, you will be part of collaborative architecture and design initiatives, giving you the opportunity to work on a diverse variety of live projects. Recent examples include urban revival in Brindleyplace, exploring the 15-minute campus concept, and strategic landscape planning for Birmingham's green infrastructure. The course hosts a programme of lectures and workshops from a large variety of renowned international professionals.

Taught in the Parkside Building, overlooking Eastside Park on the multi-million-pound City Centre Campus, providing access to digital studios, 3D design workshops, traditional print, ceramics, metal and woodworking spaces.

An optional Professional Placement Year allows you to spend a whole year with an employer following successful completion of your second year — a great way to find out more about your chosen career.

## Course Structure

The course is structured as a foundation year followed by three years of the BA Landscape Architecture programme, each with progressive themes. All modules are assessed through 100% coursework. Students may undertake an optional Professional Placement Year between Year 2 and Year 3.

### Foundation Year (Year 0)
*120 credits total — all CORE*

The foundation year introduces fundamental principles of design thinking across multiple design disciplines:
- **Drawing and Visual Communication** — Core skills for design representation
- **Spatial Design** — Introduction to spatial thinking and design processes
- **Model-making** — Physical and digital model-making techniques
- **Digital Skills** — Introduction to CAD and digital design tools
- **Design Context** — Understanding design in its cultural, social and environmental context
- **Creative Problem-solving** — Developing design thinking abilities
- **Academic Skills** — Research, critical reflection and portfolio development
- **Specialised Final Project** — A focused project in a chosen design field

### Year 1
*120 credits total — all CORE*
- **Inspiration** (20 credits) — A design project responding to a chosen aspect of site, exploring concept development and the design process through hand drawing, model making and digital techniques
- **Skills** (20 credits) — Introduction to a range of skills for landscape architecture practice including hand and digital techniques: surveying, CAD, GIS, and Adobe Creative Cloud
- **Influence** (20 credits) — Study of historical and contemporary influences on landscape design and the cultural context of landscape architecture
- **Components** (20 credits) — Understanding of materials, construction techniques and planting design as fundamental components of landscape architecture
- **Formation** (40 credits) — A comprehensive design project exploring the formation of space through landscape architecture, integrating skills and knowledge from across Year 1

### Year 2
*100 credits CORE + 20 credits OPTIONAL*
- **Construction and Planting Design** (20 credits) — Detailed exploration of construction detailing and planting design principles for landscape architecture projects
- **Context** (20 credits) — Analysis of the physical, social and environmental context of landscape design projects
- **Work Placement (Praxis)** (20 credits) — Professional work placement providing real-world experience in a landscape architecture practice
- **Urban Design** (20 credits) — Exploration of urban design principles and their application to landscape architecture in the public realm
- **Designed Ecologies** (20 credits) — Exploration of multi-layered systems incorporating and integrating social and ecological components within the design of space, from strategic analysis to detail design for enhanced ecology and visitor experience

*Choose one of the following (20 credits):*
- **Collaborative Practice** — Interdisciplinary project with students from complementary disciplines, developing skills of collaboration and critical reflection
- **Live project** — Apply knowledge and skills to an external professional brief set by an external client or agency in consultation with your supervisor

### Optional Professional Placement Year
Between Year 2 and Final Year, students may undertake a 40-week professional placement (120 credits). Fees during the placement year are equivalent to 20% of the total full-time course fee.

### Final Year (Year 3)
*120 credits total — all CORE*
- **Major Project: Landscape Architecture** (40 credits) — A comprehensive design project demonstrating mastery of landscape architecture principles and practice
- **Professionalism** (20 credits) — Development of professional skills and understanding of the legal, ethical and institutional framework of landscape architecture practice
- **Major Project: Define** (20 credits) — Research and definition phase of the major design project, establishing theoretical and contextual foundations
- **Major Project: Deliver** (40 credits) — Resolution and delivery of the major design project, demonstrating comprehensive design ability from concept to detailed proposal

## Placements

The course integrates work placement within the Year 2 Praxis module, and offers an optional full Professional Placement Year between Year 2 and Year 3. The University's extensive network of local, regional and national landscape architecture practices provides unrivalled work experience opportunities.

Recent placement partners include practices such as Fira, a nationally recognised practice of landscape architects, urban designers, master planners and architects. Students have worked on projects at various stages from early design to on-site construction.

Career paths include:

- Urban Designer
- Graduate Landscape and Urban Designer
- Landscape Assistant (Planning and Policy)
