# Art and Design BA (Hons) (3 years)

> ⚠️ **Course Renamed:** This course has been renamed to **Art and Design with Creative Technologies BA (Hons)** for 2026/27 entry. The original URL (`art-and-design-ba-hons-2026-27`) returns 404. All data below is extracted from the replacement course page.

## Overview

The Art and Design BA (Hons) at Birmingham City University explores the intersection of traditional art practice and creative technologies. Students develop skills in digital fabrication, creative coding, and mixed media alongside core art practice. The programme is designed for the evolving creative industries, combining conceptual strategies with hands-on making across three progressive years.

The course encourages students to experiment with a wide range of materials, processes, and technologies, developing an individual creative voice while building technical proficiency in both analogue and digital media. From Year 2, students specialise through electives in coding, digital fabrication, and collaborative practice, culminating in a major project in Year 3.

## Course Structure

The course is structured around three progressive years, each comprising 120 CATS credits (360 total). All modules are core and assessed through 100% coursework — no written exams.

### Year 1 — Exploring Practice
*120 credits total*
- **Exploring Materials and Processes** (20 credits) — Introduction to a wide range of 2D, 3D, and time-based materials and processes
- **Contextualising Art Practice** (20 credits) — Historical and theoretical contexts for contemporary art and design
- **Creative Technologies: Exploring Digital Practice** (20 credits) — Foundational digital skills for creative practice
- **Art Practice: Conceptual Strategies** (40 credits) — Developing conceptual approaches to art-making
- **Art Practice: Specialisms** (20 credits) — Exploring specialist areas within art and design

### Year 2 — Developing Practice
*120 credits total*
- **Creative Technologies: Coding for Creatives** (20 credits) — Introduction to creative coding and computational thinking
- **Creative Technologies: Digital Fabrication** (20 credits) — Digital manufacturing techniques including 3D printing, laser cutting, and CNC
- **Art Practice: Ideas in Translation** (40 credits) — Translating concepts across media and contexts
- **Contextualising Art Practice: Ideas and Audience** (20 credits) — Engaging audiences and understanding the role of the artist in society
- **Art Practice: Collaboration and Exchange** (20 credits) — Collaborative and interdisciplinary projects

### Year 3 — Professional Practice
*120 credits total*
- **Art Practice: Major Project** (60 credits) — Substantial self-directed project demonstrating creative independence
- **Creative Technologies: Professional Practice** (20 credits) — Professional skills and industry engagement
- **Contextualising Art Practice: Research Essay** (20 credits) — Extended written research on a chosen topic
- **Art Practice: Professional Futures** (20 credits) — Career development and professional positioning
