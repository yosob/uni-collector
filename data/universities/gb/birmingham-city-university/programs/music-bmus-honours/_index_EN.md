# Music BMus (Hons)

## Overview

The BMus (Hons) degree at Royal Birmingham Conservatoire will help you realise your potential and prepare you for a career in the music profession, whether that is in performance, composition, or music technology. The programme will develop your specialist musical skills as well as your theoretical, historical, and practice-based knowledge, all the while encouraging your individual creativity. As a result, you will become an independent learner, a critical interpreter, and a reflective practitioner.

You will have the opportunity to study in one of three Principal Study areas:
- **Performance** (Instrumental or Vocal)
- **Composition**
- **Music Technology**

You will graduate with a named award that reflects the Principal Study area chosen. During your time at the Conservatoire, you will study within a particular Department that is determined by your Principal Study area. Our departments include: Brass; Composition; Historical Performance; Keyboard; Music Technology; Percussion; Strings (bowed and plucked); Vocal and Operatic; and Woodwind.

- **UCAS Tariff:** 32 points
- **A Levels:** EE (or equivalent)
- **IB:** 24 points overall
- **IELTS:** 5.5 in all four skills areas (international/EU students)
- **Audition:** Required — entry is by audition only via UCAS Conservatoires
- **BTEC:** 32 UCAS Tariff points from a relevant Diploma
- **Access to HE:** Pass with 60 credits, 45 at Level 3
- **T Level:** Pass overall (E or above on the core)

### Audition Requirements

- **Performance:** Demonstrate general musical knowledge and musicianship skills, including harmony and aural
- **Composition:** Submit a portfolio of at least three compositions with a high-quality recording of at least one work
- **Music Technology:** Submit a portfolio of at least three pieces (recordings, productions, compositions, sound to picture, or other appropriate material)

## Course Structure

### Year One (Level 4)

Students build foundational skills in their Principal Study area alongside core musical knowledge.

**Principal Study Modules (60 credits — choose one):**
- Principal Study: Performance 1
- Principal Study: Percussion 1
- Principal Study: Composition 1
- Principal Study: Music Technology 1

**Core Modules (60 credits):**
- Language of Music 1 (20 credits) — Historical awareness, aural perception and harmonic understanding from ancient Western Art music to the Age of Common Practice (c. 1650–1900)
- Contextual Studies: Performance Traditions 1 (20 credits) — Performance styles and traditions from c. 1600–1900
- Professional Portfolio 1: Community Engagement (20 credits) — Practical introduction to music activities in community/education settings

### Year Two (Level 5)

Students deepen their Principal Study practice and broaden their professional skills.

**Principal Study Modules (60 credits — choose one):**
- Principal Study: Performance 2
- Principal Study: Percussion 2
- Principal Study: Composition 2
- Principal Study: Music Technology 2

**Core Modules (60 credits):**
- Language of Music 2 (20 credits) — Advanced aural perception, stylistic harmony (trio sonata, string quartet, voice and piano)
- Contextual Studies: Performance Traditions 2 (20 credits) — Wider range of performance issues in historical and contemporary idioms
- Professional Portfolio 2: Pedagogy and Practice (20 credits) — Pedagogical aspects of the principal-study discipline

### Year Three (Level 6)

Students specialise and refine their musical identity.

**Principal Study Modules (60 credits — choose one):**
- Principal Study: Performance 3
- Principal Study: Percussion 3
- Principal Study: Composition 3
- Principal Study: Music Technology 3

**Core Modules (40 credits):**
- Language of Music: Specialism 1 (20 credits)
- Professional Portfolio 3: Collaborative Project (20 credits)

**Plus 20 credits from a range of option modules**

### Year Four (Level 7)

Students undertake a major final project demonstrating professional-level achievement.

**Principal Study Modules (60 credits — choose one):**
- Final Recital (Performance)
- Final Portfolio (Composition)
- Final Portfolio (Music Technology)

**Core Modules (60 credits):**
- Language of Music: Specialism 2 (20 credits)
- Professional Portfolio 4 (40 credits)
