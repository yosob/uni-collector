# MDes Games Design and Development

## Programme Overview

The **Master of Design (MDes) in Games Design and Development** provides students with a unique opportunity to **design and develop their own computer game** to the highest industry standards while gaining hands-on experience with the product lifecycle, gathering technical and professional skills, and further practical entrepreneurship knowledge. It is the perfect stepping stone to smoothly transition to design and programming roles in the games industry.

Led by an **award-winning indie developer**, you'll acquire sought-after skills in game design, 3D graphics, UI/UX, and more. Our expert faculty will guide you through gaining real-world experience in designing, building, and releasing your own games.

## Why Choose This Programme?

- **Design your own game** — from concept to release, following industry-standard production processes
- Study in the **£19m GRID building** with a purpose-built Games Studio — one of only a handful of such facilities worldwide
- Learn from an **award-winning indie developer** (SOLAS 128) and experienced faculty
- Edinburgh is a **thriving games industry hub** — home to Rockstar North, Ninja Kiwi, and numerous indie studios
- Gain skills in **game design, 3D graphics, UI/UX, entrepreneurship** and more
- Annual **graduate showcase event** to present your game to industry professionals
- **Future Shapers Scholarship** — up to 100% tuition fee coverage available

## Entry Requirements

- **Academic:** Minimum 2:2 honours degree (or non-UK equivalent) in a subject area which includes university-level mathematics and programming. Games experience is valuable; a detailed statement of purpose is acceptable.
- **English Language:** IELTS 6.0 overall with no score lower than 5.5
- Pre-sessional English courses available for applicants below IELTS 6.0

## Career Prospects

Graduates pursue diverse roles across the games and interactive entertainment industry:

- **Game UX Designer** — enhances user experience and interface design
- **Game Developer** — develops code and software components
- **Game Artist** — designs visual assets, characters, environments and animations
- **Game Producer** — manages the development process
- **Game Writer/Narrative Designer** — crafts story and narrative elements
- **Game Sound Designer** — creates and implements audio elements
- **Game AI Programmer** — develops AI systems for game behaviours

Edinburgh's thriving games industry provides excellent networking and employment opportunities throughout your studies and beyond.
