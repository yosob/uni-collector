# MSc Design Management

## Programme Overview

MSc Design Management has been developed for those already within a **creative industry** who are looking to gain core and relevant skills of **business and management**, and for those in an established business environment looking to gain experience and knowledge in **developing creative strategies**.

You'll approach design management by applying **innovative process analysis, a new way of thinking and professional application** to current industry problems to achieve positive outcomes. The programme blends **creativity, innovation, entrepreneurship, and management**.

## Why Choose This Programme?

- Gain core business and management skills within a creative industry context
- Develop **creative strategies** for established business environments
- Focus on **sustainability, transition design, and net-zero initiatives**
- **Strategic Design Management** for sustainable innovation in creative organisations
- Study at the **Scottish Borders Campus** — a purpose-built design environment
- Specialised courses in strategic design, change management, and user experience design
- September and January intake options
- **20% alumni discount** available for Heriot-Watt graduates

## Entry Requirements

- **Academic:** Minimum 2:2 honours degree (or non-UK equivalent) in any subject with evidence of management, creative subjects or engineering; personal statement demonstrating interest required
- **CV:** Required as part of application — work or project experience in a related role taken into consideration
- **English Language:** IELTS 6.0 overall with no score lower than 5.5 (writing must be 6.0 minimum)
- Pre-sessional English courses available for applicants below IELTS 6.0

## Career Prospects

Graduates pursue diverse roles across design management and creative strategy:

- **Design Manager / Creative Strategist**
- **Brand Manager / Innovation Consultant**
- **UX Strategy Lead / Design Operations Manager**
- **Sustainable Design Strategy Consultant**

The interdisciplinary approach prepares graduates for leadership roles requiring creative vision and business acumen in commercial and organisational contexts.
