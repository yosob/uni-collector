# BA (Hons) Design for Textiles (Fashion, Interior, Art)

## Programme Overview

Our BA (Hons) Design for Textiles (Fashion, Interior, Art) programme provides expertise in designing and producing innovative fabrics for a variety of outcomes. You'll develop your own visual handwriting and learn to translate and express this using a variety of media.

At the core of the programme is an appreciation of **raw materials**, **innovation in technique**, an in-depth **design process**, and designing **sustainably and responsibly** to meet global and local challenges. The programme underpins originality and innovation with a strong **technological understanding**, ensuring graduates are well equipped to join the textile industry.

The programme covers **knitted, woven and printed textiles**, digital and **Computer-Aided Design (CAD)** technologies, marketing strategy, and sustainable practices within the fashion, interior, and textile art industries. In Year 3, you'll specialise in **print, weave, or knit** and have the opportunity to complete an industry placement or collaborative industry project.

Taught at our **Scottish Borders Campus**, close to the heart of Scotland's luxury textile industry, the programme combines traditional craftsmanship with cutting-edge textile innovation.

## Programme Structure

### Year 1 — Foundation

Build a foundation for future development with a combination of lectures and practical classes designed to help you choose your specialism. Practical projects in printed, woven and knitted textiles and CAD are underpinned by design development projects covering drawing, painting and material manipulation.

| Semester | Module |
|----------|--------|
| September | Design Context and Process |
| September | Design Communication |
| January | Design Prototyping |
| January | Design Challenge |

### Year 2 — Specialisation

Develop skills in printed, woven and knitted textile design with outcomes for interiors, fashion or art. Organised study tours provide opportunities for visual research, market trend analysis, and attending industry events such as London Design Week.

**Mandatory:**

|----------|--------|
| September | Design Thinking |
| September | Design for Circularity — Concepts for Sustainable Textiles and Fashion |

- Circular Economy for Design

## Career Prospects

Graduates pursue diverse careers in the textile and design industries:

- **Textile Designer** — printed, woven or knitted fabrics for fashion and furnishing
- **Surface Pattern Designer** — wall coverings, gift wrap, stationery
- **Colour and Trend Forecaster**
- **New Textile Product Developer**
- **Gallery Artist / Practitioner**
- **Entrepreneur** — sustainable textile design, visible mending
- **Educator** in textile design

Awards at New Designers from: **The Worshipful Company of Weavers, John Lewis, The Clothworkers Company**.
