# MA Fashion and Textiles Design

## Programme Overview

Our MA Fashion and Textile Design gives you the opportunity to explore concepts and contemporary topics in design, fashion and textiles through **practice-based learning** and an in-depth study of design. You'll challenge traditional and contemporary uses of fashion and textiles and look at ways to create opportunities to promote new approaches and processes.

The programme is aimed at those with an art and design background who are looking to **challenge established practice** and offer new insights. You'll study an area of fashion and textiles that interests you most, while working on inter-disciplinary projects across the School of Textiles and Design to foster design innovation.

## Why Choose This Programme?

- Practice-based learning with an in-depth study of design
- Interdisciplinary collaboration across the School of Textiles and Design
- Study at the **Scottish Borders Campus** — the heart of Scotland's luxury textile industry
- Focus areas include Fashion & Textile Technology, Sustainability, Material Futures, Knitwear Design, and Smart Fashion
- September and January intake options
- **20% alumni discount** available for Heriot-Watt graduates

## Entry Requirements

- **Academic:** Minimum 2:2 honours degree (or non-UK equivalent) with a portfolio demonstrating design-led processes
- **Portfolio:** Required — fashion/textiles design or broad creative practice evidence; CV required
- **English Language:** IELTS 6.0 overall with no score lower than 5.5 (writing must be 6.0 minimum)
- Pre-sessional English courses available for applicants below IELTS 6.0

## Career Prospects

Graduates pursue diverse roles across the fashion and textile industries:

- **Fashion Designer / Textile Designer**
- **Creative Director / Design Consultant**
- **Knitwear Designer / Surface Pattern Designer**
- **Sustainable Fashion Consultant**
- **Textile Technologist**

The interdisciplinary approach prepares graduates for roles requiring both creative and strategic thinking in global and local contexts.
