# BA (Hons) Interior Architecture and Design

## Programme Overview

Our BA (Hons) Interior Architecture and Design programme offers a **creative blend of design skills** to equip you for industry. You'll apply conceptual methods to imaginatively adapt or re-use interior environments. Key areas include **sustainability**, **human-centred design**, hand and digital making skills, materials, drawing, furniture and detailing, technology and digital communication.

You'll learn about innovative new technology to bring your interior design ideas to life, working on **real projects for industry** with brands such as **Adidas, Herman Miller** and **Accor Hotel Group**.

The programme is taught at our **Scottish Borders Campus**, with the **Go Global** mobility programme offering study opportunities at our Dubai campus or partner universities worldwide.

## Why Choose This Programme?

- **3rd in Scotland** for Interior Design (Guardian University Guide 2026)
- Chartered architects and research-active academics as teaching staff
- Real industry projects with **Adidas, Herman Miller, Accor Hotel Group**
- **Go Global** mobility programme — study at partner universities worldwide
- Graduates employed by **AECOM, Area, IKEA**
- Optional Year 3 industry placement and live projects

## Entry Requirements

- **Academic (Year 1 Standard):** Highers BBBB / A-Levels BCC + digital portfolio / IB 29 points / BTEC Extended Diploma DMM
- **Minimum (Fair Access):** Highers BBBC / A-Levels BCC / IB 29 points
- **Year 2 Direct Entry:** Advanced Highers BB / A-Levels BBC + digital portfolio / IB 30 points
- **Year 3 Direct Entry:** HND BB in relevant subject (3D Design, Interior Design)
- **English Language:** IELTS 6.0 overall with no score lower than 5.5
- **Portfolio:** Digital portfolio required (10-20 images showing research, design development, technical skills, visual communication)

## Programme Structure

### Year 1 — Foundation (Common Level 1)

A broad common year for all Fashion, Communication, Marketing and Retailing students, developing individual strengths in analysis, evaluation and contextualisation.

| Semester | Module |
|----------|--------|
| September | Design Context and Process |
| September | Design Communication |
| January | Design Prototyping |
| January | Design Challenge |

### Year 2 — Specialisation

Develop interior architecture fundamentals including construction, residential design and workspace design.

**Mandatory:**

|----------|--------|
| September | Design Thinking |
| September | Construction for Interior Architecture |
| September | Design Studio 1 — Residential Design |
| January | Design Research Methods |
| January | Design Studio 2 — Workspace Design |

**Optional (January — choose one):**

- Artistically Intelligent Design
- Design Simulation and Modelling
- Trend Forecasting and Media Content Production

- Circular Economy for Design

- **Spatial Designer / Architectural Consultant**
- **Design Manager / Creative Director**
- **Furniture and Product Designer**
- **Exhibition and Event Designer**
