# BA (Hons) Communication Design

## Programme Overview

Our BA (Hons) Communication Design provides expertise in **conceptual**, **applied**, and **entrepreneurial** design practices to communicate information through a variety of media. The programme uses design thinking to transform and interpret data, developing knowledge into ethical, meaningful, and inclusive communication design experiences.

You will gain experience in **moving image and animation**, **photography**, **graphic design** (including illustration), and **interaction design** (UX/UI/Experiential) — establishing your skills across various creative approaches and making you highly employable upon graduation.

The programme is taught at our **Scottish Borders Campus**, with opportunities to study abroad at international exchange partners in Europe, India, and the USA through the **Go Global** programme.

## Why Choose This Programme?

- Covers the full spectrum of communication design: graphic design, animation, photography, and UX/UI
- Strong emphasis on **ethical, inclusive, and socially responsible** design
- **Go Global** mobility programme — study at partner universities in Europe, India, and the USA, or transfer to Dubai campus
- Dedicated Mac labs, photography studios, and specialist design workshops
- Year 3 optional **industry placement** and **live projects**
- Final year **Design Showcase** with industry exposure

## Entry Requirements

- **Academic (Year 1 Standard):** Highers BBBB / A-Levels BCC + digital portfolio / IB 29 points / BTEC Extended Diploma DMM
- **Minimum (Fair Access):** Highers BBBC / A-Levels BCC / IB 29 points
- **Year 2 Direct Entry:** Advanced Highers BB / A-Levels BBC / IB 30 points
- **Year 3 Direct Entry:** HND BB in graded units (relevant subject e.g. Computer Arts, Visual Communication)
- **English Language:** IELTS 6.0 overall with no score lower than 5.5
- **Portfolio:** Digital portfolio required for all applicants — PDF with 10–20 images showing research, design development, technical skills, and visual communication

## Programme Structure

### Year 1 — Foundation

A broad common year for all Fashion, Communication, Marketing and Retailing students, developing fundamentals in design, visualisation, photography, visual development, and design thinking.

| Semester | Module |
|----------|--------|
| September | Design Context and Process |
| September | Design Communication |
| January | Design Prototyping |
| January | Design Challenge |

### Year 2 — Specialisation

Introduces specialist topics such as typography, videography, advertising, motion graphics, and creative writing.

**Mandatory:**

| Semester | Module |
|----------|--------|
| September | Design Thinking |
| September | Creative Advertising |
| September | MoGraph and Content Creation |
| January | Design Research Methods |
| January | Phygital Experiences and Environments |

- Trend Forecasting and Media Content Production

### Year 3 — Professional Practice

Develops professional skills with pathways in design management, fashion communication, interaction design, and graphics and illustration.

|----------|--------|

| January | Planning Design Research |

### Year 4 — Honours Year

Consolidate your skills through a two-semester honours project with supervisory support.

| Period | Module |
|--------|--------|

## Career Prospects

Graduates pursue diverse roles across the creative industries:

- **Graphic Designer** — branding, editorial, packaging, signage
- **UX/UI Designer** — digital products and experiences
- **Art Director / Creative Director** — advertising and media
- **Motion Graphics Designer** — animation and film
- **Brand Strategist** — corporate identity and communication
- **Interaction Designer** — experiential and immersive design
- **Visual Merchandiser** — retail and spatial communication

*Average graduate salary: £24,000 (15 months after graduation).*
