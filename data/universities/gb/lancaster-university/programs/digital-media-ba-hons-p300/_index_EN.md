# Digital Media BA Hons (P300)

## Overview

Developments in digital technology range from overly friendly chatbots to inconspicuous algorithms. But what role do digital media technologies, in their many forms, play in our everyday lives, our local communities and cultures, and the global societies we live in? The skills you'll develop on this programme will prepare you for a dynamic career in one of the many areas where digital innovation is fundamental.

This course explores the question of how digital technologies, in their many forms, function in our everyday lives and the global societies we live in. You will develop awareness of how the design and deployment of digital technologies orders, shapes and disrupts society, often having more complex consequences than imagined or acknowledged.

You will discover how digital media is constantly evolving, exploring the past, present and future of media and its role in society. You will analyse key themes in digital societies, such as artificial intelligence and creativity, social media, digital tracking, algorithmic power, surveillance and quantification, and how these themes are presenting both opportunities and challenges within our everyday lives and the creative industries.

## Course Structure

### Year 1

**Core Modules:**
- **Understanding Media and Culture** — Introduction to major debates, theories and thinkers in studying media and culture. Explore diverse media including television, film, news, advertisements, social media, video games and more. Develop essential skills in critical thinking and analysis.
- **Transformations in Digital Media** — Explores how "old" and "new" media integrate and the ongoing transformations in the media industries. Examines changing relations between consumers and producers as the boundaries of media cultures shift.
- **Screens and Selves** — Explore the relationships between screen media and the formation of identities, exploring how we present ourselves online and offline.

**Optional Modules:**
- Art Histories and Contexts
- Consumers and Communications
- Design Contexts
- Media Power and Identity
- Principles of Marketing
- Public Sociology: Understanding and Creating Social Change
- Researching Society: Principles and Practices
- The Reflective Design Process
- The Arts and Identity (Discovery module)

### Year 2

**Core Modules:**
- **Designing Media Research** — Develop your research skills for studying digital media, learning both qualitative and quantitative methods.
- **Digital Cultures** — Examine how digital technologies shape cultural practices, identities and communities.

**Optional Modules:**
- Art Theories and Contexts
- Digital Marketing and Analytics
- Gender and Sexuality in Popular Culture
- Global Social Theory: Connecting Sociologies
- Innovation and New Product Development
- Interaction and Experience Design
- Media in Question
- Organising in the Digital Age
- Visual Cultures
- Welfare States: Poverty, Policy and Politics
- Employability, Purpose and Social Impact (Discovery module)
- Taking an Idea and Making it Happen (Discovery module)

### Year 3

**Core Modules:**
- **The Making of Media and AI Futures** — Explore how past visions of technological futures inform speculative digital landscapes. Examine the social, cultural, and political implications of technologies including personalisation, surveillance, platform governance, and digital inequalities.
- **Media Independent Project** — An in-depth study on a topic of your own choice, drawing on knowledge gained throughout your studies. The final project may focus on academic literature, empirical research, or incorporate elements of media practice.

**Optional Modules:**
- Audiences, Fans and Participatory Cultures
- Design Leadership
- Environment and Society: Crisis, Justice and Change
- Media Industries
- Mediating the Body
- Power, Technology and Society
- Writing for the Arts
- People Power and Collective Action (Discovery module)
- Confronting the Climate Crisis (Discovery module)

## Careers

As a graduate of this programme, you will have impressive knowledge of digital media and its impact on society combined with skills in critical analysis, research, presentation and writing. This means you will be well-placed to enter careers in the varied digital media and creative sectors.

You can build your creative portfolio by getting involved with student media societies. Lancaster University has a television station, a newspaper, a radio station and a cinema where you can take on roles from social media management and digital content creation to presenting live.

The department organises bespoke careers sessions on employment opportunities in the media and creative sectors, with expert-led workshops, creative sessions with media activists, and visits to heritage organisations.
