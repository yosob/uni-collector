# Fine Art BA Hons (W100)

## Overview

Unleash your ideas and develop your artistic expression through Fine Art at Lancaster. You will develop a unique contemporary art practice that engages with current ideas and issues to make valuable contributions to environment, culture and community. Through hands-on workshops and technical demonstrations, you will explore painting, drawing, sculpture, installation, video, digital art and their many hybrids.

Fine Art at Lancaster is about finding your voice and deciding what matters to you in today's art landscape. With engaging seminars in art history and theory, you'll be supported in shaping your values and understanding the evolving role of art. As you develop these ideas in your own studio space, you'll be challenged to broaden your understanding of contemporary Fine Art through a wide range of hands-on projects and skills-based workshops.

Previously, students have worked on live briefs in partnership with arts organisations such as Deco Publique, Good Things Collective, Maritime Museum, FACT Liverpool and the National Trust.

## Key Features

- **Your own studio space** available 24/7 from day one, with expert technical support on hand
- **7th for Art and Design** — The Complete University Guide (2026)
- **12th for Art and Design** — The Times and Sunday Times Good University Guide (2026)

- **Purpose-built studios** including high-tech seminar facilities, a digital art lab, installation spaces, and fully equipped workshops for wood, metal, plaster, ceramics, and printmaking
- **Live briefs** with external arts organisations and cultural partners

## Module Details

### Year 1

**Contemporary Art Practices**
This studio-based module develops your self-directed art practice. You will explore a range of materials and processes, developing your ideas through hands-on workshops and technical demonstrations in purpose-built facilities.

**Creative Attention**
A transformational module that introduces you to Fine Art studies at Lancaster, developing your ability to look, think, and make with greater awareness and intention.

**Art Histories and Contexts**
Explore art history from modernism to the present day, building a critical understanding of how art movements, ideas and social contexts shape contemporary practice.

### Year 2

**Expanded Art Practices**
Develop your self-directed practice further, specialising in your chosen area. Work in painting, drawing, sculpture, sound/video, digital, text, or installation. Benefit from regular tutorials and peer feedback to build a strong studio community.

**Art Theories and Contexts**
Deepen your understanding of the relationship between art, theory and the social world. From representations of the landscape and concepts of power, to museum studies and gender theory — explore a range of critical perspectives.

**Creative Transformations**
A core module providing the opportunity to develop creative responses to real-world challenges, working with external partners on collaborative projects.

### Year 3

**Advanced Art Practices**
This year-long module provides extended space and support for your growth as an artist. Refine your practical skills as you develop and present a professional body of practical work appropriate for a Fine Art degree show. Working in dedicated Fine Art studio spaces, you will bring together the skills and ideas developed throughout your degree.

**Writing for the Arts**
Develop your writing skills in the context of contemporary art practice. The module equips you with essential skills for careers involving arts criticism, gallery texts, artist statements, funding applications, and creative writing.

## Teaching and Assessment

Teaching is delivered through a combination of:
- Studio-based practice and workshops
- Technical demonstrations
- Seminars and lectures
- Group tutorials and individual supervisions
- Live briefs with external partners

Assessment is primarily through coursework, including studio practice, portfolios, written assignments, and exhibition presentations. All assessments respond to your individual practice.

Graduates of Fine Art go on to pursue careers in the following areas:

- **Professional Artist**
- **Curating**
- **Gallery Education Programmes**
- **Exhibition Management**
- **Digital Communications Manager**
- **Museum and Gallery Management**
- **Teaching**
- **Artefact Conservation**
- **Video and Media Production**
- **TV and Film Scenography**
- **Arts Event Management**

With a deep understanding of fine art and a diverse skill set, graduates are also well-prepared to pursue postgraduate study, with some branching into fields such as material engineering, design, advertising and marketing.

Lancaster runs a paid internship scheme specifically for arts, humanities and social sciences students, supported by a specialist Employability Team. The team offers individual consultations and tailored application guidance, as well as careers events, development opportunities, and resources.
