## Overview

Fine Art and Film at Lancaster is an exciting degree that combines creative practice with critical film studies. This programme brings together studio-based fine art work with film analysis, theory and production, allowing you to explore both disciplines in depth and develop your own creative voice.

You will benefit from the rich cross-disciplinary environment of the Lancaster Institute for the Contemporary Arts (LICA), where artists, filmmakers, designers and performers work side by side. The programme encourages you to think critically about both art and cinema while developing practical skills in drawing, painting, sculpture, film production and more.

Throughout the degree, you will have access to purpose-built studios, film editing suites, specialist equipment and technical support. You will develop skills in close analysis, creative thinking, visual communication and collaborative working — all highly valued by employers.

## Why Study Fine Art and Film at Lancaster?

- **Creative cross-disciplinarity**: Combine your passion for visual art with a deep understanding of cinema from around the world
- **Industry-standard facilities**: Access to purpose-built studios, darkrooms, film editing suites and specialist equipment
- **Expert teaching**: Learn from practising artists and leading film scholars in a research-intensive university
- **Flexible programme**: Choose from a wide range of optional modules to tailor the degree to your interests
- **Professional practice**: Develop a portfolio of creative work and a dissertation project in your final year
- **Career preparation**: Build transferable skills in visual analysis, critical thinking, communication and project management

## Course Structure

The programme is structured across three years. In each year you will take a combination of core and optional modules, allowing you to build a strong foundation while developing your own interests.

### Year 1

**Core Modules:**

| Module | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Introduction to Film Studies | Introduces key approaches to film theory, analysis and history while integrating practical filmmaking experience. You will examine cinematography, editing, sound and performance through close analysis of case study films from Hollywood and global traditions. |
| Art Histories and Contexts | Provides a solid grounding in art history, from the modernist period to the present day. Beginning with the emergence of Modernism in Europe, you will follow its impact across the globe and consider the relevance of these contexts to your own practice. |

**Optional Modules:**

| Module | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Creative Attention | A transformational module that introduces you to Fine Art studies at undergraduate level. Focussing on how artists investigate the world through looking, listening, touch, gesture and mark-making, you will explore a range of diverse media. |
| Understanding Media and Culture | Introduces major debates, theories and thinkers in studying media and culture. You will encounter a diverse range of material from television, film, news and digital media. |

### Year 2

**Core Modules:**

| Module | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Global Cinemas | Explore the stylistic, industrial and cultural diversities of global cinema through the close study of films from different historical and regional contexts. You will examine variations in cinema style and form while developing your analytical skills. |

**Optional Modules:**

| Module | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Art Theories and Contexts | Building on the relationship between theoretical understanding and artistic practice, this module introduces a broad range of theories and ideas relevant to contemporary art, including new materialisms, decolonisation and identity politics. |
| Creative Transformations | Imagination, experimentation, creative risk-taking and transformation are at the heart of this module. You will engage creatively with workshops that encourage experimentation and risk-taking across a range of media and processes. |
| Film Theory and Aesthetics | Move beyond introductory concepts to explore film theory in depth, examining how films construct meaning through form, representation and audience engagement. You will critically assess cinema's relationship with artistic expression and spectatorship. |
| Short Film Production | Collaboratively develop, produce and complete a short dramatic film while engaging with sustainable industry-standard production practices. The emphasis is on technical competence, creative problem-solving and reflective practice. |

### Year 3

**Core Modules:**

| Module | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Independent Project (Film, English, Creative Writing and Media) | The culmination of your studies, combining everything you have learnt to undertake an in-depth, self-directed research project on the topic of your choice. You will propose an area of investigation and work with an appropriate supervisor. |
| Advanced Art Practices | Refine your practical skills as you develop and present a professional body of practical work appropriate for a Fine Art degree show. Your growth as an artist will be supported through lectures, workshops, tutorials and group critiques. |

**Optional Modules:**

| Module | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Global Film Genres | Examining the evolution of film genres in a global context, this module focuses on how contemporary filmmakers innovate within established and classic genre traditions, including Science Fiction, Horror and Comedy. |
| Documentary Production | An advanced, practice-based module providing an industry-informed approach to documentary filmmaking. Working in small groups, you will research, develop and produce a short documentary. |
| Experimental Film Production | A hands-on module that explores experimental film production and how it challenges mainstream narratives and disrupts traditional modes of spectatorship through critical discussions and practical workshops. |

There may also be additional costs for books, stationery, printing, photocopying and general subsistence on trips and visits.

## Careers

Graduates of Fine Art and Film develop a wide range of transferable skills that are valued by employers across many sectors. Potential career paths include:

- **Creative industries**: Artist, filmmaker, curator, art director
- **Media and communications**: Film production, video editing, content creation
- **Education**: Teaching, lecturing, gallery education
- **Cultural sector**: Museum and gallery work, arts administration, cultural heritage
- **Further study**: Postgraduate study in fine art, film studies, curatorial practice or related fields

The combination of creative practice and critical thinking prepares you for roles that require both visual literacy and analytical skills. Many graduates also establish themselves as independent artists and filmmakers.
