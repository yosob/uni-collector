## Overview

Fine Art and Theatre at Lancaster offers a unique opportunity to explore and develop the diverse dialogues between fine art and theatre. Situated in the Lancaster Institute for the Contemporary Arts (LICA), this combined degree brings together studio-based fine art practice with the study and making of theatre, allowing you to work creatively across both disciplines.

The programme encourages you to think critically about the relationship between visual art and performance, developing your skills as both an artist and a theatre practitioner. You will have the freedom to experiment across media — from painting, sculpture and installation to devised performance, physical theatre and applied theatre — supported by expert academics and practising artists.

Throughout the degree, you will benefit from purpose-built studios, performance spaces, workshops and technical support. You will develop skills in creative thinking, visual communication, performance-making and collaborative working, preparing you for a wide range of careers in the creative and cultural industries.

## Why Study Fine Art and Theatre at Lancaster?

- **Creative cross-disciplinarity**: Combine your passion for visual art with theatre-making in a programme that encourages dialogue between the two disciplines
- **Industry-standard facilities**: Access to purpose-built studios, performance spaces, workshops and specialist equipment
- **Expert teaching**: Learn from practising artists, theatre-makers and leading scholars in a research-intensive university
- **Flexible programme**: Choose from a wide range of optional modules to tailor the degree to your creative interests
- **Professional practice**: Develop a portfolio of creative work and a major performance project in your final year
- **Career preparation**: Build transferable skills in visual analysis, performance, critical thinking, communication and project management

## Course Structure

The programme is structured across three years. In each year you will take a combination of core and optional modules, allowing you to build a strong foundation while developing your own creative interests.

### Year 1

**Core Modules:**

| Module | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Understanding Culture | Introduces you to the study of culture and the ways in which it shapes and is shaped by the arts. You will explore key concepts and critical approaches that underpin the study of both fine art and theatre. |
| Creative Attention | A transformational module that introduces you to Fine Art studies at undergraduate level. Focussing on how artists investigate the world through looking, listening, touch, gesture and mark-making, you will explore a range of diverse media. |

**Optional Modules:**

| Module | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Writing as Practice | Explores the relationship between writing and creative practice, developing your ability to use text as a creative and critical tool within both art and theatre contexts. |
| Creative Transformations | Imagination, experimentation, creative risk-taking and transformation are at the heart of this module. You will engage creatively with workshops that encourage experimentation and risk-taking across a range of media and processes. |

### Year 2

**Core Modules:**

| Module | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Art Theories and Contexts | Building on the relationship between theoretical understanding and artistic practice, this module introduces a broad range of theories and ideas relevant to contemporary art, including new materialisms, decolonisation and identity politics. |
| Making Theatre 2: Intermediate Performance Project | Develop your skills in theatre-making through an intermediate-level performance project. You will explore a range of approaches to creating performance work, developing your abilities as a collaborative theatre-maker. |

**Optional Modules:**

| Module | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Performance Composition | Explore the principles of composing performance, examining how space, time, movement and material can be organised to create compelling theatrical experiences. |
| Applied Theatre | Investigate how theatre and performance can be applied in community, educational and social contexts, developing your understanding of participatory and socially-engaged performance practices. |
| Writing for the Arts | Develop your writing skills for a range of arts contexts, including critical writing, reviews, artist statements and creative texts. |

### Year 3

**Core Modules:**

| Module | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Advanced Art Practices | Refine your practical skills as you develop and present a professional body of practical work appropriate for a Fine Art degree show. Your growth as an artist will be supported through lectures, workshops, tutorials and group critiques. |
| Making Theatre 3: Advanced Performance Project | The culmination of your theatre studies, in which you will conceive, develop and present an advanced performance project, drawing on the skills and knowledge developed throughout your degree. |

**Optional Modules:**

| Module | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Writing for the Arts | Develop your writing skills for a range of arts contexts, including critical writing, reviews, artist statements and creative texts. |
| Live Performance Criticism | Develop your critical voice through the analysis and discussion of live performance, building skills in writing about and evaluating contemporary theatre and performance work. |
| Festival of the Future | An immersive module that explores the role of festivals in contemporary culture, culminating in the planning and delivery of a public-facing festival event. |

## Careers

Graduates of Fine Art and Theatre develop a wide range of transferable skills that are valued by employers across many sectors. Potential career paths include:

- **Creative industries**: Artist, theatre-maker, performer, director, curator
- **Arts administration**: Festival management, arts organisations, cultural programming
- **Education**: Teaching, lecturing, gallery and theatre education
- **Cultural sector**: Museum and gallery work, arts policy, community arts
- **Performance and media**: Acting, directing, stage management, production
- **Further study**: Postgraduate study in fine art, theatre, performance studies or related fields

The combination of creative practice and critical thinking prepares you for roles that require both visual literacy and collaborative performance skills. Many graduates also establish themselves as independent artists and theatre practitioners.
