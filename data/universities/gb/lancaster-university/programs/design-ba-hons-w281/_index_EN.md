## Programme Description

BA Design at Lancaster is for creative thinkers who want more than a traditional design course — it's for people who want to challenge the present and design better futures. You'll explore big questions about society, business, technology and the planet, tackling real-world problems and deciding on which design approaches work best rather than being limited to one type of design.

Alongside developing core design skills such as in graphics and user experience (UX) design, you'll learn how to question current ways of doing things, imagine alternatives, and propose new ways of living and interacting. You'll learn how to design for sustainable futures, work with real clients and lead creative projects.

Taught by experts from **ImaginationLancaster**, our internationally recognised design-led research lab, you'll graduate with a bold portfolio, creative confidence, and the kind of versatile and adaptable skills that employers are actively seeking.

## Curriculum

### Year 1 (Part I) — Foundations

Students build core design skills and understanding through three 40-credit modules.

| Module | Credits | Term |
|--------|---------|------|
| Design Research and Creative Thinking | 40 | Full Year |
| Design Foundations | 40 | Full Year |
| Design in Context | 40 | Full Year |

Students also take one additional subject from a range of options across the University.

### Year 2 (Part II) — Development and Practice

Students advance their design practice and begin working with real clients.

| Module | Credits | Term |
|--------|---------|------|
| Design Consultancy | 40 | Full Year |
| Design: Making and Doing | 40 | Full Year |
| Design: Provotypes and Speculative Design | 20 | Michaelmas |
| Optional Module | 20 | Lent/Summer |

### Year 3 (Part III) — Specialism and Major Project

Students lead a major self-directed project and develop professional practice skills.

| Module | Credits | Term |
|--------|---------|------|
| Design: Advanced Design Practice | 40 | Full Year |
| Design Dissertation | 40 | Full Year |
| Design: Professional Practice | 20 | Michaelmas |
| Optional Module | 20 | Lent/Summer |

> Total: 360 credits across three years. Optional modules vary each year. Modules are indicative and subject to change.

### Study Abroad and Placement Options

- **Study Abroad (W283)** — Spend a year at a partner university overseas

## Teaching & Assessment

**Teaching** combines lectures, seminars, studio-based workshops, live briefs with real clients, tutorials, and guest lectures from industry professionals. Students work in the ImaginationLancaster design research lab environment, fostering creative experimentation and interdisciplinary collaboration.

**Assessment** is primarily through coursework, including design portfolios, presentations, written assignments, and a major project and dissertation in the final year.

## Career Prospects

Graduates are prepared for a broad range of design roles, including:

- **UX/UI Designer** — Creating user-centred digital experiences
- **Service Designer** — Designing better public and private services
- **Design Consultant** — Advising organisations on design strategy
- **Product Designer** — Developing physical and digital products
- **Social Innovation Designer** — Addressing societal challenges through design
- **Design Researcher** — Conducting research to inform design decisions
- **Creative Director** — Leading creative teams and projects

Previous graduates have gone on to work in design agencies, tech companies, healthcare organisations, public services, and social enterprises.
