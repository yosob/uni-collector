## Programme Description

**Develop your own writing under the guidance of celebrated author-tutors at one of the very first UK universities to teach Creative Writing.**

Through a mix of masterclasses, workshops and tutorials you will learn from author-tutors who together can offer specialisms from right across the spectrum of literary genres and forms. At all times the focus will be your own writing and progressing, if you wish, toward publication.

### World-Class Recognition

- **World Top 40** in QS World Subject Rankings 2026 (English Language and Literature)

### The Quartet Structure

This programme is founded on a unique quartet — two series of masterclasses and two series of workshops:

- **Masterclasses** focus on Form, Theme & Genre, enabling you to enhance your work through learning from each tutor's own writing
- **Workshops** focus on Voice, Process, Shaping & Editing, enabling you to develop your work through having it discussed by your tutors and peers

### Breadth of Genres

You will acquire a rich overview of all the traditional genres (poetry, prose, short fiction, drama, scriptwriting) as well as newer forms such as song lyric, graphic novel, fantasy, science fiction, memoir, queer fiction, game writing, writing for young adults, and the lyric or personal essay.

### Specialise in Genre or Form

Each series of masterclasses and workshops is overseen by one author-tutor but taught by several — some poets, some novelists, some playwrights. When submitting work, you can specialise in one particular genre or form and receive guidance from tutors who write your preferred genre.

### Writing in the World

The programme guides you through writing's many professional dimensions — from redrafting and working with editors through to publishing and promotion. It also explores how writing flourishes in fields such as museums, film, journalism, new media, podcasting, gaming and fashion.

### Why Lancaster?

- Study close to the beautiful Lake District and the Wordsworth Museum at Grasmere
- Enjoy a rich programme of literary events on campus, online, and in the city's historic Castle Quarter
- Gain experience with student-run literary journals and partners including Lancaster's long-running LitFest
- Study writing for graphic novels and comics — Lancaster is the first British university to have a Professor in Graphic Fiction and Comic Art
- Close ties with the Lakes International Comics Art Festival and a unique graphic novel collection in the campus library

### Literary Life

At Lancaster you will be part of a dynamic literary community with free literary events in the Castle Quarter, the October Evening and May Gathering at Lancaster's ancient Priory, an annual student anthology with a public reading launch, and participation in Lancaster LitFest — one of the UK's oldest literary festivals.

The unique partnership with the Wordsworth Museum at Grasmere includes an annual study retreat day, free entry year-round, and the chance to apply for one of four residential internships.

## Curriculum

The programme is structured around the unique quartet of masterclasses and workshops. Specific modules are reviewed and enhanced continually. Students work towards a final creative writing portfolio and can specialise in their chosen genre or form.

> Modules are continually reviewed and enhanced. The programme combines masterclasses, workshops, tutorials, and independent writing toward a final portfolio.

## Career Prospects

Graduates pursue careers in a wide range of fields:

- **Published Author** — Developing and publishing original creative work
- **Publishing** — Working in editorial, agenting, and publishing houses
- **Journalism** — Writing and reporting across media platforms
- **Television/Media** — Scriptwriting and content creation
- **Teaching** — At schools and universities worldwide
- **Librarianship** — Managing literary collections and archives
- **PhD Research** — Continuing academic study in Creative Writing

Graduates of Lancaster's MA in Creative Writing teach at universities worldwide.
