# Architecture BA Hons (K100) — Lancaster University

## Overview

**Radical creativity meets a planet-protecting mindset.** Join us to learn from inspiring architects at our green and spacious campus, close to industrial cities and the Lake District. We will teach you how to think innovatively and prioritise sustainability while you perfect your practical skills.

### Why Lancaster?

- Study in a school that guarantees a dedicated studio space for every student, available 24/7
- Take inspiration from our location — a rural, green campus on the edge of industrial cities and the Lake District
- Learn from experts shaping conversation on technology, environment, and architecture
- Benefit from top facilities — from hand tools, 3D printers and laser scanners to CNC routers, large-scale printers and specialist software
- Immerse yourself in a programme that puts design innovation at the forefront through our dedicated research lab, Imagination Lancaster
- Enhance your CV with live project work, networking events, practical experience, summer school and mentoring

### Radical Creativity with a Conscience

Studying architecture at Lancaster will give you the know-how to be agile and ethical in your architectural practice. Tackling three key areas: future practice, digital crafts and the climate emergency, this course will teach you to build with sustainability in mind and design innovative spaces that match the needs of the communities that will use them.

With sustainability at the heart of all that we teach, we empower students to search for innovative solutions that repurpose materials and extend the lifecycle of buildings. We help students to become pioneers of future technologies like digital fabrication, rapid prototyping and the maker movement.

We are one of the first courses in the country to implement the latest ARB and RIBA guidance on teaching ethics, professionalism, fire safety and sustainability, ensuring your knowledge is fully up-to-date when you graduate.

### Hands-on Learning

Our campus is an inspirational setting for studying architecture. You will experience a great contrast in the Architecture of our working spaces — an Arts and Crafts manor house and our award-winning contemporary arts building. Our studio layout allows you to experience the culture of a professional practice and work collaboratively with other students.

Alongside lectures, seminars and practical workshops you will have one-to-one tutorials with award-winning academics who have first-hand experience in the industry. With plenty of opportunities for hands-on work in the studio, our small class sizes allow us to get to know all our students well, which makes for a friendly and productive environment.

In your first year, you will develop small-scale projects around Lancaster and explore their context and architectural principles. Your second year will give you the opportunity to design a retrofit live project and work directly with a client. You will then consolidate your knowledge in your final year developing a longer retrofit and new build housing project with mixed use facilities.

## Professional Accreditation

- **Architects Registration Board (ARB):** This course is prescribed by the ARB at Part 1 level for purposes of registration as an Architect in the UK. The ARB is currently revising its requirements; prescription of Part 1 qualifications will cease on 31st Dec 2027 for all UK courses. This will not impact any student's ability to complete qualification as a UK registered architect.
- **Royal Institute of British Architects (RIBA):** Both the BA (Hons) Architecture (Part I) and the MArch Master of Architecture (Part II) are accredited by the RIBA. RIBA will continue to validate architecture qualifications at Parts 1, 2 and 3.

## Course Structure

### Year 1 (Core Modules)

#### Architectural Design Studio 1A
Focusing on the fundamentals of architectural design, this module will engage you in spatial exploration and visual representation, culminating in the development of a small structure or building. You will learn digital and analogue techniques to represent architectural space and form, developing hand-drawn skills for visual communication, including orthogonal drawing and layout conventions. At the beginning of this module, you will take part in the Lancaster Architecture Design Challenge (Charrette), a vertical architectural design activity taking place in each year of your degree.

#### Architectural Design Studio 1B
Building on your representation skills learned in Studio 1A, this module will help you develop your ability to survey and analyse a site. You will learn how to observe, map, and understand the dynamics and human interactions of a place, leading toward a building design proposal. You will explore materials and representative techniques to present both cultural and social understanding of spaces, developing a critical understanding of design generators and conceptual approaches to the resolution of a small building design.

#### Architectural Humanities 1
Covering issues in architectural history, theory, culture, design and urbanism, this diverse module will give you an insight into globalised and urban visionaries, country-specific influences and globalised contexts. Through a series of lectures with extended historical narratives, you will explore architectural theory connected to history, focusing on wider issues of environment, culture, design and urbanism from prehistoric times to the present day.

#### Architectural Technology
Gain an entry-level insight into structural principles, materiality and tectonics, whilst discussing environmental and technical contexts surrounding architectural technology. You will explore building technology based on case studies and design studio modules, with a focus on materials and assembly, lean construction, and passive architecture principles.

### Year 2 (Core Modules)

#### Architectural Design Studio 2A
Through live project experience, this module will help you gain practical experience in design, while learning how to participate in collaborative processes, strengthen communication skills, and engage meaningfully with communities. You will enhance your digital skills through CAD, 3D modelling, rendering, and digital fabrication, using industry-standard software. At the beginning of this module, you will take part in the Lancaster Architecture Design Challenge (Charrette).

#### Architectural Design Studio 2B
Focusing on the UK building regulations, this module introduces you to urban design principles and planning frameworks. You will learn to consider the social and cultural impacts of your work whilst creating an urban masterplan. You will design a public building or collective housing project of approximately 750 sqm, developing it to the detailed design stage, and gain an understanding of the UK model for building design and construction, including the RIBA Plan of Work.

#### Architectural Humanities 2
Expanding on the foundation laid in Architectural Humanities 1, this module deepens your understanding of architectural history with a focus on more recent developments. It examines architecture as a form of activism and an expanded mode of practice, exploring the architect's roles and responsibilities in relation to ethics and moral decision-making. You will engage in discussions about the climate crisis and how architects can respond with environmentally and ecologically responsible solutions, exploring topics like shared practice, co-creation, co-design, co-production and participatory architecture.

#### Architectural Technology and Professional Skills
Exploring contemporary construction technologies, this module provides a strong understanding of architectural technology, passive and active systems, energy efficiency and eco-technology. It offers a clear introduction to the regulatory framework, focusing on key aspects of building regulations and the broader professional context. You will explore the physical and material qualities of building components through detailed drawings and models.

### Year 3 (Core Modules)

#### Architectural Design Studio 3A
This module brings together and deepens the skills and knowledge developed in Years 1 and 2. Through a comprehensive urban and architectural analysis of a given site, you will develop a deep understanding of the context to establish your own position and develop a brief and strategy. Working to RIBA Stage 2, this module focuses on regenerative design strategies and encourages exploration of sustainable, circular, ecological and biophilic approaches.

#### Architectural Design Studio 3B
Building on the analysis and initial design development from Studio 3A, this module focuses on the development and resolution of your proposals at a detailed level. Working to RIBA Stages 3 and 4, you will demonstrate your ability to coordinate the spatial, technical, environmental and regulatory requirements of a comprehensive building project. This includes developing detailed plans, sections, elevations and constructional details.

#### Architectural Humanities 3
Building upon your visual research skills developed in Architectural Humanities 1 and 2, this module encourages you to address the complexities of contemporary architectural production. Working in groups, you will analyse an architectural controversy and visually 'map' the many people and forces involved in bringing a built project to life. Individually, you will articulate your understanding of the major external factors influencing an architectural controversy of your choice and argue your position on the ethical and moral debates involved.

#### Integrated Technology
Evaluating the role of the architect in relation to your design studio work, this module will see you demonstrate your understanding of regulatory compliance, technical requirements and the ethical considerations involved in your design work. You will choose a relevant precedent and create a case study report, analysing sustainability, regulations, and ethical issues. In the second part, you will focus on integrating structural, technological, and environmental aspects into your ongoing design studio project, producing an 'Integrated Technology Report'.

#### Professional Practice
Focusing on both individual and collective understanding of professional practice, this module explores an architect's responsibilities at different stages of a project. You will be tested on contracts and procurement, regulations and legislation governing building design, and professional and socially responsible behaviours of architects. You will also undertake group work to establish a mock architectural practice, developing a portfolio in response to a specific practice-related scenario such as a competition bid or planning committee review.

## Careers

Many of our students progress to postgraduate study and continue their journey to becoming fully qualified architects. Career paths include:

- Architectural technician/assistant
- Landscape architect
- Interior architect
- Spatial designer
- Urban designer
- Property developer
- Project and construction manager

Previous students have gone on to work in architectural practices across Lancashire, Manchester, Birmingham, London, and overseas.
