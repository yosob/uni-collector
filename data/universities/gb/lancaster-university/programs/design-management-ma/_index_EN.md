## Programme Description

**Join one of the largest design research centres in the world and work with Lancaster's experts in future-facing design.**

Design management is all about how we organise and deploy design solutions to solve problems and add value for users, organisations and society. The MA in Design Management helps you assess the far-reaching impact when design methods are strategically deployed to address key issues of today and for the future. You'll focus on much more than design, with topics such as delivery to market, the supply chain and sustainability being key aspects of this course.

You'll consider big issues — how can design be used to help a city reach net zero? How can data privacy rules be applied when working with the Internet of Things? How can we give people better access to health services?

### Why Lancaster?

- **Work alongside internationally renowned design leaders** in one of the largest design research centres in the world, ImaginationLancaster
- **Learn from world-class researchers** — be among the first to hear about the latest research and innovative ways design is taken in new directions
- **Explore complex design challenges** in the cutting-edge Imagination Lab — a purpose-built, project-based design studio
- **Gain real-world experience** with clients from a range of organisations
- **Immerse yourself in futures approaches**, develop visions and set design strategies

### Imagination Lab

Experience the excitement of creating and pitching novel design solutions in the Imagination Lab. Charities or businesses set real briefs, giving you the sense of working with a client in real life. Your major research project is a chance to experiment with new design methods and focus on a real research question — for example, using speculative design to consider topics such as artificial intelligence, distributed production and digital space.

## Curriculum

The programme includes taught modules covering design management, strategy, futures design, and sustainability, alongside a major research project. Students work in the Imagination Lab on live client briefs. Specific module details are indicative and subject to annual review.

> Modules are continually reviewed and enhanced. The programme combines academic study with real-world client projects and a major research project.

## Career Prospects

Graduates are prepared for a broad range of design leadership and management roles:

- **Design Manager** — Leading design teams and processes within organisations
- **Project Manager** — Managing complex design and innovation projects
- **Strategic Planner** — Developing design strategies for businesses
- **Design Consultant** — Advising organisations on design-led solutions
- **UX Designer** — Creating user-centred digital experiences
- **Service Designer** — Designing better public and private services
- **Futures Consultant** — Helping organisations prepare for future challenges
- **Policy Designer** — Applying design thinking to public policy

Graduates may also pursue further study through the PhD in Design at ImaginationLancaster.
