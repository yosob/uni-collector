## Overview

Unlock the art of storytelling by combining film studies with the craft of writing. From early cinema innovations and global film movements to novels and theatre, you'll engage with a broad spectrum of cinematic and literary topics. At the same time, you'll hone your writing and film-making skills across different genres.

Studying at Lancaster gives you the chance to work with practising creative writers and published authors, as well as leading researchers in film, creative writing and media. In your first year, you'll gain foundational knowledge of both film studies and creative writing. Year 2 deepens your understanding with core modules in global cinemas and writing practice, alongside optional modules that let you specialise. In your final year, you'll undertake an independent project and explore advanced creative and critical work.

You'll benefit from our strong industry links and vibrant creative community, with opportunities for placements, field trips and attending talks by visiting professionals.

## Modules

### Year 1

**Core Modules:**
- Introduction to Film Studies
- Introduction to Creative Writing

**Optional Modules:**
- Understanding Media and Culture
- The Arts and Identity (Discovery module)
- British Cinema
- Re-Making Tradition

### Year 2

**Core Modules:**
- Global Cinemas
- Developing Your Writing Practice
- Writing Fiction

**Optional Modules:**
- Screenwriting
- Classic Hollywood Genres
- Film, Gender, Race and Ideology
- Writing Poetry
- Playwriting
- Film Theory and Aesthetics
- Short Film Production
- American Literature
- Taking an Idea and Making It Happen (Discovery module)

### Year 3

**Core Modules:**
- Independent Project (Film, English, Creative Writing and Media)
- Writing Beyond the Text

**Optional Modules:**
- Global Film Genres
- Documentary Production
- Queer Futures (Discovery module)
- Artistic Fusions (Discovery module)
- Film Circulation, Exhibition and Audiences
- Film Industries, Practices and Movements
- Experimental Film Production
- Writing Beyond the Self
- Writing Beyond the Moment

## Careers

Graduates of this course go on to careers in filmmaking, screenwriting, journalism, publishing, marketing, arts administration, education, and further postgraduate study. The combination of analytical and creative skills makes you highly employable across a wide range of industries.
