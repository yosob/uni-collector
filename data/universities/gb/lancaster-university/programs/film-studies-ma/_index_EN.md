## Programme Description

**Study film theory, history and culture with expert film scholars, filmmakers and critics at Lancaster.**

The Film Studies MA provides the opportunity to study film theory, history and culture — particularly transnational cinemas — while also developing practical skills in filmmaking. The programme combines academic rigour with creative practice, allowing you to specialise in areas such as British cinema, Hong Kong cinema, Asian filmmaking, African-American popular culture, Middle Eastern cinemas, European arthouse cinema, and Hollywood.

### Why Lancaster?

- **Learn from expert practitioners** — the teaching team includes film scholars, filmmakers and critics, all passionate about film in its many forms and genres
- **Explore transnational cinemas** — Lancaster has particular strengths in global and transnational film cultures
- **Combine theory and practice** — the programme offers both analytical study and hands-on filmmaking
- **Flexible final project** — choose between a traditional dissertation or a practice-based project with critical commentary
- **Excellent facilities** — access to professional digital cameras, editing suites and dedicated screening rooms

### Research Project Flexibility

You have the flexibility to submit either a traditional independent research dissertation or undertake a practice-based project (e.g. a short film) accompanied by a critical written piece for your final major research project.

## Curriculum

### Core Modules

- **Research Methods: Contemporary Film Theory** — Introduces key research methods and theoretical approaches in contemporary film studies
- **Transnational Cinemas** — Explores cinema across national boundaries, examining global flows of film culture and production
- **Major Research Project: Dissertation** — A substantial independent research project (traditional dissertation or practice-based with critical commentary)

### Optional Modules (indicative)

- Contemporary Gothic
- Film Theory and the Creative Process of Writing for the Screen
- Digital Journalism & Visual Storytelling
- Additional modules available within LICA and the Faculty of HASS

> Modules are continually reviewed and enhanced. Optional module offerings vary each year depending on staff availability.

## Career Prospects

Graduates are prepared for a broad range of roles in the film, media and cultural industries:

- **Film Researcher** — Conducting research for film productions and cultural institutions
- **Film Critic** — Writing reviews and critical analysis for publications
- **Screenwriter** — Developing scripts for film and television
- **Film Director / Producer** — Creating and managing film productions
- **Film Programmer/Curator** — Selecting films for festivals and cultural venues
- **Cultural Journalist** — Reporting on arts, culture and media
- **Multimedia Author** — Creating digital content across platforms
- **Academic Researcher** — Pursuing doctoral study (PhD) in film or related fields
