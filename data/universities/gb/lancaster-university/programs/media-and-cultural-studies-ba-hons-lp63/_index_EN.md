# Media and Cultural Studies BA Hons (LP63)

## Overview

Media shapes who we are, what we think and what we value. At Lancaster, we are passionate about understanding media in all its complexity, exploring how media and culture shape our social realities.

Media and Cultural Studies will provide you with a critical understanding of media industries, practices and productions. You will learn how digital media is both reinventing older media forms, like newspapers and television, and producing new digital cultures. This course examines the media's role in constructing identities, shaping public opinion, and framing social and political debates.

You will explore how the media represents different groups and issues, how audiences make sense of media content, and how media power operates in society. From social media to streaming platforms, from news to advertising, you will develop the analytical tools to decode the messages that surround us every day.

### Key Features

- Study in our cutting-edge **Digital Media Studio** with specialist media equipment
- Develop practical media skills alongside a critical understanding of the media's impact on society
- Taught by world-leading experts shaping conversations in media power, digital cultures and identity
- Access bespoke careers sessions, networking opportunities, guest lectures, workshops with industry professionals, and field trips
- Produce a major independent media project in your final year

## Course Structure

### Year 1

**Core Modules:**
- **Understanding Media and Culture** — Introduction to major debates, theories and thinkers in studying media and culture. Explore diverse media including television, film, news, advertisements, social media and video games. Develop essential skills in critical thinking and analysis.
- **Transformations in Digital Media** — Explores how "old" and "new" media integrate and the ongoing transformations in the media industries. Examines changing relations between consumers and producers as the boundaries of media cultures shift.
- **Media, Power and Identity** — Examine the ways in which media representations shape social identities, including class, gender, race and sexuality, and how power operates through media institutions and practices.
- **Screens and Selves** — Explore the relationships between screen media and the formation of identities, examining how we present ourselves online and offline.

**Optional Modules:**
- Thinking Sociologically
- The Arts and Identity (Discovery module)
- British Cinema
- Public Sociology: Understanding and Creating Social Change
- Consumers and Communications
- Language in Mind and Society

### Year 2

**Core Modules:**
- **Key Perspectives in Global Media and Culture** — Examine media and culture from a global perspective, exploring transnational flows of media content and the impact of globalisation on cultural identities and practices.
- **Media in Question** — Engage with critical debates about the role and impact of media in contemporary society, developing your analytical and argumentation skills.
- **Designing Media Research** — Develop your research skills for studying media, learning both qualitative and quantitative methods to investigate media industries, audiences and texts.

**Optional Modules:**
- Gender and Sexuality in Popular Culture
- Literary Adaptation
- Race, Racism and Inequality
- Visual Cultures
- Digital Cultures
- Short Film Production
- Transnational Debates on Gender and Society
- Welfare States: Poverty, Policy and Politics
- Language and Creativity in Advertising
- Employability, Purpose and Social Impact (Discovery module)

### Year 3

**Core Modules:**
- **Media Independent Project** — An in-depth study on a topic of your own choice, drawing on knowledge gained throughout your studies. The final project may focus on academic literature, empirical research, or incorporate elements of media practice.

**Optional Modules:**
- Media Industries
- Audiences, Fans and Participatory Cultures
- Mediating the Body
- Documentary Production
- Health, Bodies and Social Reproduction
- Artistic Fusions (Discovery module)
- People Power and Collective Action (Discovery module)
- Confronting the Climate Crisis (Discovery module)
- Queer Futures (Discovery module)
- The Making of Media and AI Futures
- Contemporary Issues in Marketing
- Festival of the Future
- Environment and Society: Crisis, Justice and Change
- Migration, Inequalities and Societies

## Careers

Studying Media and Cultural Studies at Lancaster will give you a head start in preparing for your future career. You will develop impressive skills in critical analysis, research, presentation and writing — transferable skills that are highly valued across a wide range of sectors.

Graduates have gone on to work in the media and creative sectors, journalism, podcasting, media activism, museums and galleries, marketing, public relations, arts administration, policy research and more.

You can build your creative portfolio by getting involved with student media societies. Lancaster University has a television station, a newspaper, a radio station and a cinema where you can take on roles from social media management and digital content creation to presenting live.

The department organises bespoke careers sessions on employment opportunities in the media and creative sectors, with expert-led workshops, creative sessions with media activists, and visits to heritage organisations.
