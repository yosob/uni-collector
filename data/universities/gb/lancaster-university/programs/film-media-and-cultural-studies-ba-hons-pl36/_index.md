## Overview

Film, Media and Cultural Studies at Lancaster combines the exciting practice of hands-on filmmaking with the study of media and culture, offering a diverse and interdisciplinary approach to understanding how films, television programmes, media platforms and cultural phenomena shape our understanding of the world.

You will develop essential critical and analytical skills for examining and evaluating a wide range of media texts, from popular cinema and television to social media and digital platforms. Alongside this, you will gain practical experience in film production, including cinematography, editing and sound design.

A distinctive feature of this course is its emphasis on exploring the relationship between media and culture in a global context. You will examine media and culture from a range of national and transnational perspectives, gaining an understanding of how media operates across different cultural and geographic contexts.

Students have access to top-of-the-line production facilities including cinema-ready digital cameras with prime lenses, DSLR cameras, versatile set lighting, grip equipment and full access to Adobe Creative Cloud in acoustic editing suites. The university's partnership with the British Film Institute (BFI) provides free access to BFI Player resources.

## Course Structure

### Year 1

**Core Modules:**
- Introduction to Film Studies
- Understanding Media and Culture
- British Cinema

**Optional Modules:**
- Transformations in Digital Media
- The Reflective Design Process
- Media, Power and Identity
- World Literatures

### Year 2

**Core Modules:**
- Global Cinemas
- Key Perspectives in Global Media and Culture

**Optional Modules:**
- Classic Hollywood Genres
- Film, Gender, Race and Ideology
- Screenwriting
- Gender and Sexuality in Popular Culture
- Short Film Production
- Film Theory and Aesthetics
- Visual Cultures

### Year 3

**Core Modules:**
- Independent Project (Film, English, Creative Writing and Media)
- Media Industries

**Optional Modules:**
- Documentary Production
- Global Film Genres
- Audiences, Fans and Participatory Cultures
- Film Industries, Practices and Movements
- Film Circulation, Exhibition and Audiences
- Experimental Film Production
- The Making of Media and AI Futures
- Mediating the Body

## Careers

The pace of change in media industries and jobs means that preparing for your career is about being able to provide insights and unique combinations of experience that set you apart. Our degree exposes you to a variety of media practices that are central to how media industries are transforming today.

Graduates pursue careers in:
- Film and Media Production
- Journalism (Broadcast, Print and Digital)
- Advertising and Marketing
- Content Creation (TV, Film, Video, and Social Media)
- Film Programming and Curation
- Media Research and Analysis
- Public Relations and Communications
- Arts Administration
- Teaching and Education
- Digital Media Strategy
