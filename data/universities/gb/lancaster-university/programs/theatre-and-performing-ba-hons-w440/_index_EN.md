# Theatre and Performing BA Hons (W440)

## Overview

The future of theatre is yours to make. Studying Theatre and Performing at Lancaster gives you the freedom to explore what theatre can be — from acting and directing to playwriting, applied theatre and experimental performance. You will develop the skills to create and perform theatre that reflects the world around you, while building a deep understanding of how theatre shapes society.

Theatre and Performing is a new course designed for students who are passionate about making theatre happen — on stage, in communities, and beyond traditional performance spaces. Through a combination of practical workshops, collaborative projects and critical study, you will grow as a confident, versatile theatre-maker ready to shape the future of performance.

Lancaster is a hub of art and cultural activity, and the Lancaster Institute for the Contemporary Arts (LICA) brings together Art, Architecture, Design, Film, and Theatre in a vibrant creative community. This interdisciplinary environment enriches your learning, offering opportunities to collaborate across art forms and develop a broad creative toolkit.

## Module Details

### Year 1

**Making Theatre 1: Acting, Directing and Collaborating**
This foundational module introduces you to the core skills of theatre-making. Through practical workshops, you will explore acting techniques, directing strategies and collaborative creation processes. Working in small groups, you will develop your ability to create compelling performance from initial idea to final presentation.

**Living Theory**
Explore the key theoretical frameworks that underpin contemporary theatre and performance. This module connects critical theory with practice, helping you understand how ideas shape the theatre we make and the theatre we watch. You will engage with a wide range of thinkers and develop your own critical voice.

**Raw Materials of Performance**
Discover the fundamental elements that make up performance — body, voice, space, time, and text. Through practical exploration, you will learn how to manipulate these raw materials to create engaging theatrical experiences.

**Accessibility and Inclusion in Creative Industries**
An essential module that equips you with the knowledge and skills to create inclusive, accessible theatre. Explore how accessibility and inclusion are shaping the creative industries and learn to embed these principles in your own practice.

### Year 2

**Making Theatre 2: Beyond the Stage**
Build on your Year 1 skills to create theatre that moves beyond traditional stage spaces. This module explores site-specific performance, community theatre, digital performance and other contemporary forms. You will develop your ability to adapt theatre-making to different contexts and audiences.

**Sustainable Practice**
Explore what sustainability means for theatre and the creative industries. From environmental sustainability to sustainable career models, this module helps you develop a practice that is both ethically informed and professionally viable.

**Theatre and Society 2**
Examine the relationship between theatre and its social, political and cultural contexts. Explore how theatre can challenge, reflect and transform society, and develop your understanding of theatre's role in contemporary life.

### Year 3

**Theatre Independent Project**
This substantial module gives you the opportunity to pursue a self-directed project in an area of theatre and performing that interests you most. With supervision from academic staff, you will research, develop and present a major piece of work — whether a performance, a written dissertation, or a combination of both.

**Making Theatre 3: Advanced Performance Project**
This is the culmination of your practical theatre-making journey. Working collaboratively, you will conceive, develop and present an advanced performance project that demonstrates the range and depth of skills you have developed throughout the course. The project culminates in a public performance.

## Department

**School of Arts**, Faculty of Humanities, Arts and Social Sciences

The Lancaster Institute for the Contemporary Arts (LICA) is a dynamic interdisciplinary department that brings together Art, Architecture, Design, Film, and Theatre. This unique combination creates a vibrant creative community where students can collaborate across disciplines and develop a broad understanding of the creative arts. LICA's facilities include the Nuffield Theatre — one of the largest studio theatres in the UK — as well as dedicated performance studios, rehearsal spaces, and technical workshops.

Lancaster is a city rich in cultural activity, home to award-winning festivals such as Light Up Lancaster, Lancaster Words, and Litfest literary festival. The University regularly partners with local and national arts organisations, providing students with valuable professional experience and networking opportunities.
