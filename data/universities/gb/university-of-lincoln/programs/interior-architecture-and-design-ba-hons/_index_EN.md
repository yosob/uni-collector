# BA (Hons) Interior Architecture and Design — University of Lincoln

## Overview

The **BA (Hons) Interior Architecture and Design** at the University of Lincoln is a design-led programme that aims to develop creative and professional designers capable of working across a range of spatial design disciplines.

Students explore the design of interior spaces, considering how people interact with their environments. The programme covers design studio practice, technical skills, historical and theoretical contexts, and professional practice.

## Curriculum

### Year 1 (Level 4) — Foundations in Spatial Design

- **Design 1** (ARC1202)
- **Interpret 1** (ARC1201)
- **Realise 1** (ARC1203)

### Year 2 (Level 5) — Exploring Practice

- **Design 2A** (INT2202), **Design 2B** (INT2203)
- **Interpret 2A** (INT2200), **Interpret 2B** (INT2201)
- **Interdisciplinary Collaboration** (GRA2197)
- **Practice 2** (INT2204), **Realise 2** (INT2205)
- Year in Industry / Study Abroad — Optional

### Year 3 (Level 6) — Independent Design Identity

- **Design 3** (INT3200)
- **Interpret 3** (INT3201)
- **Realise 3** (INT3202)
