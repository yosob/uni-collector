# BA (Hons) Illustration — University of Lincoln

## Overview

The **BA (Hons) Illustration** at the University of Lincoln aims to develop students into highly skilled illustrators with their own creative voice. The programme focuses on visual storytelling, drawing, digital media, and professional practice.

Students explore both traditional and digital illustration techniques, developing skills in drawing, sequential art, book illustration, and editorial illustration. The programme encourages experimentation and risk-taking.

## Curriculum

### Year 1 (Level 4)

- **Contextual Studies 1** — Creative theory, critical thinking
- **Digital and Sequential Illustration** — Digital media creation, sequential narrative
- **Drawing and Process** — Drawing techniques, traditional and experimental processes
- **Introduction to Illustration** — Illustration principles and practice

### Year 2 (Level 5)

- **Audience and Message** — Audience analysis, visual response
- **Books and Storytelling** — Book illustration, narrative structure
- **Thinking and Making** — Material experimentation, concept exploration

### Year 3 (Level 6)

- **Contemporary Illustration** — Current trends, live competition projects
- **Contextual Studies 3** — Independent research/dissertation
- **Negotiated Illustration** — Self-directed practice
- **Professionalism and Commissions** — Professional practice, client management
