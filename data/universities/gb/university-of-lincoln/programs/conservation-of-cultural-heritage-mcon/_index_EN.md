# MCon Conservation of Cultural Heritage — University of Lincoln

## Overview

The **MCon Conservation of Cultural Heritage** is a four-year **Integrated Master's** degree that blends theory with practical conservation skills. Students gain extensive hands-on experience working with genuine historic objects from museums, historic houses, and private collections.

Years 1–3 follow the BA (Hons) programme. Year 4 explores topics in greater depth, including advanced project work designed to prepare students for professional practice or further research.

Students study in high-specification, purpose-built laboratories within the University's Peter de Wint Building. The city of Lincoln's rich cultural heritage makes it an ideal location for conservation study.

## Curriculum

### Year 1 (Level 4)

**Core (7):** Becoming a Professional, Conservation Techniques, Introduction to Heritage Materials, Introduction to Heritage Science, Introduction to Interventive Conservation Practice, Introduction to Visual and Material Culture, Visual Recording and Reporting

### Year 2 (Level 5)

**Core (3):** Analytical Techniques in Conservation, Applied Practical Conservation 2, Collections Care and Management

**Options:** Conservation Placement, Study at a Partner Institution, Industry Placement, plus extensive humanities and heritage options

### Year 3 (Level 6)

Advanced practical conservation, dissertation, and public exhibition of work.

### Year 4 (Master's Level)

Advanced specialist project work, professional practice preparation, and public lecture presentation.
