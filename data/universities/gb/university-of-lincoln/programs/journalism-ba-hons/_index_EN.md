# BA (Hons) Journalism — University of Lincoln

## Overview

The **BA (Hons) Journalism** at the University of Lincoln is accredited by the **NCTJ (National Council for the Training of Journalists)** and provides students with the skills and knowledge needed for a career in journalism and the media industries.

Students learn essential journalism skills including news reporting, feature writing, shorthand, media law, and digital production. The programme benefits from industry-standard facilities including dedicated newsrooms and broadcast studios.

## Curriculum

### Year 1 (Level 4) — 7 Core Modules

- Essential Journalism 1
- Essential Law & Crime Reporting
- Journalism Production One
- Politics for Journalists
- Shorthand
- Sport and the Media
- Sport Writing and Reporting

### Year 2 (Level 5) — 5 Core + 4 Optional

**Core:** Essential Journalism 2, Journalism Production Two, Law, Ethics and Regulation, Specialist Reporting and Production, Sport and Society, The Business of Sport

**Options:** Industry Placement, Journalism International Exchange, Shorthand

### Year 3 (Level 6) — 4 Core

- Dissertation
- Journalism Production Three: News and Sport Production Days
- Journalism Project
- Sport, PR and Communication

## Career Prospects

Graduates work as reporters, news editors, broadcast journalists, online journalists, feature writers, press officers, and communications specialists. Employers include BBC, ITV, Sky News, and regional newspaper groups.
