# BA (Hons) Conservation of Cultural Heritage — University of Lincoln

## Overview

Conservators play a key role in safeguarding our cultural heritage, ensuring that artworks, architecture, archaeological finds, and museum collections are preserved for future generations.

The **BA (Hons) Conservation of Cultural Heritage** is a three-year undergraduate degree that blends theory with practical conservation skills. Students have the opportunity to gain extensive hands-on experience working with genuine historic objects provided by museums, historic houses, and private collections.

Students study in high-specification, purpose-built laboratories within the University's Peter de Wint Building, developing expertise across a wide range of materials, time periods, and collections in their historical context.

The city of Lincoln is bursting with history and the city's rich cultural heritage makes it an ideal location in which to study conservation. The course covers remedial treatment, preventive conservation and collections management, and investigative and digital heritage techniques. There may also be opportunities to work with the University's commercial conservation consultancy, **Lincoln Conservation**.

## How You Study

Conservation is a multidisciplinary course, drawing together skills in art, science and history, and applying these to cultural heritage. The course aims to offer a balance between learning both the theory and practice of conservation.

Throughout the course, students carry out conservation treatments and scientific analysis of historical artefacts — from simple objects in the first year to more complex work as skills increase. The course culminates in an exhibition of work at the end of the final year.

The second term of year two offers the opportunity to study overseas at a partner institution, choose optional modules, or undertake an extended work placement.

Teaching methods include lectures, practical sessions, seminars, and workshops.

## Curriculum

### Year 1 (Level 4)

| Module | Status | Description |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **Becoming a Professional** | Core | Planning and preparing for professional life, university study skills, and career navigation. |
| **Conservation Techniques** | Core | Theory and application of basic conservation principles — observation, documentation, condition assessment, cleaning, adhesives, consolidants. |
| **Introduction to Heritage Materials** | Core | Theoretical knowledge of historic materials, conservation techniques, and deterioration processes. |
| **Introduction to Heritage Science** | Core | Basic chemistry concepts and scientific study of cultural heritage materials. |
| **Introduction to Interventive Conservation Practice** | Core | Generic practical skills for treatment of historic objects — laboratory and bench skills, documentation. |
| **Introduction to Visual and Material Culture** | Core | History of art and architecture, historical archaeology, and conservation of historical buildings. |
| **Visual Recording and Reporting** | Core | Recording skills for conservators — documentation, photography, lighting techniques. |
