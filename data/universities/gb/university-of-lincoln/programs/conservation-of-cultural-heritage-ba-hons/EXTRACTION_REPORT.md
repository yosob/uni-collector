## Modules Summary

### Year 1 (Level 4) — 7 Core
- **Core:** Becoming a Professional, Conservation Techniques, Introduction to Heritage Materials, Introduction to Heritage Science, Introduction to Interventive Conservation Practice, Introduction to Visual and Material Culture, Visual Recording and Reporting

### Year 2 (Level 5) — 3 Core + 32 Options
- **Core:** Analytical Techniques in Conservation, Applied Practical Conservation 2, Collections Care and Management
- **Notable Options:** Conservation Placement (Full/Part Time), Digital Heritage, Material Histories: Objects, Interpretation, Display, Study at a Partner Institution: Conservation, plus a wide range of humanities modules (history, philosophy, classical studies)

### Year 3 (Level 6) — 5 Core
- **Core:** Applied Practical Conservation 3.1, Applied Practical Conservation 3.2, Applied Preventive Conservation, Conservation Exhibition, Conservation Independent Study: Dissertation

## Notes

- Subject area ranked **3rd overall in the UK** (Complete University Guide 2025, out of 56 ranking institutions).
- Students work with **genuine historic objects** provided by museums and private collections.
- Facilities include **high-specification, purpose-built laboratories** in the Peter de Wint Building.
- Students can specialise in **paper, stone, wood, or metals conservation**.
- Links to **Lincoln Conservation**, a professional conservation company based on campus.
- Lab coat, tool kit, and goggles are provided to all students.
- Year 2 optional modules span a wide range of humanities subjects, reflecting the programme's interdisciplinary nature.
- **Contextual offers** available: one grade or 8 UCAS point reduction for eligible applicants.
- Fees: £9,250 per year (UK students); international fees available on the university website.
