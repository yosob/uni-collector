# Arts Foundation Year — University of Lincoln

## Overview

The **Arts Foundation Year** at the University of Lincoln is a pre-degree pathway that provides an alternative route of entry onto a range of creative arts degree programmes. It is designed for students who may not meet the direct entry requirements for their chosen degree or who wish to build their skills and confidence before starting a full degree.

The foundation year equips students with essential creative, academic, and professional skills through interdisciplinary study, preparing them for progression onto one of ten pathway degrees.

## How You Study

The foundation year introduces students to core creative skills including drawing, design thinking, digital tools, and contextual studies. Teaching methods include studio workshops, lectures, seminars, and tutorials.

## Career Prospects

The foundation year leads directly into one of the creative arts degree programmes, opening pathways to careers in animation, architecture, graphic design, illustration, photography, product design, and more.
