# BA (Hons) Drama, Theatre and Performance — University of Lincoln

## Overview

The **BA (Hons) Drama, Theatre and Performance** at the University of Lincoln is designed to develop the skills needed for a career in the performing arts and creative industries. Students can engage with a range of performance practices, develop critical and creative thinking, and explore how drama connects with wider cultural and social issues.

The programme is taught in the purpose-built Lincoln Arts Centre, providing access to a professional theatre venue. Students benefit from workshops with visiting practitioners, opportunities for public performance, and strong links to the professional theatre sector.

## Curriculum

### Year 1 (Level 4)

**Core (4):** Performance Perspectives, Making Performance 1, Making Performance 2, Exploring 1: The Creative Arts

**Options (8):** Introduction to Prose, Introduction to Stage Design, Project Space Plus, Screen Performance, Solo Performance, Staging a Musical 2, Technical Theatre Technology, The Physical Performer

### Year 2 (Level 5)

**Core (1):** Specialist Elective

**Options (9):** Acting the Song, Contemporary Performance Technologies, Contemporary Production Practices, Digital Performance, Industry Placement, LSCA Study Abroad, Musical Theatre Futures, Stage Combat, Teaching Practices

### Year 3 (Level 6)

**Core (2):** Exploring 3: Investigating Creative Practice, Staging a Play

**Options (12):** Arts and Cultural Industries, Counterculture, Forming A Company, Podcasting, Production Design and Realisation, Scriptwriting for Stage and Screen, The Craft of Creative Non-Fiction, The Craft of Fiction, plus additional creative arts options

## Career Prospects

Graduates can pursue careers as actors, directors, playwrights, arts administrators, drama therapists, community theatre practitioners, educators, and producers. The Lincoln Arts Centre provides valuable industry networking opportunities.
