# BArch (Hons) Architecture — University of Lincoln

## Overview

The **BArch (Hons) Architecture** degree at the University of Lincoln aims to prepare students for a career in the exciting field of architectural practice, placing a strong emphasis on design and creativity.

The historic city of Lincoln has roots in Roman Britain, providing an inspiring context in which to explore architecture and learn about heritage and archaeology in relation to contemporary architectural design. The Lincoln School of Architecture and the Built Environment is a cooperative, multidisciplinary environment for sharing ideas, supporting students in discovering their own design approach.

The course is **validated by the Royal Institute of British Architects (RIBA)** as providing exemption from Part 1 examinations and forms the first stage of a three-part programme of education and training for aspiring architects. The University of Lincoln also runs RIBA/ARB Parts 2 and 3 programmes.

## How You Study

Students study the underlying concepts of architecture and the ways in which a building can meet the needs of its occupants and of society. Teaching methods include:

- Lectures, tutorials, and seminars
- Studio-based design workshops
- Computer-aided design (CAD)
- Hands-on modelling and material exploration
- Site and live project visits

The programme follows three core streams that run through each year:
1. **Design** — Creative design studio practice
2. **Interpret** — Critical research, history and theory
3. **Realise** — Technical resolution and material exploration

## Curriculum

### Year 1 (Level 4)

The first year establishes the creative foundations of architectural learning.

| Module | Code | Status | Description |
|--------|------|--------|-------------|
| **Design 1** | ARC1202M | Core | Establishes creative foundations through design studios and workshops. Students explore two- and three-dimensional form, space and composition through 'thinking through drawing and making.' |
| **Interpret 1** | ARC1201M | Core | Grounds creative explorations in research and critical thinking. Broad historical survey of pivotal architectural movements, narrowing to regional case studies. |
| **Realise 1** | ARC1203M | Core | Tests and refines design concepts through visual and material enquiry — from analogue sketch studies to physical models and digital prototypes. |

### Year 2 (Level 5)

Core modules continue the Design, Interpret, and Realise streams at an advanced level. Optional opportunities include:

| Module | Code | Status | Description |
|--------|------|--------|-------------|
| **Design & Architecture Year in Industry** | DES2003 | Option | Full-time placement year in the creative industries. |
| **Semester Study Abroad** | ARC2019 | Option | Spend a semester at a partner institution (60 credits equivalent). |
| **Study Abroad Module** | ARC2018 | Option | Study at a partner institution with potential internship component. |

### Year 3 (Level 6)

Third-year students benefit from the RIBA Mentoring Scheme and work on advanced design projects preparing them for architectural practice and Parts 2/3 study.

## Assessment

Assessment is primarily through coursework and design portfolio submissions, including:
- Design projects and portfolio submissions
- Written assignments and essays
- Presentations and critiques
- Studio-based reviews

## Career Prospects

This course forms **Part 1 of the three-part programme** required to register as an architect in the UK. Career paths include:

- Architect (after completing Parts 2 and 3)
- Interior architect
- Urban designer
- Design consultant
- Architectural technologist
- Heritage consultant
- Sustainable design specialist
