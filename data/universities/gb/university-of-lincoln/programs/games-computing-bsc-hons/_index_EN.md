# BSc (Hons) Games Computing — University of Lincoln

## Overview

The **BSc (Hons) Games Computing** at the University of Lincoln aims to develop the skills needed for a career in the games industry. Students study game design, programming, and development, alongside computer science fundamentals.

The programme combines theoretical knowledge with practical skills, covering areas such as game engine development, artificial intelligence, computer graphics, and human-computer interaction.

## Curriculum

### Year 1 (Level 4)

All core modules: Fundamentals of Computer Science, Game Design, Mathematical Concepts for Computer Science, Object-Oriented Programming, Problem Solving, Systems Architecture.

### Year 2 (Level 5)

All core modules: Advanced Game Development, Artificial Intelligence, Computer Graphics, Database Systems, Human-Computer Interaction, Software Engineering.

### Year 3 (Level 6)

**Core (3):** Project (individual), Professional Practice, Advanced Topics in Games Computing

**Options (7):** Choose from advanced computing modules including machine learning, cyber security, and specialised game development topics.
