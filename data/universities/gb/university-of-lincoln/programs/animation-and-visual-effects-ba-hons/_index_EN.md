# BA (Hons) Animation and Visual Effects — University of Lincoln

## Overview

**Animation and Visual Effects at Lincoln** offers an introduction to the innovative world of animation and visual effects through moving image, digital visualisation, and contemporary narrative.

The course enables students to develop into creative animators and artists with the flexibility to practise their craft in a variety of media. It covers a range of skills, including the fundamentals of animation, storytelling, performance, design, drawing, 3D modelling, 2D and computer-generated animation, compositing, animation theory, and the production of short films.

The Lincoln School of Film and Media has had success at the Royal Television Society awards. In 2019, student film *Papier* won Best Animation at the awards. The programme is also available with an **Arts Foundation Year**, providing an alternative route of entry onto the full degree programme.

## How You Study

This course aims to provide a thorough grounding in classical animation principles as well as an introduction to digital techniques for animation and visual effects.

Life drawing forms an integral part of the programme, combined with developing narrative, character design, and animation techniques. Students are encouraged to participate in various stages of production, such as character animation, art direction and lighting, digital compositing and effects, post-production techniques, and production management.

In the final year, students can work as part of a team to produce a short film that sets the premise for their personal showreel. Industry practitioners including film, television, and video games animators are part of the teaching team.

## Curriculum

### Year 1 (Level 4)

| Module | Status | Description |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **Animation Principles** | Core | Core principles of animated movement — timing, spacing, weight, performance. Industry-standard software practice. |
| **CGI Principles** | Core | Core ideas of computer-generated imagery — digital form, light, material. Real-time rendering and procedural CG. |
| **Cultivating Creativity** | Core | Interdisciplinary module developing creative thinking and industry awareness. Curiosity, ideation, and professional skills. |
| **Design for Animation** | Core | Character, prop, and environment design. Visual language, composition, and colour for storytelling. |
| **Story and Film Language** | Core | Animation pipeline fundamentals — story, character development, film language. Storyboards and animatics. |
| **Creative Criticism** | Option† | Historical development and cultural practices of criticism across film, television, and screen media. |
| **Emerging Technologies** | Option† | Adaptive and inquisitive engagement with new technologies. Video essay or critically informed presentation. |
| **Expanded Film** | Option† | Experimental and artist-led moving-image practice — expanded cinema, installation, video essay. |
| **Experimental Animation Practices** | Option† | Experimental approaches to animation production and creative expression. |
| **Sound for Visual Media** | Option† | Sound design and audio production for visual media projects. |

### Year 2 (Level 5)

Core and optional modules advancing skills in animation production, CGI, and specialisation areas.

### Year 3 (Level 6)

Students work on advanced projects including a team-produced short film and personal showreel development.
