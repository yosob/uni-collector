# BA (Hons) Dance — University of Lincoln

## Overview

The **BA (Hons) Dance** programme at the University of Lincoln is designed to develop versatile dance practitioners through a combination of technique training, choreographic practice, and critical engagement with dance as an art form.

Students explore a range of dance styles and approaches, developing skills in performance, choreography, teaching, and arts management. The programme encourages creative exploration and critical thinking within a supportive and collaborative environment.

## Curriculum

### Year 1 (Level 4)

**Core:** Choreo 1: Improvisation and Composition, Collaborative Performance Making, Dance Anatomy, Dance Technique 1, Exploring 1: The Creative Arts

**Options:** Introduction to Prose, Introduction to Stage Design, Project Space Plus, Screen Performance, Solo Performance, Staging a Musical 2, Technical Theatre Technology, The Physical Performer

### Year 2 (Level 5)

**Core:** Choreo 2: Contact Practices, Dance Studies 2, Dance Technique 2: Dance and Fitness, Dance Technique 2: Somatics, Exploring 2: Place and Space, Specialist Elective

**Options:** Acting the Song, Arts and Health, Contemporary Performance Technologies, Contemporary Production Practices, Creative Audio Technologies, Digital Performance, Industry Placement, LSCA Industry Placement Year, LSCA Study Abroad, Musical Theatre Futures, Stage Combat, Teaching Practices, The Craft of Creative Non-Fiction, The Craft of Fiction

### Year 3 (Level 6)

**Core:** Choreo 3: Performance, Dance Company and Education, Dance Management and Production, Dance Technique 3: Performance and Leadership, Exploring 3: Investigating Creative Practice

**Options:** Arts and Cultural Industries, Counterculture, Forming A Company, Podcasting, Production Design and Realisation, Scriptwriting for Stage and Screen
