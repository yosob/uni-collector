# BA (Hons) Graphic Design — University of Lincoln

## Overview

The **BA (Hons) Graphic Design** at the University of Lincoln encourages students to explore and develop their individual approach to design. The programme focuses on visual communication, creative thinking, and professional practice.

Students work on live briefs, international competitions (RSA, D&AD, ISTD), and collaborative projects. The course provides access to specialist facilities including screen printing, letterpress, Risograph, darkroom, laser cutting, and 3D printing.

## Curriculum

### Year 1 (Level 4)

- **Contextual Studies 1** — Creative theory and critical thinking
- **Design Fundamentals 1** — Visual design principles, digital tools, user-centred design
- **Graphic Communication 1** — Foundation of graphic communication design
- **Visual Expression 1** — Visual expression, experimentation and personal style

### Year 2 (Level 5)

- **Contextual Studies 2** — Creative industries economics, design ethics, social engagement
- **Design Fundamentals 2** — UI/UX design, interaction design, digital media
- **Graphic Communication 2** — Concept-driven, client projects, professional practice
- **Visual Expression 2** — Complex information communication, creative risk-taking

### Year 3 (Level 6)

- **Contextual Studies 3** — Independent research/dissertation
- **Design Fundamentals 3** — Student-to-designer transition, industry engagement
- **Graphic Communication 3** — Self-directed projects, specialisation
- **Visual Expression 3** — Self-directed creative practice, personal visual language
