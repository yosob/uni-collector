## Curriculum Summary

**Year 1 (Common First Year):**
- Core creative skills in idea development, design, pre-production and production techniques
- Introduction to visualisation techniques for character, environment and concept design
- Creative hack lab: experimental approaches to games art
- Professional contexts and creative thinking
- Collaborative projects with students from other creative disciplines

**Year 2:**
- Designing Game Worlds: Theory, Art and Innovation (30 credits)
- 3D character and environment art development
- Concept art and visual development for games
- Contextual and Professional Explorations
- Collaborative and interdisciplinary projects
- Industry practices in a simulated games studio environment

**Year 3 (Final Year):**
- Extended Study in Games Art (major project / final portfolio)
- Contextual Studies and Critical Research
- Professional Consolidation and Career Development
- Portfolio and showreel development
- Industry-facing project or placement opportunity

Optional placement year available between Year 2 and Year 3 (Sandwich mode, additional fee: £1,250 for UK students).

The course features a common first year shared with other creative programmes in the School of Arts and Creative Industries. The course emphasises industry-standard practices delivered in a simulated games studio environment.

**Assessment methods:** Artefacts, practical coursework, digital coursework, individual work, group work, live/video/poster presentations, video/academic essays, projects, individual assignments, portfolios.
