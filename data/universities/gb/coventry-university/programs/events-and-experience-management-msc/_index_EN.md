## Curriculum Summary

**Semester 1:**
- Understanding the Events and Experience Industries (30 credits) — Compulsory
- Experience Design (30 credits) — Compulsory

**Semester 2:**
- Marketing and Funding (20 credits) — Compulsory
- Leadership and Relationship Management (20 credits) — Compulsory
- Health and Safety, Risk, and Crisis Management (20 credits) — Compulsory

**Semester 3 (Summer):**
- Final Project / Dissertation (60 credits) — Compulsory

This course is designed for individuals with diverse academic or work experience backgrounds, providing opportunities to gain industry-aligned skills in leadership, project management, and sustainable event organisation. The course has a strong problem-based teaching approach, grounding theories and assignments in real-world event and experience scenarios. It addresses the principles employed in making strategic decisions and dynamic operational decision-making, offering insight on leading and managing within the events and experience sectors. Key themes include leadership and stakeholder management, event creation and management following the PMBOK framework, and sustainability and innovation with emerging trends such as AI in event management. Students benefit from real-world teaching, simulations and live projects, diverse assessment types promoting creativity and collaboration, industry connections and volunteering opportunities, and a focus on contemporary issues including planning sustainable events, promoting equality and inclusion, and responsible leadership.

**Assessment methods:** Coursework, presentations, live project reports, portfolio submissions, written assignments, simulations, group work, dissertation.
