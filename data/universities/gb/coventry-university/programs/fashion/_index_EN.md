## Curriculum Summary

**Year 1 – Level 4:**
- Fashion Communities (Semester 1, 20 credits): Explores collaborative practice and building creative communities within fashion; developing communication skills
- Fashion Futures (Semester 1, 20 credits): Introduction to fashion design practice, innovative fashion artefacts and forward-thinking design approaches
- Reshaping Fashion (Semester 1, 20 credits): Examines contemporary and historical fashion contexts; reshaping understanding of fashion's role in society
- Disruptive Practice (Semester 2, 20 credits): Challenges conventional fashion design through experimental and disruptive creative methods
- Fashion Activators (Semester 2, 20 credits): Hands-on making and production skills; activating fashion ideas through garment and artefact creation
- Creative Revolutions (Semester 2, 20 credits): Creative exploration of materials, media and processes; developing individual design identity

**Year 2 – Level 5:**
- Fashion Enterprise (Semester 1, 20 credits): Entrepreneurial skills, industry engagement and professional practice in fashion
- Fashion Innovation (Semester 1, 20 credits): Innovative design approaches, new materials and technologies in fashion practice
- Fashion Futures: Industry Ready (Semester 1, 20 credits): Industry-set briefs, competitions and preparation for professional fashion contexts
- International Fashion Exploration (Semester 2, 20 credits): Global fashion markets, intercultural awareness and international design perspectives
- Developing Specialisms (Semester 2, 20 credits): Specialist workshops in garment manufacturing, digital printing, jewellery and footwear; individual direction
- Fashion Statement (Semester 2, 20 credits): Developing personal design voice and creating a cohesive fashion statement

**Optional Enhancement Year (between Year 2 and Year 3):**
- Professional Enhancement Year: UK or international industry placement
- International Enhancement Year: Study abroad at partner institution and/or international placement

**Year 3 – Level 6 (Final Year):**
- Future Fashion Concepts (Semester 1, 20 credits): Conceptual thinking, sustainability and ethical fashion futures
- Future Fashion Ideation (Semester 1, 20 credits): Generating and developing innovative design ideas for final collection
- Fashion Futures: Graduate Ready (Semester 1, 20 credits): Professional portfolio development and career preparation
- Future Fashion Realisation (Semester 2, 60 credits): Major self-directed graduation project; realisation of a complete fashion collection and showcase

**Assessment methods:** Coursework-based, including practical design projects, garment and artefact production, portfolio submissions, group critiques, presentations, live briefs, competition entries and self-initiated projects. Assessment increases in complexity from guided projects at Level 4 to independent, specialised work at Level 6.

**Industry links:** Students have worked with companies including Sainsbury's TU, George, Pentland, Reebok, Murray, Tatty Devine, PETA, Piñatex, Kitty Joseph, General Motors and DesignLab.

**Study visits:** Day-visits to fashion exhibitions, trade events and fashion weeks in the UK; international visits to Première Vision Paris, International Fashion Weeks and Shanghai Fashion Week. Collaborative Online International Learning (COIL) projects with partner institutions including Communications University Zhejiang (China) and Raffles (Singapore).

**Graduate showcase:** Opportunity to exhibit at Graduate Fashion Week, London.

## Scholarship Info

Vice-Chancellor Scholarship: Fee waiver up to £5,000 for self-funded international students. Undergraduate (3 years): £4,000 total (£2,000 in Year 1, £1,000 in each subsequent year). Not applicable for UK/EU citizens. Coventry also offers the Academic Excellence Scholarship and sports scholarships. EU Support Bursary available for eligible EU students.
