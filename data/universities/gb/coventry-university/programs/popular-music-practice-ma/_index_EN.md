## Curriculum Summary

**Semester 1 (September entry):**
- Performance and Technology (30 credits) — Compulsory
- Collaborative Project: Enterprise (20 credits) — Compulsory
- The Practitioner's Toolbox (10 credits) — Compulsory

**Semester 2:**
- The Musician's Craft (30 credits) — Compulsory
- Collaborative Project: Community and Sustainability (20 credits) — Compulsory
- The Practitioner's Context (10 credits) — Compulsory

**Semester 3 (Summer):**
- Presence, Persona and Identity (30 credits) — Compulsory
- Creative Frontiers (20 credits) — Compulsory
- Creative Futures and Social Change (10 credits) — Compulsory (RSA accredited)

The MA Popular Music Practice course is designed for popular music performers, recording artists and songwriters seeking to challenge themselves and develop their creative, critical and technical skills in music-making. The course cultivates artistic, analytical, technical and culturally sensitive abilities as a contemporary music professional, with a strong emphasis on practice.

Students work across independent and collaborative briefs to produce work in professional contexts, including live scenarios developed in partnership with community, the academy and industry. The course is designed as a carousel with three entry points (September, January, May), where each semester has set modules. Students benefit from expert instruction and meaningful connectivity with research and practice-based expertise from across broader media, production and performance disciplines.

Students have the opportunity to collaborate across MA courses and externally on project-oriented modules that promote interdisciplinary understanding. Via collaborative project modules, students engage directly with the application of their discipline in contexts including commercial application of ideas, tools for international sustainable development and empowering communities to tackle socio-political challenges. The Creative Futures and Social Change module is accredited by the Royal Society of Arts (RSA), providing students with a 12-month RSA fellowship upon completion.

Students can also apply for an extended route offering professional experience through three semesters of internship or placement. Facilities include five recording studios with Neve, SSL and Dolby Atmos capabilities.

**Assessment methods:** Coursework, practical performances, portfolios, collaborative project presentations, written assignments, creative projects, research documentation.
