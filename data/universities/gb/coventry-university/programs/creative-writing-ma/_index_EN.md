## Curriculum Summary

**Semester 1:**
- Emerging Screenwriting (15 credits) — Compulsory
- Graphic Novels and Illustrated Storytelling (15 credits) — Compulsory
- Role Playing and Interactive Narratives (15 credits) — Compulsory
- Creative Nonfiction (15 credits) — Compulsory

**Semester 2:**
- Developing Academic Skills for Postgraduate Study (15 credits) — Compulsory
- Critical Reading and Writing Skills for Postgraduate Literary Study (15 credits) — Compulsory
- Utopian and Dystopian Writing: Identity, Education and Ecology (15 credits) — Compulsory
- Gothic Nature (15 credits) — Compulsory

**Semester 3 (Summer):**
- Creative Futures and Social Change (10 credits) — Compulsory
- Final Project (50 credits) — Compulsory

This course focuses on new and emerging genres of writing, as well as writing for digital media and platforms, including streaming, video games and other interactive narratives, graphic novels, the contemporary novel, and creative non-fiction. It will appeal to applicants from a broad range of educational backgrounds who wish to hone their craft in writing for contemporary and emerging industries and sectors. Students will learn to write for diverse media and platforms, with a focus on industry-specific storytelling techniques and audience engagement. The course explores the relationship between technology, authorship and creativity, examining digital tools, AI and emerging narrative options in modern industries. Students develop professionally through collaboration, portfolio creation, and insights from industry experts, gaining skills to market, pitch, and publish their work effectively.

Students form small writers' groups which work collaboratively to provide support and peer feedback on writing drafts in both the classroom and online via their writers' blogs, forging collaborative skills that are essential to working in the media and games industries. Students have the opportunity to work with local, national and international partners, including Ubisoft, HMP Rye Hill, local government and charity organisations, culture and heritage organisations, editors, publishers and literary agents. An extended route with professional experience placement is available for students wishing to gain additional industry experience. The Professional Practice and Innovation module is endorsed by the Royal Society of Arts (RSA). Modules on the course frame the creation and critique of contemporary narratives in the context of global challenges and opportunities, such as the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals.

**Assessment methods:** Coursework, creative writing portfolios, written assignments, critical essays, collaborative projects, writers' blog contributions, pitch presentations, final creative project (body of original creative work with supporting critical documentation).
