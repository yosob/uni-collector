## Curriculum Summary

**Semester 1:**
- Collaboration in Practice (30 credits) — Compulsory
- Automotive and Transport Design Specialism 1 (30 credits) — Compulsory

**Semester 2:**
- Professional Context (20 credits) — Compulsory
- Automotive and Transport Design Specialism 2 (30 credits) — Compulsory
- Specialist Investigation (30 credits) — Compulsory
- Transnational Professional Development (10 credits) — Compulsory

**Semester 3 (Summer):**
- Automotive and Transport Design Final Project (30 credits) — Compulsory

The course fosters critical and creative responses to the automotive and transport design sector. Students explore briefs and investigate global design drivers, markets and emerging opportunities to produce solutions at the forefront of the sector. The overall goal is to innovatively challenge existing or future design through investigative and creative approaches. Students build upon existing skills and knowledge to develop professional design competencies that are industry relevant, with options for 2D/3D analogue and/or digital visualisation including model making, digital portfolios and online content. Key features include industry connections, international travel experiences and Collaborative Online International Learning (COIL) projects. Students have the option to apply for a 'professional experience' opportunity (extending the course to 2 years) designed to further develop skills and knowledge with the aim of maximising employability prospects.

**Assessment methods:** Design projects, coursework, presentations, portfolio submissions, written assignments, specialist investigation report, final major project, degree show.
