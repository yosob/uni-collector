## Admission Requirements

- An honours degree 2:2 or above in Architecture with exemption from Part 1 of the RIBA Examinations (or its equivalent as formally recognised by the RIBA/ARB)
- Portfolio: A convincing portfolio of relevant architectural design work (presented at interview or submitted by post/online)
- Interview may be required (virtual interview available)
- International: 60% in a 3-year undergraduate degree or 55% in a 4-year undergraduate degree

## Curriculum Summary

**Year 1:**
- Design, Process and Communication (30 credits)
- Comprehensive Design (30 credits)
- Construction Technology and Environmental Design (20 credits)
- Cultural Context (20 credits)
- Integrated Project (10 credits)

**Year 2:**
- Research for Design (15 credits)
- Design Thesis (60 credits)
- Professional Practice and Management (10 credits)
- Critical Investigations (30 credits)

The course is prescribed by the Architects Registration Board (ARB) for the purposes of entry onto the United Kingdom Register of Architects, satisfying Part 2 of the qualifications needed to become a registered architect. Prescription is currently valid until 31 December 2028.

**Assessment methods:** Design projects, coursework, presentations, written assignments, portfolio submissions, design thesis.
