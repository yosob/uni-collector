## Curriculum Summary

**Year 1 – Level 4 (Design Cluster Shared Year):**
- Designer – 30 credits: Explores historical, contemporary and emerging design approaches; how design choices carry meaning
- User – 30 credits: Focuses on user-centred design, audience research and empathetic communication
- Co-Creator – 30 credits: Collaborative projects shared with Automotive & Transport Design and Product Design students
- Maker – 30 credits: Hands-on making, material exploration and technical skill development

**Year 2 – Level 5:**
- Design Narrative – 20 credits: Storytelling through graphic design; connecting and engaging emotions
- Design System – 20 credits: Systematic approaches to branding, identity and visual systems
- Design Interaction – 20 credits: Interactive and digital design, UX/UI principles
- Design Specialism – 20 credits: Directed focus towards personal areas of interest
- Design Activism – 20 credits: Socially engaged design; addressing wicked problems and complex briefs
- Design Profession – 20 credits: Professional practice, live briefs, portfolio development

**Year 3 – Level 6 (Final Year):**
- Design Experiment – 20 credits: Speculative inquiry, experimentation and risk-taking
- Design Culture – 20 credits: Critical thinking, design writing and contextual research
- Design Agility – 20 credits: Adaptive practice; responding to changing contexts and technologies
- Design Adventure – 20 credits: Self-directed major project; personal voice and specialisation
- Design Showcase – 20 credits: Degree show preparation, exhibition design and public presentation
- Design Identity – 20 credits: Professional identity, portfolio curation and career strategy

Optional sandwich year (Professional Enhancement Year or International Enhancement Year) available between Year 2 and Year 3.

**Course vision pillars:** Community, Employability, Exploration, Critical Thinking, Technical Skill, Problem Solving, Storytelling.

**Assessment methods:** Coursework-based, including practical design projects, portfolio submissions, group critiques, presentations, written assignments, self-initiated briefs and competition entries (e.g. D&AD, Penguin Cover Design Award).

## Scholarship Info

Vice-Chancellor Scholarship: Fee waiver up to £5,000 for self-funded international students. Undergraduate (3 years): £4,000 total (£2,000 in Year 1, £1,000 in each subsequent year). Not applicable for UK/EU citizens. Coventry also offers the Academic Excellence Scholarship and sports scholarships.
