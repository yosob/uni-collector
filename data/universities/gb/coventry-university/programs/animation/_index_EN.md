## Curriculum Summary

**Year 1 (Common First Year):**
- Core creative skills in idea development, design, pre-production and production techniques
- Introduction to animation principles and visual storytelling
- Introduction to 2D and 3D animation technologies
- Professional contexts and creative thinking

**Year 2:**
- Animation Practice (core spine module)
- Character Animation and Performance
- Digital Animation Techniques (rigging, modelling, stop-motion, hand-drawn)
- Contextual and Professional Explorations
- Collaborative and interdisciplinary projects

**Year 3 (Final Year):**
- Extended Study in Animation (major project / final showreel)
- Contextual Studies and Critical Research
- Professional Consolidation and Career Development
- Portfolio and showreel development

Optional placement year available between Year 2 and Year 3 (additional fee applies).

The course supports specialisms in 2D animation, 3D animation, character animation, environment design and storytelling.

**Assessment methods:** Artefacts, practical coursework, digital coursework, individual work, group work, live/video presentations, reports, projects, individual assignments, showreels, portfolios.

Vice-Chancellor Scholarship: Fee waiver up to £5,000 for self-funded international students. Undergraduate (3 years): £4,000 total (£2,000 in Year 1, £1,000 in each subsequent year). Not applicable for UK/EU citizens.
