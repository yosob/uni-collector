## Curriculum Summary

**Semester 1:**
- Collaboration in Practice (30 credits) — Compulsory
- Design Management Specialism 1 (30 credits) — Compulsory

**Semester 2:**
- Professional Context (20 credits) — Compulsory
- Design Management Specialism 2 (30 credits) — Compulsory
- Design Management Specialist Investigation (30 credits) — Compulsory
- Transnational Professional Development (10 credits) — Compulsory

**Semester 3 (Summer):**
- Design Management Final Project (30 credits) — Compulsory

This course has been designed in collaboration with industry practitioners and prepares students to manage creative design practice, practitioners and processes in a corporate context across a broad range of creative and manufacturing industries. The curriculum embraces business management, marketing, design strategy, entrepreneurship, innovation, sustainability, design leadership and design practice. Students develop knowledge of management processes including project management, milestone reporting, decision-making and budget management, as well as core business practice in areas like intellectual property and finance. The course is structured around three themes: communication, collaboration and creativity. Students have the opportunity to work with professionals from the creative design industry, including companies such as Philips, Canon and Samsung, and to work on briefs set by industry teams. The course focuses on challenges and creative culture, with a deeper understanding of design processes, evaluation and expectations.

**Assessment methods:** Coursework, presentations, portfolio submissions, written assignments, project reports, specialist investigation report, final major project.
