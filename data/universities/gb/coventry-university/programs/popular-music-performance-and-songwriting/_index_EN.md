## Overview

Popular Music Performance and Songwriting BA (Hons) at Coventry University is a practice-based, outward-facing course that prepares you to be a versatile, flexible, and adaptable operator within the twenty-first-century music industry. The course is designed to develop you into a creative musical artist and skilled popular music professional.

The course seeks to produce critically astute musicians who are confident performers and songwriters, working with today's technologies to make, reproduce, and disseminate music, and who understand the potential income streams available to them in the profession.

It features a common first year shared with Music and Audio Production BSc (Hons), enabling cross-disciplinary collaboration before specialisation. Students have access to professional recording studios equipped with SSL, Audient, and Neve mixing consoles, along with dedicated performance and rehearsal spaces in the Ellen Terry Building.

## Modules

### Year 1 (Common First Year)

| Module | Credits |
|---|---|
| Live Music | 30 |
| Music Business | 30 |
| Music on the Move | 30 |
| Songwriting and Studio Production | 30 |

**Live Music** — Cultivates confident and effective musical performance across settings, emphasising technical mastery, artistic interpretation, stage presence, and audience connection. Integrates branding and marketing strategies within the live music landscape.

**Music Business** — Explores the contemporary music industry covering copyright, licensing, professional online presence, networking, marketing, and self-promotion strategies with an emphasis on entrepreneurial skills.

**Music on the Move** — Introduces fundamentals of popular music production at home, covering sound capture, basic mixing, advanced DAW operation, beat production, and sound design with hands-on learning.

**Songwriting and Studio Production** — Comprehensive exploration of songwriting and studio recording in a collaborative environment, focusing on creative processes and core production techniques.

### Year 2

| Module | Credits |
|---|---|
| Band Performance | 20 |
| Writing about Popular Music | 20 |
| Applied Performance and Songwriting | 30 |
| Music and the Moving Image | 20 |
| Studio Project | 10 |
| The Online Artist | 10 |
| Add+Vantage | 10 |

**Band Performance** — Develops ensemble performance skills within a band context, focusing on rehearsal techniques, arrangement, musicianship, and live delivery across diverse popular music genres.

**Writing about Popular Music** — Builds critical writing and analytical skills through engaging with popular music scholarship, reviews, journalism, and academic discourse on music and culture.

**Applied Performance and Songwriting** — Advanced exploration of performance and songwriting craft, developing individual artistic voice through practical projects, collaborative songwriting, and live performance.

**Music and the Moving Image** — Examines the relationship between music and visual media, covering composition for film, television, and online content, with practical projects in scoring and soundtracking.

**Studio Project** — A focused studio-based project that develops recording, production, and creative technical skills in a professional studio environment.

**The Online Artist** — Explores how musicians can build and sustain an online presence, covering digital distribution, social media strategy, content creation, and monetisation in the digital music landscape.

**Add+Vantage** — A university-wide module allowing students to develop additional skills in areas such as languages, enterprise, or employability.

### Placement Year (Optional)

Students can opt for a sandwich year with either a UK work placement, international study, or international work placement to gain industry experience.

### Final Year (Year 3)

| Module | Credits |
|---|---|
| Advanced Performance and Songwriting | 40 |
| Popular Music Research Project | 20 |
| Working Briefs | 20 |
| Music Collaboration | 20 |
| The Music Industry | 10 |
| Add+Vantage | 10 |

**Advanced Performance and Songwriting** — A substantial capstone module integrating advanced performance techniques and songwriting craft into a major creative project, culminating in a public performance and professional-level portfolio.

**Popular Music Research Project** — An independent research project exploring a chosen area of popular music studies, developing critical thinking, academic writing, and analytical skills.

**Working Briefs** — Simulates professional industry practice by responding to real-world creative briefs, developing project management, client communication, and professional delivery skills.

**Music Collaboration** — Fosters collaborative musicianship through interdisciplinary projects, working with peers across music and creative disciplines to produce original work.

**The Music Industry** — Critical examination of the contemporary music industry, covering business models, rights management, live music economics, and emerging industry trends.

**Add+Vantage** — A university-wide module allowing students to develop additional skills in areas such as languages, enterprise, or employability.

## Teaching & Assessment

**Teaching Methods:** Lectures, seminars, tutorials, individual instrumental lessons, ensemble workshops, masterclasses, and practical studio sessions.

**Teaching Hours:** Approximately 12–15 contact hours per week in Year 1, reducing in subsequent years as independent study increases.

**Assessment Methods:** Coursework-based including live performances, recorded submissions, songwriting portfolios, written essays, research projects, presentations, and collaborative group work. No formal written examinations.
