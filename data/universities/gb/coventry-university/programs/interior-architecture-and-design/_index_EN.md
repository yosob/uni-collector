## Admission Requirements

- A-level: BBB / BBC (minimum 112 UCAS points from A-levels, BTEC or equivalent)
- GCSE: Minimum five graded 9-4 including English and Mathematics
- BTEC: DMM
- IB Diploma: 31 points
- Portfolio submission required (design, drawing or visualisation)
- Interview may be required
- UCAS code: W250

## Curriculum Summary

**Year 1:**
- The Language of Space
- Spatial Imagination and Function
- Transitory Spaces
- Materials and Structures
- Form and Colour for Interior Architecture and Design
- Making for Interior Architecture and Design
- Professional Development
- Tech 1 for Interior Architecture and Design
- Add+vantage module

**Year 2:**
- Spatial and Temporal Layers
- Exploring Fractured Spatial Realities
- Emotional Cartography of Space
- Materials, Methods and Construction
- Branding and Interior Architecture and Design
- Tech 2 for Interior Architecture and Design
- Professional Development
- Interior Architecture and Design Specialist Skills
- Add+vantage module

**Optional Placement Year:**
- Professional placement or study abroad (between Year 2 and Final Year)

**Year 3 (Final Year):**
- Interior Architecture and Design: Introduction to Final Major Project
- Design Enquiry for Interior Architecture and Design
- Professional Development 3
- Interior Architecture and Design: Final Major Project
- Add+vantage module

> **Note:** The course covers a range of projects dealing with small to large spaces — from branding to workspaces, health and education, retail, commercial and domestic settings. A user-centred and multidisciplinary approach spans retail, domestic, commercial, architectural, transport and marine interiors. Year 1 and 2 module names are sourced from UCAS and third-party data; some variation may exist from the official course specification.

**Assessment methods:** Artefacts, practical coursework, digital coursework, individual work, group work, live/video/poster presentations, projects, individual assignments, design portfolios, project briefs, critiques, written research.

## Scholarship Info

Coventry University offers international scholarships, merit-based awards, early payment discounts, and talent/creativity-based awards. Some awards are tied to strong portfolios or excellence in 2D/3D design or digital visualisation. Vice-Chancellor Scholarship: Fee waiver up to £5,000 for self-funded international students. Hardship funds and discretionary awards are also available.
