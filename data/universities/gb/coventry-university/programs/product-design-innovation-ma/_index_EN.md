## Admission Requirements

- An honours degree 2:2 or above in product design, industrial design or a related subject
- Portfolio required demonstrating design skills, creative practice and 3D design ability
- Interview may be required (virtual interview available)
- International: 60% in a 3-year undergraduate degree or 55% in a 4-year undergraduate degree
- Students from select Indian universities (Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai, Anna, Amity, Andhra Pradesh, Aligarh Muslim, Pune, Bangalore, JNTU) may be considered with 55% in a 3-year undergraduate degree

## Curriculum Summary

**Semester 1:**
- Collaboration in Practice (30 credits) — Compulsory
- Product Design Innovation Specialism 1 (30 credits) — Compulsory

**Semester 2:**
- Professional Context (20 credits) — Compulsory
- Product Design Innovation Specialism 2 (30 credits) — Compulsory
- Product Design Innovation Specialist Investigation (30 credits) — Compulsory
- Transnational Professional Development (10 credits) — Compulsory

**Semester 3 (Summer):**
- Product Design Innovation Final Project (30 credits) — Compulsory

The course prepares students for global employment as creative, innovative and strategic problem solvers. Students work collaboratively in an internationalised, cross-disciplinary and transcultural context. The focus is on employing research and critical and creative thinking skills to solve complex 3D design problems with imagination and insight. Research and critical and creative thinking are at the heart of all projects, with an emphasis on sustainable and ethical approaches. Students have the option to apply for a 'professional experience' opportunity designed to further develop skills and knowledge with the aim of maximising employability prospects.

**Assessment methods:** Design projects, coursework, presentations, portfolio submissions, written assignments, specialist investigation report, final major project, degree show.
