- A-level: BBC (to include at least one Art or Design subject)
- GCSE: Minimum five graded 9-4 (or equivalent), including English and Maths
- BTEC: DDM in an Art or Design subject
- IB Diploma: 27 points (to include Visual Arts or Design Technology at Higher Level)
- Portfolio submission required
- Interview may be required
- UCAS code: WHG3

## Curriculum Summary

**Year 1:**
- Form Design and Visual Communication
- Design Studio Methods and the Industries
- Design Context
- Design, Function and Users

**Year 2:**
- Design Innovation
- Design Application
- Design and the Professions
- Design Futures

**Placement Year (optional, between Year 2 and Final Year):**
- Work Placement Year or Study Abroad Year
- Upon completion, enhanced degree award title: "with Professional Enhancement" or "with International Enhancement"
- Non-credit bearing, no additional tuition fees

**Final Year:**
- Concept Creation (50 credits)
- Design Delivery (60 credits)

> **Note:** This is an award-winning course with a long history of achievement. The programme examines issues of autonomy, sustainability, ergonomics and the user experience and interaction in relation to exterior and interior design, surface and colour applications, lifestyle and future design solutions. Also available as MDes (4 years). Module names sourced from third-party sites as the official page is blocked by a cookie consent wall.

**Assessment methods:** Practical coursework, design projects, individual work, group work, presentations, portfolio submissions, reports.

## Scholarship Info

Vice-Chancellor Scholarship: Fee waiver up to £5,000 for self-funded international students. Undergraduate (3 years): £4,000 total (£2,000 in Year 1, £1,000 in each subsequent year). Not applicable for UK/EU citizens.
