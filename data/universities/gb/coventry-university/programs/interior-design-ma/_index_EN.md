## Admission Requirements

- An honours degree 2:2 or above in interior design, architecture, product design or architectural technology
- Portfolio: 10-15 examples or 3-4 major projects from the past two years, demonstrating creative problem-solving and skill integration, with project summaries, software used, and dates
- Interview may be required (virtual interview available)
- International: 60% in a 3-year undergraduate degree or 55% in a 4-year undergraduate degree

## Curriculum Summary

**Semester 1:**
- Collaboration in Practice (20 credits)
- Interior Design Specialism 1 (40 credits)
- Creative Futures and Social Change (20 credits)

**Semester 2:**
- Professional Context (20 credits)
- Interior Design Specialism 2 (40 credits)
- Collaboration in Practice continuation (20 credits)

The course is designed to develop advanced knowledge of the principles, applications and practices of professional interior architecture and design. It encourages original, creative responses to challenging interior design and spatial design problems. The course encompasses interior design within architectural and transport design to expand employment opportunities. Taught at the Delia Derbyshire building, the designated home for the creative industries, using a studio-centric working environment.

**Assessment methods:** Design projects, coursework, presentations, portfolio submissions, written assignments, major project.
