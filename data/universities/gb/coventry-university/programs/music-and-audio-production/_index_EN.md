## Overview

Music and Audio Production BSc (Hons) at Coventry University provides hands-on experience with professional-grade equipment and industry-informed projects. Students gain practical skills in music production, sound design, and audio engineering while developing a strong portfolio for careers in music, film, TV, radio, and interactive media.

The course features a common first year shared with Popular Music Performance and Songwriting BA (Hons), enabling cross-disciplinary collaboration before specialisation. Students have access to professional recording studios equipped with SSL, Audient, and Neve mixing consoles.

Past students have worked on projects ranging from designing innovative sound experiences for Jaguar Land Rover, contributing to game audio projects with Mojang Studios (creators of Minecraft), and collaborating with the BBC on major movies and TV shows.

Joint 1st for Learning Resources in Music across England HEIs (National Student Survey 2025).

## Modules

### Year 1 (Common First Year)

| Module | Credits |
|---|---|
| Live Music | 30 |
| Music Business | 30 |
| Music on the Move | 30 |
| Songwriting and Studio Production | 30 |

**Live Music** — Cultivates confident and effective musical performance across settings, emphasising technical mastery, artistic interpretation, stage presence, and audience connection. Integrates branding and marketing strategies within the live music landscape.

**Music Business** — Explores the contemporary music industry covering copyright, licensing, professional online presence, networking, marketing, and self-promotion strategies with an emphasis on entrepreneurial skills.

**Music on the Move** — Introduces fundamentals of popular music production at home, covering sound capture, basic mixing, advanced DAW operation, beat production, and sound design with hands-on learning.

**Songwriting and Studio Production** — Comprehensive exploration of songwriting and studio recording in a collaborative environment, focusing on creative processes and core production techniques.

### Year 2

| Module | Credits |
|---|---|
| Glitch, Beat, Score: Producing Sound and Music for Game | 30 |
| The Game Audio Lab | 30 |
| Sound Design: Shaping Your Musical Story | 30 |
| Producing, Mixing, Mastering, Repeat | 30 |

**Glitch, Beat, Score** — Focuses on creating sound effects, foley sounds, and original music for games using industry-standard software, crafting dynamic audio elements that enhance gameplay and storytelling.

**The Game Audio Lab** — Implements game audio and music into interactive environments, gaining practical experience with audio implementation techniques using industry-standard software.

**Sound Design: Shaping Your Musical Story** — Explores composing music and building soundscapes to enhance storytelling across media. Develops creative skills using MIDI and synthesis techniques alongside understanding commercial aspects including licensing.

**Producing, Mixing, Mastering, Repeat** — Advanced production techniques focusing on the complete production, mixing, and mastering workflow.

### Placement Year (Optional)

Students can opt for a sandwich year with either a UK work placement, international study, or international work placement to gain industry experience.

### Final Year (Year 3)

| Module | Credits |
|---|---|
| eXtended Realities in Sound | 30 |
| Audio Coding, AI and Interactive Music | 30 |
| Entrepreneurship and Innovation | 30 |
| The Complete Producer | 30 |

**eXtended Realities in Sound** — Explores XR (Extended Reality) and its impact on sound design and music creation, covering immersive audio techniques, spatial sound design, and the role of sound in VR/AR, including AI applications.

**Audio Coding, AI and Interactive Music** — Examines the intersection of audio coding, AI, and interactive music, learning how AI transforms music production, coding techniques for audio applications and games, and developing interactive music experiences.

**Entrepreneurship and Innovation** — Explores entrepreneurship in music and creative industries, focusing on innovative business models, entrepreneurial skills, and overcoming challenges in creative ventures.

**The Complete Producer** — A capstone module where students apply their skills to develop and execute a comprehensive music or audio project, culminating in a tangible release such as a music distribution platform release, game launch, or equivalent.

## Teaching & Assessment

**Teaching Methods:** Lectures, seminars, tutorials, presentations, group projects, workshops, and practical studio sessions.

**Teaching Hours:** Approximately 12–15 contact hours per week in Year 1, reducing in subsequent years as independent study increases.

**Assessment Methods:** Artefacts, practical coursework, digital coursework, individual work, group work, live presentations, poster presentations, and individual assignments.
