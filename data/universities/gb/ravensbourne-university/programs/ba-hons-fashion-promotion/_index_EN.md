curriculum_summary: 'Year 1 (Level 4): Introduces core methodologies and skills underpinning
  contemporary concepts and the fundamental principles of fashion promotion, communication,
  media production and content creation. Students explore the creative processes and
  techniques adopted by the fashion industry, and how the fashion media creative can
  begin to apply this knowledge in practice. Working on engaging and challenging project
  briefs, students experiment with and develop visual communication and practice-based
  research skills.


  Year 2 (Level 5): Focuses on the development of the student''s personal professional
  practice and skills for industry. An exploration of creative strategies, media design
  and world building. Using the skills and knowledge developed in Year 1, with a focus
  on applying contemporary methodologies to form ambitious and challenging development
  of ideas. Emphasis on narrative and refinement of fashion media production skills,
  developing unique signature style and skills for industry as well as preparing students
  for a placement or future employment. Work-based learning opportunity available.


  Year 3 (Level 6): Students further their knowledge, refine skills needed to develop
  contemporary content creation, showcase their personal professional practice, develop
  their industry portfolio, and complete a final major project. The self-directed
  project demonstrates transdisciplinary knowledge and skills, evidencing comprehensive
  understanding and critical awareness of fashion media production, promotion, and
  visual communication practices, creating work that challenges and provides creative
  solutions tailored for chosen audiences or fashion brands.
