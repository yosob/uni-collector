## Overview

The MArch Architecture course at Ravensbourne University London is designed to equip emerging architects with the advanced skills required to navigate and shape complex, contemporary practice utilising leadership skills and emerging technologies. It merges rigorous design exploration and resolution, critical research, and professional principles to prepare graduates to address the most pressing social, environmental, and technological challenges facing the built environment.

The programme is structured as a RIBA Part II equivalent, enabling progression toward professional practice experience and registration as an architect in the UK. Students engage with a rich and varied curriculum spanning design, environmental performance, technology, research methods, leadership, and ethical and sustainable practice.

Practical application of design skills is central to the course, with studio-based projects, industry live briefs, and case studies shaping the curriculum. The agendas explored across modules reflect current and nascent priorities within the built environment and a global discourse. At least 50% of modules are focused on design.

The course cultivates a collaborative and interdisciplinary learning environment, drawing on academic expertise and industry engagement to nurture reflective, responsible, and innovative emerging professionals. It blends conceptual thinking with technical resolution and business acumen.

## Course Structure

### Year 1

- **Design 1: Adapt** — Design module addressing contextual, social and environmental demands
- **Regenerate** — Focus on ecological resilience and natural environments
- **Critical Context** — Design research methodologies and critical thinking
- **Ethics and Business** — Practice management, leadership, and ethical practice

### Year 2

- **Design 2: Collaborate** — Collaborative design with stakeholders
- **Final Major Practice Research** — Research-led practice project
- **Design 3: Appraise** — Advanced design resolution and performance
- **Performance** — Building modelling and performance techniques
- **Future Practice** — The architect's role in the built environment ecosystem
- **Final Masters Project** — Comprehensive design thesis

### Assessment

50% of the course is delivered through design projects, requiring portfolio submissions. Other assessment methods include reports, presentations, case studies, vivas, and essays. A large proportion of assessments are practical (presentation, performance viva) and coursework-based (portfolio, report, case study, essay, peer and self-assessment).

## Entry Requirements

### UK Students

- A lower second-class honours degree in architecture, OR
- An equivalent EU/international qualification, OR
- A professional qualification recognised as equivalent to an honours degree
- Normally at least one year of relevant professional experience

Applicants applying directly from an undergraduate degree without professional practice experience must demonstrate a strong portfolio of academic work and professional acumen mapped to professional body competences.

### International Students

Contact the university for international qualification equivalency and English language requirements.

## Career Prospects

Graduates will typically progress into careers blending architectural and spatial design skills, technical expertise, leadership, and strategic decision-making across the built environment:

- Architectural Designer
- Architectural Trainee
- Urban Design Consultant
- Sustainability or Environmental Design Specialist
- BIM or Digital Design Coordinator
- Design Manager
- Development and Planning Consultant
- Wider Built Environment Specialist Roles
