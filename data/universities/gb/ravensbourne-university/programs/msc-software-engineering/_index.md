## Overview

The MSc Software Engineering programme at Ravensbourne University London uniquely blends advanced software development practices with creative technology and innovation. From financial services to manufacturing, and from gaming to virtual reality, industries worldwide depend on innovative software solutions. This programme encapsulates Ravensbourne's commitment to blending technical expertise with creativity, preparing students to design software solutions across diverse sectors.

The course combines advanced software development with creative technology and innovation, preparing students to design software solutions across industries like finance, gaming, and virtual reality. Ravensbourne's emphasis on industry-led practical activities and links with creative sectors sets this programme apart.

Students will develop not only technical expertise but also problem-solving and teamwork skills. With advanced technology training and a focus on design thinking, graduates will be equipped to shape the future of software engineering.

Teaching sessions take place Monday to Friday between 9am and 9pm, and may include some evening teaching.

## Why Study This Degree?

1. Master advanced software engineering practices, including architectural design, robust testing methodologies and secure coding principles, aligned with industry demands
2. Integrate emerging technologies and frameworks to design and develop innovative, cutting-edge software solutions, particularly within creative contexts
3. Develop independent analytical and problem-solving capabilities through engagement with complex, real-world software engineering projects
4. Lead software projects using agile methodologies and advanced technologies, including artificial intelligence and machine learning
5. Gain the professional skills and adaptability required to drive progress and thrive in a rapidly evolving software engineering landscape

## Course Structure

### Key Study Topics

- **Advanced software development practice** — Core software engineering principles and best practices
- **Design thinking in a creative context** — Applying creative problem-solving to software design
- **Object-oriented design and development methodologies** — OOP principles, patterns and frameworks
- **Artificial intelligence and machine learning** — AI/ML techniques to solve real-world problems
- **Business strategy in a digital setting** — Digital transformation and strategic thinking
- **Creative and research-based thinking** — Innovation and research methodology

## Entry Requirements

### UK Students

- A lower second-class honours degree (2:2) or higher in a relevant subject (at least 50% of modules related to computer science), OR
- An equivalent professional qualification in a related subject
- Submission of CV/Resume required
- Applicants without professional practice experience must demonstrate good knowledge of the chosen subject area

### International Students

- Equivalent international qualifications accepted — check the university's country-specific entry requirements
- Submission of CV/Resume required
- English language requirement: IELTS 6.0 overall (minimum 5.5 in each sub-test) or CEFR Level B2 in each component
- Contact postgrad@rave.ac.uk for more information

### Selection Criteria

The university welcomes applicants from a wide range of backgrounds and experience. Selection considers enthusiasm and interest in the subject area, how you manage the creative process, previous study and your professional skill-set. It is important that applicants can demonstrate an aptitude for learning and continued professional development through a research-led approach.
