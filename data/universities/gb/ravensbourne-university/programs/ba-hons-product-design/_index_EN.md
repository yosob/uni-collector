curriculum_summary: 'Year 1 (Level 4): Students will learn key skills in 2D and 3D
  design, user research, the iterative design process, and current design software.
  They will develop skills in sketching, model making, and prototyping while exploring
  fundamental product design principles including ergonomics, aesthetics, and usability.
  Projects focus on understanding user needs and translating ideas into tangible design
  outcomes.

  Year 2 (Level 5): Students develop their own approach to design, deepening skills
  in concept generation, material specification, and component design for manufacture.
  They will begin developing their own applied design research methodology. Collaborative
  briefs and live industry projects provide real-world experience. Greater emphasis
  on sustainability, inclusive design, and the application of emerging technologies.

  Year 3 (Level 6): Students bring together all their learning in a final major negotiated
  project, producing a detailed design ready for production. The year culminates in
  a comprehensive portfolio and public exhibition showcasing industry-ready work.
  Professional practice preparation covers intellectual property, design ethics, and
  career development strategies.
