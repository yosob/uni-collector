## Overview

The MBA Fashion: Business and Entrepreneurship at Ravensbourne University London is uniquely designed for all those interested in pursuing a career within the fashion industry. This course puts strategy and innovation at the forefront of fashion business, providing advanced knowledge in leadership, business strategy and innovation within fashion contexts.

You will study among the UK's finest creatives in a design-led, technological and business environment, and participate in a collaborative working environment. During the course, you will develop your leadership and management skills and gain experience through simulated tasks which emulate real-world practice.

The programme offers a learning-by-doing approach to fashion leadership through simulated tasks reflecting real-world practice. You will enhance employability by expanding professional networks and engaging with a tech-literate, creative community.

## Key Study Topics

- **Fashion business and global environment** — Understanding the global landscape of fashion and the forces shaping it
- **Research methods and practice** — Fundamental methodologies underpinning academic and professional practice
- **Strategic management and leadership** — Developing entrepreneurial skills through strategy-focused topics
- **Finance and risk management** — Financial decision-making in the fashion business context
- **Digital acceleration and data for fashion** — Exploring how digital innovation is supported by data
- **Entrepreneurship and brand innovation** — Building new ventures and driving brand innovation in fashion

## Why Study This Degree?

1. Develop advanced skills in business and entrepreneurship with a specific focus on the global fashion sector
2. Gain master's level expertise in leadership, business strategy and innovation within fashion contexts
3. Build the confidence and capability to excel in the competitive fashion business environment
4. Experience a learning-by-doing approach to fashion leadership through simulated tasks reflecting real-world practice
5. Enhance employability by expanding professional networks and engaging with a tech-literate, creative community

## Course Structure

### Semester 1

Through semester 1, you will be exposed to the global landscape of fashion and learn the research methods which will underpin your learning. Topics focusing on strategy and leadership will inform your practice and help to develop your entrepreneurial skills.

**Modules:**
- The Fashion Business and Global Fashion Business Environment
- Research Methods and Practice
- Strategic Management and Leadership

### Semester 2

You will explore brand innovation as well as financial management and will have the opportunity to work on a project exploring how digital acceleration is supported by data.

**Modules:**
- Finance and Risk Management
- Digital Acceleration and Data for Fashion
- Entrepreneurship and Brand Innovation

### Semester 3

You will work to resolve a long-standing business research problem and develop a final project which aims to support existing research findings, as well as your own. This can include a commissioned industry report or an entrepreneurial business concept, which includes a business plan and pitch proposing a new business start-up concept.

**Module:**
- Final Fashion Business Project

## Entry Requirements

### UK Students

- A lower second-class honours degree (2:2) or higher in a relevant subject, OR
- An equivalent professional qualification in a related subject
- Submission of CV/Resume required
- Applicants without professional practice experience must demonstrate good knowledge of the chosen subject area

### International Students

- Equivalent international qualifications accepted — check the university's country-specific entry requirements
- Submission of CV/Resume required
- English language requirement: IELTS 6.0 overall (minimum 5.5 in each sub-test) or CEFR Level B2 in each component
- Contact postgrad@rave.ac.uk for more information

### Selection Criteria

We welcome suitably-qualified applicants who are able to demonstrate a good level of skills in areas associated with visual communication. This may include but is not limited to: graphic design, branding, art direction, advertising, motion graphics, information design, data visualisation, UX/UI design and AR/VR experience design.
