curriculum_summary: 'Year 1 (Level 4): Introduces the fundamentals of illustration
  practice — exploring drawing and image-making skills from a broad methodology including
  observation and interpretation, traditional image-making techniques and screen-based
  digital media. Modules include: Introduction to Illustration Practice (20 credits),
  Introduction to Visual Culture (20 credits), Narrative Concepts and Communication
  (20 credits), Visual Culture Futures (20 credits), and Professional Life Practice
  (20 credits × 2). Builds core competencies in visual storytelling and illustration
  expression, exploring illustration''s applications across graphic design, publishing,
  advertising and more.


  Year 2 (Level 5): Deepens specialisms through self-authorship, expanded practice
  and cross-disciplinary collaboration. Modules include: Self-Authorship and Publishing
  (20 credits), The Expanded Practice (40 credits), Collaborate (20 credits), Professional
  Life Practice (20 credits) and Work Based Learning (20 credits). Develops personal
  visual voice and expands the boundaries of illustration practice, exploring editorial
  illustration, product packaging, animation and character design through industry
  partnerships and live briefs.


  Year 3 (Level 6): Independent project and career preparation — establishing a personal
  creative identity through competitions and live briefs (20 credits), self-directed
  major projects (40 credits), self-promotion and professional practice (40 credits),
  and professional life practice (20 credits). Students graduate with an online portfolio
  of both self-directed and project-led personal and professional work that emerges
  from developing their own distinctive visual voice.
