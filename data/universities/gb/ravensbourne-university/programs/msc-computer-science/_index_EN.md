## Overview

The MSc Computer Science programme at Ravensbourne University London offers modules in artificial intelligence, cyber security, cloud computing, Internet of Things and leading-edge Web3 technologies such as blockchain. The curriculum takes a systems architecture approach, encouraging students to work both within and across these technologies.

The programme is part of Ravensbourne's suite of MSc programmes, which capitalises on the university's established international reputation for innovation at the intersection of design and digital media. Students are empowered to draw on and learn from this rich history of innovative design thinking and apply it to the creation of new technologies.

The course develops innovation-focused, socially responsible and entrepreneurial skills through a learning-by-doing approach to computer science education. Students enhance their employability by expanding their professional network and engaging with a tech-literate, creative community.

Teaching sessions take place Monday to Friday between 9am and 9pm, and may include some evening teaching.

## Key Study Topics

- **Systems architecture** — Designing and managing complex computing systems
- **Artificial intelligence foundations** — Core AI concepts and applications
- **Cyber security** — Security principles, threat management and protection strategies
- **Innovation and responsibility** — Ethical and responsible innovation in technology
- **Cloud computing** — Cloud infrastructure, services and deployment models
- **Business development** — Entrepreneurial and commercial skills for tech professionals
- **Web3** — Blockchain, decentralised technologies and the next-generation web
- **Internet of Things** — Connected devices, sensor networks and smart systems
- **Dissertation project** — Independent research project on a chosen topic

## Why Study This Degree?

1. Develop master's-level expertise in computer science, including systems architecture, artificial intelligence and cyber security
2. Build advanced skills in cloud computing and the Internet of Things to meet current and emerging industry demands
3. Explore cutting-edge technologies shaping the next iteration of the web and digital ecosystems
4. Develop innovation-focused, socially responsible and entrepreneurial skills through a learning-by-doing approach
5. Enhance employability by expanding your professional network and engaging with a tech-literate, creative community

## Entry Requirements

### UK Students

- A lower second-class honours degree (2:2) or higher in a relevant subject (at least 50% of modules related to computer science), OR
- An equivalent professional qualification in a related subject
- Submission of CV/Resume required
- Applicants without professional practice experience must demonstrate good knowledge of the chosen subject area

### International Students

- Equivalent international qualifications accepted — check the university's country-specific entry requirements
- Submission of CV/Resume required
- English language requirement: IELTS 6.0 overall (minimum 5.5 in each sub-test) or CEFR Level B2 in each component
- Contact postgrad@rave.ac.uk for more information

### Selection Criteria

The university welcomes applicants from a wide range of backgrounds and experience. Selection considers enthusiasm and interest in the subject area, creative process management, previous study and professional skill-set.
