## Course Overview

MA Screenwriting is a practice-led, one-year full-time master's programme at Ravensbourne University London that blends craft, production insight, and cutting-edge tools to develop bold, market-ready scripts for film and high-end TV. The course culminates in a major project pitched to industry professionals.

Designed with flexibility in mind, the course is delivered one evening a week in London and one evening online, minimising time on campus while maximising time for focused writing — ideal for working professionals.

## Why Study This Degree?

1. Develop the craft, strategy and industry awareness needed to write for today's global screen landscape
2. Learn to use cutting-edge digital tools shaping modern screen storytelling
3. Work on briefs that mirror real development and production challenges
4. Build meaningful connections with writers, producers and industry partners
5. Enjoy the hybrid learning experience — including evening classes, online sessions and a Writer's Retreat

Students explore both the craft and industry of storytelling in a fast-evolving global market. They learn to shape compelling characters, build emotionally resonant narratives, and communicate ideas with clarity and confidence.

The course blends classical theory — from Aristotle to John Yorke — with contemporary practice, embracing adaptation, collaboration, and emerging forms. Students also experiment with AI-assisted development, table reads, previsualisation, and editing, understanding the screenplay as a blueprint for production.

## Key Study Topics

- Communicating story vision clearly to align creative and production teams
- Key screenwriting models, narrative theory and interdisciplinary storytelling practice
- Creative leadership, collaboration and learning within writers' rooms and production environments
- Ethical, research-informed storytelling and professional industry standards
- Global storytelling strategies shaped by responsibility, representation and audience awareness
- Professional screenwriting skills using modern digital and AI-assisted tools
- Using technology to develop, test and communicate story ideas
- Sharing screen stories across interactive and digital development platforms

## Entry Requirements

**Home applicants**: A higher second-class honours degree (2:1) or equivalent non-UK qualification in a relevant subject, or an equivalent professional qualification in a related field. CV/Resume required. Applicants directly from an undergraduate degree without professional experience must demonstrate a strong understanding of the chosen subject area.

**International applicants**: International qualifications are accepted — check country-specific entry requirements. Academic qualifications and CV/Resume must be submitted.

**English language**: IELTS 6.0 overall (minimum 5.5 in each sub-test) or CEFR Level B2 in each component.

**Selection criteria**: The university welcomes applicants from a wide range of backgrounds. Enthusiasm and interest in the subject area, creative process management, previous study and professional skill-set are all considered.
