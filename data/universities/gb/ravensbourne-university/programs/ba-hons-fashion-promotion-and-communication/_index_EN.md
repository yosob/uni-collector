curriculum_summary: 'Year 1 (Level 4): Core modules include Key Concepts in Fashion
  Media, Culture and History; Promotion and Communication Design 1; Exploring Fashion
  Marketing and Advertising; Promotion and Communication Design 2; Professional Life
  Practice. Students learn the core methodologies and skills underpinning contemporary
  fashion promotion, communication, media production and content creation. They explore
  the creative processes and techniques adopted by the fashion industry and develop
  visual communication and practice-based research skills through engaging and challenging
  project briefs.


  Year 2 (Level 5): Core modules include Critical Discourse in Fashion Promotion and
  Communication; Digital Production, Interaction Design and Visual Communication in
  Fashion; Fashion Narrative, Interfaces and Creation; Professional Life Practice:
  Applying your Practice; Work-based learning. Focuses on developing personal professional
  practice and skills for industry, deepening creative strategies, media design and
  world building. Emphasis on narrative and refinement of fashion media production
  skills, developing unique signature style and preparing students for placement or
  future employment. Fully accredited work placement included.


  Year 3 (Level 6): Core modules include Concept Development and Experimentation;
  Final Major Project (Body of Work); Fashion Media Curation, Presentation and Public
  Installation; Professional Life Practice: Situating your Practice; Dissertation.
  Students further their knowledge, refine skills for contemporary content creation,
  and complete a self-directed final major project from inception to realisation.
  They participate in national and international competitions, demonstrate transdisciplinary
  knowledge and critical awareness of fashion media production, promotion and visual
  communication practices, and build a well-rounded professional portfolio.
