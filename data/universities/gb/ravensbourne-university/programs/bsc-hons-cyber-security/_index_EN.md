## Overview

BSc (Hons) Cyber Security at Ravensbourne University London is a three-year programme that equips students with the technical skills and knowledge to protect digital systems, networks, and data from cyber threats. Set within Ravensbourne's creative and interdisciplinary environment, the course combines core computing fundamentals with specialist security modules including ethical hacking, digital forensics, secure programming, and cloud security.

Students develop hands-on skills through workshops, collaborative projects, and a Work-Based Learning module in Year 2 that provides real industry experience. The programme culminates in a Final Project and the creation of an outward-facing professional portfolio.

## Course Structure

### Year 1 (Level 4)

Students build foundational skills in computing and security through a combination of workshops, lectures, seminars, and self-directed study. The emphasis is on establishing professional currency and driving enterprise and employability from the start.

**Modules:**
- Software Design and Development
- Computer Networks & Technology
- Professional Life Practice — Developing your Practice
- Web Design and Databases
- Cyber Security Principles
- Professional Life Practice — Exploring your Practice

### Year 2 (Level 5)

Skills acquired in Year 1 are developed further through workshops, lectures, seminars, group exercises, self-directed study, and individual or group tutorials. Students apply their knowledge in collaborative scenarios through Professional Life Practice and Work-Based Learning modules, offering industry-aligned opportunities.

In Semester 2, students gain industry experience through the Work-Based Learning module, enhancing employability and entrepreneurship skills.

**Modules:**
- Operating Systems and Cloud
- Computer Vision & AI
- Ethical Hacking
- Professional Life Practice — Applying your Practice
- Work-Based Learning

### Year 3 (Level 6)

Skills from Years 1 and 2 are developed and perfected through lectures, seminars, workshops, self-directed study, and individual tutorials. Students take on professional attitudes to time and project management. Visiting lecturers deliver sessions related to their area of work, and students develop an outward-facing portfolio to aid graduate progression.

**Modules (subject to change):**
- Advanced Secure Programming
- Professional Life Practice — Situating your Practice
- Final Project
- Risk and Project Management

## Entry Requirements

### BSc (Hons) Cyber Security (3-year)

- **UCAS tariff:** 112 points
- GCSE Grade 4 or above in English and Maths
- Level 3 STEM subject required
- Accepted qualifications:
  - Three A Levels at grade BBC or above
  - BTEC Extended Diploma at grade DMM
  - UAL Level 3 Extended Diploma
  - T Level (pass or above)
  - Access to Higher Education Diploma
  - International Baccalaureate at grade 24 or above
  - Other/Mixed qualifications equivalent to 112 UCAS Tariff points

### BSc (Hons) Cyber Security with Integrated Foundation Year (4-year)

- **UCAS tariff:** 64 points
- GCSE Grade 4 or above in English and Maths
- Accepted qualifications:
  - Two A Levels at grade CC or above
  - BTEC Extended Diploma at grade MPP
  - Other/Mixed qualifications equivalent to 64 UCAS Tariff points

**No interview or portfolio requirements** for this course.
