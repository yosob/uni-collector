## Course Overview

MA Illustration is a one-year full-time master's programme at Ravensbourne University London that helps students engagingly convey complex information to effectively tell stories. Students experiment with an array of storytelling and narrative methods, mastering the roles of illustration and how to take advantage of immersive technologies.

The programme scored 91% student satisfaction for creativity and design, and 93% satisfaction overall in the Postgraduate Taught Experience Survey 2020.

**Note: This course is not available for 2026 entry.**

## Why Study This Degree?

1. Embrace the natural storyteller in you
2. Explore cutting-edge storytelling and narrative methods
3. Augment your understanding of immersive illustration technologies
4. Supercharge your illustration business
5. Tap into exceptional industry connections

Throughout the course, students explore the roles of illustration today and its wide-ranging professional applications. They experiment across narrative and storytelling methods, enabling them to present and reposition information in a strategic, accessible and engaging fashion. Students examine complex knowledge, data and information and bring it to life with empathy, telling stories that need to be told.

By taking on consultancy and organisational roles, as well as developing their own illustration practice, this master's programme is designed to make students truly industry ready.

## Key Study Topics

- How creative narrative and storytelling methods can be used to make complex information accessible
- Expanded models of professional illustration, including collaborative, interdisciplinary, facilitation and commercial practices
- The use of illustration to foster community, facilitate dialogue, witness and document human experience and knowledge
- Illustration as a distinctive empathetic, intersubjective communication discipline operating with specific behaviours, mechanisms and principles
- The expansive application of illustration to communicate and transfer knowledge across disciplines and professional fields, with an emphasis on social practice
- How to develop your creative practice with exposure to different methods of traditional and digital illustration and physical production
- Storytelling techniques using immersive technologies to engage mass audiences
- How narratives can be told across media and platforms in linear, nonlinear, interactive, participatory and immersive ways

## Entry Requirements

**Home applicants**: A lower second-class honours degree (2:2) or equivalent non-UK qualification in a relevant subject, or an equivalent professional qualification in a related field. CV/Resume required. Applicants without professional experience must demonstrate a good knowledge of the chosen subject area.

**International applicants**: Must meet country-specific entry requirements and submit academic qualifications along with CV/Resume.

**English language**: IELTS 6.0 overall (minimum 5.5 in each sub-test) or CEFR Level B2 in each component.

**Selection criteria**: Applicants from a wide range of backgrounds and experience are welcome, including those who may not possess formal academic qualifications. Demonstrated enthusiasm for the subject area, aptitude for learning and continued professional development through a research-led approach are valued.

**Interview and portfolio requirements**: Applicants should prepare their portfolio, CV, and personal statement for the academic interview. Knowledge of why they want to study the course, how it can benefit their future, research into a current industry that inspired them, and their aims for research in both theory and practice are expected.
