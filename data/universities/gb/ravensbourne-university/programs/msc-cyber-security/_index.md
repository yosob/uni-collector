## Overview

The MSc Cyber Security programme at Ravensbourne University London equips students with technical expertise in cyber security and secure system design. Through modules such as systems architecture and cyber incident management, students develop the skills to protect digital environments and respond effectively to security challenges.

The programme features a unique open-source intelligence module, blending cyber security, forensics, and intelligence. Additionally, business development, innovation, and responsibility modules explore how to commercialise and apply your skills.

Students will enhance their employability by expanding professional networks and engaging with a tech-literate, creative community. The learning-by-doing approach to cyber security education develops innovation-focused, socially responsible and entrepreneurial skills.

Teaching sessions take place Monday to Friday between 9am and 9pm, and may include some evening teaching.

## Why Study This Degree?

1. Develop master's-level expertise in cyber security, including systems architecture, network security and incident response
2. Build advanced skills in computer forensics and open-source intelligence to address contemporary cyber threats
3. Understand current challenges facing cyber security professionals and develop capabilities in responsible innovation
4. Gain business and entrepreneurial skills through a learning-by-doing approach to cyber security education
5. Enhance employability by expanding professional networks and engaging with a tech-literate, creative community

## Course Structure

### Key Study Topics

- **Cyber security** — Core principles of protecting digital environments and secure system design
- **Network security** — Securing network infrastructure, protocols and communications
- **Cyber incident management** — Responding to and managing security breaches and incidents
- **Cyber forensics** — Digital forensic investigation techniques and evidence handling
- **Open source intelligence (OSINT)** — Gathering and analysing intelligence from open sources
- **Innovation and responsibility** — Ethical and responsible innovation in cyber security
- **Business development** — Entrepreneurial and commercial skills for security professionals
- **Dissertation** — Independent research project on a chosen cyber security topic

## Entry Requirements

### UK Students

- A lower second-class honours degree (2:2) or higher in a relevant subject (at least 50% of modules related to computer science), OR
- An equivalent professional qualification in a related subject
- Submission of CV/Resume required
- Applicants without professional practice experience must demonstrate good knowledge of the chosen subject area

### International Students

- Equivalent international qualifications accepted — check the university's country-specific entry requirements
- Submission of CV/Resume required
- English language requirement: IELTS 6.0 overall (minimum 5.5 in each sub-test) or CEFR Level B2 in each component
- Contact postgrad@rave.ac.uk for more information

### Selection Criteria

The university welcomes applicants from a wide range of backgrounds and experience. Selection considers enthusiasm and interest in the subject area, how you manage the creative process, previous study and your professional skill-set. It is important that applicants can demonstrate an aptitude for learning and continued professional development through a research-led approach.
