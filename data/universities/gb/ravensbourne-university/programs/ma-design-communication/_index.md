## Course Overview

MA Design Communication is a one-year full-time postgraduate programme at Ravensbourne University London. The course enables students to construct a critical practice to analyse, experiment, and convey messages, meaning, and experiences through a diverse range of media. This interdisciplinary programme supports students in producing a body of work tailored to their interests and motivations, positioning themselves in the creative and cultural industries.

> ⚠️ **Note**: This course is **not available for 2026 entry**. The university advises prospective students to browse related postgraduate courses.

## Why Study This Degree?

1. Engage with emerging technologies and interdisciplinary methodologies to inform contemporary design practice
2. Establish an experimental, critical, and sustainable design approach informed by cultural, historical, and political contexts
3. Develop design projects grounded in community engagement, collaboration, and meaningful audience interaction
4. Analyse complex information and communicate insights effectively through advanced visual communication techniques
5. Collaborate with industry professionals to gain practical experience and build valuable professional networks

## Key Study Topics

- Experiment across design systems and communication design strategies, focusing on user-centred design thinking and production
- Explore information design systems, hierarchies, networks, and spaces of experiences (both physical and digital)
- Create powerful ideas and utilise the best channels and tools to reach markets and target audiences effectively
- Explore advanced professional practice in contemporary communication design: visual identity, publishing, design authorship, marketing communications, digital marketing, and social engagement
- Innovative communication solutions based on design thinking, systems thinking, and business strategy
- Specialist design skills and emerging technologies within digital media, creative industries, and commercial and not-for-profit sectors

## Entry Requirements

**Home**: A lower second-class honours degree (2:2) or equivalent non-UK qualification in a relevant subject, or an equivalent professional qualification in a related field. CV/Resume required.

**International**: Must meet country-specific entry requirements. Academic qualifications and CV/Resume required.

**English language**: IELTS 6.0 overall (minimum 5.5 in each sub-test) or CEFR Level B2 in each component.

**Selection criteria**: Applicants should demonstrate skills associated with design communication. The university considers enthusiasm, creative process management, previous study, and professional skill-set holistically.

## Schedule

Classes are Monday to Friday, between 9:00am and 9:00pm, with some evening sessions included.

## Learning Outcomes

Upon completing the course, students will have:

- Practical design thinking skills to turn creative ideas into engaging assets
- Business acumen to inspire audiences across all sectors
- Confidence to drive change and influence decision-making at senior levels
