## Course Overview

MA/MFA Digital Photography at Ravensbourne University London is a postgraduate programme that explores contemporary and commercial photographic practice as both a practical skills acquisition platform and a rigorous academic subject area. The course examines image making as a modern platform for communication and sets the agenda for photography 'now' and 'next' in terms of its relationship to technology and the dissemination and distribution of the digital image.

The course is based on a strategy of **Print, Screen and Experiential** practice, integrating lens-based practices into contemporary aspects of the medium such as **computational photography, artificial intelligence, machine learning, and augmented reality**. It is available as both MA (3 semesters FT) and MFA (4 semesters FT) pathways.

The course is recognised by the **Association of Photographers (AOP)** — the leading UK professional sector body — and works closely with industry partners such as **Capture One Software**.

## Why Study This Degree?

1. Engage with contemporary and commercial photographic practice at an advanced level
2. Explore the intersection of photography with emerging technologies including AI, machine learning, and augmented reality
3. Benefit from industry partnerships with the AOP and Capture One Software for professional currency and networking
4. Develop a resolved body of work aligned with your career aspirations in the creative industries
5. Access to specialist facilities including the CEET Lab, Creative Lab, Prototyping Workshop, Atelier, and comprehensive Kit Store
6. Evening delivery option available, making it attractive for working professionals

## Course Structure

**Semester 1** — Introduces a series of approaches and principles of image making alongside appropriate research methods. Reviews previous work and recontextualises it into a more appropriate framework. Covers camerawork, lens choices, and advanced professional workflow based on a range of software. Includes a diagnostic aspect for reviewing and enhancing personal skill sets.

**Semester 2** — Develops practice and networking within a client-led environment. Defines a more applied strategy to practice with in-depth use of appropriate workflows. Develops understanding of camera systems and software defined by industry partners and mentors, including mobile photography, computational photography, and access to university facilities such as the CEET Lab.

**Semester 3 (MA final / MFA continuing)** — Synthesises knowledge and learning into a final project: a resolved body of work embodying personal career aspirations and position within contemporary image making. Utilises the full photographic facilities, Creative Lab, CEET Lab, Prototyping, Atelier, and Kit Store.

**Semester 4 (MFA only)** — Additional semester for MFA students to extend their final project with greater depth and ambition.

The course also encourages **interdisciplinary and collaborative working** with other courses at Ravensbourne, recognising that all aspects of the creative industries rely heavily on photographic images, visual assets, and collaboration.

## Entry Requirements

**Home**: A lower second-class honours degree (2:2) or equivalent non-UK qualification in a relevant subject, or an equivalent professional qualification in a related field. CV/Resume required.

**International**: Must meet country-specific entry requirements. Academic qualifications and CV/Resume required.

**English language**: IELTS 6.0 overall (minimum 5.5 in each sub-test) or CEFR Level B2 in each component.

**Portfolio**: Required. Applicants should demonstrate skills associated with digital photography. The university considers enthusiasm, creative process management, previous study, and professional skill-set holistically. 15–20 images recommended.
