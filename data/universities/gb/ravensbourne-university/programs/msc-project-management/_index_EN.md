## Course Overview

MSc Project Management is a one-year full-time master's programme at Ravensbourne University London, designed to equip students with the strategic, technical, and practical skills required to lead complex projects across a variety of industries, including technology, construction, creative sectors, and public services. The course integrates contemporary project management theory with real-world practice, fostering critical thinking, data-driven decision-making, and leadership capabilities.

The programme comprises six core taught modules and a final dissertation totalling 180 credits. Students will gain expertise in key areas such as project planning, procurement, sustainability, operations, digital transformation, and entrepreneurship. The course supports skill development in stakeholder management, governance, contract negotiation, risk mitigation, and the application of project data analytics using industry tools.

## Why Study This Degree?

1. Gain advanced knowledge and practical competencies in project strategy, governance, planning, and execution, enabling you to manage complex projects across diverse sectors and environments
2. Develop critical, reflective, and analytical thinking skills supporting evidence-based decision-making, ethical leadership, and risk awareness
3. Build interdisciplinary skills through collaborative problem-solving and engagement with real-world challenges, preparing you to lead, innovate, and adapt in a rapidly evolving project landscape
4. Curriculum aligned with the APM Body of Knowledge, integrating digital competencies, and building capability in communication, stakeholder engagement, and sustainable practice
5. Study at Ravensbourne's North Greenwich campus, in close proximity to London's key business, design, and innovation districts

## Course Structure

The programme is delivered across three trimesters.

### Trimester 1

- **PRM25701** — Evidence Based Project Management Decisions (20 credits)
- **PRM25704** — Digital Innovation and Sustainability in Projects (20 credits)

### Trimester 2

- **PRM25702** — Contemporary Project Management Strategy (20 credits)
- **PRM25703** — Principles of Project Management (20 credits)
- **PRM25705** — Project Innovation and Entrepreneurship (20 credits)
- **PRM25706** — Advanced Project Operations, Procurement and Governance (20 credits)

### Trimester 3

- **BUS25707** — Final Project / Dissertation (60 credits)

## Entry Requirements

**Academic**: A lower second-class honours degree (2:2) or equivalent non-UK qualification in a relevant subject, or an equivalent professional qualification in a related field.

**English language**: IELTS 6.0 overall (minimum 5.5 in each sub-test) or CEFR Level B2 in each component. Students requiring a Student Visa must meet this requirement.

**Other**: Applicants applying directly from an undergraduate degree course without experience or professional practice must demonstrate a good knowledge of the chosen subject area.

**Accreditation of Prior Learning**: Applications are welcomed from those who may not possess formal entry qualifications. Such applicants should demonstrate sufficient aptitude and potential to complete the course successfully and will be assessed at interview.

## Career Prospects

Graduates will be well-positioned to pursue roles such as:
- Project Manager
- Operations Manager
- Procurement and Contracts Specialist
- Programme Coordinator
- Business Transformation Consultant
- Project Analyst
- PMO Lead
- Sustainability and Innovation Advisor

The course also provides a strong foundation for those seeking professional accreditation (e.g., APM, PRINCE2) or aiming to develop careers in project leadership, consultancy, or entrepreneurship.

## Professional Accreditation

The programme is aligned with the APM Body of Knowledge and is pursuing accreditation from the Association for Project Management (APM) and the Chartered Management Institute (CMI) Level 7 qualification.

## Teaching Methods

The course employs a range of inclusive and interactive teaching methods, including case-based learning, group projects, guest lectures, simulations, and live business briefs. Students have access to expert supervision during their capstone dissertation project, providing the opportunity to explore a real-world project challenge in depth.

## Interim Awards

Students who exit the course without completing it may be entitled to:
- Postgraduate Certificate (PGCert) in Project Management
- Postgraduate Diploma (PGDip) in Project Management
