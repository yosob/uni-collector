## Course Overview

MA Design Management is a one-year full-time postgraduate programme at Ravensbourne University London. The course equips students with the skills and knowledge to manage design processes, lead creative teams, and drive innovation within organisations. It bridges the gap between design practice and business strategy, preparing graduates for leadership roles in the creative and cultural industries.

> ⚠️ **Note**: This course is **not available for 2026 entry**. The university advises prospective students to browse related postgraduate courses.

## Why Study This Degree?

1. Develop a comprehensive understanding of design management principles and their application in professional contexts
2. Learn to lead and manage creative teams and design projects from conception to delivery
3. Gain strategic thinking skills to align design activities with business objectives and organisational goals
4. Explore the intersection of design innovation, sustainability, and responsible business practice
5. Build professional networks through collaboration with industry partners and peers

## Key Study Topics

- Design management frameworks, methodologies, and best practices for leading creative processes
- Strategic design thinking and its role in driving organisational innovation and competitive advantage
- Project management techniques tailored to design and creative industry contexts
- Business strategy, financial management, and entrepreneurship within the creative economy
- Sustainability and ethical considerations in design management and leadership
- Research methods and critical analysis applied to design management challenges
- Professional practice and industry engagement through live projects and case studies

## Entry Requirements

**Home**: A lower second-class honours degree (2:2) or equivalent non-UK qualification in a relevant subject, or an equivalent professional qualification in a related field. CV/Resume required.

**International**: Must meet country-specific entry requirements. Academic qualifications and CV/Resume required.

**English language**: IELTS 6.0 overall (minimum 5.5 in each sub-test) or CEFR Level B2 in each component.

**Selection criteria**: Applicants should demonstrate skills and knowledge associated with design management. The university considers enthusiasm, creative process management, previous study, and professional skill-set holistically.

## Schedule

Classes are Monday to Friday, between 9:00am and 9:00pm, with some evening sessions included.

## Learning Outcomes

Upon completing the course, students will have:

- Strategic design management skills to lead creative projects and teams effectively
- Business acumen to bridge design practice with organisational objectives
- Confidence to drive innovation and manage change in professional design contexts
