## Course Overview

MA International Film and TV Business is a one-year full-time master's programme at Ravensbourne University London — the first of its kind worldwide to focus specifically on the international business of both film and television. The course combines academic insight with practical learning, preparing students to navigate development, financing, production, distribution, and exhibition across global screen markets.

Students will immerse themselves in the dynamics, power structures and opportunities that define the global screen economy, exploring each stage of the value chain — from development and financing through production, distribution and exhibition.

## Why Study This Degree?

1. Explore the creative, financial, strategic and industry intelligence shaping contemporary film and television
2. Investigate cutting-edge digital platforms and tools driving modern screen production, distribution, broadcasting and exhibition
3. Work on live briefs that reflect real-world challenges spanning the entire industry value chain
4. Understand how to build meaningful connections with creatives, producers, executives and international partners

## Key Study Topics

- Building understanding of the global film and television business and the financial and creative approaches needed to thrive
- Examining how major and independent studios function, their business strategies and operational structures
- Studying all elements in the value chain — from development, through production, to distribution, theatrical exhibition, streaming, linear television and all other platforms
- Exploring the importance of connecting films and television works with their target audience through marketing, sales and distribution
- Developing "good work" skills for the audiovisual sector to enable fairer, more diverse and inclusive work practices
- Looking at digital technologies in the audiovisual sector with a focus on streaming services and the opportunities and challenges of artificial intelligence
- Exploring the major financial and regulatory frameworks governing the film and television industry worldwide
- Identifying global creative and business strategies shaped by responsibility, representation and audience awareness

## Entry Requirements

**Home applicants**: A higher second-class honours degree (2:1) or equivalent non-UK qualification in a relevant subject, or an equivalent professional qualification in a related field. CV/Resume required. Applicants should ideally have at least two years of experience in a related or relevant industry. Applicants directly from an undergraduate degree without professional experience must demonstrate a strong understanding of the chosen subject area.

**International applicants**: Must meet country-specific entry requirements and submit academic qualifications along with CV/Resume.

**English language**: IELTS 6.0 overall (minimum 5.5 in each sub-test) or CEFR Level B2 in each component.

**Selection criteria**: Applicants from a wide range of backgrounds and experience are welcome. Demonstrated enthusiasm for the subject area, aptitude for learning and continued professional development through a research-led approach are valued.
