curriculum_summary: 'Year 1 (Level 4): Introduction to the different aspects of built
  environment study, the design process and underpinning skills and theory. The first
  two terms comprise a largely shared curriculum across BA (Hons) IDEAs, BA (Hons)
  Architecture, and BA (Hons) Urban Landscape Architecture. In the third term a specialist
  Landscape Unit is offered. Modules include Specialist Study 1: Structure, Specialist
  Study 2: Introduction to Construction, Specialist Study 3: Introduction to Landscape
  Architecture, Architectural Theory and History, and Introduction to Professional
  and Business Practice.

  Year 2 (Level 5): Shifts towards interpretation of knowledge and application of
  skills acquired in Level 4. All Design Studio units are specialist units for Urban
  Landscape, while History and Theory units are shared with other courses. Briefs
  encourage collaboration as part of a design team. Modules include Design Studio:
  Site and Ecology, Design Studio: Urban and Social Ecologies, Design Studio: Scales
  of Urbanism, and Urbanism: History and Theory of the City.

  Year 3 (Level 6): Culminate learning in a final major design project making key
  urban landscape design decisions. Produce a long-form dissertation showcasing research
  and critical thinking. Graduate with a portfolio demonstrating ability to address
  challenges such as dense populations and resource competition. Modules include Dissertation,
  Major Project, Negotiated Brief, and Preparing for Professional Practice.
