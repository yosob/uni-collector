  design directives, and current industry software and technology, with emphasis on
  understanding fashion with a focus on signature and direction. Modules: Concept
  1: Research, Design and Portfolio, Form and Silhouette 1, Concept 2: Research, Design
  and Portfolio, Professional Life Practice: Developing your Practice, Professional
  Life Practice: Exploring your Practice.


  Year 2 (Level 5): Greater understanding of creative collaboration and building a
  skills-based portfolio. Work placement supported in the final term. Students solidify
  ideas about signature pieces and brand. Modules: Industry Project 1: Product Innovation,
  Industry Project 2: Innovation and Collaboration, Industry Project 3: Contemporary
  Tailoring, Professional Life Practice: Applying your Practice, Work-based learning.
  Digital Technology for Fashion pathway available: Digital Industry Project 1–3,
  Professional Life Practice: Applying your Practice, Work-based learning.


  Year 3 (Level 6): Self-directed design project ready for show or exhibition at the
  degree show. Contextual studies research in a long-form dissertation. Industry-ready
  portfolio and external competitions. Modules: Pre-collection: Concept Development
  and Prototyping, Professional Life Practice: Situating your Practice, Final Collection:
  Realisation, Portfolio. Digital Technology for Fashion pathway: Digital Pre-collection:
  Concept Development and Prototyping, Professional Life Practice: Situating your
  Practice, Digital Final Collection: Realisation, Digital Portfolio.
